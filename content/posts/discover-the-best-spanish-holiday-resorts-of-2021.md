Discover the Best Spanish Holiday Resorts of 2021

Spain is a popular holiday destination for many people around the world. With its warm climate, sandy beaches, and cultural heritage, it’s easy to see why. In this travel article, we’ll explore some of the best Spanish holiday resorts of 2021.

1\. Ibiza

Ibiza is known for its lively nightlife, but it’s also a great destination for families and couples. Its blue seas and sandy beaches are perfect for sunbathing and swimming. The island also has plenty of historic landmarks to explore, like the ancient city of Dalt Vila and the Es Canar hippy market.

2\. Mallorca

Mallorca is the largest of the Balearic Islands and is popular for its stunning beaches, crystal clear waters, and picturesque landscapes. Visitors can also explore the island’s rich history, which includes Roman and Moorish influences.

3\. Tenerife

Tenerife is one of the Canary Islands and has a rich cultural history. Its beaches and natural parks offer visitors plenty of activities to enjoy, such as hiking, whale watching, and surfing. Other popular attractions include the Teide National Park and the Siam Park water park.

4\. Costa del Sol

Costa del Sol, which means “Coast of the Sun,” lives up to its name with an average of 300 days of sunshine a year. It’s a popular destination for beach lovers, but also has many historic landmarks to explore, like the Alcazaba fortress and the Cathedral of Malaga.

5\. Costa Brava

The Costa Brava is situated in the northeast of Spain and is known for its rugged coastline and clear waters. Visitors can explore charming villages, historical monuments and museums. Other popular activities include hiking the Mediterranean coastal paths and swimming in crystal clear turquoise waters.

6\. Gran Canaria

Gran Canaria is another of the Canary Islands and is known for its sandy beaches and ancient history. It is home to many museums, galleries, and churches, so it’s perfect for those who love culture and history. With its warm climate, Gran Canaria is also a great destination for year-round activities like hiking, biking, and water sports.

7\. Costa Blanca

Costa Blanca is located on the southeastern coast of Spain and boasts endless beaches, charming seaside towns, and historic landmarks. It’s also home to the largest palm grove in Europe, Elche, and the Castle of Santa Barbara.

8\. Majorca

Another Balearic Island, Majorca is known for its sandy beaches, crystal clear waters and stunning natural landscapes. The island also has many historic landmarks to explore, like the Bellver Castle and the Almudaina Palace.

9\. Valencia

Valencia is home to the Ciudad de las Artes y las Ciencias (City of Arts and Sciences), a complex of modern buildings housing museums, galleries, and a planetarium. It’s also known for its beaches and lively nightlife, making it a great destination for both families and party-goers.

10\. Andalusia

Andalusia is a region in southern Spain known for its flamenco dancing, exquisite cuisine, and stunning architecture. It’s home to many famous landmarks, like the Alhambra palace and the Royal Alcazar of Seville, which attract millions of visitors every year.

FAQs:

1\. What is the best time to visit Spain?

Spain is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is from March to May and from September to November. During these months, the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller.

2\. What is the currency used in Spain?

The currency used in Spain is the Euro.

3\. Do I need a visa to visit Spain?

If you are a citizen of the European Union, you do not need a visa to visit Spain. However, if you are from a non-EU country, you may need a visa depending on the length and purpose of your stay.

4\. Is it safe to visit Spain?

Spain is generally a safe country to visit, but it’s always best to be cautious and aware of your surroundings, especially in major cities and tourist areas.

5\. What is the official language of Spain?

The official language of Spain is Spanish, but many people also speak English and other languages in tourist areas.
