A Perfect Roman Holiday: A Week-Long Itinerary for Your Trip to Italy's Capital

Rome, the capital city of Italy, is a dream destination for many. With its stunning architecture, rich culture, and mouthwatering cuisine, the city is a must-visit for every traveler. Rome is a city that is rich in history and art, and there is no shortage of things to do and see. Whether you are a history buff, a foodie, or an art lover, Rome is sure to delight.

To make the most of your trip to Rome, here is a one-week itinerary that covers all the top sights and experiences.

Day 1: The Vatican

Begin your Roman holiday with a trip to the Vatican. The Vatican City is nestled within Rome and is home to some of the most iconic artwork in the world, including the Sistine Chapel. Visit St. Peter's Basilica, gaze upon Michelangelo's masterpiece, and wander through the Vatican Museums.

Day 2: Colosseum and Roman Forum

On day two, start your Roman adventure at the Colosseum, which can hold up to 50,000 spectators. It is a must-visit for any history lover. Afterward, wander through the Roman Forum, the ancient city center of Rome where you can marvel at well-preserved arches, columns and temples.

Day 3: Appian Way & Catacombs

For day three, escape the crowds and head to the Appian Way, one of the oldest roads in Rome. Here, you can visit the Catacombs and explore the underground burial chambers of early Christians and Jewish individuals.

Day 4: Trastevere

Spend day four exploring Trastevere, one of Rome's most charming neighborhoods. It is located across the Tiber river, and it is filled with cobblestone streets, colorful buildings, and exquisite restaurants.

Day 5: Piazza Navona, Trevi Fountain and Pantheon

Venture out and explore the picturesque Piazza Navona, home to three fountains that date back to the 1600s, indulge in Italian gelato, toss a coin in the iconic Trevi Fountain, and end the day at the Pantheon, a former pagan temple that dates back to the year 125 AD.

Day 6: Borghese Gallery & Gardens

For day six, a visit to the Borghese Gallery and Gardens is a must. It houses one of the best art collections in Rome, including pieces from Raphael, Caravaggio, and Bernini. After spending your morning admiring the art, take a relaxing stroll through the stunning Borghese Gardens.

Day 7: Testaccio Neighborhood

End your trip with a visit to Testaccio, a vibrant neighborhood filled with delicious food and great nightlife. It is home to some of the best Roman street food vendors, including the world-famous Trapizzino stall.
