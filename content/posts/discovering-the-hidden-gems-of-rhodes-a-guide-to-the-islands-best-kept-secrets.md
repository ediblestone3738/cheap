Rhodes is a Greek island located in the southeastern Aegean Sea. Its rich history and diverse culture make it an amazing travel destination. Rhodes is famous for its beautiful beaches, ancient sites, and stunning scenery. While many of the island's most popular tourist attractions are well known, it is also home to many hidden gems that are waiting to be discovered. This guide will help you find the best kept secrets of Rhodes and make your trip an unforgettable experience.

Hidden Gems

1\. Monolithos Castle

The Monolithos Castle is one of Rhodes' most underrated treasures. Located on the west coast of the island, this castle offers panoramic views of the Aegean Sea. The castle was built in the 15th century by the Knights of Saint John and was used to protect the island from invaders. Today, the castle is in ruins, but its impressive walls and towers are still worth a visit.

2\. Profitis Ilias Mountain

Profitis Ilias is the highest mountain on Rhodes and offers unparalleled views of the island. You can take a scenic drive up to the top or hike up the mountain for a more challenging adventure. At the top, you can visit a small chapel dedicated to the prophet Elijah.

Afandou Beach is a beautiful, unspoiled beach located on the east coast of Rhodes. This beach is less crowded than some of the more popular beaches on the island, making it a great place to relax and soak up the sun. The beach is also home to a variety of water sports activities, such as windsurfing and jet skiing.

5\. Seven Springs

Seven Springs is a natural oasis located in the heart of Rhodes. This hidden gem is a perfect spot to escape the bustling crowds and enjoy the peace and quiet of nature. You can wander along the wooden paths that wind through the forest and around the lake, or take a refreshing dip in the crystal-clear waters of the natural spring.

6\. Fourni Beach

Fourni Beach is a hidden gem located on the east coast of Rhodes. This secluded beach is surrounded by stunning cliffs and offers crystal-clear water and white sand. It is a great place for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.

7\. Mount Tsambika

Mount Tsambika is a hidden gem located on the east coast of Rhodes. This mountain offers stunning views of the sea and the surrounding countryside. You can take a scenic drive up to the top or hike up the mountain for a more challenging adventure. At the top, you can visit a small chapel dedicated to the Virgin Mary.

8\. Kolymbia Beach

Kolymbia Beach is a beautiful, unspoiled beach located on the east coast of Rhodes. This beach is less crowded than some of the more popular beaches on the island, making it a great place to relax and soak up the sun. The beach is also home to a variety of water sports activities, such as windsurfing and kayaking.

9\. Kallithea Springs

Kallithea Springs is a hidden gem located on the east coast of Rhodes. These natural springs were used as a therapeutic spa by the ancient Greeks and Romans, and have been restored to their former glory. The stunning architecture and crystal-clear waters make it a perfect spot for swimming and relaxation.

10\. Lindos

Lindos is a picturesque village located on the east coast of Rhodes. This charming village is full of winding alleys and whitewashed houses that lead up to the ancient Acropolis. The Acropolis offers breathtaking views of the sea and the surrounding countryside.
