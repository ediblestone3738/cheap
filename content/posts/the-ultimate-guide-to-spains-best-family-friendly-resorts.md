Spain is one of the most popular holiday destinations in Europe, and it's easy to see why. With its sunshine, stunning coastline, and fantastic food, it's the perfect place for a family vacation. If you're planning a family trip to Spain, here's our ultimate guide to the best family-friendly resorts in the country.

1\. Costa Brava
The Costa Brava is a beautiful stretch of coastline that runs from the town of Blanes to the French border. It's a popular destination for families, thanks to its beaches, watersports, and great restaurants. One of the best resorts in the area is Lloret de Mar, which offers a mix of outdoor activities and cultural attractions.

2\. Costa del Sol
The Costa del Sol is another popular choice for families. Located in the south of Spain, it offers plenty of sunshine and warm weather throughout the year. Resorts like Marbella and Estepona are perfect for families, with their beaches, water parks, and zoos.

3\. Tenerife
Tenerife is one of the Canary Islands and offers year-round sunshine and stunning landscapes. It's also home to several family-friendly resorts, including Costa Adeje and Los Cristianos. The island is known for its water parks, theme parks, and hiking trails, making it a great destination for families with a range of interests.

4\. Costa Blanca
The Costa Blanca is home to several family-friendly resorts, including Benidorm, Alicante, and Torrevieja. Each offers plenty of activities for families, including beaches, water parks, and golf courses. The area is also known for its culture and history, with several museums and historical sites to explore.

5\. Mallorca
Mallorca is a popular holiday destination for families thanks to its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and warm climate. The island is home to several family-friendly resorts, including Alcudia and Palma Nova. Families can enjoy a range of activities, from water sports to hiking, and there are plenty of child-friendly restaurants and cafes.

6\. Menorca
Menorca is another of the Balearic Islands offering beautiful beaches and stunning landscapes. It's quieter than Mallorca and Ibiza, making it a great choice for families looking for a more relaxed holiday. Resorts like Cala Galdana and Son Bou offer a range of activities for families, including water sports, horse riding, and cycling.

7\. Salou
Located on the Costa Dorada, Salou is a popular resort for families. It's home to several theme parks, including PortAventura World, which is one of the biggest theme parks in Europe. There are also plenty of beaches, water parks, and outdoor activities to enjoy.

8\. Lanzarote
Lanzarote is another of the Canary Islands and offers year-round sunshine and stunning landscapes. It's home to several family-friendly resorts, including Costa Teguise and Playa Blanca. The island is known for its volcanic landscapes and offers plenty of outdoor activities, including hiking, surfing, and windsurfing.

9\. Ibiza
Ibiza is known for its nightlife, but it's also a great destination for families. Resorts like Santa Eulalia and Playa d'en Bossa offer a range of activities for families to enjoy, including beaches, water sports, and boat trips.

10\. Benalmadena
Located on the Costa del Sol, Benalmadena is a popular resort for families. It's home to several attractions, including Tivoli World, which is a theme park, and Sea Life Benalmadena, which is an aquarium. There are also plenty of beaches, restaurants, and bars.

FAQs

Q: Are the resorts in Spain suitable for families with young children?
A: Yes, many of the resorts in Spain are perfect for families with young children, offering facilities such as kids' clubs and children's pools.

Q: What kind of activities can families do in Spain?
A: Families can enjoy a range of activities in Spain, including water sports, theme parks, outdoor activities such as hiking and cycling, and cultural attractions such as museums and historical sites.

Q: Is it safe to travel to Spain with children?
A: Yes, Spain is a safe destination for families. However, it's always important to take precautions when travelling with children, such as keeping an eye on them in busy areas and staying hydrated in hot weather.
