New Delhi, the capital of India, is a bustling metropolis that has something to offer for everyone. The city is a mix of modernity, history, and spirituality, making it an ideal destination no matter what you’re looking for. From the magnificent monuments to the bustling markets, New Delhi is a city that is full of life and energy. Here are the best things to do in New Delhi.

1\. Visit the Red Fort
The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most iconic landmarks in New Delhi. Its stunning architecture and intricate details make it an impressive sight to see. Built in the 17th century by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan, the fort was the imperial residence of the Mughal dynasty until 1857.

2\. Take a stroll through India Gate
India Gate, the national monument of India, is a war memorial for soldiers who died in World War I. It is located in the heart of New Delhi and is the perfect place for a leisurely stroll. You can also enjoy a picnic in the surrounding lawns or take a boat ride on the nearby lake.

3\. Explore the Qutub Minar Complex
The Qutub Minar is a towering monument that is considered to be one of the finest examples of Mughal architecture. The complex comprises of several other ancient structures, including the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque and the Iron Pillar, which is known for its rust-resistant properties. The Qutub Minar Complex is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is worth exploring for its historical and architectural significance.

4\. Visit the Lotus Temple
The Lotus Temple, also known as the Bahai House of Worship, is a unique structure that is shaped like a lotus flower. It is a prominent landmark of New Delhi and attracts tourists from around the world. The temple is open to people of all religions and is a place for meditation and prayer.

5\. Take a tour of the Rashtrapati Bhavan
The Rashtrapati Bhavan, the official residence of the President of India, is a grand and majestic building that boasts of stunning architecture and intricate details. Visitors can take a guided tour of the building and its grounds, which include the Mughal Gardens, the main lawn, and the Durbar Hall.

6\. Go shopping in Chandni Chowk
Chandni Chowk, one of the oldest and busiest markets in Old Delhi, is a must-visit for all shopping enthusiasts. The market is famous for its textiles, jewelry, electronic goods, and traditional Indian sweets. Walking through the narrow alleys and bazaars of Chandni Chowk is an experience that is not to be missed.

7\. Enjoy a street food feast in Delhi
Delhi is famous for its street food, and there are several places where you can indulge in some of the city’s most delicious delicacies. From the chaat at Chandni Chowk to the parathas at Paranthe Wali Gali, Delhi’s street food is a gastronomic delight that is sure to leave you craving for more.

8\. Discover the history of the Humayun’s Tomb
Humayun’s Tomb is a magnificent mausoleum that was built in the 16th century for the Mughal emperor Humayun. The tomb is a fine example of Mughal architecture and is surrounded by beautiful gardens and water fountains. It is also considered to be a precursor to the Taj Mahal.

9\. Explore the art and culture at the National Museum
The National Museum of India is a treasure trove of Indian art, history, and culture. The museum has an extensive collection of artifacts, including sculptures, paintings, and manuscripts, dating back to over 5,000 years. It is a must-visit for anyone interested in learning about India’s rich cultural heritage.

10\. Take a stroll through Connaught Place
Connaught Place, also known as CP, is a popular shopping and entertainment hub in the heart of New Delhi. The area is surrounded by colonial architecture and has some of the best restaurants, bars, and cafes in the city. It is also home to several famous monuments, including the Jantar Mantar and the Gurudwara Bangla Sahib.

FAQs:

Q. What is the best time to visit New Delhi?
A. The best time to visit New Delhi is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and comfortable.

Q. How do I get around New Delhi?
A. New Delhi has a vast network of public transportation, including the metro, buses, and taxis. You can also rent a car or bike to get around.

Q. What is the currency used in New Delhi?
A. The currency used in New Delhi is the Indian Rupee.

Q. What are some of the best places to stay in New Delhi?
A. Some of the best places to stay in New Delhi are The Leela Palace, The Imperial, and The Taj Mahal Hotel.

Q. What are some of the must-try dishes in New Delhi?
A. Some of the must-try dishes in New Delhi are butter chicken, biryani, chaat, and kebabs.
