Uncover the Mysteries of Thailand: A Guide to the Land of Wonders

Thailand is a country located in Southeast Asia and is known for its spiritual richness and mystic landmarks. Its landscapes are incomparable with any other in the world, and the country's cultural significance stretches back over 700 years. If you are looking for a place that is packed with unique culture, stunning landscapes, and enchanting histories, then Thailand should be on top of your list. In this article, we'll be giving you a comprehensive guidebook to help you uncover the mysteries of Thailand.

Getting there

Thailand receives millions of visitors yearly from all corners of the world. The most convenient and efficient way of arriving in Thailand is by air. The Bangkok Suvarnabhumi airport is the major gateway for international flights into the country, with several direct flights from destinations around the world like London, Beijing, and New York. Thailand is also served by low-cost airlines like Air Asia, Nok Air, and Thai Lion Air.

Language

Thai is the official language of Thailand, but most of the locals speak English, especially in the tourist areas. You can learn a few Thai phrases such as "Kop Khun" (thank you) or "Sawatdee ka" (hello) to show some basic courtesy.

Currency

The currency used in Thailand is Thai Baht (THB). One euro is approximately equal to 35 Thai Baht. Credit cards are largely accepted in Thailand. But, keep in mind that some shops and restaurants may only accept cash, so it's always good to have some on hand.

Visa Requirements

Tourists from a majority of countries can enter Thailand for up to 30 days without a visa. Passport holders of some specific countries are also exempt from visa requirements. However, it's best to check visa regulations based on your nationality before traveling to Thailand. You can apply for a visa to Thailand via the Royal Thai Embassy.

Best Time to Visit Thailand

Thailand is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is from November to April when the weather is cooler, the skies are clearer, and the sun shines. The months between May and October are the rainy season, although it still receives plenty of sunshine.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Thailand

Now that you know how to travel to Thailand let's explore some of the most popular tourist attractions that Thailand has to offer:

1\. The Grand Palace in Bangkok

The Grand Palace is a majestic landmark and the most important cultural site in Bangkok. It was constructed in the late 1700s and is an iconic example of the marriage between the Thai traditional and European styles.

2\. Phi Phi Islands

The Phi Phi Islands, situated in the Andaman Sea, are one of Thailand's most stunning attractions. Here you'll find crystal-clear waters and white sandy beaches. It is a popular spot for snorkeling and diving, and you'll find boat tours here that will take you across famous Koh Phi Phi Leh – the island made famous by the movie "The Beach."

3\. Wat Arun - Temple of Dawn

Wat Arun, otherwise called the Temple of Dawn, is a pagoda situated by the Chao Phraya River in Bangkok. It is the most famous landmark in the country and is undoubtedly the most stunning structure in all of Thailand.

4\. The Ancient City of Ayutthaya

Once an ancient Siamese city, Ayutthaya is today a UNESCO World Heritage Site located about 2 hours from Bangkok. Here you can explore ancient temples, ruined palaces, and towering Buddha statues, all dating back to the 14th century.

5\. The Islands of Koh Tao, Koh Samui, and Koh Phangan

The Gulf of Thailand is home to the stunning islands of Koh Tao, Koh Samui, and Koh Phangan. These islands offer an irresistible mix of secluded beaches, turquoise waters, and world-class diving.

6\. The Bridge on the River Kwai

The Bridge on the River Kwai is an iconic landmark, whose history is woven into the fabric of World War II. Considered by many as a symbol of human resilience, the bridge is located in Kanchanaburi.

7\. Chiang Mai

Chiang Mai is a city located in northern Thailand and has been described as the ‘Rose of the North.' It's a place with a rich culture and incredible architectural splendor – a historic city surrounded by stunning mountains and countryside.

8\. Koh Samet

Koh Samet is a popular choice for those seeking sunshine-filled adventures. The island is known for having some of the best beaches in Thailand and is an excellent spot for sunbathing, swimming, and even snorkelling.

9\. The Erawan Waterfall

The Erawan Waterfall is located in the Erawan National Park and is one of the most stunning waterfalls in Thailand. It's a multi-tiered waterfall surrounded by tropical forests, and each level has crystal-clear pools for swimming.

10\. The Ancient City of Sukhothai

Sukhothai is an ancient city and the first capital of Siam. It is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and here you can explore ancient temples, ruined palaces, and sprawling gardens.

FAQs Section

Here are some commonly asked questions and their answers that will help you better navigate your trip to Thailand.

How much does it cost to travel to Thailand?

The cost of traveling to Thailand varies depending on your travel style and budget. On average, you will spend between 30-50 US dollars a day.

What is the main religion in Thailand?

Buddhism is the dominant religion in Thailand, accounting for around 95% of the population.

Is Thailand safe for tourists?

Thailand is generally a safe country to travel in, but as a traveler, you should be mindful of petty theft and scams targeted towards tourists.

What is the best way to get around Thailand?

The best way to travel around Thailand is by train, bus, or ferry. They are convenient and affordable modes of transport.

Can I wear shorts in Thailand?

Yes, wearing shorts is fine in Thailand, but it's recommended to wear something a little more conservative when visiting temples and other religious places.

In Conclusion

To uncover the mysteries of Thailand, immerse yourself in its distinctive culture, exquisite cuisine, and those mystic landmarks. From the pristine beaches to the ancient temples, Thailand is a land like no other. With this guide to Thailand, you're ready to explore one of the most visited countries in the world. Don't forget to take pictures as you make lifetime memories.
