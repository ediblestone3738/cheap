Exploring the Best Hidden Gems in the Czech Republic

The Czech Republic is a landlocked country in Central Europe known for its Gothic architecture, beer, and rich history. However, there is much more to the country than its famous tourist attractions. The Czech Republic is full of hidden gems waiting to be explored. From quaint towns to stunning natural landscapes, there is something for everyone. Here are some of the best-hidden gems to explore in the Czech Republic.

1\. Moravian Karst
The Moravian Karst is a natural wonder located in the eastern part of the Czech Republic. The highlight of the area is the Punkva Caves, which is one of the largest and most beautiful cave systems in the country. Visitors can explore the underground river and see the impressive Macocha Abyss, the deepest sinkhole in Central Europe.

2\. Holasovice
Holasovice is a small village located in southern Bohemia that is often overlooked by visitors. The village is a UNESCO World Heritage site because of its well-preserved 18th-century architecture. Holasovice is the perfect place to experience traditional Czech culture and enjoy the peaceful countryside.

3\. Lednice-Valtice Cultural Landscape
The Lednice-Valtice Cultural Landscape is a stunning area located in the south of the Czech Republic. The area is home to numerous castles, palaces, and gardens, making it a perfect destination for history and architecture enthusiasts. The landscape is also known for its wine, with many vineyards in the area.

4\. Macocha Gorge
Located in the Moravian Karst, the Macocha Gorge is a breathtaking natural wonder. The gorge is over 138 meters deep and is known for its striking rock formations and crystal clear waters. Visitors can take a boat tour through the gorge or hike along the surrounding trails.

FAQs

Q: What is the best time to visit the Czech Republic?
A: The best time to visit the Czech Republic is during the summer months between June and August when the weather is warm and sunny.

Q: What is the currency used in the Czech Republic?
A: The currency used in the Czech Republic is the Czech koruna (CZK).

Q: Is English widely spoken in the Czech Republic?
A: While Czech is the official language of the country, English is widely spoken in tourist areas and major cities.

Q: Do I need a visa to enter the Czech Republic?
A: Citizens of the United States, Canada, Australia, and many other countries do not need a visa to enter the Czech Republic for stays up to 90 days. However, it is always best to check with your country's embassy or consulate for the most up-to-date information.

Q: What is the legal drinking age in the Czech Republic?
A: The legal drinking age in the Czech Republic is 18 years old.
