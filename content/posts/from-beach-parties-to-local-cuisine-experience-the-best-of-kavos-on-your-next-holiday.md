Kavos, located in the southern part of the Greek island of Corfu, is known as one of the top party destinations in Europe. However, there's much more to Kavos than just beach parties. From delicious local cuisine to stunning natural beauty, there's so much to experience in Kavos on your next holiday.

Experience the Best of Kavos through its Beach Parties

One of the most popular things to do in Kavos is to party on the beach. Kavos has a long stretch of sandy shoreline where you can find a variety of beach clubs and bars that offer parties every night. One of the most famous beach clubs in Kavos is Atlantis Beach Club, which features nightly events with international DJs, dancers, and performers. Another popular spot is The Venue, which has an extraordinary cocktail menu, great music, and free entry.

Besides these famous beach clubs, you can also find a myriad of small bars and cafes set up along the beach, making it an ideal place to relax and soak up the sun.

Enjoy the Local Cuisine

If you're seeking local flavours and delicious Greek cuisine, Kavos has a variety of outstanding eateries giving you a chance to taste everything from souvlakis to seafood. Situated in Kavos' busy centre, Sunset Taverna serves fresh fish and traditional Greek dishes at affordable prices. And if you're looking for something a bit more upscale, then you must try out The Steak House in the heart of Kavos. It's a fantastic place to savour a good steak and a bottle of Greek wine.

Take in the Stunning Views

Kavos does not disappoint when it comes to breathtaking natural beauty. There are various idyllic spots to admire the picturesque views, such as the neighbouring island of Paxos, with its blue Waters and white sand beaches. Take a stroll on the wilder, untamed west coast of Corfu, or hike to Mount Pantokrator, located at 906 metres where you can glance at the west coast of the island.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q: What is the best time to visit Kavos?

A: The best time to visit Kavos is from May to October. It's high season, with suitable weather conditions for the beach parties and water sports.

Q: Can we visit Kavos with kids?

A: Kavos is commonly seen as a partying destination, making it less suitable for travelling with kids. Alternatively, Corfu island has some family-friendly destinations which are perferct for spending time with the kids.

Q: Can we find traditional Greek food in Kavos?

A: Yes, there are several typical Greek restaurants in Kavos where you can taste local Greek dishes such as souvlaki, moussaka or a freshly made Greek salad.

Q: What is the drinking age in Kavos?

A: Drinking age in Kavos is 18 years old.

Q: What are the most popular water sports in Kavos?

A: The most trendy water sports in Kavos are Banana boating, wakeboarding, jet skiing, and water skiing.

In conclusion, Kavos offers much more than just beach parties. It's an exquisite destination to enjoy traditional Greek delicacies, admire the natural beauty or try out a variety of thrilling water sports. With its beautiful beaches and warm Mediterranean climate, Kavos is perfect for anyone who wants to experience the best of Greece's bustling nightlife and mesmerising natural scenery.
