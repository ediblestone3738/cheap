Greece has long been a popular destination for couples looking for a dreamy vacation. With stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, charming villages, and a rich history, it's no wonder why. But with over 200 inhabited Greek islands, it can be overwhelming to decide which ones to visit. Here is a couple's guide to the best Greek Islands for a dreamy vacation.

1\. Santorini
Santorini is one of the most well-known Greek Islands for its iconic white-washed buildings perched on the cliffs overlooking the Aegean Sea. The island is famous for its stunning sunsets, picturesque villages, and black-sand beaches. Couples can enjoy walks through the village of Oia, wine tastings at one of the many wineries, and a private yacht tour around the island.

2\. Mykonos
Mykonos is a popular island known for its party scene, but it also has a romantic side. Couples can explore the winding streets of Chora, visit the iconic windmills, and relax on the beautiful beaches. Mykonos is also known for its luxury accommodations and fine dining restaurants.

3\. Crete
Crete is the largest Greek Island, and it offers something for everyone. Couples can explore the ancient ruins of Knossos or hike the Samaria Gorge. Crete also has beautiful beaches, charming villages, and delicious cuisine, making it an ideal destination for a romantic getaway.

4\. Paros
Paros is a lesser-known island that offers a quieter alternative to some of the more popular destinations. The island has stunning beaches, beautiful villages, and a relaxed atmosphere that is perfect for couples looking to unwind. Paros is also known for its delicious food, including fresh fish and locally produced wine.

5\. Naxos
Naxos is another beautiful island that offers a mix of history and relaxation. Couples can explore the ancient ruins of the Temple of Apollo or relax on one of the island's many beaches. Naxos is also known for its traditional villages and excellent food, including local specialties like graviera cheese and potatoes cooked in tomato sauce.

6\. Rhodes
Rhodes is a historic island that is famous for its medieval architecture and ancient ruins. Couples can explore the Old Town of Rhodes or visit the famous Acropolis of Lindos. Rhodes also has beautiful beaches, including the iconic Anthony Quinn Bay.

7\. Corfu
Corfu is a beautiful island located off the northwest coast of Greece. The island has a rich history, including Venetian architecture, Byzantine churches, and ancient ruins. Couples can explore the charming town of Corfu or relax on one of the island's many gorgeous beaches.

8\. Skiathos
Skiathos is a small island known for its stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters. The island has over 60 beaches, ranging from secluded coves to popular spots with water sports and beach bars. Couples can also visit the charming town of Skiathos or take a boat tour around the island.

9\. Zakynthos
Zakynthos, also known as Zante, is a beautiful island with stunning blue waters and sandy beaches. Couples can visit the iconic Navagio Beach, also known as Shipwreck Beach, or explore the charming town of Zakynthos. The island also has a vibrant nightlife and delicious cuisine, including fresh seafood and local wine.

10\. Folegandros
Folegandros is a small island known for its picturesque villages, stunning beaches, and relaxed atmosphere. Couples can explore the traditional town of Chora or relax on one of the island's many beaches. Folegandros also has excellent hiking trails and delicious local cuisine, including fresh fish and locally produced cheese.

FAQs

Q: What is the best time to visit the Greek Islands?
A: The best time to visit the Greek Islands is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny. July and August are the busiest months, so it's best to avoid those if you're looking for a quieter vacation.

Q: Is it safe to travel to the Greek Islands?
A: Yes, the Greek Islands are generally safe for travelers. However, it's always a good idea to take precautions, such as avoiding carrying large amounts of cash and following safety guidelines when swimming.

Q: What is the currency used in Greece?
A: The currency used in Greece is the euro.

Q: Do I need a visa to travel to Greece?
A: If you are a citizen of the EU, US, or many other countries, you do not need a visa to travel to Greece for up to 90 days. However, it's always best to check with your embassy before traveling.
