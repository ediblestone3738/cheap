# Nice Christmas Towns To Visit

The holiday season is a magical time of year, and there's no better way to get into the festive spirit than by visiting a charming Christmas town. From twinkling lights to bustling markets, these towns offer a delightful escape into a winter wonderland. Here are some of the nicest Christmas towns to visit, each offering its own unique blend of holiday cheer.

## 1. Rothenburg ob der Tauber, Germany

Rothenburg ob der Tauber is a picturesque medieval town that transforms into a Christmas wonderland during the holiday season. Known for its well-preserved architecture and enchanting atmosphere, Rothenburg is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience a traditional German Christmas.

- **Christmas Market**: The Reiterlesmarkt is one of the oldest and most charming Christmas markets in Germany, offering handmade crafts, delicious treats, and festive decorations.
- **Night Watchman Tour**: Join the Night Watchman for a guided tour through the cobblestone streets, learning about the town's history and holiday traditions.
- **Christmas Museum**: Explore the German Christmas Museum to discover the history of Christmas decorations and customs.

## 2. Strasbourg, France

Strasbourg is often referred to as the "Capital of Christmas" and for good reason. This French city boasts one of the oldest and most famous Christmas markets in Europe, dating back to 1570.

- **Christkindelsmärik**: Located in the heart of the city, this market features over 300 stalls selling everything from ornaments to gourmet food.
- **Cathedral Light Show**: The Strasbourg Cathedral is illuminated with a stunning light show that captivates visitors each evening.
- **Alsatian Cuisine**: Indulge in local specialties such as tarte flambée and mulled wine while exploring the festive streets.

## 3. Bruges, Belgium

Bruges is a fairy-tale city that becomes even more enchanting during the Christmas season. With its cobblestone streets, medieval buildings, and canals, Bruges offers a magical setting for holiday celebrations.

- **Ice Skating**: Glide across the ice rink set up in the Market Square, surrounded by historic buildings and festive lights.
- **Christmas Market**: Browse the stalls for unique gifts and enjoy Belgian waffles and hot chocolate.
- **Horse-Drawn Carriage Rides**: Experience the charm of Bruges with a cozy carriage ride through the decorated streets.

## 4. Rovaniemi, Finland

Rovaniemi is the official hometown of Santa Claus and a dream destination for anyone who loves Christmas. Located in the Arctic Circle, this Finnish town offers a truly unique holiday experience.

- **Santa Claus Village**: Meet Santa himself and explore his village, complete with reindeer and elves.
- **Northern Lights**: Witness the breathtaking Aurora Borealis, a natural light display that adds to the magic of the season.
- **Snow Activities**: Enjoy snowmobiling, husky sledding, and other winter sports in the snowy landscape.

## 5. Quebec City, Canada

Quebec City is a charming destination that feels like stepping into a European Christmas card. With its historic architecture and festive decorations, this Canadian city offers a warm and inviting holiday atmosphere.

- **German Christmas Market**: Experience a traditional German market with wooden stalls, festive music, and delicious treats.
- **Old Quebec**: Wander through the cobblestone streets of Old Quebec, adorned with lights and decorations.
- **Winter Carnival**: Although it takes place after Christmas, the Quebec Winter Carnival is a must-see event featuring parades, ice sculptures, and more.

## 6. Colmar, France

Colmar is a quaint Alsatian town that comes alive with holiday spirit during the Christmas season. Its half-timbered houses and canals create a picturesque backdrop for festive celebrations.

- **Christmas Markets**: Colmar hosts several markets throughout the town, each with its own unique charm and offerings.
- **Little Venice**: Stroll through the Little Venice district, beautifully decorated with lights and ornaments.
- **Wine Tasting**: Sample local Alsatian wines and enjoy the region's culinary delights.

## 7. Tallinn, Estonia

Tallinn is a hidden gem in Northern Europe that offers a magical Christmas experience. The city's medieval Old Town is a UNESCO World Heritage site and provides a stunning setting for holiday festivities.

- **Christmas Market**: Held in the Town Hall Square, this market features traditional crafts, food, and a towering Christmas tree.
- **Historic Architecture**: Explore the well-preserved medieval buildings and cobblestone streets, all adorned with festive decorations.
- **Winter Activities**: Enjoy ice skating, sledding, and other winter sports in the nearby parks and forests.

## 8. Vienna, Austria

Vienna is a city that knows how to celebrate Christmas in style. With its grand architecture, classical music, and festive markets, Vienna offers a sophisticated holiday experience.

- **Christkindlmarkt**: Visit the market at Rathausplatz for a wide selection of gifts, food, and entertainment.
- **Concerts**: Attend a Christmas concert at one of Vienna's famous concert halls or churches.
- **Coffee Houses**: Warm up with a cup of Viennese coffee and a slice of Sachertorte in one of the city's historic coffee houses.

## 9. Prague, Czech Republic

Prague is a city that exudes charm and romance, especially during the Christmas season. Its stunning architecture and vibrant markets make it a perfect holiday destination.

- **Old Town Square Market**: Explore the bustling market in the heart of the city, offering handmade gifts, traditional foods, and live performances.
- **Charles Bridge**: Walk across the iconic Charles Bridge, beautifully lit and decorated for the holidays.
- **Czech Traditions**: Experience local customs such as the lighting of the Advent wreath and the celebration of St. Nicholas Day.

## 10. Edinburgh, Scotland

Edinburgh is a city that embraces the festive spirit with open arms. From its historic streets to its lively markets, Edinburgh offers a warm and welcoming holiday experience.

- **Edinburgh Christmas Market**: Located in East Princes Street Gardens, this market features a variety of stalls, rides, and attractions.
- **Hogmanay**: Celebrate New Year's Eve with Edinburgh's famous Hogmanay festival, featuring fireworks, concerts, and street parties.
- **Edinburgh Castle**: Visit the iconic castle, beautifully illuminated and offering stunning views of the city.

## 11. Nuremberg, Germany

Nuremberg is home to one of the most famous Christmas markets in the world, the Christkindlesmarkt. This German city offers a rich history and a festive atmosphere that is hard to beat.

- **Christkindlesmarkt**: Browse the stalls for traditional crafts, gingerbread, and mulled wine.
- **Nuremberg Castle**: Explore the historic castle, offering panoramic views of the city and its holiday decorations.
- **Toy Museum**: Discover the history of toys and enjoy special holiday exhibits.

## 12. Salzburg, Austria

Salzburg is a city that combines stunning natural beauty with rich cultural heritage. During the Christmas season, it becomes a magical destination filled with music and festive cheer.

- **Salzburg Christmas Market**: Held in the historic city center, this market offers a wide range of gifts, food, and entertainment.
- **Sound of Music Tour**: Explore the filming locations of the famous movie, beautifully decorated for the holidays.
- **Advent Singing**: Attend a traditional Advent singing concert, showcasing the region's musical talent.

## 13. Copenhagen, Denmark

Copenhagen is a city that knows how to create a cozy and inviting holiday atmosphere. With its charming streets and festive markets, Copenhagen offers a delightful Christmas experience.

- **Tivoli Gardens**: Visit the famous amusement park, transformed into a winter wonderland with lights, decorations, and rides.
- **Christmas Markets**: Explore the various markets throughout the city, offering unique gifts and delicious treats.
- **Hygge**: Embrace the Danish concept of hygge by enjoying cozy moments with friends and family.

## 14. Krakow, Poland

Krakow is a city that offers a rich history and a vibrant holiday atmosphere. Its stunning architecture and festive markets make it a perfect destination for Christmas celebrations.

- **Main Market Square**: Visit the market in the heart of the city, offering traditional crafts, food, and entertainment.
- **Wawel Castle**: Explore the historic castle, beautifully decorated for the holidays.
- **Nativity Scenes**: Discover the unique Polish tradition of creating elaborate nativity scenes, displayed throughout the city.

## 15. New York City, USA

New York City is a destination that needs no introduction, especially during the holiday season. With its iconic landmarks and festive events, NYC offers a holiday experience like no other.

- **Rockefeller Center**: See the famous Christmas tree and go ice skating at the iconic rink.
- **Holiday Windows**: Admire the elaborate holiday window displays at department stores like Macy's and Saks Fifth Avenue.
- **Broadway Shows**: Catch a holiday-themed show or a classic Broadway performance.

These Christmas towns offer a variety of experiences, from traditional markets to unique cultural events. Whether you're looking for a cozy getaway or a bustling city adventure, there's a perfect Christmas town waiting for you to explore. Embrace the holiday spirit and create unforgettable memories in these enchanting destinations.
