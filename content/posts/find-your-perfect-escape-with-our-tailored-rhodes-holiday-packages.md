Find Your Perfect Escape with Our Tailored Rhodes Holiday Packages!

Rhodes, the beautiful island located in the southeast of Greece, is a perfect destination for those seeking both adventure and relaxation on their holiday. Our holiday packages offer you an opportunity to explore the island’s charming local culture, rich history and beautiful landscapes, and more. You'll visit iconic sites such as the Rhodes City, Palace of the Grand Master Knights of Rhodes, Tsambika Beach, and many others. Here are a few of the top tourist attractions you should experience while in Rhodes:

1\. Old Town of Rhodes: The Old Town of Rhodes is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most popular tourist attractions in Rhodes. The town is surrounded by fortified walls dating back to the 14th century and dotted with beautiful cobbled streets, monuments and architecture.

2\. Palace of the Grand Master Knights of Rhodes: located at the heart of Old Town Rhodes, the palace was built in the 14th century and served as the seat of the Grand Master of the order of Knights of St. John. Today, it’s a museum that helps visitors understand the rich history and culture of Rhodes.

3\. The Butterfly Valley: The Butterfly Valley is a unique natural phenomenon where thousands of butterflies gather on the island between June and September every year. It’s an exceptional place to observe the grace and beauty of these beautiful creatures.

4\. Lindos: Lindos is a popular tourist destination located in the southeast of Rhodes. It’s known for its rich history, stunning views of the Aegean Sea, and Acropolis, which is one of the must-visit sites on the island.

5\. Tsambika Beach: Tsambika Beach is one of the most beautiful beaches on the island. This stunning white sandy beach is crowned by a green hill on one side and crystal-clear water on the other.

6\. Filerimos Monastery: Filerimos is a beautiful monastery in Rhodes known for its impressive architecture and gardens. It’s a perfect spot for those who love peace and quiet surrounded by stunning scenery.

7\. Lindos Acropolis: The Acropolis of Lindos is one of the most impressive monuments of ancient Greece. It’s a must-visit site for those seeking history and culture in Rhodes.

8\. Seven Springs: The Seven Springs is a beautiful spot just outside the village of Kolymbia. It’s a natural corridor with seven underground streams that form crystal-clear lakes. This is a perfect destination for those who love nature and adventure.

9\. Rhodes City: Rhodes City is the capital of the island and offers tourists some of the best shopping, dining and nightlife opportunities. You'll also find picturesque marinas, lively tavernas and street fairs that make for a lively atmosphere.

10\. Kallithea: Kallithea is a popular destination located in the east of Rhodes. It’s known for its beautiful thermal springs, known to have therapeutic properties that make it great for relaxation.

FAQs

1\. How to get to Rhodes Island?

You can get to Rhodes Island by plane or ferry. It has a well-connected airport with regular flights from many European cities. If you’re coming from Athens, you can take a ferry which takes about 12 hours to get to Rhodes.

2\. What time of year is best to visit Rhodes Island?

The best time to visit Rhodes is between May to June and September to October when the weather is mild and crowds are less. If you prefer warmer weather and more activity, then visiting between July and August is perfect.

3\. What is the official currency?

The official currency is the Euro.

4\. What language do people speak in Rhodes Island?

The official language on the island is Greek, but many locals speak English fluently.
