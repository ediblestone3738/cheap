Discover the Untouched Beauty of Kefalonia this Summer

Kefalonia, also known as Cephalonia, is a gorgeous island located in the Ionian Sea just west of mainland Greece. Known for its turquoise waters, lush greenery, and mountainous terrain, this island is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the true beauty of Greece.

In this article, we’ll explore the best things to see and do in Kefalonia, as well as answer some of the most frequently asked questions about the island.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Kefalonia

1\. Myrtos Beach - This breathtaking beach is one of the most photographed beaches in the world thanks to its crystal-clear waters and white sand. The surrounding cliffs add to the beach’s picturesque scenery.

2\. Melissani Cave - This underground cave features an underground lake that’s said to be the home of the nymphs. Visitors can take a tour on a boat and admire the stunning array of colors reflected in the water.

3\. Assos Village - This charming fishing village is home to colorful houses, narrow streets, and a castle overlooking the bay. Visitors can enjoy the calm atmosphere while sipping on a traditional Greek coffee.

4\. Drogarati Cave - This cave is over 150 million years old and features stalactites and stalagmites. Visitors can take a guided tour and admire the impressive formations.

5\. Fiskardo Village - This picturesque village is a popular spot for yachts to dock and grab a meal or shop. It has also maintained its traditional architecture despite its popularity.

6\. Mount Ainos - This mountain is the highest on the island, reaching 1,628 meters. Visitors can hike to the top for breathtaking views of the island.

7\. Saint Gerasimos Monastery - This monastery is dedicated to the patron saint of Kefalonia, Saint Gerasimos. Visitors can learn about the history of the monastery while admiring its beautiful architecture.

8\. Agios Thomas Beach - This beach is located on the western coast of the island and is known for its crystal-clear waters and stunning sunsets.

9\. Argostoli - This is the capital city of Kefalonia and is home to beautiful architecture, a lively local market, and an array of restaurants and cafes.

10\. Petani Beach - This beautiful beach is located on the western side of the island and is known for its turquoise waters and surrounding cliffs.

Frequently Asked Questions about Kefalonia

Q: How do I get to Kefalonia?

A: Kefalonia can be accessed by plane or ferry. There are flights available from Athens and other international destinations. Ferries can be taken from nearby islands or the Greek mainland.

Q: When is the best time to visit Kefalonia?

A: The best time to visit Kefalonia is from May to September, when the weather is warm and the island is most vibrant. July and August are the busiest months, so visitors should plan accordingly.

Q: What currency is used in Kefalonia?

A: The currency used in Kefalonia is the Euro.

Q: What language is spoken in Kefalonia?

A: The official language of Kefalonia is Greek, but many locals also speak English.

Q: What are the best outdoor activities in Kefalonia?

A: Kefalonia is perfect for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, swimming, and snorkeling. The island’s stunning natural beauty makes it an ideal location for exploring the outdoors.

Q: What is the local cuisine like in Kefalonia?

A: The local cuisine in Kefalonia focuses on fresh seafood, meats, and vegetables. Popular dishes include moussaka, souvlaki, and fresh grilled fish.

Q: Is Kefalonia safe for tourists?

A: Kefalonia is a very safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take normal safety precautions.

Conclusion

Kefalonia is a true gem of the Greek islands. With its stunning beaches, breathtaking natural scenery, and rich cultural heritage, it’s the perfect destination for anyone looking to experience the beauty of Greece. From exploring the island’s picturesque villages to hiking its rugged mountain terrain or simply lounging on its beautiful beaches, Kefalonia has something for everyone. So why not plan your next summer getaway to discover the untouched beauty of Kefalonia?
