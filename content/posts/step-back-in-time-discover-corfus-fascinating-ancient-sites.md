Step Back in Time: Discover Corfu's Fascinating Ancient Sites

Corfu is an island located in the Ionian Sea, on the west coast of Greece. It is a popular tourist destination, known for its beautiful beaches, crystal-clear water, and lush vegetation. However, what many people don't know about Corfu is that it is also rich in history and ancient sites. In this article, we will take a closer look at Corfu's ancient sites and how they offer visitors a unique opportunity to step back in time.

Corfu's ancient history can be traced back to the 8th century BC when it was first inhabited by the Corinthians. Throughout the centuries, the island was ruled by various empires, including the Romans, the Byzantines, and the Venetians. Each of these civilizations left their mark on Corfu's landscape, and today visitors can explore the ruins and remnants of their architectural legacy.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Corfu's Ancient Sites

1\. The Old Town of Corfu
The Old Town of Corfu is a UNESCO-listed site that features a mix of Venetian, French, and British architecture. Visitors can wander through the town's narrow streets and alleys, exploring its historic buildings, churches, and squares.

2\. Achilleion Palace
Achilleion Palace was built in the late 19th century by Empress Elisabeth of Austria. It is situated on a hill overlooking the sea, and its gardens are filled with statues inspired by Greek mythology.

3\. Mon Repos
Mon Repos is a neoclassical villa that was built in the 19th century by the British Commissioner of Corfu. It is surrounded by lush gardens and offers stunning views of the sea.

4\. Angelokastro Castle
Angelokastro Castle is a Byzantine fortress that sits atop a rocky hill overlooking the sea. Visitors can explore its walls and towers, which offer panoramic views of Corfu's coastline.

5\. Kassiopi Castle
Kassiopi Castle is a 14th-century fortress that was built by the Venetians. It offers stunning views of the Ionian Sea and the town of Kassiopi.

6\. Paleokastritsa Monastery
Paleokastritsa Monastery is situated on a hill overlooking Paleokastritsa Bay. Visitors can explore the monastery's church, which features stunning frescoes and icons.

7\. Corfu Archaeological Museum
The Corfu Archaeological Museum is housed in a neoclassical building that was built in the 1960s. It features artifacts from Corfu's ancient history, including pottery, sculptures, and coins.

8\. Roman Baths
The Roman Baths are located near the village of Benitses and were built in the 2nd century AD. Visitors can explore the ruins of the baths, which offer insights into the lives of Roman citizens.

9\. Fontana d'oro
Fontana d'oro is a 17th-century Venetian fountain located in the heart of Corfu Town. It features elaborate carvings and is surrounded by historic buildings and shops.

10\. Port Venetian Fortress
Port Venetian Fortress was built by the Venetians in the 16th century to protect the island's harbor. Visitors can explore its walls and towers, which offer panoramic views of the sea and the town of Corfu.
