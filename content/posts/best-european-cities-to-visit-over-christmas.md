# Best European Cities To Visit Over Christmas

Europe is a magical place during the Christmas season, with its historic cities adorned in twinkling lights, festive markets, and a warm, inviting atmosphere that captures the spirit of the holidays. Whether you're looking for a winter wonderland, traditional holiday markets, or unique cultural experiences, Europe offers a plethora of destinations that come alive with Christmas cheer. Here are some of the best European cities to visit over Christmas.

## Vienna, Austria

Vienna is a city that truly embraces the Christmas spirit. Known for its imperial architecture and rich cultural history, Vienna transforms into a winter wonderland during the holiday season.

- **Christmas Markets**: Vienna is home to some of the most famous Christmas markets in Europe. The Christkindlmarkt at Rathausplatz is a must-visit, offering a wide array of handcrafted gifts, festive decorations, and delicious treats like roasted chestnuts and mulled wine.
- **Ice Skating**: The city offers several ice skating rinks, including the popular one in front of the Rathaus, where you can glide under the glow of festive lights.
- **Concerts and Performances**: Vienna's musical heritage shines during Christmas, with numerous concerts and performances, including the famous Vienna Boys' Choir and classical concerts at the Musikverein.
- **Cultural Experiences**: Explore the city's rich history by visiting its many museums and historic sites, such as the Schönbrunn Palace, which hosts a charming Christmas market of its own.

## Prague, Czech Republic

Prague's fairy-tale architecture and cobblestone streets make it an enchanting destination any time of year, but it becomes particularly magical during the Christmas season.

- **Old Town Square Market**: The heart of Prague's Christmas celebrations is the market at Old Town Square, where you can find traditional Czech crafts, festive foods, and a towering Christmas tree.
- **Charles Bridge**: Take a stroll across the iconic Charles Bridge, which offers stunning views of the city and is beautifully illuminated during the holidays.
- **Traditional Czech Cuisine**: Warm up with hearty Czech dishes like trdelník (a sweet pastry) and svařák (Czech mulled wine) as you explore the city's festive offerings.
- **Historic Sites**: Don't miss the chance to visit Prague Castle and St. Vitus Cathedral, both of which are adorned with Christmas decorations and offer breathtaking views of the city.

## Strasbourg, France

Known as the "Capital of Christmas," Strasbourg is home to one of the oldest and most famous Christmas markets in Europe. The city's half-timbered houses and charming canals provide a picturesque backdrop for holiday festivities.

- **Christkindelsmärik**: Established in 1570, this market is one of the oldest in Europe and offers a wide variety of crafts, decorations, and culinary delights.
- **La Petite France**: Wander through this historic district, where the narrow streets and medieval buildings are beautifully decorated for the holidays.
- **Gastronomy**: Enjoy Alsatian specialties such as tarte flambée and bretzels, along with a glass of vin chaud (hot wine) to keep you warm.
- **Cultural Events**: Strasbourg hosts numerous concerts, exhibitions, and performances throughout the Christmas season, making it a vibrant cultural destination.

## Copenhagen, Denmark

Copenhagen is a city that combines Scandinavian charm with festive cheer, offering a unique Christmas experience that is both cozy and vibrant.

- **Tivoli Gardens**: This famous amusement park transforms into a winter wonderland, complete with festive lights, Christmas markets, and seasonal rides.
- **Nyhavn**: The colorful waterfront district is adorned with Christmas lights and hosts a charming market where you can enjoy Danish treats like æbleskiver (pancake balls) and gløgg (mulled wine).
- **Hygge**: Embrace the Danish concept of hygge by cozying up in one of Copenhagen's many cafes, where you can enjoy a warm drink and soak in the festive atmosphere.
- **Cultural Attractions**: Visit the city's many museums and historic sites, such as Rosenborg Castle, which hosts a royal Christmas exhibition.

## Tallinn, Estonia

Tallinn's medieval old town, a UNESCO World Heritage site, provides a stunning backdrop for a Christmas getaway. The city's well-preserved architecture and festive markets make it a must-visit destination during the holidays.

- **Town Hall Square Market**: This charming market is set against the backdrop of Tallinn's historic town hall and offers a variety of Estonian crafts, foods, and holiday decorations.
- **Winter Activities**: Enjoy ice skating in the heart of the old town or take a horse-drawn carriage ride through the snow-covered streets.
- **Traditional Cuisine**: Sample Estonian holiday treats such as piparkoogid (gingerbread cookies) and verivorst (blood sausage) as you explore the city's festive offerings.
- **Cultural Heritage**: Discover Tallinn's rich history by visiting its many museums and historic sites, including the impressive Alexander Nevsky Cathedral.

## Budapest, Hungary

Budapest is a city that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and festive cheer during the Christmas season. With its stunning architecture and vibrant holiday markets, it's a destination that shouldn't be missed.

- **Vörösmarty Square Market**: This popular market offers a wide array of Hungarian crafts, foods, and holiday decorations, set against the backdrop of Budapest's beautiful architecture.
- **Thermal Baths**: Warm up in one of Budapest's famous thermal baths, such as the Széchenyi or Gellért Baths, which offer a relaxing escape from the winter chill.
- **Danube River Cruise**: Experience the beauty of Budapest from the water with a festive river cruise, where you can enjoy stunning views of the city's illuminated landmarks.
- **Cultural Experiences**: Explore Budapest's rich history and culture by visiting its many museums, historic sites, and concert halls, where you can enjoy performances of classical music and traditional Hungarian folk dances.

## Edinburgh, Scotland

Edinburgh is a city that knows how to celebrate Christmas in style, with its historic streets and iconic landmarks providing a stunning backdrop for holiday festivities.

- **Edinburgh Christmas Market**: Located in the heart of the city, this market offers a wide variety of crafts, foods, and festive activities, including an ice skating rink and a Ferris wheel.
- **Hogmanay**: Edinburgh is famous for its New Year's Eve celebrations, known as Hogmanay, which include street parties, concerts, and a spectacular fireworks display.
- **Historic Attractions**: Explore the city's rich history by visiting its many historic sites, such as Edinburgh Castle and the Royal Mile, which are beautifully decorated for the holidays.
- **Scottish Cuisine**: Warm up with traditional Scottish dishes like haggis, neeps, and tatties, along with a dram of whisky to keep the cold at bay.

## Krakow, Poland

Krakow is a city that combines history, culture, and festive cheer to create a magical Christmas experience. With its stunning architecture and vibrant holiday markets, it's a destination that shouldn't be missed.

- **Main Market Square**: The heart of Krakow's Christmas celebrations is the market at Main Market Square, where you can find traditional Polish crafts, foods, and holiday decorations.
- **Wawel Castle**: Visit this historic castle, which is beautifully decorated for the holidays and offers stunning views of the city.
- **Traditional Polish Cuisine**: Sample Polish holiday treats such as pierogi (dumplings) and oscypek (smoked cheese) as you explore the city's festive offerings.
- **Cultural Experiences**: Discover Krakow's rich history and culture by visiting its many museums and historic sites, including the impressive St. Mary's Basilica.

## Conclusion

Europe is a continent that truly comes alive during the Christmas season, with its historic cities offering a unique blend of festive cheer, cultural experiences, and stunning architecture. Whether you're looking for traditional holiday markets, winter activities, or unique cultural experiences, these European cities provide the perfect backdrop for a magical Christmas getaway. So pack your bags, grab your winter coat, and get ready to explore the best European cities to visit over Christmas.
