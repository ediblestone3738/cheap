# Fun Christmas Places To Go

The holiday season is a magical time of year when cities and towns around the world transform into winter wonderlands, filled with twinkling lights, festive markets, and joyful celebrations. Whether you're looking to experience a white Christmas or simply want to soak up the holiday spirit, there are countless destinations that offer unique and unforgettable Christmas experiences. In this article, we'll explore some of the most fun Christmas places to visit, each offering its own special charm and festive activities.

## 1. New York City, USA

New York City is synonymous with Christmas magic. The city's iconic landmarks and bustling streets come alive with holiday cheer, making it a must-visit destination during the festive season.

- **Rockefeller Center Christmas Tree**: Witness the lighting of the iconic tree, a tradition that dates back to 1931. The tree is adorned with thousands of lights and a Swarovski crystal star.
- **Ice Skating at Rockefeller Center**: Glide across the ice at this world-famous rink, surrounded by the dazzling cityscape.
- **Holiday Window Displays**: Stroll along Fifth Avenue to admire the elaborate holiday window displays at stores like Macy's and Saks Fifth Avenue.
- **Radio City Christmas Spectacular**: Enjoy a performance by the Rockettes, a beloved holiday tradition.
- **Christmas Markets**: Explore the Union Square Holiday Market and Bryant Park Winter Village for unique gifts and festive treats.

## 2. Lapland, Finland

For a truly magical Christmas experience, head to Lapland, the official home of Santa Claus. This winter wonderland offers a unique blend of natural beauty and festive activities.

- **Santa Claus Village**: Visit Santa's official residence in Rovaniemi, where you can meet Santa and cross the Arctic Circle.
- **Reindeer Sleigh Rides**: Experience the thrill of a reindeer-pulled sleigh ride through snowy forests.
- **Northern Lights**: Witness the breathtaking Aurora Borealis, a natural light display that illuminates the night sky.
- **Snowmobile Safaris**: Explore the pristine wilderness on a thrilling snowmobile adventure.
- **Ice Hotels**: Stay in a hotel made entirely of ice and snow for a truly unique experience.

## 3. Strasbourg, France

Known as the "Capital of Christmas," Strasbourg is home to one of the oldest and most famous Christmas markets in Europe. The city's charming medieval architecture provides a picturesque backdrop for holiday festivities.

- **Christkindelsmärik**: Wander through the historic Christmas market, which dates back to 1570, and discover handcrafted gifts and delicious Alsatian treats.
- **Grande Île**: Explore the UNESCO World Heritage-listed island, adorned with festive decorations and lights.
- **Cathedral of Notre Dame**: Visit the stunning Gothic cathedral, which hosts a beautiful nativity scene during the holiday season.
- **Christmas Concerts**: Enjoy classical music performances in the city's historic churches.
- **Alsatian Cuisine**: Savor traditional dishes like choucroute and tarte flambée at cozy local restaurants.

## 4. Vienna, Austria

Vienna's imperial architecture and rich cultural heritage make it a captivating destination year-round, but the city truly shines during the Christmas season.

- **Vienna Christmas Markets**: Explore the city's numerous markets, including the famous Christkindlmarkt at Rathausplatz, where you can find handcrafted gifts and seasonal treats.
- **Belvedere Palace**: Visit the Christmas Village at this stunning baroque palace, featuring festive stalls and an ice rink.
- **Vienna State Opera**: Attend a holiday performance at one of the world's most prestigious opera houses.
- **St. Stephen's Cathedral**: Admire the beautiful nativity scene and attend a Christmas concert in this iconic Gothic cathedral.
- **Traditional Viennese Coffeehouses**: Warm up with a cup of Viennese coffee and a slice of Sachertorte in one of the city's historic cafes.

## 5. Quebec City, Canada

Quebec City offers a charming blend of European and North American holiday traditions, set against the backdrop of its historic Old Town.

- **Old Quebec**: Stroll through the cobblestone streets of this UNESCO World Heritage site, beautifully decorated for the holidays.
- **German Christmas Market**: Experience a traditional European market with wooden stalls offering crafts, food, and mulled wine.
- **Tobogganing**: Try the thrilling toboggan run at Terrasse Dufferin, offering stunning views of the St. Lawrence River.
- **Carnaval de Québec**: Although it takes place in January, the festive spirit begins in December with parades and ice sculptures.
- **Château Frontenac**: Stay at this iconic hotel, which is beautifully adorned with holiday decorations.

## 6. Tokyo, Japan

Tokyo may not be the first place that comes to mind for Christmas, but the city embraces the holiday with enthusiasm and creativity.

- **Illuminations**: Marvel at the stunning light displays throughout the city, including the famous ones at Roppongi Hills and Shibuya.
- **Christmas Markets**: Visit European-style markets at locations like Yokohama Red Brick Warehouse and Tokyo Midtown.
- **Tokyo Disneyland**: Experience the magic of Disney with special holiday parades and decorations.
- **Christmas Eve Dinner**: Enjoy a traditional Japanese Christmas meal, which often includes fried chicken and strawberry shortcake.
- **Shopping**: Explore the city's bustling shopping districts for unique gifts and holiday sales.

## 7. Prague, Czech Republic

Prague's fairy-tale architecture and rich history make it a captivating destination, especially during the Christmas season.

- **Old Town Square Christmas Market**: Discover one of Europe's most beautiful markets, set against the backdrop of the Gothic Tyn Church and the Astronomical Clock.
- **Charles Bridge**: Take a stroll across this historic bridge, adorned with festive lights and decorations.
- **Prague Castle**: Visit the castle's Christmas market and enjoy panoramic views of the city.
- **Czech Traditions**: Experience local customs, such as the lighting of the Advent wreath and the baking of traditional Christmas cookies.
- **Classical Concerts**: Attend a holiday concert in one of Prague's historic churches or concert halls.

## 8. Edinburgh, Scotland

Edinburgh's historic charm and festive spirit make it a delightful destination for a Christmas getaway.

- **Edinburgh Christmas Markets**: Explore the markets in East Princes Street Gardens, offering crafts, food, and an ice rink.
- **Hogmanay**: Celebrate the New Year with Scotland's famous Hogmanay festival, featuring fireworks, concerts, and a torchlight procession.
- **Edinburgh Castle**: Visit the castle for stunning views of the city and special holiday events.
- **Royal Mile**: Stroll along this historic street, beautifully decorated for the season.
- **Scottish Cuisine**: Warm up with traditional dishes like haggis and whisky in cozy pubs.

## 9. Nuremberg, Germany

Nuremberg is home to one of the most famous Christmas markets in the world, offering a quintessential German holiday experience.

- **Christkindlesmarkt**: Explore the historic market, known for its handcrafted ornaments, gingerbread, and mulled wine.
- **Nuremberg Castle**: Visit the castle for panoramic views of the city and special holiday events.
- **Toy Museum**: Discover the city's rich toy-making history at this fascinating museum.
- **Nuremberg Sausages**: Savor the city's famous sausages, served with sauerkraut and mustard.
- **Christmas Concerts**: Enjoy classical music performances in the city's historic churches.

## 10. Sydney, Australia

For a unique twist on Christmas, head to Sydney, where you can celebrate the holiday season in the summer sun.

- **Bondi Beach**: Spend Christmas Day on the beach, enjoying a festive barbecue and the warm Australian weather.
- **Sydney Christmas Markets**: Explore markets like The Rocks Christmas Market for unique gifts and holiday treats.
- **Carols by Candlelight**: Attend a traditional carol concert at the Domain, a beloved Australian holiday tradition.
- **Sydney Harbour**: Take a cruise on the harbor to see the city's iconic landmarks adorned with festive lights.
- **New Year's Eve Fireworks**: Stay for the spectacular fireworks display over Sydney Harbour to ring in the New Year.

## Conclusion

Whether you're dreaming of a white Christmas or a sunny holiday by the beach, these destinations offer a wide range of festive experiences to suit every traveler's taste. From the enchanting markets of Europe to the vibrant celebrations in Asia and beyond, the world is full of magical places to visit during the holiday season. So pack your bags, embrace the spirit of adventure, and make this Christmas one to remember by exploring these fun and festive destinations.
