# Exploring the Rich History of New Delhi

New Delhi is the capital of India and is one of the oldest cities in the world. It is a city of great historical importance, and it has been the center of many empires and dynasties. New Delhi is a city of many contrasts, with modern skyscrapers and ancient monuments, bustling markets and peaceful gardens, and a rich culture and history. Exploring the rich history of New Delhi is an experience that will leave visitors with a deep appreciation of the city’s past.

## History of New Delhi

New Delhi has a long and rich history that dates back to the 7th century BC. The city was once known as Indraprastha, and it was the site of the Pandava kingdom in the Mahabharata. It was also the site of the ancient city of Lal Kot, which was founded by the Tomar Rajputs. During the Mughal period, the city became a major center of art, culture, and architecture. The Mughals built the Red Fort and other grand buildings, and the city was a major center of the Mughal Empire.

After the Mughal period, New Delhi was ruled by the British, who built a new city in the area. This new city was designed by architect Edwin Lutyens and was named New Delhi. After India gained independence in 1947, New Delhi was declared the capital of the country. The city has since grown to become a major metropolis with a population of over 18 million people.

## Things to See and Do in New Delhi

New Delhi is a city of many attractions and activities. Visitors to the city can explore its ancient monuments and buildings, its bustling markets, and its many parks and gardens. Here are some of the top attractions in New Delhi:

1\. Red Fort: The Red Fort is a majestic red sandstone fort that was built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century. It is one of the most important monuments in India and is a symbol of the country’s rich history and culture.

2\. India Gate: The India Gate is a 42-meter-high archway that was built in 1931 to honor the Indian soldiers who died in World War I. It is one of the most iconic monuments in New Delhi and is a popular tourist attraction.

3\. Humayun’s Tomb: Humayun’s Tomb is a magnificent Mughal-era tomb that was built in 1570 for the Mughal emperor Humayun. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is one of the most beautiful monuments in New Delhi.

4\. Jama Masjid: Jama Masjid is the largest mosque in India and was built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in 1656. It is one of the most important religious sites in India and is a popular tourist attraction.

5\. Qutub Minar: The Qutub Minar is a 73-meter-high tower that was built by the Mughal emperor Qutb-ud-din Aibak in 1193. It is one of the most iconic monuments in New Delhi and is a popular tourist attraction.

6\. Lotus Temple: The Lotus Temple is a beautiful Bahá’í temple that was built in 1986. It is one of the most popular tourist attractions in New Delhi and is a symbol of peace and harmony.

7\. Akshardham Temple: Akshardham Temple is a Hindu temple complex that was built in 2005. It is one of the most important religious sites in India and is a popular tourist attraction.

8\. Rashtrapati Bhavan: Rashtrapati Bhavan is the official residence of the President of India and was built in 1931. It is one of the most iconic buildings in New Delhi and is a popular tourist attraction.

9\. Lodhi Garden: Lodhi Garden is a beautiful park that was built in the 15th century. It is one of the most popular parks in New Delhi and is a popular tourist attraction.

10\. Jantar Mantar: Jantar Mantar is an 18th-century observatory that was built by Maharaja Jai Singh II. It is one of the most popular tourist attractions in New Delhi and is a popular tourist attraction.
