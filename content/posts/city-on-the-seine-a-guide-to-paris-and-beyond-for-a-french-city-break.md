City on the Seine: A Guide to Paris and Beyond for a French City Break

The City of Love, Paris, is undoubtedly one of the world’s most enchanting and romantic destinations. With stunning architecture, world-renowned museums, Michelin-starred restaurants, and grand monuments, the capital of France has it all. Beyond Paris, there are a wealth of amazing destinations to discover in the region, from the rolling hills of Champagne to the glittering coastline of the Cote d’Azur. Here’s your ultimate guide to a French [City Break](/holidaydestinations/the-best-last-minute-city-breaks/ "The best Last minute city breaks"), from Paris and beyond.

Getting to Paris

Paris has two international airports; Charles de Gaulle and Orly, both of which are well connected by public transport. Charles de Gaulle airport is the larger of the two, with flights arriving from all corners of the globe. From the airport, the RER B train will take you into the city center in around 30 minutes. Alternatively, taxis and private airport transfer services are available.

Orly airport is smaller and closer to the city center, with the Orlyval train connecting the airport to the RER B and RER C for onward journeys. Taxis and private airport transfer services are also available.

If you’re traveling from elsewhere in France or Europe, the excellent high-speed TGV trains make getting to Paris a breeze.

Navigating Paris

Paris has an extensive and efficient public transport system, with buses, trains, and the Metro network all interconnected. For those looking to explore beyond the city center, renting a car can be a good option, although it’s worth noting that driving in Paris can be challenging, with narrow streets and heavy traffic.

The city is also designed for walking, and many of the top attractions are within walking distance of each other. The Seine River runs through the heart of the city, and taking a boat tour along the river is a unique way to see the sights.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in City on the Seine: A Guide to Paris and Beyond for a French City Break

1\. The Eiffel Tower
No visit to Paris is complete without a trip to the iconic Eiffel Tower. Towering over the city at 324 meters, the tower offers spectacular views of the city.

2\. The Louvre Museum
The Louvre is the world’s most famous art museum, home to over 35,000 works of art, including the Mona Lisa and Winged Victory of Samothrace.

3\. Notre-Dame Cathedral
This stunning Gothic cathedral is one of the world’s most famous churches, with stunning stained glass windows, soaring arches, and intricate carvings.

4\. Montmartre
This charming neighborhood is known for its narrow cobbled streets, quaint cafes, and stunning views of the city. Don’t miss the stunning Sacre-Coeur Basilica, perched atop the hill.

5\. The Champs-Elysees
This iconic tree-lined avenue is home to some of the city’s best shopping and dining, with designer stores, high-end cafes, and world-famous restaurants.

6\. The Palace of Versailles
Located just outside Paris, the Palace of Versailles is a masterpiece of French Baroque architecture, with stunning gardens, opulent interiors, and a fascinating history.

7\. The Arc de Triomphe
Located at the top of the Champs-Elysees, the Arc de Triomphe is a triumphal arch commemorating French victories.

8\. The Catacombs
Beneath the streets of Paris lie the Catacombs, a network of underground tunnels lined with the skeletal remains of over six million souls.

9\. Centre Pompidou
This stunning museum of modern art is located in the heart of Paris, and features works by Picasso, Matisse, and Dali.

10\. Disneyland Paris
For families traveling with kids, a visit to the magical Disney theme park is an absolute must.

FAQs

Q: When is the best time to visit Paris?
A: The best time to visit Paris is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller.

Q: What language is spoken in Paris and France?
A: French is the primary language spoken in Paris and throughout France.

Q: Is it safe to travel to Paris?
A: Paris is generally safe for visitors, but as with any major city, it’s wise to keep an eye on your belongings and be aware of pickpockets.

Q: What is the currency in France?
A: The currency in France is the Euro.

Q: What is the dress code in Paris?
A: Parisians tend to dress elegantly, so it’s a good idea to pack stylish, chic clothes for your trip.
