# Explore the Big Apple: 10 Must-See Attractions in New York City

New York City is one of the most exciting cities in the world. It is a city of dreams, a city of opportunity, and a city of endless possibilities. It is also a city of iconic landmarks and attractions that draw millions of visitors every year. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a long-time resident, there is something for everyone to explore in the Big Apple. From world-famous museums to iconic architecture, here are 10 must-see attractions in New York City.

## 1\. Empire State Building

The Empire State Building is one of the most iconic landmarks in New York City and it is the most visited attraction in the city. The 102-story Art Deco skyscraper stands 1,454 feet tall and offers breathtaking views of the city from the observation deck on the 86th floor. The building is open daily from 8am to 2am and visitors can purchase tickets online or at the ticket office.

## 2\. Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is another iconic landmark in New York City and it is a symbol of freedom and democracy. The statue stands 305 feet tall and is located on Liberty Island in the New York Harbor. Visitors can take a ferry to the island and explore the grounds or take a guided tour of the statue.

## 3\. Central Park

Central Park is an 843-acre urban oasis in the heart of Manhattan. It is one of the most visited attractions in the city and is home to a variety of activities and attractions. Visitors can explore the park’s trails and paths, visit the Central Park Zoo, or take a boat ride on the lake. The park is open daily from 6am to 1am and admission is free.

## 4\. American Museum of Natural History

The American Museum of Natural History is one of the largest and most renowned natural history museums in the world. The museum is home to over 32 million specimens and artifacts and offers a variety of interactive exhibits and educational programs. The museum is open daily from 10am to 5:45pm and admission is free.

## 5\. Metropolitan Museum of Art

The Metropolitan Museum of Art is one of the largest and most comprehensive art museums in the world. The museum is home to over two million works of art from around the world and offers a variety of exhibitions and educational programs. The museum is open daily from 10am to 5:30pm and admission is free.

## 6\. 9/11 Memorial & Museum

The 9/11 Memorial & Museum is a memorial to the victims of the September 11th terrorist attacks. The memorial features two reflecting pools in the footprints of the Twin Towers and a museum with artifacts and stories from the attacks. The memorial is open daily from 7:30am to 9pm and admission is free.

## 7\. High Line

The High Line is an elevated park built on an abandoned railway line in the Chelsea neighborhood of Manhattan. The park features a variety of gardens, art installations, and performance spaces, as well as stunning views of the city. The High Line is open daily from 7am to 7pm and admission is free.

## 8\. Brooklyn Bridge

The Brooklyn Bridge is one of the oldest and most iconic bridges in the world. The bridge spans the East River and connects Manhattan to Brooklyn. Visitors can take a walk across the bridge and enjoy stunning views of the city skyline. The bridge is open daily from 6am to 10pm and admission is free.

## 9\. Times Square

Times Square is one of the most iconic and recognizable places in the world. The square is home to a variety of shops, restaurants, and entertainment venues, and is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike. The square is open daily from 8am to midnight and admission is free.

## 10\. Grand Central Terminal

Grand Central Terminal is one of the most iconic train stations in the world. The terminal is home to 44 platforms and over 75 shops and restaurants, as well as a variety of art installations and historical artifacts. The terminal is open daily from 5:30am to 2am and admission is free.

# FAQs

## What are the most popular attractions in New York City?

The most popular attractions in New York City are the Empire State Building, the Statue of Liberty, Central Park, the American Museum of Natural History, the Metropolitan Museum of Art, the 9/11 Memorial & Museum, the High Line, the Brooklyn Bridge, Times Square, and Grand Central Terminal.

## What is the best time to visit New York City?

The best time to visit New York City is in the spring or fall. The weather is mild and there are fewer crowds. However, the city is a popular destination year-round and there are plenty of attractions and activities to enjoy no matter when you visit.

## Are there any free attractions in New York City?

Yes, there are plenty of free attractions in New York City. Central Park, the 9/11 Memorial & Museum, the High Line, the Brooklyn Bridge, Times Square, and Grand Central Terminal are all free to visit.

## What is the best way to get around New York City?

The best way to get around New York City is by public transportation. The city has an extensive subway system that can take you to all the major attractions and neighborhoods. You can also take a taxi, ride-sharing service, or hop-on/hop-off bus tour.
