When one thinks of Spain, images of flamenco dancers, bullfights, and tapas are likely what comes to mind. However, Spain’s Mediterranean coastline is equally enchanting and full of wonderful surprises. The region is home to some of the country’s most iconic and exciting tourist destinations, offering something for everyone. In this article, we will explore the secrets of Spain’s iconic Mediterranean resorts and all that they have to offer.

Costa Brava

Costa Brava is a breathtakingly beautiful coast that spans 214 kilometers along the Mediterranean Sea. The region boasts white sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, charming fishing villages, rocky coves, and a dazzling array of flora and fauna. You can take a walk along the Camino de Ronda, a coastal path that runs along the coastline and offers stunning views of the sea. Costa Brava is also home to several medieval towns, historic castles, and museums that showcase the art and culture of the region.

Costa Dorada

Located in Catalonia, Costa Dorada (Golden Coast) is a popular destination for families and couples alike. The region has a coastline of 92 kilometers and is home to several theme parks, including PortAventura World, Europe's largest amusement park. The area is also known for its impressive Roman ruins, including the Amphitheater in Tarragona, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Costa Blanca

Costa Blanca is famous for its clear, blue waters and sandy beaches, making it a perfect destination for sun worshipers. The region is home to several coastal towns such as Benidorm, Altea, Calpe, and Javea, each providing its own unique charm. Visitors can explore the idyllic white villages of the Costa Blanca or spend their days hiking through the natural parks scattered throughout the region.

FAQs

Q: What is the best time to visit Spain’s Mediterranean coast?
A: The best time to visit Spain’s Mediterranean coast is from May to October when the weather is warm and sunny.

Q: Can I use credit cards in Spain?
A: Yes, you can use credit cards in Spain. Most establishments, including restaurants, hotels, and shops, accept credit cards.

Q: Which airport is best to fly into for the Mediterranean coast?
A: The best airport to fly into depends on your destination. Barcelona Airport is ideal for the Costa Brava and Costa Dorada while Alicante Airport is better for the Costa Blanca.

Q: Is it safe to travel to Spain?
A: Yes, Spain is a safe country but like any other country, travelers should exercise caution and take necessary precautions to avoid becoming victims of crime.
