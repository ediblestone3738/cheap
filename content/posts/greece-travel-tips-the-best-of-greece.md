# Discover the Best of Greece: A 2-3 Week Itinerary for History, Nature, and Relaxation

![Greece travel tips](/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/ryan-spencer-XGKaRnWjv1c-unsplash.jpg)

Greece is a country that offers visitors a wealth of experiences, from ancient history to breathtaking natural landscapes to relaxing island getaways. Read on to find our best Greece travel tips. With so much to see and do, it can be overwhelming to plan a trip. That's why we've put together a 2-3 week itinerary that covers some of the best cities and sights in Greece. Read on to discover the best times to travel, scenic beaches, charming towns, and more.

## Athens:

The Capital of Greece. Your journey in Greece begins in [Athens](/holidaydestinations/discover-the-ancient-city-of-athens-a-guide-to-exploring-its-rich-history/), the capital city. Home to some of the most famous ancient landmarks in the world. Start by visiting the Acropolis, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. See the iconic Parthenon, the ancient temple dedicated to the goddess Athena. Take a walk around the National Archaeological Museum and explore the country's ancient history.

## Peloponnese Peninsula:

Historical Sites and Picturesque Towns. Next, head south to the Peloponnese peninsula, where you can visit the ancient city of Corinth,. Famous for its canal and ancient ruins. Explore the charming town of Nafplio, with its cobbled streets and Venetian architecture. Take a day trip to the ancient theater of Epidaurus, which dates back to the 4th century BC and is known for its exceptional acoustics.

## Delphi:

The Ancient Oracle and the Temple of Apollo. From the Peloponnese, travel north to the town of Delphi, one of the most important archaeological sites in Greece. This is where the famous Oracle of Delphi was located in ancient times, and it was considered the center of the world by the ancient Greeks. Visit the Temple of Apollo and admire the panoramic views of the valley below.

## Meteora:

The Stunning Rock Formations and Monasteries. After Delphi, head northwest to the stunning rock formations of Meteora, which are listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The towering cliffs are home to a series of monasteries built on top of them, some of which date back to the 14th century. Take a guided tour to learn about the history and significance of these remarkable structures.

## Thessaloniki:

A Vibrant City in Northern Greece. Continuing north, visit the vibrant city of Thessaloniki, which has a rich history and culture. Explore the White Tower, a landmark monument that has become a symbol of the city, and the Archaeological Museum of Thessaloniki, which houses some of the most significant ancient artifacts from the region. Don't forget to try the local cuisine, which is known for its fresh seafood, meze dishes, and delicious desserts.

## Cyclades Islands:

Relaxation and Adventure. Finally, head to the Cyclades islands for some relaxation and adventure. This group of islands in the Aegean Sea includes popular destinations such as Mykonos, Santorini, and Naxos. Spend a few days lounging on the beaches, or take a hike in the hills to enjoy the stunning views. Take a kayak tour around the island of Naxos, or explore the picturesque towns and villages on foot.

### Greece travel tips FAQ:

Q: What is the best time to visit Greece?

A: The best time to visit Greece is during the shoulder seasons of spring (April to June) and fall (September to October), when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. However, Greece has a Mediterranean climate, so you can also visit during the summer months (July to August) when the weather is hot and sunny, but be prepared for larger crowds and higher prices.

Q: How should I travel around Greece? A: Greece has a well-developed transportation system, and you can travel around the country by plane, train, bus, or ferry. A combination of these modes of transportation can be used to [create an itinerary](/holidaydestinations/sun-sea-and-sights-how-to-plan-your-perfect-greek-holiday/) that suits your needs and preferences.

Q: What are some must-see historical sites in Greece? A: Greece is home to numerous historical sites, including the Acropolis in Athens, the ancient city of Corinth, the Oracle of Delphi, and the monasteries of Meteora. These sites offer visitors a glimpse into Greece's rich ancient history and are must-see destinations for history buffs.

Q: What are some of the best beaches in Greece? A: Greece has some of the most beautiful beaches in the world, with crystal-clear waters and golden sands. Some of the best beaches include Balos Beach in Crete, Myrtos Beach in Kefalonia, Navagio Beach in Zakynthos, and Red Beach in Santorini. There are also many hidden gems to discover along the way.

Q: Can I visit the Cyclades islands year-round? A: The Cyclades islands are mostly visited during the summer months, but you can visit year-round if you want to avoid the crowds. The winter months can be chilly, but the islands are still beautiful and offer a different experience, with fewer tourists and a more relaxed atmosphere.

## Greece travel tips:

The Cyclades Islands are located in the Aegean Sea, south of Athens. If you are starting in Thessaloniki, the easiest way to reach the Cyclades would be to fly to Athens first and then take a ferry or a domestic flight to the islands.

There are frequent flights from Thessaloniki to Athens, with a flight time of about 1 hour. From Athens, you can take a ferry from the port of Piraeus to the Cyclades Islands. The ferry journey can take anywhere from 3 to 7 hours, depending on which island you're heading to. Alternatively, you can take a domestic flight from Athens to one of the island airports, which can take anywhere from 20 minutes to an hour.

It's important to note that ferry schedules can vary depending on the time of year, so it's a good idea to check the schedules in advance and book your tickets early, especially during peak season. You can also check for flights and book tickets online through various travel websites or directly through the airlines.

Overall, getting to the Cyclades Islands from Thessaloniki will involve a combination of air and sea travel, but with a little planning and preparation, it's definitely doable and well worth the effort to experience the beauty of these stunning islands.

## In conclusion:

Greece is a destination that offers something for everyone, from history buffs to nature enthusiasts. With its stunning beaches, charming towns, and rich ancient history, it's no wonder that it's a popular travel destination. By planning a 2-3 week itinerary that includes Athens and other top destinations, you can make the most of your time in Greece and create memories that will last a lifetime. Whether you're interested in exploring historical sites, relaxing on the beach, or immersing yourself in Greece's natural beauty, there's something for everyone in this beautiful country. So start planning your trip today and get ready for an adventure you'll never forget!
