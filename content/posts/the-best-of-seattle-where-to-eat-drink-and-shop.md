The Best of Seattle: Where to Eat, Drink, and Shop

Seattle, Washington is a city full of culture, history, and most importantly, delicious food and drinks. In this guide, we'll explore some of the best places to eat, drink, and shop in Seattle while taking note of the top-rated tourist attractions that you cannot miss out on during your visit.

Where to Eat

Pike Place Market - A landmark and top tourist attraction in Seattle featuring fresh seafood, produce, crafts, and eateries. Head to this iconic market and grab a classic savory bite from Beecher's Handmade Cheese, Piroshky Piroshky's Russian Bakery or one of the cosy cafes in there.

Japonessa Sushi - This unique fusion sushi spot serves both traditional and contemporary sushi, along with delicious cocktails. Enjoy their twist of rolls on a happy hour menu.

The Pink Door - This restaurant is located in Post Alley offering a taste of Italy with a beautiful view of Elliot Bay. The Pink Door has been loved for years by locals as well as tourists for their Italian food with a modern twist.

Matt's In The Market - For an intimate dining experience in Seattle, Matt's In The Market is recommended. With stunning views of Elliott Bay and the Pike Place Market, you will love dining in this market kitchen.

Ezell's Famous Chicken - This fried chicken quick-service restaurant is popular for its juicy and flavorful chicken. The founder and his wife used to hand out small pieces of their signature chicken to their friends, then it became popular by word of mouth and 25 years down the road it's still one of Seattle's most loved fast food chains. Don't forget to try their famous side dishes too.

Where to Drink

Canon - This cocktail bar is considered one of the world's best and has won multiple awards for its internationally acclaimed drinks. Sip on their signature drink "The Penicillin" alongside a delicious snack before heading out.

Linda's Tavern - This iconic tavern in Capitol Hill is a Seattle staple. With a unique and classic interior, affordable drinks, and frequent live music, it's loved by locals and tourists alike.

Rachel's Ginger Beer - This locally made ginger beer is available in multiple flavors and used to create specialty cocktails. Take a bottle home to mix with your own drinks, or stop by one of their two locations to try one of their special drinks of either beer or wine.

The Walrus and the Carpenter - This oyster bar is a must-visit for seafood lovers. Settle in for an exotic local oyster, a glass of wine or a creative cocktail.

Unicorn - An innovative bar with over-the-top carnival decor combining magic, games, and alcohol. Come through for a drink or a bite, and on a rare chance, see a unicorn.

Where to Shop

University Village - A top shopping destination located in the University District, featuring over 120 stores and restaurants. Find national retail stores, high-end fashion boutiques or a unique gift from one of their independently-owned shops.

Northgate Mall - A traditional mall and shopping center located in north Seattle with plenty of top fashion retail stores and a food court.

Pioneer Square - A classic district located in Seattle's downtown offering art galleries, local boutiques, vintage treasures, and cafes.

Macy's Seattle - Spend an afternoon in the Macy's downtown Seattle flagship store. Alongside countless shopping options make sure to check out the visited Starbucks Coffee on the 3rd level or visit the also inside Macy's bicycle rental.

Fremont - Learn the way of the Fremont district with various quirky local's shops from vintage-inspired clothing to handmade chocolates and bath products.

FAQs

Q: Is Seattle an expensive city?

A: Seattle can be expensive, but there are ways to save while visiting. Lookout for cozy Airbnbs, enjoy free public transportation that is available in several parts of the city and try eating at some of the crucial spots we've suggested.

Q: How is Seattle's public transport system?

A: Seattle has an extensive public transport network, including buses, trains, streetcars, and water taxis. Visit the King County Metro website to review information about public transport services with ease from your phone or computer.

Q: What's the best way to get around Seattle?

A: Public transportation is an affordable and convenient way to get around Seattle, but walking and biking are equally great options as most attractions are centrally located.

Top Ten Tourist Attractions located in The Best of Seattle: Where to Eat, Drink, and Shop

1\. Space Needle - Visit the top of this 605-ft observation tower offering a stunning panoramic view of Seattle.

2\. Pike Place Market - Walkthrough the market halls and buy fresh local foods, crafts, and souvenirs.

3\. Chihuly Garden and Glass - Adventure and witness the dynamic glass sculptures of artist Dale Chihuly and his featured exhibit.

4\. Museum of Pop Culture - Feel entertained by the exhibits showcasing popular culture memorabilia from movies, television shows, and music icons.

5\. Waterfront Park - A destination for all ages showcasing shops, restaurants and tours set against the backdrop of Elliott Bay.

6\. Fremont Troll - The famous Fremont neighbourhood landmark is the Troll hiding under Aurora bridge. Take an Instagram shot with it and generate a stoery to tell.

7\. Seattle Art Museum - Visit this downtown museum showcasing international and local art from various eras.

8\. Seattle Aquarium - See eye to eye with Northwest Marine marine life and learn about the region's marine environment.

9\. Olympic Sculpture Park - Walk in the park with it's contemporary artistic placements with Seattle's waterfront as your backdrop.

10\. Woodland Park Zoo - Take a walk on the wild side visiting more than 300 animal species located in 92-acres green space of Woodland Park.
