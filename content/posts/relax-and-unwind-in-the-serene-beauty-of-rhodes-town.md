Rhodes Island, the largest of the Dodecanese islands and part of Greece, is known for its ancient history, beautiful beaches, and stunning landscape. The island is not only rich in history but also a popular tourist destination for travelers seeking relaxation, adventure, and culture. One of the most popular places on the island is Rhodes Town. Located on the northern tip of Rhodes Island, Rhodes Town is one of the best places to enjoy a relaxing vacation in the serene beauty of Greece.

Rhodes Town is full of beautiful promenades, stunning architecture, historic landmarks, and markets. The town is divided into two major areas- the old town and the new town. The old town is a UNESCO-listed World Heritage Site and is famous for its beautiful medieval architecture, narrow streets, and charming squares. Meanwhile, the new town is modern and lively with plenty of bars, restaurants, and shops. The combination of the two makes Rhodes Town a beautiful and tranquil holiday destination. So, if you’re thinking of a holiday to relax and unwind, here’s what you can expect from Rhodes Town.

Things to Do in Rhodes Town

1\. Visit the Palace of the Grand Master

One of the most popular landmarks in Rhodes Town is the Palace of the Grand Master. Located in the heart of the old town, this beautiful medieval castle was built in the 14th century and served as the residence of the Grand Masters of the Knights of Rhodes. Today, the palace is a museum that showcases the history of the Knights of Rhodes and exhibits ancient artifacts from the island.

2\. Take a Walk Along the Mandraki Harbor

Mandraki Harbor is one of the most beautiful parts of Rhodes Town. The harbor is lined with beautiful yachts, traditional fishing boats, and colorful cafes. From here, you can take a boat tour around the island, watch the sunset, or enjoy a stroll along the promenade.

3\. Admire the Architecture of the Old Town

The old town of Rhodes is a beautiful place to explore. The medieval architecture is stunning, with Gothic arches, cobblestone streets, and fortified walls. The old town is full of narrow streets, and every turn uncovers an incredible view or landmark.

4\. Visit the Acropolis of Rhodes

Located on a hill overlooking Rhodes Town, the Acropolis of Rhodes is an ancient site that dates back to the 7th century BC. A visit to the site rewards you with stunning views of the city and the island beyond.

5\. Relax on the Beach

Rhodes is famous for its beautiful beaches, and Rhodes Town is no exception. The Elli Beach is a popular spot with crystal clear waters and a long stretch of sand. The beach is lively and offers plenty of water sports activities.

6\. Take a Cooking Class and Learn to Make Greek Food

If you love Greek food, then why not learn to cook it? Take a cooking class, and you’ll learn how to make traditional Greek dishes such as moussaka, tzatziki, and more. These classes are a fun way to learn about Greek culture and cuisine.

7\. Visit the Rhodes Archaeological Museum

The Rhodes Archaeological Museum is the perfect place to learn about the history of the island. The museum showcases ancient artifacts, including pottery, sculptures, and more. A visit to the museum is both informative and inspiring.

8\. Go on a Wine Tour

Rhodes is known for its delicious wine. Take a wine tour, and you’ll get to visit local vineyards, taste different varieties of wine, and learn about the history of winemaking on the island.

9\. Take a Boat Tour

Explore the island from a different perspective and take a boat tour around the island. The boat tour will take you to secluded beaches and hidden coves that are only accessible by boat. You’ll also get to see the island’s stunning coastline and crystal-clear waters.

10\. Discover Rhodes at Night

Rhodes Town comes to life once the sun goes down. The town has a great nightlife scene, with plenty of bars, clubs, and restaurants. Experience the vibrant nightlife and dance the night away with the locals.

FAQs

Q. What is the best time to visit Rhodes Town?

A. The best time to visit Rhodes Town is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny.

Q. What is the currency used in Rhodes Town?

A. The currency used in Rhodes Town is the Euro.

Q. How do I get to Rhodes Town?

A. You can fly to Rhodes Island and then take a shuttle bus or taxi to Rhodes Town.

Q. Is it safe to travel to Rhodes Town?

A. Yes, Rhodes Town is a safe destination for travelers.

Q. What is the local cuisine in Rhodes Town?

A. The local cuisine in Rhodes Town is based on traditional Greek dishes, including moussaka, tzatziki, and fresh seafood.
