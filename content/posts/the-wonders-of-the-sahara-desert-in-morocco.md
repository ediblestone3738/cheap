The Wonders of the Sahara Desert in Morocco

Located in the North African nation, Morocco, the Sahara desert offers a mystical and awe-inspiring escape from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. At around 9 million square kilometers, the Sahara is the largest hot desert in the world, spanning over 3,600,000 square miles across North Africa, and it makes up about 10 percent of the African continent. The Sahara encompasses some territories of Algeria, Chad, Egypt, Libya, Mali, Mauritania, Morocco, Niger, Western Sahara, Sudan, and Tunisia. But today, let's focus on exploring the Wonders of the Sahara Desert in Morocco.

To experience the Sahara Desert's beauty will leave anyone in utter awe. It brings along a magical and almost surrealistic feeling that can hardly be explained. The vastness of the desert, with its miles of sand, towering dunes, and unique flora and fauna, will make any traveler experience a sense of calmness and beauty that is rarely found elsewhere. Its sheer size and harsh landscape have been an inspiration to countless intrepid explorers, writers, poets, and painters throughout history. In this article, we will be discussing the Wonders of the Sahara Desert in Morocco, including its top destinations, FAQs, and other essential information.

Top Ten Tourist Attractions in The Wonders of the Sahara Desert in Morocco

1\. Erg Chebbi Dunes

Erg Chebbi Dunes are one of the most iconic sand dunes in the Sahara desert. These dunes, located on the edge of the Atlas Mountains, paint a vivid image of how extraordinary the desert can be with its vivid color of gold and orange dunes. At almost 160 meters high, it is the highest sand dune in the region. Tourists can take part in various activities such as dune bashing, sandboarding, and camel riding to experience the true essence of Erg Chebbi.

2\. Draa Valley

The Draa Valley lies between the Atlas Mountains and the Sahara Desert, stretching over 200 Km. The Draa Valley is one of the most scenic places in Morocco, with its stunning landscape consisting of the vast river, lush green palm groves, and reflective dunes. Tourists can enjoy a camel ride through the valley to experience this surrealistic atmosphere.

3\. Mhamid

Mhamid is a small village located about 98 kilometers from Zagora. It is the last village and gateway to the Sahara desert. Mhamid boasts stunning oasis, deep blue skies, and golden sand dunes that stretch as far as the horizon. Visitors can spend the night under the stars in a traditional Bedouin camp or take part in camel trekking.

4\. Tafraoute

Tafraoute is a collection of unique rock formations engraved by the local community. The mountain range is known for its shades of pink, with the shapes varying from the human face to a camel. It's an ideal destination for those who love to hike, as there are many trails available.

5\. Ait Benhaddou

Ait Benhaddou is an old Kasbah that is listed among the UNESCO World Heritage Sites. It has been filmed in several Hollywood movies, such as Gladiator, The Mummy, and Game of Thrones. Visitors can wander along the narrow alleys and admire the mudbrick walls that date back to the 17th century.

6\. Zagora

Zagora is a provincial capital and a popular tourist destination located along the Draa Valley. It is the home of the Sun Gate, which reads, 'Tombouctou 52 days.' Visitors can visit the old Kasbah, Draa Valley, and Zagora's sweet dates.

7\. Merzouga

Merzouga is a small village near the Moroccan-Algeria border. It is renowned for its gigantic dunes, which often reach 150 meters in height. It's the ideal spot to experience a camel ride and watch the sunrise and sunset.

8\. Dades valley

The Dades Valley lies in the High Atlas Mountains and comprises some of the most mesmerizing ranges and canyons. With its lush green palmeries, tranquility, and rugged landscapes, many tourists arrive to experience its immense beauty.

9\. Tinghir

Tinghir is a small town located in the foothills of the High Atlas Mountains. It is home to Todra Gorge, a steep canyon that offers a stunning view of nature. It's a popular destination for rock climbing and nature enthusiasts.

10\. Fez

Fez is a well-known destination in Morocco with narrow car-free streets filled with bazaars, shops, and numerous historical sites. The city is famous for its University of Al Quaraouiyine, which dates back to the 9th century and is regarded as the oldest university in the Arab world.

FAQs About The Wonders of the Sahara Desert in Morocco

Q. Does the Sahara desert experience rain?

A. The Sahara desert is one of the driest areas globally, with less than an inch of annual rainfall.

Q. How much area does the Sahara desert cover in Morocco?

A. The Sahara desert covers a significant area of southern Morocco, approximately 31% of the country's total area, making it the largest hot desert in the world.

Q. What is the best time to visit the Sahara desert?

A. The ideal time to visit the Sahara desert is during the months of October to March when temperatures are mild.

Q. Do I need to carry cash while visiting the Sahara desert?

A. While most tourist destinations in Morocco accept credit cards, it's still advisable to carry cash as most vendors and restaurants do not accept credit cards.

Q. Is it safe to travel to the Sahara Desert in Morocco?

A. Travelers are advised to follow all necessary precautions and security measures while traveling to the Sahara desert in Morocco. As with any location, travelers are advised to be aware of their surroundings and take necessary safety precautions.
