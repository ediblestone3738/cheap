The Startup Nation

Israel's startup ecosystem has become well-known worldwide, with the country earning the nickname "Startup Nation". This moniker is well-deserved, with Israel consistently ranking among the top countries in the world for startup activity and venture capital investment.

Some of the factors that have contributed to Israel's success in the startup world include its highly-educated workforce, with a strong emphasis on science and technology education; its entrepreneurial culture, with a high tolerance for risk and failure; and its government support, with a variety of programs and incentives designed to encourage innovation and entrepreneurship.

One of the most notable success stories to come out of Israel's startup scene is the ride-sharing company, Gett. Founded in Tel Aviv in 2010, Gett has grown to become the largest on-demand mobility company in Europe, with operations in over 100 cities worldwide.

Another successful Israeli startup is Waze, a GPS navigation app that uses real-time traffic data and crowd-sourced information to provide users with the most efficient route possible. Waze was founded in 2006 and was acquired by Google in 2013 for over $1 billion.

In recent years, Israel has also become a hub for research and development in fields such as biotechnology, cybersecurity, and artificial intelligence. With a number of world-class research institutions, including the Weizmann Institute of Science and the Technion-Israel Institute of Technology, Israel has become a leader in cutting-edge research and innovation.

Cybersecurity

One area where Israel's expertise has particularly shone is in the field of cybersecurity. Israel has become a global leader in cybersecurity, with a growing number of cybersecurity startups and a strong government focus on cybersecurity research and development.

The Israeli military has been particularly active in cybersecurity, developing a number of cutting-edge technologies and methods to protect against cyber attacks.
