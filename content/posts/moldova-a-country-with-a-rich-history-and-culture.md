# Moldova: A Country with a Rich History and Culture

Moldova is a small, landlocked country located in Eastern Europe, bordered by Ukraine and Romania. It is the least visited country in Europe and is often overlooked by tourists. Despite its small size, Moldova has a rich history and culture that is worth exploring. From its ancient monasteries to its vibrant cities, Moldova has something for everyone.

## History of Moldova

Moldova has been inhabited for thousands of years and has been the site of many important events in European history. It was part of the Roman Empire for centuries and later became a part of the Moldavian Principality. In 1812, it was annexed by the Russian Empire and remained under Russian rule until the fall of the Soviet Union in 1991. Since then, Moldova has been an independent nation.

## Culture of Moldova

Moldova has a unique culture that is a mix of Eastern European and Mediterranean influences. The country is home to many different ethnicities, including Moldovans, Ukrainians, Russians, Gagauz, and Bulgarians. Each of these ethnic groups has their own distinct culture and traditions.

The national language of Moldova is Romanian, but many people also speak Russian and Gagauz. Moldova is a predominantly Christian country, and the majority of the population is Orthodox. Additionally, there are small minorities of Muslims and Jews.
