Denmark, a small Scandinavian country known for its charming architecture, picturesque landscapes, and delicious cuisine, has also gained recognition for being the world's happiest country. According to the World Happiness Report 2021, Denmark ranked at number two, just behind Finland. This accolade draws attention to the country's exceptional public welfare system, friendly citizens, and sustainable lifestyle. Here's a closer look at Denmark - the world's happiest country.

Getting there

Denmark is located in Northern Europe and is accessible via air or land. Copenhagen Airport is Denmark's largest airport and offers direct flights to most international destinations. There are also domestic airports in Aarhus, Billund, and Ronne. The country is well-connected by train, bus, and ferry services too.

Language and Currency

The official language spoken is Danish; however, English is widely spoken and understood. The local currency is the Danish Krone (DKK), and most places accept credit cards.

Weather

Denmark has a temperate climate with mild winters and cool summers. The best time to visit Denmark is from May to September when the weather is pleasant and daylight lasts longer.

Accommodation

Denmark offers a variety of accommodation options from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Airbnb is also popular among tourists. Some unique accommodation options include staying in a traditional Danish cottage or an eco-friendly farm.

Food and Drink

Denmark's culinary scene boasts Michelin-star restaurants, trendy cafes, and street food markets. Some must-try dishes include Danish open sandwiches, Smørrebrød, and the iconic Danish pastry, Wienerbrød. Denmark is also well-known for its beer and is home to popular breweries like Carlsberg.

FAQs

Q. What is Denmark's public welfare system like?
Denmark has a strong welfare system that provides free healthcare, education, and unemployment benefits. The government also provides financial assistance to families with children.

Q. Is it safe to travel to Denmark?
Yes, Denmark is considered one of the safest countries in the world, and crimes against tourists are low.

Q. What is the best way to get around Denmark?
Denmark's public transport system is efficient, and most cities are bike-friendly. Renting a bike is a popular and sustainable way to explore Denmark.

Q. What are some popular souvenirs to buy in Denmark?
Danish design products, including furniture, homeware, and accessories, make excellent souvenirs. Some popular brands include Georg Jensen and LEGO.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Denmark

1\. Tivoli Gardens - An amusement park located in the heart of Copenhagen, featuring thrilling rides, beautiful gardens, and live entertainment.

2\. Nyhavn - A picturesque canal lined with colorful townhouses, cafes, and restaurants.

3\. The Little Mermaid - A bronze statue of the fairy tale character located on the waterfront in Copenhagen.

4\. Egeskov Castle - A beautiful Renaissance castle surrounded by landscaped gardens, a maze, and a treetop walk.

5\. Viking Ship Museum - A museum dedicated to Viking ships and the seafaring lifestyle of the Vikings.

6\. Legoland Billund - A popular theme park that features LEGO-themed rides and attractions for all ages.

7\. National Museum of Denmark - A museum that showcases Denmark's cultural history, with exhibits dating back to the Stone Age.

8\. Aarhus Old Town - An open-air museum that showcases life in Denmark in the 19th century.

9\. Kronborg Castle - A UNESCO World Heritage Site that was the setting for Shakespeare's Hamlet.

10\. Bornholm Island - A picturesque island that offers stunning scenery, cycle paths, and traditional fishing villages.

Conclusion

Denmark's enduring happiness is the result of conscious efforts made towards social welfare policies, sustainability, and a close-knit community. The country's vibrant cities, picturesque landscapes, and rich cultural history make it the perfect destination for a fulfilling and memorable trip.
