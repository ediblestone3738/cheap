# Best Weekend Trips For Christmas

The holiday season is a magical time of year, and what better way to celebrate than by embarking on a weekend getaway? Whether you're looking to experience a winter wonderland, indulge in festive activities, or simply relax and enjoy the holiday spirit, there are countless destinations perfect for a Christmas weekend trip. Here are some of the best weekend trips for Christmas that will leave you with cherished memories.

## 1. New York City, New York

New York City is a quintessential Christmas destination, offering a dazzling array of holiday attractions and activities. The city transforms into a winter wonderland, with festive decorations and events that capture the magic of the season.

- **Rockefeller Center Christmas Tree**: Witness the iconic Christmas tree at Rockefeller Center, adorned with thousands of lights and a stunning Swarovski star.
- **Ice Skating**: Glide across the ice at the Rockefeller Center or Bryant Park skating rinks.
- **Holiday Markets**: Explore the Union Square Holiday Market and the Columbus Circle Holiday Market for unique gifts and festive treats.
- **Broadway Shows**: Catch a holiday-themed Broadway show, such as "The Nutcracker" or "A Christmas Carol."

## 2. Quebec City, Canada

Quebec City offers a European-style Christmas experience without leaving North America. The city's cobblestone streets and historic architecture provide a charming backdrop for holiday festivities.

- **Old Quebec**: Stroll through the beautifully decorated streets of Old Quebec, a UNESCO World Heritage site.
- **German Christmas Market**: Visit the German Christmas Market for traditional crafts, food, and mulled wine.
- **Tobogganing**: Enjoy a thrilling toboggan ride on the Dufferin Terrace.
- **Winter Carnival**: Experience the Quebec Winter Carnival, featuring parades, ice sculptures, and outdoor activities.

## 3. Vienna, Austria

Vienna is renowned for its elegant Christmas markets and classical music concerts, making it an ideal destination for a festive weekend getaway.

- **Christkindlmarkt**: Explore the Christkindlmarkt at Rathausplatz, one of the most famous Christmas markets in Europe.
- **Concerts**: Attend a classical concert at the Vienna State Opera or the Musikverein.
- **St. Stephen's Cathedral**: Visit St. Stephen's Cathedral for a Christmas Eve mass or a holiday concert.
- **Sacher Torte**: Indulge in a slice of the famous Sacher Torte at Café Sacher.

## 4. Nuremberg, Germany

Nuremberg is home to one of the oldest and most famous Christmas markets in the world, offering a traditional German Christmas experience.

- **Nuremberg Christkindlesmarkt**: Wander through the stalls of the Christkindlesmarkt, offering handmade ornaments, toys, and delicious gingerbread.
- **Nuremberg Castle**: Explore the historic Nuremberg Castle, offering panoramic views of the city.
- **Toy Museum**: Visit the Toy Museum to see a collection of toys from different eras.
- **Bratwurst and Glühwein**: Savor traditional bratwurst and warm up with a cup of Glühwein (mulled wine).

## 5. Lapland, Finland

For a truly magical Christmas experience, head to Lapland, the home of Santa Claus. This winter wonderland offers a range of activities for all ages.

- **Santa Claus Village**: Meet Santa Claus and his reindeer at the Santa Claus Village in Rovaniemi.
- **Northern Lights**: Witness the breathtaking Northern Lights, a natural phenomenon visible in Lapland.
- **Husky Sledding**: Experience the thrill of husky sledding through snowy landscapes.
- **Igloo Hotels**: Stay in a glass igloo hotel and enjoy the beauty of the Arctic sky from the comfort of your bed.

## 6. Prague, Czech Republic

Prague's fairy-tale architecture and festive atmosphere make it a perfect destination for a Christmas weekend trip.

- **Old Town Square**: Visit the Christmas market in Old Town Square, featuring a large Christmas tree and a nativity scene.
- **Charles Bridge**: Take a stroll across the Charles Bridge, beautifully illuminated during the holiday season.
- **Prague Castle**: Explore Prague Castle and attend a Christmas concert at St. Vitus Cathedral.
- **Trdelník**: Try a traditional trdelník, a sweet pastry rolled in sugar and cinnamon.

## 7. Edinburgh, Scotland

Edinburgh offers a unique blend of history, culture, and festive cheer, making it an ideal destination for a Christmas getaway.

- **Edinburgh Christmas Market**: Explore the Christmas market in East Princes Street Gardens, offering crafts, food, and an ice rink.
- **Edinburgh Castle**: Visit Edinburgh Castle and enjoy panoramic views of the city.
- **Hogmanay**: Experience Hogmanay, Scotland's famous New Year's celebration, with torchlight processions and fireworks.
- **Whisky Tasting**: Warm up with a whisky tasting tour at one of Edinburgh's many distilleries.

## 8. Bruges, Belgium

Bruges is a picturesque city that comes alive during the Christmas season, with its medieval architecture and festive markets.

- **Bruges Christmas Market**: Discover the Christmas market in the historic city center, offering Belgian chocolates, waffles, and mulled wine.
- **Canal Cruise**: Take a romantic canal cruise to see the city's illuminated buildings and bridges.
- **Belfry of Bruges**: Climb the Belfry of Bruges for a panoramic view of the city.
- **Choco-Story Museum**: Visit the Choco-Story Museum to learn about the history of chocolate and enjoy tastings.

## 9. Reykjavik, Iceland

Reykjavik offers a unique Christmas experience, with its stunning natural landscapes and festive traditions.

- **Yule Lads**: Learn about Iceland's 13 mischievous Yule Lads, who visit children during the holiday season.
- **Blue Lagoon**: Relax in the geothermal waters of the Blue Lagoon, surrounded by snow-covered landscapes.
- **Northern Lights**: Go on a Northern Lights tour to witness this spectacular natural phenomenon.
- **Christmas Buffet**: Enjoy a traditional Icelandic Christmas buffet, featuring dishes like smoked lamb and fermented shark.

## 10. Asheville, North Carolina

Asheville is a charming city nestled in the Blue Ridge Mountains, offering a cozy and festive Christmas experience.

- **Biltmore Estate**: Visit the Biltmore Estate, beautifully decorated for the holiday season with thousands of lights and ornaments.
- **Downtown Asheville**: Explore the vibrant downtown area, featuring holiday markets, local shops, and festive events.
- **Winter Lights**: Experience the Winter Lights at the North Carolina Arboretum, a dazzling display of lights and holiday decorations.
- **Craft Breweries**: Sample seasonal brews at one of Asheville's many craft breweries.

## Conclusion

Whether you're seeking a traditional Christmas market, a snowy adventure, or a cozy retreat, these destinations offer something for everyone. Each location provides a unique way to celebrate the holiday season, creating memories that will last a lifetime. So pack your bags, embrace the festive spirit, and embark on a magical weekend trip this Christmas.
