Part I - About Gyeongbokgung Palace

1\. History and Background of Gyeongbokgung Palace

Gyeongbokgung Palace is the largest and most beautiful palace in Seoul, and it was the main palace of the Joseon Dynasty. It was first built in 1395 by King Taejo, the founder of the Joseon Dynasty, as a symbol of the newly established dynasty. Gyeongbokgung Palace was destroyed in the Imjin War in 1592, but King Gojong rebuilt it in 1867.

Gyeongbokgung Palace was also used as the seat of the kings of the Joseon Dynasty, and it was the center of political and cultural activities. It hosted important state ceremonies and royal events, including coronations and birthday celebrations. After the Japanese occupation of Korea, much of the palace was destroyed, but it has since been restored to its original grandeur.

2\. Architecture and Design of Gyeongbokgung Palace

Gyeongbokgung Palace covers an area of 410,000 square meters, and it has 5,792 rooms, including a throne hall, banquet halls, and the living quarters of the kings and their families. The palace was built using traditional Korean architectural techniques, and it is a perfect example of the traditional Korean palace complex.

The palace is designed in a symmetrical pattern, and each building has a specific function. The main buildings are the throne hall, the royal banquet hall, and the living quarters of the king and his family. The palace compound also includes landscaped gardens, fountains, and lotus ponds.

3\. The Layout and Style of Gyeongbokgung Palace

The layout and style of Gyeongbokgung Palace is based on principles of feng shui and the traditional Korean architectural style. The palace is structured around several courtyards, with each courtyard serving as a reception area for visiting guests.

The buildings are arranged along the central axis, with the throne hall located in the center. The buildings on either side of the throne hall are the council hall and the banquet hall. The living quarters are located in the rear of the palace, with the king's and queen's quarters on the east and west sides of the complex.

Part II - Things to Do and See in Gyeongbokgung Palace

1\. The Changing of the Guard

The Changing of the Guard ceremony takes place every day at the main gate of Gyeongbokgung Palace. The ceremony is a reenactment of the traditional Changing of the Guard ceremony that was held during the Joseon Dynasty.

Visitors can witness the ceremony, which takes about 20 minutes, and see the guards dressed in traditional Korean clothing and performing a variety of impressive moves.

2\. The National Palace Museum of Korea

The National Palace Museum of Korea is located inside Gyeongbokgung Palace and is home to an impressive collection of Royal artifacts from the Joseon Dynasty. The museum also features exhibits on the history and culture of the Joseon Dynasty, as well as temporary exhibits.

Some of the most popular exhibits include the royal robes, crown jewels, and the wax figures of the royal family.

3\. The National Folk Museum of Korea

The National Folk Museum of Korea is also located inside Gyeongbokgung Palace, and it houses collections of traditional Korean folk art, everyday objects, and tools. The museum features hands-on exhibits, as well as interactive displays that explore the daily life and rituals of the Korean people.

Visitors can see various exhibits that showcase traditional Korean clothing, pottery, and furniture, as well as agricultural and fishing tools.

4\. The Gyeonghoeru Pavilion

The Gyeonghoeru Pavilion is located in the center of a man-made lake in Gyeongbokgung Palace, and it was used by the kings of the Joseon Dynasty for important state ceremonies and banquets. The pavilion is a masterpiece of Korean architecture and is considered one of the most beautiful structures in the palace.

Visitors can enjoy a peaceful walk around the man-made lake and take stunning pictures of the pavilion and the surrounding gardens.

5\. The Hyangwonjeong Pavilion

The Hyangwonjeong Pavilion is a small but beautiful pavilion located on an island in the middle of a pond near the palace. The pavilion is named after its beauty, which is said to rival that of the fragrance of lotus flowers.

Visitors can walk across a small bridge to reach the pavilion and enjoy the stunning view of the palace and the surrounding scenery.
