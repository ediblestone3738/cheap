Unveiling the Hidden Treasures of Fort Lauderdale

Located in Florida’s southeastern coast, Fort Lauderdale is a beautiful city renowned for its white sand beaches, beautiful waterways, and incredible yachts. Known as the Venice of America because of its expansive network of canals that attracts tourists from around the world, Fort Lauderdale is much more than just a scenic paradise. The city also boasts an impressive cultural scene that features stunning art exhibitions, an exciting nightlife, and delicious cuisines from around the world. However, what many visitors don’t know is Fort Lauderdale’s hidden treasures. This article will delve into some of the city’s best-kept secrets and unveil its hidden treasures.

The Bonnet House

Located in the heart of Fort Lauderdale, the Bonnet House is a hidden gem. This art museum features beautiful gardens, an impressive art collection, and historical architecture. Once the winter retreat of famous artists Frederic and Evelyn Bartlett, the Bonnet House has been transformed into a museum that showcases the couple’s art collection, which includes paintings, photographs, and sculptures. The Bonnet House also has scenic gardens and wildlife that adds to its allure.

Hugh Taylor Birch State Park

Hugh Taylor Birch State Park is an oasis in the middle of Fort Lauderdale. This beautiful park features walking trails and bike paths that offer a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. Visitors can hike, bike or stroll along the scenic beach that is lined with palm trees, and if you’re lucky, you may see some of the park’s resident wildlife.

The Fort Lauderdale Antique Car Museum

If you’re a fan of cars and the history behind them, then the Fort Lauderdale Antique Car Museum is a must-see. Located in downtown Fort Lauderdale, this museum houses an extensive collection of antique cars, including rare models such as the 1906 Cadillac and the 1918 Pierce Arrow. In addition to its impressive car collection, the museum also displays an array of memorabilia, including vintage gas pumps, license plates, and road signs.

The Museum of Discovery and Science

The Museum of Discovery and Science is a perfect getaway for families. The museum features interactive exhibits that are designed to educate visitors about science, nature, and technology. Visitors can explore exhibits such as a simulated space shuttle launch, 3D IMAX movie theatre, and interactive displays that feature animals and marine life.

The Stranahan House

The Stranahan House is a historic landmark located in downtown Fort Lauderdale, and rightfully sits on the National Register of Historic Places. This beautiful house provides a glimpse into the city’s past, it dates back as far as 1901 and features beautiful Victorian architecture. The Stranahan House was the home of Frank and Ivy Stranahan, one of the city’s founding families. The house has been carefully restored and is now open to the public for tours.
