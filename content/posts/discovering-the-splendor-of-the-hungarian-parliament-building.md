Discovering the Splendor of the Hungarian Parliament Building

Budapest is one of the few European cities that can truly be described as awe-inspiring. Known for its stunning architecture, picturesque streets, and delicious food, Budapest is the perfect destination for anyone looking for a mix of history and modernity. And at the center of the city stands one of Budapest's most iconic landmarks, the Hungarian Parliament Building.

Built between 1885 and 1904, the Hungarian Parliament Building is an architectural masterpiece that dominates the skyline of Budapest. The building was designed by Imre Steindl, a Hungarian architect who drew inspiration from Gothic and Renaissance architecture.

The Hungarian Parliament Building is a true symbol of Hungarian identity and democracy. In fact, the building serves as the home of the Hungarian parliament, which makes it an important political institution for the country. But even if you're not interested in politics, a visit to the Hungarian Parliament Building is a must-do when you're in Budapest.

In this article, we'll take a closer look at the history and architecture of the Hungarian Parliament Building, as well as the top tourist attractions located in its vicinity. We've also included a FAQs section to answer some of the most common questions about visiting the Hungarian Parliament Building.

Exploring the History and Architecture of the Hungarian Parliament Building

The Hungarian Parliament Building stretches for 268 meters along the bank of the Danube River and has a floor area of 18,000 square meters. With its stunning facade, intricate details, and towering spires, it's no wonder that the Hungarian Parliament Building is one of Budapest's most popular tourist attractions.

The building took 20 years to construct, and it was well worth the wait. The Hungarian Parliament Building is built in the Neo-Gothic style, with a combination of red brick and white limestone as its primary building materials. The building has a symmetrical facade, which is made up of 242 sculptures, including the Four Evangelists, Hungarian monarchs, and military figures.

The interior of the Hungarian Parliament Building is no less impressive. Visitors can marvel at the Grand Staircase, which leads up to the main entrance, and the Dome Hall, which is decorated with frescoes and stained glass. The most impressive room in the building, however, is the Hungarian Crown Jewels Hall, which houses the crown jewels of Hungary, including the Holy Crown of Hungary, which dates back to the early 12th century.

What to See and Do at the Hungarian Parliament Building

1\. Take a Guided Tour - The best way to explore the Hungarian Parliament Building is to take a guided tour. The tours are available in multiple languages and provide visitors with an in-depth look at the history and architecture of the building. You can also book your tour online before your visit to guarantee access.

2\. Attend a Parliamentary Session - If you're interested in politics, you can attend a parliamentary session at the Hungarian Parliament Building. The sessions are open to the public, and you can watch the proceedings from the visitors' gallery.

3\. Marvel at the Crown Jewels - The Crown Jewels Hall is definitely worth a visit. You can see the Holy Crown of Hungary, as well as other crowns, scepters, and orbs. Make sure to arrive early, as the hall can get quite crowded.

4\. Admire the Views - The Hungarian Parliament Building offers stunning views of the Danube River and the Buda Castle. You can take a walk along the riverbank or climb up to the top of the building's spires for an even better vantage point.

5\. Watch the Changing of the Guard - Every hour, on the hour, the changing of the guard takes place in front of the Hungarian Parliament Building. It's a great opportunity to see soldiers in traditional Hungarian costumes and equipment.
