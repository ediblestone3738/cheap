Zakynthos, or as it is commonly known, Zante, is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Greece. With its turquoise waters, pristine beaches, and verdant landscapes, Zante's beauty is unmatched. But one of the island's most charming destinations has to be Laganas, a town located on the southern part of the island. Laganas is a bustling tourist hotspot that has something for everyone, making it perfect for a vacation with family or friends. From nightlife to natural wonders, Laganas has it all. Read on to discover everything you need to know before you visit this charming town.

Getting to Laganas

The easiest way to get to Laganas is by flying directly to Zakynthos airport, which is approximately six kilometers from the town. There are also several ferries that connect the island to the mainland, but these are often less frequent and time-consuming. Once you're on the island, you can take a taxi, bus or rent a car to get to Laganas.

Accommodation

Laganas has a wide variety of accommodation options to choose from, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the most popular choices include the Astir Palace Hotel, the Majestic Hotel & Spa, and the Atlantis Hotel. If you're looking for a more authentic experience, you can also check out some of the local bed and breakfasts or apartments available for rent.

Best time to visit

The best time to visit Laganas largely depends on what you're looking for. If you're interested in soaking up the sun and enjoying the island's natural beauty, then the summer months from June to September are ideal. However, if you're looking to avoid the crowds and experience more of the local culture, then spring or fall is the best time to visit.

Things to do

There's no shortage of things to do in Laganas, whether you're looking for a relaxing day at the beach or an adventurous nightlife. Here are 10 fantastic things to do while you're in town:

1\. Explore the Keri Caves - Take a boat tour to the Keri Caves, where you can snorkel, swim, and explore the island's stunning coastline.

2\. Marathonisi - This small island off the coast of Laganas is home to the endangered Caretta-Caretta turtles, and it's a great place to spend the day sunbathing, swimming, or simply enjoying the pristine scenery.

3\. Navagio Beach - This stunning beach is one of the most famous on the island, thanks to its turquoise waters and the shipwreck that sits on the sand.

4\. Visit the Solomos Square - This historic square is named after the famous Greek poet Dionysios Solomos and features a statue of the poet and his muse, surrounded by cafes and restaurants.

5\. Take a hike in the Keri Forest - The lush greenery of this forest is a great place to explore on foot, with hiking trails leading to stunning views of the sea and the town.

6\. Visit the Venetian Castle - Located in the hillside above the town, this castle was built in the 15th century and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area.

7\. Head to Cameo Island - This small island located just off the coast of Laganas is a popular spot for sunbathing and swimming, thanks to its clear waters and secluded location.

8\. Enjoy the nightlife - Laganas is famous for its nightlife, with several clubs and bars staying open until the early hours of the morning.

9\. Visit the Agios Sostis Church - This historic church dates back to the 16th century and is a great place to learn about the island's religious and cultural heritage.

10\. Take a wine tour - Zante is famous for its wines, and there are several vineyards in the area that offer tours and tastings.

FAQs

Q: Is Laganas safe for tourists?

A: Yes, Laganas is generally considered a safe destination for tourists. However, like any tourist hotspot, it's always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions such as avoiding dark alleys and keeping an eye on your belongings.

Q: What currency is used in Laganas?

A: The euro is the currency used in Laganas and throughout Greece.

Q: Are there any cultural differences I should be aware of when visiting Laganas?

A: Being a tourist destination, Laganas is relatively cosmopolitan and doesn't have many cultural differences. However, it's always a good idea to be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly in churches and other religious sites.

Q: What language is spoken in Laganas?

A: Greek is the official language of Greece, but English is widely spoken in Laganas, especially at tourist establishments.

Q: Can I use my credit card in Laganas?

A: Yes, most establishments in Laganas accept credit cards, but it's a good idea to carry some cash with you just in case.

Conclusion

Laganas is a remarkable destination that has something to offer for everyone. Whether you're interested in natural wonders, cultural experiences, or nightlife, this charming town has it all. With its beautiful beaches, active nightlife, and historic landmarks, Laganas is the perfect spot to enjoy a perfect holiday. Plan a visit to Laganas now and experience the charm of this Greek island for yourself.
