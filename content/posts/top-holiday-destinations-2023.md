It’s never too early to start thinking about where to go on holiday in 2023. With so many incredible places to choose from, it can be hard to decide where to spend your hard-earned time off. To make things a little easier, we've put together a list of top holiday destinations that you don't want to miss in 2023.

1\. Galapagos Islands, Ecuador

The Galapagos Islands are a true natural wonder, known for their unique flora and fauna. This volcanic archipelago is home to some of the most incredible creatures on the planet, including giant tortoises, marine iguanas, and blue-footed boobies. It's the perfect destination for anyone who loves wildlife and outdoor adventures. There are plenty of opportunities to go snorkelling or scuba diving and see exotic fish, sharks and stingrays underwater.

2\. Bhutan

Hidden deep in the Himalayas, Bhutan is a small country that's often referred to as the "Last Shangri-La". It's a Buddhist kingdom that's focused on sustainable development and Gross National Happiness. There are countless monasteries, temples, and fortified dzongs to explore, as well as stunning natural scenery like the Tiger's Nest Monastery, a building perched on a cliff overlooking the Paro Valley.

3\. Scotland, United Kingdom

Scotland is a country that's steeped in history and culture. It's known for its stunning landscapes, from the rugged highlands to the rolling hills of the Borders. Edinburgh is a must-visit city with its castle, the Royal Mile, and the art galleries and theatres. It's also home to some of the world's best whisky distilleries.

4\. Bali, Indonesia

Bali is an island paradise that's perfect for beach-lovers and surfers. It's known for its beautiful beaches, towering volcanoes, and lush rice paddies. The island is also home to many Hindu temples, including the iconic Uluwatu Temple, and some of the world's most beautiful resorts.

5\. Iceland

Iceland is a land of fire and ice, where glaciers and volcanoes coexist. It’s a remote, mysterious and beautiful country that boasts awe-inspiring landscapes from bubbling mud pools to icy landscapes at Vatnajökull glacier. It’s also a top destination for Northern Lights viewing, a unique phenomenon that draws many tourists to Iceland every year.

6\. Japan

Japan is a country known for its rich culture and history. It's the perfect destination for anyone who loves food, art, and technology. From the towering skyscrapers of Tokyo to the beautiful gardens in Kyoto, there's something for everyone in Japan. Don’t forget to try some sushi, explore the beautiful temples and enjoy the beautiful cherry blossoms in the spring.

7\. Botswana

Botswana is one of the best safari destinations in Africa with abundant wildlife and beautiful natural reserves. The country has a diverse range of landscapes, from the Kalahari Desert to the Okavango Delta, a vast wetland that's home to many different species of birds and animals. It's also possible to become fully immersed in the local culture by staying with the indigenous San people.

8\. New Orleans, Louisiana, USA

New Orleans is a city in the Deep South of the USA with a rich history, cultural melting pot of food and music. Known for its vibrant nightlife, jazz music, delicious Cajun and Creole cuisine, and annual Mardi Gras festival, you will never get bored in this city.

9\. Namibia

Namibia is a unique destination known for its extraordinary sand dunes, wildlife and diverse array of cultures, landscapes and photogenic scenery. It's home to the towering dunes of Sossusvlei, the rugged Skeleton Coast, and diverse wildlife in Etosha National Park.

10\. Greece

Greece is a hot destination for travelers looking for sun, sea, rich culture and a rich history. With beautiful beaches, Mediterranean cuisine, and ancient ruins, Greece is a must-visit destination. It's home to iconic places such as Santorini with its turquoise blue water, Oia village with its gloriously picturesque sunsets, Parthenon in Athens and the beautiful island of Mykonos.

FAQs about the top holiday destinations for 2023

Q: When is the best time to visit the Galapagos Islands?
A: The Galapagos Islands are a year-round destination but the best time to visit is between June and December when the seas are cooler and there’s an abundance of wildlife.

Q: What's the weather like in Iceland in winter?
A: In winter, Iceland can be very cold with long nights and occasional snowstorms. However, it's also one of the best times to see the Northern Lights.

Q: Is Bali expensive?
A: Bali is a very affordable destination with many options for accommodation, dining, and transportation.

Q: What's the best way to get around Scotland?
A: The best way to travel around Scotland is by car, as it'll give you the freedom to explore the highlands, islands and coastal roads at your own pace.

Q: What's the weather like in Japan in April?
A: Japan in April is the beautiful cherry blossom season with beautiful pink blooms. The temperature is mild and pleasant, with average highs of around 18°C.

Q: Is Botswana a safe destination for tourists?
A: Botswana is a safe destination for tourists, but it's important to take precautions when going on safari, such as staying in protected areas, and following the guidance of experienced guides.

Q: What visas do I need to visit Namibia?
A: Many nationalities, including US and UK, can visit Namibia without a visa for up to 90 days, but check your visa requirements before leaving home.

Q: What is the Mardi Gras festival?
A: Mardi Gras is a carnival that takes place in New Orleans and other cities around the world before Lent. It’s known for their parades, lively music, and colourful floats.

Q: What's the currency used in Greece?
A: Greece uses the Euro as its currency.

Whether you're looking for adventure, relaxation, or culture, there's a destination on this list that's perfect for you.
