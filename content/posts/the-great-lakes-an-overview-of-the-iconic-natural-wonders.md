# The Great Lakes: An Overview of the Iconic Natural Wonders

The Great Lakes are an iconic natural wonder of North America. Spanning over 750 miles from east to west, these five lakes—Superior, Michigan, Huron, Erie, and Ontario—are the largest group of freshwater lakes in the world. They are a source of beauty, recreation, and commerce, and are home to numerous species of birds, fish, and other wildlife.

The Great Lakes are a treasure trove of history, culture, and outdoor recreation. Native Americans have long inhabited the region, and the lakes were explored by French explorers in the 17th century. The Great Lakes were also the site of some of the most important battles of the War of 1812.

Today, the Great Lakes are a popular tourist destination. From stunning vistas of crystal-clear waters to bustling cities and quaint towns, the Great Lakes offer something for everyone. Here’s a look at what makes this region so special.

## Geography

The Great Lakes are a series of interconnected freshwater lakes located in the upper midwestern United States and southeastern Canada. They are bounded by the Canadian provinces of Ontario and Quebec to the north, and the U.S. states of Minnesota, Wisconsin, Michigan, Illinois, Indiana, Ohio, Pennsylvania, and New York to the south.

The five lakes are all connected by a system of canals and rivers, and are the largest group of freshwater lakes in the world. Together, the Great Lakes hold about 20% of the world’s fresh surface water.

## Wildlife

The Great Lakes are home to a wide variety of wildlife. More than 200 species of fish, including trout, salmon, and bass, inhabit the lakes. In addition, the lakes are a popular destination for migratory birds, such as ducks and geese.

The Great Lakes are also home to numerous species of mammals, including beavers, otters, and deer. The region is also home to a variety of reptiles, amphibians, and insects.

## Recreation
