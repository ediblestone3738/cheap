Rhodes on a Shoestring: How to Enjoy the Island for Cheap

Located in the southeastern Aegean Sea, Rhodes is the largest island of the Dodecanese archipelago in Greece. The island is a perfect destination for anyone seeking a mix of history, culture, and relaxation. From the ancient ruins of the Acropolis of Rhodes to the picturesque villages of Lindos, the island has something to offer for everyone. However, Rhodes is also known for its luxurious resorts and high-end restaurants which can make it difficult for budget travelers to enjoy the island without breaking the bank. In this article, we’ll explore how to enjoy Rhodes on a shoestring, without compromising on the experiences.

Accommodation

Accommodation is one of the main expenses when traveling, especially in tourist hotspots like Rhodes. However, there are many affordable accommodation options available on the island. Hostels and guesthouses are great for budget travelers looking to save money on accommodation. Some popular hostels in Rhodes include Stay Hostel, Als City Hotel, and Old Town Hostel. The prices start at as low as €10 per night for a bed in a dormitory.

Another alternative is Airbnb. The platform offers many affordable options for private rooms and apartments. Staying outside the main tourist areas like Rhodes Town, Faliraki, and Lindos can also help save some money.

Food and Drink

Eating out in Rhodes can be expensive, especially in popular tourist areas. However, there are many ways to save money while enjoying the local cuisine. The island is known for its delicious street food, which is both tasty and affordable. Some popular street food items include souvlaki, gyro, and bougatsa. There are many local street food vendors scattered around Rhodes Town and other tourist areas.

Cooking your meals is also a great way to save money. Many hotels and hostels offer shared kitchens where you can cook your meals. Grocery stores like Lidl and AB Vasilopoulos offer a variety of affordable ingredients to make delicious meals at home.

Transportation

Getting around Rhodes can be expensive if relying on taxis. However, public transportation is also available on the island. Buses are the most affordable way to get around Rhodes with tickets starting at €2.40. The buses connect most of the island’s tourist destinations like Rhodes Town, Lindos, and Faliraki.

Renting a car or scooter can also be a good option, especially for those planning to explore the island independently. Rental prices start at around €20 per day for a scooter and €30 per day for a car.

Free Activities

One of the best ways to enjoy Rhodes on a shoestring is to take advantage of the many free activities available on the island. Rhodes has many beautiful beaches where you can spend a day swimming and lounging. Some popular free beaches include Faliraki Beach, Tsambika Beach, and Agathi Beach.

The island is also home to many historical landmarks like the Acropolis of Rhodes, the Palace of the Grand Master of the Knights of Rhodes, and the Ancient Kamiros Ruins. Most of these attractions are free to enter, and they offer a great opportunity to learn about the island’s rich history and culture.

FAQs

Q: What is the best time to visit Rhodes?
A: The best time to visit Rhodes is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny.

Q: How much money do I need per day in Rhodes?
A: The daily budget will depend on the traveler’s preferences but a budget of €30-€50 per day is reasonable.

Q: Are there any free activities in Rhodes?
A: Yes, there are several free activities in Rhodes like visiting the beaches, historical monuments, and hiking trails.
