If you're an adventure-seeker looking for an island paradise, look no further than Paros. Located in the Cyclades islands of Greece, Paros is a hidden gem that is often overlooked by travelers. From its stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters to its rich history and culture, Paros has something for everyone.

Here's our guide to discovering the treasures of Paros:

Getting There

The easiest way to reach Paros is by ferry from Athens. The trip takes around 3-4 hours, depending on the type of ferry you take. There are also direct ferries from other nearby islands, such as Mykonos and Naxos.

If you prefer to fly, you can take a domestic flight from Athens to Paros National Airport, which is located on the northeast side of the island.

Transportation

Once you arrive on the island, the best way to get around is by car or scooter. There are several rental companies located in the main town of Parikia, as well as in other popular tourist areas such as Naoussa and Piso Livadi.

If you prefer not to drive, there are also buses that run between the main towns and villages on the island.

Accommodations

There are a variety of accommodations to choose from on Paros, from luxury hotels and villas to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels. Some popular areas to stay include the towns of Naoussa and Parikia, as well as the beachside villages of Piso Livadi and Logaras.

Activities

1\. Watersports

One of the most popular activities on Paros is watersports. The island is known for its strong winds, making it a favorite destination for windsurfers and kitesurfers. There are several schools and rental shops located in the popular beaches of Golden Beach and New Golden Beach.

If you prefer something more relaxing, you can also try paddleboarding or kayaking in the calm waters of Kolymbithres beach.

2\. Hiking

Paros is also a great destination for hikers, with several scenic trails to explore. One of the most popular hikes is the ancient path that connects the villages of Lefkes and Prodromos, offering stunning views of the island's countryside and coastline.

Another popular trail is the Byzantine Road, which starts from the village of Kostos and takes you through an old paved path to the village of Marpissa.

3\. Cultural Attractions

Paros is rich in history and culture, with several museums and historical sites to explore. Some popular attractions include:

\- The Archaeological Museum of Paros, located in the main town of Parikia, which houses ancient artifacts and sculptures.

\- The Ancient Quarries, located in the village of Marathi, where the famous Parian marble was extracted.

\- The Church of Ekatontapyliani, also known as the Church of 100 Doors, located in the town of Parikia. This early Christian church is one of the oldest and most important in Greece.

FAQs

Q: What's the best time to visit Paros?
A: The best time to visit Paros is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and dry. However, if you prefer to avoid the crowds, you can also visit in the spring or fall when the weather is still pleasant but there are fewer tourists.

Q: Do I need a visa to visit Paros?
A: If you're a citizen of the European Union, you don't need a visa to visit Paros. If you're from a non-EU country, check the visa requirements before your trip.

Q: Is Paros expensive?
A: Paros can be quite expensive compared to other Greek islands, especially during the peak summer months. However, there are also budget-friendly options if you're willing to do some research.

Paros is a beautiful island that is sure to delight any adventure-seeker. From its stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters to its rich history and culture, there's something for everyone on this hidden gem in the Cyclades. So what are you waiting for?
