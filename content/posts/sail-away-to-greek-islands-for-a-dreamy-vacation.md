As the travel restrictions begin to ease up and the wanderlust within us grows, the Greek Islands remain an all-time favorite holiday destination, rightfully being known as one of the best destinations in the world. With their crystal clear waters, long stretches of golden sand, vibrant nightlife, and truly aromatic cuisine, Sail Away to Greek Islands for a Dreamy Vacation should definitely be on your travel list.

From the sun-kissed beaches of Santorini to the breathtaking blue domes of Mykonos, exploring the Greek Islands by sailboat will be an experience you will never forget. Greece has about 6,000 islands scattered in the Aegean and Ionian seas with only 227 inhabited, with the most popular being Santorini, Mykonos, and Crete. The diverse beaches and villages, the rich history, and the vibrant culture are some of the reasons why people love to come back to enjoy sun, sea, and sand in this Mediterranean paradise.

Best Time to Visit the Greek Islands

With warm weather most of the year, the best time to visit is between April and October. High season starts in June through August, where the islands are overcrowded with tourists, but also the liveliest with parties, and warm summer nights. July and August are the busiest months to travel to Greece, so, expect longer lines at the most popular attractions and higher prices during these peak times.

FAQs

Q: Are the Greek Islands safe to visit?
A: Yes, the Greek Islands are generally safe, but travelers should be aware of the pickpocketing in crowded areas.

Q: What is the best way to get around the Greek Islands?
A: The best way to travel in the Greek Islands is by ferry or private yacht, as there are only limited flights to some of the bigger islands. However, there are some islands where you can drive a car too.

Q: What is the currency in Greece, and do credit cards work?
A: The official currency of Greece is the euro. Credit cards are widely accepted in most of the tourist areas.

Q: Do they speak English in Greece?
A: Yes, most people in the tourist industry in Greece speak English.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in the Greek Islands

1\. Acropolis of Athens- The Acropolis, Athens’ crowning glory, has been standing tall since the 5th century BC.
2\. Santorini’s Caldera- Known as one of the most stunning landscapes on earth, the tranquil blue waters of the Aegean contrast with the white cobblestone houses nestled atop a volcanic cliff.
3\. Golden Beach- Golden Beach is one of the best beaches in Paros for windsurfing and other water sports.
4\. Kleftiko Beach- Located on Milos, one of the prettiest Greek islands with white and pink volcanic beaches and clear, turquoise waters, Kleftiko beach is best found by boat.
5\. Lindos- The village of Lindos is one of the prettiest villages on the island of Rhodes.
6\. Little Venice, Mykonos- Little Venice is one of the most beautiful and romantic spots in Mykonos town.
7\. Oia- Oia is a must-visit because of its romantic scenery and sunset views from the famous windmill or a restaurant.
8\. Elafonisi Beach- Located on the island of Crete, Elafonisi Beach is known for its pink sand, crystal clear water, and stunning scenery.
9\. Navagio Beach, Zakynthos- Navagio Beach, on the island of Zakynthos, is famous for its clear blue waters and the famous old shipwreck.
10\. Medieval Town of Rhodes- Rhodes is a beautiful island with a rich history with unlike any of the other Greek Islands, where the beautiful combination of Ottoman and Venetian architecture takes precedence.
