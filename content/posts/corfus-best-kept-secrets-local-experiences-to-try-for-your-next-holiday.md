Corfu's Best-kept Secrets – Local Experiences to Try for Your Next Holiday

Corfu, an island in the Ionian Sea, is a popular tourist destination known for its stunning beaches, green landscapes, Venetian architecture, and charming villages. But beyond the usual tourist attractions, there are plenty of hidden gems waiting to be discovered. In this article, we'll take a closer look at Corfu's best-kept secrets – local experiences to try for your next holiday.

1\. Traditional food and drink

Corfu is known for its delicious cuisine, which is influenced by Italian, Greek, and Turkish flavors. Local tavernas offer a wide range of dishes, from seafood to meat-based meals. Some traditional dishes to look out for are pastitsada, a spicy beef or rooster stew, sofrito, a veal cooked in white wine and garlic, and bourdeto, a spicy fish stew.

While visiting Corfu, stop at one of the island's many coffee shops, where you can enjoy a strong Greek coffee or a refreshing frappe. For something stronger, try kumquat liqueur, made from the island's famous citrus fruit.

2\. Olive oil and wine tasting

Corfu is known for producing some of the best olive oil and wine in Greece. Take a tour of an olive mill, where you can see how the oil is made and taste the different varieties. You can also visit a local winery and sample the island's delicious wines, including the red Retsina and the white Robola.

3\. Hiking and walking trails

Corfu is a hiker's paradise, with plenty of walking and hiking trails that take you through scenic villages and stunning landscapes. A popular trail is the Corfu Trail, which runs from Kavos in the south to the northernmost point of the island, Cape Drastis. Along the way, you'll pass through olive groves, vineyards, and dense forests.

4\. Island hopping

While Corfu itself has plenty to offer, there are other nearby islands that are worth visiting, such as Paxos and Antipaxos. These smaller islands are known for their crystal-clear waters and secluded beaches.

5\. Archeological sites

Corfu has a rich history, and there are several archeological sites that are worth exploring. The most famous is the Ancient City of Corfu, which dates back to the 8th century BC. You can also visit the Old Fortress, which was built by the Venetians in the 15th century.

6\. Local festivals

Corfu is known for its colorful festivals, many of which are religious celebrations. One of the most famous is the Feast of St. Spyridon, the island's patron saint, which takes place in December. Another popular festival is the Carnival, which takes place in February and is known for its colorful parades and parties.

7\. Beaches

Corfu has some of the most beautiful beaches in Greece, with crystal-clear waters and stunning backdrops. Some of the best beaches to visit are Glyfada, Sidari, and Paleokastritsa.

8\. Water sports

If you're looking for a bit of adventure, there are plenty of water sports to try in Corfu, such as parasailing, jet skiing, and scuba diving.

9\. Local markets

Corfu has several local markets where you can buy fresh produce, handmade crafts, and souvenirs. The most popular market is the Spianada Market, which takes place on Saturday mornings.

10\. Bird watching

Corfu is home to a wide variety of bird species, making it a popular destination for bird watchers. Head to the Lake Antinioti and the wetlands of Korission to spot a range of species, including the rare pink flamingo.

FAQs

Q: When is the best time to visit Corfu?
A: The best time to visit Corfu is between May and September, when the weather is warm and sunny.

Q: What currency is used in Corfu?
A: The currency used in Corfu is the Euro.

Q: What are some traditional souvenirs to buy in Corfu?
A: Some traditional souvenirs to buy in Corfu are olive oil, wine, kumquat liqueur, and handmade ceramics.

Q: Are there any local traditions or customs to be aware of in Corfu?
A: Yes, Corfu has several local traditions, such as the Easter Saturday tradition of throwing ceramic pots out of windows and balconies.

Q: How do I get around Corfu?
A: The best way to get around Corfu is by car or motorcycle, as public transportation can be unreliable.
