# Best Places To Go On Christmas Eve

Christmas Eve is a magical time of year when cities and towns around the world transform into winter wonderlands. Whether you're looking for a snowy retreat, a cultural experience, or a tropical escape, there are countless destinations that offer unique and memorable ways to celebrate the holiday season. In this article, we'll explore some of the best places to go on Christmas Eve, each offering its own special charm and festive spirit.

## 1. New York City, USA

New York City is synonymous with Christmas magic. The city comes alive with twinkling lights, festive window displays, and iconic holiday attractions.

- **Rockefeller Center**: Witness the iconic Christmas tree and ice skate beneath its sparkling lights.
- **Radio City Music Hall**: Enjoy the world-famous Rockettes in the Radio City Christmas Spectacular.
- **Fifth Avenue**: Stroll along this famous street to see elaborate holiday window displays.
- **Central Park**: Take a horse-drawn carriage ride through the snow-covered park.

## 2. Lapland, Finland

For a truly enchanting Christmas experience, visit Lapland, the official home of Santa Claus.

- **Santa Claus Village**: Meet Santa himself and cross the magical Arctic Circle.
- **Northern Lights**: Experience the awe-inspiring Aurora Borealis in the winter sky.
- **Reindeer Sleigh Rides**: Glide through snowy landscapes pulled by reindeer.
- **Snow Hotels**: Spend a night in a hotel made entirely of snow and ice.

## 3. Vienna, Austria

Vienna is a city that embodies the elegance and charm of a traditional European Christmas.

- **Christmas Markets**: Explore the city's numerous markets, such as the Christkindlmarkt at Rathausplatz.
- **St. Stephen's Cathedral**: Attend a Christmas Eve mass in this stunning Gothic cathedral.
- **Vienna State Opera**: Enjoy a holiday performance in one of the world's most prestigious opera houses.
- **Ringstrasse**: Take a tram ride around this historic boulevard adorned with festive lights.

## 4. Quebec City, Canada

Quebec City offers a picturesque setting with its cobblestone streets and European architecture.

- **Old Quebec**: Wander through this UNESCO World Heritage site, beautifully decorated for the holidays.
- **German Christmas Market**: Experience a traditional German market with crafts, food, and entertainment.
- **Château Frontenac**: Stay in this iconic hotel that resembles a fairytale castle.
- **Tobogganing**: Slide down the historic Dufferin Terrace toboggan run.

## 5. Tokyo, Japan

Tokyo offers a unique blend of traditional and modern celebrations during the Christmas season.

- **Illuminations**: Marvel at the city's dazzling light displays, particularly in areas like Shibuya and Roppongi.
- **Tokyo Disneyland**: Experience the magic of Disney with special holiday parades and shows.
- **Christmas Cakes**: Indulge in a Japanese Christmas tradition by enjoying a beautifully decorated cake.
- **Shopping**: Explore the city's vibrant shopping districts for holiday gifts and souvenirs.

## 6. Prague, Czech Republic

Prague's fairy-tale architecture and festive atmosphere make it a perfect Christmas destination.

- **Old Town Square**: Visit the Christmas market and see the famous Astronomical Clock.
- **Charles Bridge**: Walk across this historic bridge, offering stunning views of the city.
- **Prague Castle**: Explore the castle grounds and attend a Christmas concert.
- **Vltava River Cruise**: Enjoy a scenic cruise with views of Prague's illuminated skyline.

## 7. Sydney, Australia

For a warm-weather Christmas, Sydney offers a unique holiday experience with its sunny beaches and outdoor festivities.

- **Bondi Beach**: Celebrate Christmas with a beach barbecue and swim in the ocean.
- **Carols in the Domain**: Join thousands for a festive carol-singing event in the park.
- **Sydney Harbour**: Take a cruise to see the city's iconic landmarks, including the Opera House and Harbour Bridge.
- **Taronga Zoo**: Visit the zoo for special holiday-themed events and activities.

## 8. Strasbourg, France

Known as the "Capital of Christmas," Strasbourg offers a quintessential European holiday experience.

- **Christkindelsmärik**: Explore one of the oldest Christmas markets in Europe, dating back to 1570.
- **Strasbourg Cathedral**: Admire the stunning Gothic architecture and attend a holiday mass.
- **Petite France**: Wander through this charming district with its half-timbered houses and festive decorations.
- **Alsatian Cuisine**: Savor traditional dishes like choucroute and tarte flambée.

## 9. Reykjavik, Iceland

Reykjavik offers a unique and adventurous Christmas experience with its dramatic landscapes and festive traditions.

- **Yule Lads**: Learn about Iceland's mischievous holiday figures who visit children in the 13 days leading up to Christmas.
- **Blue Lagoon**: Relax in the geothermal waters surrounded by snow-covered lava fields.
- **Northern Lights Tours**: Chase the elusive Aurora Borealis on a guided tour.
- **Christmas Markets**: Browse local crafts and enjoy traditional Icelandic treats.

## 10. Edinburgh, Scotland

Edinburgh's historic charm and lively celebrations make it a wonderful place to spend Christmas Eve.

- **Edinburgh Christmas Market**: Explore the market in East Princes Street Gardens with its festive stalls and attractions.
- **Hogmanay**: Experience Scotland's famous New Year's celebration with torchlight processions and fireworks.
- **Edinburgh Castle**: Visit the iconic castle and enjoy panoramic views of the city.
- **Royal Mile**: Stroll down this historic street lined with shops and holiday decorations.

## Conclusion

Whether you're dreaming of a white Christmas or a sun-soaked holiday, these destinations offer something for everyone. From the bustling streets of New York City to the serene landscapes of Lapland, each place provides a unique way to celebrate the magic of Christmas Eve. So pack your bags, embrace the festive spirit, and create unforgettable memories in one of these incredible locations.
