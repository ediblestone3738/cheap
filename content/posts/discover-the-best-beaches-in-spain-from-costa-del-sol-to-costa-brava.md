Spain is known for its beautiful beaches that attract millions of tourists every year. Spanning over two thousand kilometers of coastline, the country offers a diverse range of beaches and coastal towns, each with its unique charm and character. From the sunny beaches of the Costa del Sol to the rugged coves of the Costa Brava, we've rounded up the best beaches in Spain that are not to be missed.

Costa del Sol
The Costa del Sol is one of Spain's most popular beach destinations, known for its warm climate, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant nightlife. With over 320 days of sunshine per year, this stretch of coastline features some of the best beaches in Spain.

1\. Playa de la Malagueta: Located in the heart of Malaga, this urban beach is popular amongst tourists and locals alike. With its fine golden sand and crystal-clear waters, it's one of the most popular and busiest beaches on the Costa del Sol.

2\. Playa del Cristo: A hidden gem situated in the east of Estepona, Playa del Cristo is a small sandy cove that offers a peaceful escape from the summer crowds. The calm and shallow waters make it ideal for families with children.

3\. Playa de la Fontanilla: Located in the chic town of Marbella, Playa de la Fontanilla is a long sandy beach with plenty of water sports activities on offer. It's an ideal spot for sunbathing, swimming, and enjoying a refreshing drink at one of its beach bars.

Costa de la Luz
The Costa de la Luz, also known as the "coast of light," is situated on the Atlantic Ocean and boasts some of the wildest and most untouched beaches in the country.

4\. Playa de Bolonia: Located in Tarifa, Playa de Bolonia is known for its golden sand dunes and turquoise waters. It's an ideal spot for windsurfing and kiteboarding enthusiasts, and its unspoiled landscape makes it a perfect place to unwind and relax.

5\. Playa de Matalascañas: Situated in Huelva, Playa de Matalascañas is a wide and sandy beach that stretches for miles. It's a popular spot amongst families and is well-equipped with beach bars, restaurants, and water sports activities.

6\. Playa del Faro de Trafalgar: Located in the town of Barbate, Playa del Faro de Trafalgar is a long sandy beach with crystal-clear waters. It's a popular spot amongst surfers and features stunning views of the Cape Trafalgar lighthouse.

Costa Brava
The Costa Brava, situated in the north of Catalonia, is known for its rugged cliffs, hidden coves, and crystal-clear waters. It's an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering plenty of hiking trails and water sports activities.

7\. Cala Pola: Located in Tossa de Mar, Cala Pola is a small and secluded beach with crystal-clear waters. It's an ideal spot for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts, as the waters are home to plenty of marine life.

8\. Playa de Aiguablava: Situated in Begur, Playa de Aiguablava is a stunning and secluded beach with crystal-clear waters. It's an ideal spot for kayaking and paddleboarding, and its rocky cliffs offer stunning views of the coast.

9\. Playa de Tamariu: Located in Palafrugell, Playa de Tamariu is a small and picturesque sandy beach. It's an ideal spot for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying a traditional seafood meal at one of the beachfront restaurants.

FAQs
Q. Is it safe to swim at the beaches in Spain?
A. Yes, it's safe to swim at the beaches in Spain. However, it's always advisable to check the local weather conditions and any warning signs before entering the water.

Q. Are there any nude beaches in Spain?
A. Yes, there are several nude beaches in Spain. The most popular ones are situated in the Balearic Islands, Costa Brava, and Costa del Sol.

Q. Can I bring my dog to the beach in Spain?
A. It depends on the beach. Some beaches in Spain allow dogs, while others don't. It's advisable to check the local rules and regulations before taking your dog to the beach.
