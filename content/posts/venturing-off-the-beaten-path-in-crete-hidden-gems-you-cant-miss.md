Crete, the largest of the Greek islands, is a popular holiday destination due to its sunny weather, picturesque villages, and crystal-clear waters. Most tourists flock to the island's well-known destinations, such as Chania, Rethymno, and Heraklion. However, there is an abundance of hidden gems on the island that are often overlooked by visitors. If you're looking to venture off the beaten path in Crete and discover some of the island's best-kept secrets, look no further than this guide.

Hidden Gems You Can't Miss

1\. The Lasithi Plateau
Located in the eastern part of Crete, the Lasithi Plateau is a hidden gem that is not often visited by tourists. The plateau is home to traditional villages, ancient ruins, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains. Be sure to visit the windmills of Lasithi, which are still in operation and provide a glimpse into traditional Cretan life.

2\. Gorge of Samaria
The Gorge of Samaria is the longest gorge in Europe and is a must-see for any adventure seeker. The 16-km hike through the gorge takes about 5-7 hours and provides stunning views of the natural landscape, including towering rock formations, wildflowers, and the turquoise waters of the Krios Potamos river.

3\. Kato Zakros
Kato Zakros is a small seaside village located in the eastern part of Crete. The village is known for its beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and ancient ruins. Be sure to visit the Minoan Palace of Kato Zakros, which was built around 1900 BC and is considered one of the most important archaeological sites in Crete.

4\. Elounda
Elounda is a picturesque fishing village located on the eastern coast of Crete. The village is known for its luxury resorts, stunning beaches, and crystal-clear waters. Be sure to take a boat trip to the island of Spinalonga, which was once a leper colony and is now a popular tourist attraction.

5\. Frangokastello
Frangokastello is a small village located on the southern coast of Crete. The village is known for its Venetian fortress, which was built in 1371 and is still standing today. Be sure to visit in May or June when the blooming almond trees provide a stunning backdrop to the fortress.

6\. Vai Beach
Vai Beach is a secluded beach located on the eastern coast of Crete. The beach is known for its crystal-clear waters and unique palm forest, which is one of the largest in Europe. Be sure to visit in the early morning or late afternoon to avoid the crowds.

7\. Loutro
Loutro is a small fishing village located on the southern coast of Crete. The village is only accessible by boat or on foot and is known for its crystal-clear waters and stunning views of the surrounding mountains. Be sure to visit the old Venetian fortress, which provides spectacular sunset views over the sea.

8\. Kournas Lake
Kournas Lake is the only natural lake in Crete and is located in the northern part of the island. The lake is surrounded by picturesque mountains and is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and canoeing.

9\. Chania Old Town
While Chania is a popular tourist destination, the Old Town is often overlooked by visitors. The town is full of charming alleyways, historic buildings, and hidden courtyards. Be sure to visit the Venetian Harbor, which is one of the most picturesque spots in Chania.

10\. Preveli Beach
Preveli Beach is located on the southern coast of Crete and is known for its stunning scenery and crystal-clear waters. The beach is only accessible by boat or on foot and provides a peaceful retreat from the crowds of more popular beaches.

FAQs

Q: What is the best time to visit Crete?
A: The best time to visit Crete is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny. July and August can be very crowded, so if you're looking for a quieter experience, try visiting in May or September.

Q: What is the currency in Crete?
A: The currency in Crete is the Euro.

Q: Do I need a visa to visit Crete?
A: If you are an EU citizen, you do not need a visa to visit Crete. If you are a citizen of a non-EU country, check with your local Greek embassy to see if you need a visa.

Q: What is the official language in Crete?
A: The official language in Crete is Greek, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas.

Q: What is the best way to get around Crete?
A: The best way to get around Crete is by car. While there is public transportation available, having a car provides more flexibility to explore the island's hidden gems.
