Fort Lauderdale, Florida is a city with a rich and fascinating history. Fort Lauderdale was first settled by the Tequesta Native Americans in the 18th century. The fort was named after Major William Lauderdale, who was the leader of the U.S. military forces in the area at the time.

In the late 1800s, the city began to grow as a trading post and fishing village. At the same time, it became a popular spot for tourists who were looking to escape the cold winters of the north.
