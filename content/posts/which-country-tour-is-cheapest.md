Exploring the World on a Budget: 8 Cheapest Countries to Visit from India

When it comes to travelling, it’s easy to get caught up in the idea of expensive, luxurious vacations. But the truth is, there are plenty of amazing places to explore without breaking the bank. In fact, some of the most incredible destinations in the world are surprisingly affordable.

If you’re looking for a budget-friendly getaway, look no further than these 8 cheapest countries to visit from India. From lush jungles to pristine beaches, these destinations offer a wealth of experiences that won’t break the bank.

1\. Sri Lanka

Named as the top country to visit in 2020 by Lonely Planet, Ravan’s Lanka is famous for its pristine beaches and great food. Whether you’re looking for a beach getaway or a cultural experience, Sri Lanka has something for everyone.

Accommodation in Sri Lanka is surprisingly affordable, with budget hotels and guesthouses available for as little as $10 per night. Food is also incredibly cheap, with delicious meals costing as little as $2. With its beautiful beaches, friendly locals, and delicious cuisine, Sri Lanka is the perfect destination for budget travellers.

2\. Bhutan

Bhutan, “Thunder Dragon”, is located between India and China. This tiny Himalayan kingdom is home to some of the most breathtaking scenery in the world. From snow-capped mountains to lush valleys, Bhutan is a paradise for nature lovers.

The cost of living in Bhutan is surprisingly low, with accommodation and food costing a fraction of what you’d pay in other countries. A meal at a local restaurant can cost as little as $3, and a night in a hotel can cost as little as $15.

3\. Nepal

Nepal is a landlocked country in South Asia, bordered by India and China. It’s home to some of the world’s most spectacular mountain scenery, including the world’s highest peak, Mount Everest.

Nepal is a great destination for budget travellers, with accommodation and food costing a fraction of what you’d pay in other countries. A meal at a local restaurant can cost as little as $2, and a night in a hotel can cost as little as $10.
