Lisbon, the capital of Portugal is a city full of charm and unique attractions. From the intricate maze-like streets of Alfama to the lively Chiado, the city is filled with cultural, historical and culinary surprises.

Exploring this city is a treat for all the senses. Here, you can experience the aroma of freshly baked pastries, the sound of shaking tramcars and the sights of colourful buildings and artisans shops. In this article, we will explore some of the best attractions and cuisine that Lisbon has to offer.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Lisbon

1\. Belem Tower – This tower is an icon of Lisbon and stands at the mouth of the Tagus River. A UNESCO World Heritage site, Belem Tower was built in the 16th century as a defence mechanism.

2\. Jerónimos Monastery – Adjacent to Belem Tower, Jerónimos Monastery is another UNESCO World Heritage site. Built in the 16th century, the monastery is one of the highlights of the Portuguese Late Gothic Manueline style of architecture.

3\. The Castle of São Jorge – The castle is perched on top of a hill and offers some of the best views of the city. This castle has witnessed some interesting moments in history, and today gives visitors a glimpse of what life was like during the medieval times.

4\. Praça do Comércio – This is a large square in the heart of Lisbon and is one of the city’s main attractions. The square is surrounded by magnificent yellow buildings and has a statue of King José I in the centre.

5\. Rossio Square – This is another one of Lisbon’s main squares and is located in the heart of the city. Rossio Square is surrounded by colourful buildings, cafes and restaurants.

6\. Lisbon Oceanário – Located in the Parque das Nações, the Lisbon Oceanário is an aquarium that contains several different marine species. It is a must-visit for anyone who is interested in aquatic life.

7\. Elevador de Santa Justa – This is an iron lift that will take you to the top of the Santa Justa hill. It is a unique way to see the city from above and is best visited at sunset.

8\. National Museum of Ancient Art – If you are interested in art and history, then the National Museum of Ancient Art is a must-visit. The museum houses some of the finest collections of Portuguese art, including paintings, sculptures and ceramics.

9\. Alfama – This is the oldest and most picturesque neighbourhood in Lisbon. Its narrow streets are lined with colourful buildings, charming cafes and artisan shops.

10\. Chiado – Known as the cultural heart of Lisbon, Chiado is a neighbourhood filled with museums, bookstores and theatres. It is also home to some of the city’s best restaurants and shopping.

Unique Attractions in Lisbon

Lisbon is full of unique attractions that are not to be missed. Here are some of them:

Lisbon Trams – The yellow trams that run through Lisbon’s streets are an iconic part of the city’s landscape. They are a great way to get around and to see the sights.

Miradouros – Miradouros are lookouts that offer stunning views of the city. There are several Miradouros in Lisbon, including Miradouro da Graça and Miradouro Santa Luzia.

Fado Music – This is a traditional Portuguese music genre, which is most commonly heard in Lisbon. It is characterized by melancholic tunes and lyrics about love, loss and longing. Fado houses, such as Clube do Fado and A Baiuca are great places to experience this music.

Cuisine in Lisbon

Portuguese cuisine is delicious, and Lisbon has some of the best restaurants in the country. Here are some dishes that are a must-try:

Pastel de Nata – This is a classic Portuguese pastry that is famous all over the world. It is made of puff pastry and filled with a creamy custard.

Bacalhau – Codfish is a staple in Portuguese cuisine and is usually served in a variety of dishes, including stewed with potatoes and onions or grilled simply with olive oil.

Cozido – This is a traditional Portuguese stew that is usually served on special occasions. It is made of different meats, potatoes and vegetables.

Arroz de Marisco – This is a seafood dish that is a signature dish of Lisbon. It is made of rice and a variety of seafood, such as shrimp, clams and squid.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q: What is the best time to visit Lisbon?

A: The best time to visit Lisbon is from March to May or September to October, when the temperatures are milder and there are fewer crowds.

Q: How do I get around Lisbon?

A: Lisbon has an excellent public transport system, including buses, trams and the metro. Taxis and Ubers are also readily available.

Q: Is Lisbon a safe city?

A: Overall, Lisbon is considered a safe city. However, like any big city, visitors should still take precautions to ensure their personal safety.

Q: What is the official language of Lisbon?

A: The official language of Lisbon is Portuguese, but many locals also speak English.

Q: What are some must-try dishes in Lisbon?

A: Pastel de Nata, Bacalhau, Cozido and Arroz de Marisco are some must-try dishes in Lisbon.

In conclusion, Lisbon is a fascinating city that has something to offer to everyone. From its UNESCO World Heritage sites, unique attractions to delicious cuisine, it is a city worth exploring. So, why not plan a trip to Lisbon today?
