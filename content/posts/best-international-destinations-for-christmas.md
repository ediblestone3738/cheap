# Best International Destinations For Christmas

The festive season is a magical time of year, and what better way to celebrate than by exploring some of the world's most enchanting destinations? Whether you're dreaming of a white Christmas or seeking a sun-soaked holiday escape, there's a perfect place for everyone. Here are some of the best international destinations to visit during Christmas.

## 1. Vienna, Austria

Vienna transforms into a winter wonderland during the Christmas season. The city's historic architecture and festive atmosphere make it a top destination for holiday travelers.

- **Christmas Markets**: Vienna is famous for its Christmas markets, with the **Vienna Christmas World** at Rathausplatz being the most popular. Here, you can enjoy traditional Austrian treats and shop for unique gifts.
- **Ice Skating**: The city offers several ice skating rinks, including the picturesque rink in front of the Rathaus.
- **Concerts and Performances**: Attend a classical concert or a performance at the Vienna State Opera to experience the city's rich musical heritage.

## 2. New York City, USA

New York City is synonymous with Christmas cheer, offering a vibrant and bustling holiday experience.

- **Rockefeller Center**: The iconic Christmas tree at Rockefeller Center is a must-see, along with the nearby ice skating rink.
- **Holiday Windows**: Stroll down Fifth Avenue to admire the elaborate holiday window displays at department stores like Macy's and Saks Fifth Avenue.
- **Broadway Shows**: Catch a festive Broadway show or the famous Radio City Christmas Spectacular featuring the Rockettes.

## 3. Lapland, Finland

For a truly magical Christmas experience, visit Lapland, the official home of Santa Claus.

- **Santa Claus Village**: Meet Santa Claus himself and explore his village in Rovaniemi, located on the Arctic Circle.
- **Northern Lights**: Witness the breathtaking Northern Lights, a natural phenomenon that illuminates the night sky.
- **Reindeer and Husky Safaris**: Embark on a reindeer or husky safari through the snow-covered landscapes.

## 4. Prague, Czech Republic

Prague's fairy-tale charm is amplified during the Christmas season, making it a perfect destination for holiday travelers.

- **Old Town Square**: The Christmas market in Old Town Square is one of the most beautiful in Europe, featuring a stunning Christmas tree and traditional Czech crafts.
- **Charles Bridge**: Walk across the historic Charles Bridge, adorned with festive lights and decorations.
- **Czech Cuisine**: Indulge in traditional Czech holiday dishes, such as carp and potato salad, or warm up with a glass of mulled wine.

## 5. Tokyo, Japan

Tokyo offers a unique and modern take on Christmas celebrations, blending traditional customs with contemporary flair.

- **Illuminations**: The city's illuminations are a sight to behold, with dazzling light displays in areas like Shibuya, Shinjuku, and Roppongi.
- **Christmas Markets**: Visit European-style Christmas markets, such as the one at Tokyo Skytree, for festive food and gifts.
- **Theme Parks**: Experience Christmas-themed attractions at Tokyo Disneyland and DisneySea.

## 6. Quebec City, Canada

Quebec City exudes old-world charm and is a picturesque destination for a snowy Christmas getaway.

- **Old Quebec**: The historic district of Old Quebec is transformed into a charming Christmas village, complete with twinkling lights and festive decorations.
- **German Christmas Market**: Enjoy the traditional German Christmas market, offering handmade crafts and delicious treats.
- **Winter Activities**: Take part in winter activities such as tobogganing, ice skating, and snowshoeing.

## 7. Sydney, Australia

For those seeking a warm and sunny Christmas, Sydney offers a unique holiday experience down under.

- **Bondi Beach**: Celebrate Christmas with a beach barbecue at Bondi Beach, a popular spot for locals and tourists alike.
- **Carols by Candlelight**: Attend the Carols by Candlelight event at the Domain, a beloved Australian tradition.
- **Sydney Harbour**: Enjoy a Christmas cruise on Sydney Harbour for stunning views of the city skyline and iconic landmarks.

## 8. Strasbourg, France

Known as the "Capital of Christmas," Strasbourg is home to one of the oldest and most famous Christmas markets in Europe.

- **Christkindelsmärik**: Explore the Christkindelsmärik, located in the city's historic center, and discover a wide array of festive goods and delicacies.
- **Cathedral of Notre Dame**: Visit the stunning Strasbourg Cathedral, beautifully illuminated during the holiday season.
- **Alsatian Cuisine**: Savor traditional Alsatian dishes, such as choucroute and tarte flambée, at local restaurants.

## 9. Reykjavik, Iceland

Reykjavik offers a unique and enchanting Christmas experience, with its stunning natural landscapes and festive traditions.

- **Yule Lads**: Discover the Icelandic tradition of the Yule Lads, mischievous characters who bring gifts to children during the 13 nights leading up to Christmas.
- **Geothermal Pools**: Relax in one of Reykjavik's geothermal pools, such as the famous Blue Lagoon, surrounded by snow-covered landscapes.
- **Christmas Lights**: Enjoy the city's festive Christmas lights and decorations, creating a cozy and magical atmosphere.

## 10. Edinburgh, Scotland

Edinburgh is a captivating destination for a Christmas holiday, with its rich history and vibrant festivities.

- **Edinburgh Christmas Market**: Visit the Christmas market in East Princes Street Gardens, offering a variety of gifts, food, and entertainment.
- **Hogmanay**: Extend your stay to experience Hogmanay, Scotland's famous New Year's celebration, featuring fireworks, concerts, and street parties.
- **Edinburgh Castle**: Explore the historic Edinburgh Castle, offering stunning views of the city and festive events during the holiday season.

## 11. Nuremberg, Germany

Nuremberg is renowned for its traditional Christmas market, one of the most famous in the world.

- **Nuremberg Christkindlesmarkt**: Explore the Christkindlesmarkt, offering a wide range of handcrafted gifts, ornaments, and delicious treats like gingerbread and sausages.
- **Children's Market**: Visit the Children's Market, featuring rides, games, and activities for families.
- **Medieval Architecture**: Wander through the city's medieval streets, beautifully decorated for the holiday season.

## 12. Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

For a tropical Christmas celebration, Rio de Janeiro offers a lively and colorful holiday experience.

- **Copacabana Beach**: Spend Christmas Day on the famous Copacabana Beach, enjoying the sun and sea.
- **Christmas Tree**: Admire the world's largest floating Christmas tree, located on the Rodrigo de Freitas Lagoon.
- **Carnival Atmosphere**: Experience the vibrant carnival atmosphere with festive music, dance, and celebrations throughout the city.

## 13. Bruges, Belgium

Bruges is a charming and romantic destination for a Christmas getaway, with its cobblestone streets and medieval architecture.

- **Bruges Christmas Market**: Explore the Christmas market in the historic city center, offering a variety of gifts and local delicacies.
- **Ice Sculpture Festival**: Visit the Ice Sculpture Festival, featuring stunning ice and snow sculptures created by artists from around the world.
- **Canal Cruises**: Take a winter canal cruise to see the city's beautiful architecture and festive lights from the water.

## 14. Cape Town, South Africa

Cape Town offers a unique blend of festive traditions and stunning natural beauty for a memorable Christmas holiday.

- **Table Mountain**: Take a cable car ride to the top of Table Mountain for breathtaking views of the city and coastline.
- **Kirstenbosch Carols**: Attend the Kirstenbosch Carols by Candlelight, a popular event held in the beautiful botanical gardens.
- **V&A Waterfront**: Explore the V&A Waterfront, offering festive shopping, dining, and entertainment options.

## 15. Salzburg, Austria

Salzburg, the birthplace of Mozart, is a picturesque destination for a traditional Austrian Christmas.

- **Salzburg Christmas Market**: Visit the Christmas market in the historic Residenzplatz, offering a variety of crafts, ornaments, and culinary delights.
- **Silent Night Chapel**: Explore the Silent Night Chapel in nearby Oberndorf, where the famous Christmas carol was first performed.
- **Sound of Music Tour**: Take a Sound of Music tour to see the filming locations of the beloved movie, set against the stunning backdrop of the Austrian Alps.

## Conclusion

Whether you're seeking a winter wonderland or a sun-soaked escape, these international destinations offer something for everyone during the Christmas season. From traditional markets and festive lights to unique cultural experiences, each location provides a magical setting to create unforgettable holiday memories. So pack your bags and embark on a Christmas adventure to one of these enchanting destinations.
