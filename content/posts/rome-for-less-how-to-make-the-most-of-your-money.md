Rome is one of the world's most popular travel destinations, known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and delicious cuisine. However, it can also be quite an expensive city to visit. Fortunately, with a little planning and some insider tips, it's possible to make the most of your money in Rome and still experience all the beauty this city has to offer.

Here are some tips on how to save money in Rome:

1\. Walk instead of taking taxis - Rome is a relatively small city, and most of its attractions are located within walking distance of each other. Plus, walking is a great way to get some exercise while taking in the sights and sounds of the city.

2\. Use public transportation - If you do need to take public transportation, consider using a 24-hour or multi-day pass to save money. The city's metro and bus systems are efficient and convenient.

3\. Eat like a local - Avoid tourist traps by eating at smaller, locally-owned restaurants outside of the city center. Look for places that offer fixed-price lunch menus or aperitivos to save money on meals.

4\. Skip the guided tours - Many of Rome's main attractions, such as the Colosseum and the Pantheon, offer audio guides that can be rented for a fraction of the cost of a guided tour. This allows you to explore at your own pace and save money in the process.

5\. Visit attractions during off-hours - Some popular attractions, such as the Vatican Museums, offer discounted admission during off-peak hours. This can be a great way to save money while avoiding the crowds.

Top 10 tourist attractions in Rome for less:

1\. The Pantheon - This ancient temple is free to enter and offers stunning architecture and a rich history.

2\. Piazza Navona - This bustling square is full of street performers and cafes, making it an ideal spot to people-watch.

3\. The Roman Forum - This sprawling complex of ruins offers a glimpse into ancient Rome and is free to enter.

4\. The Trevi Fountain - Make a wish and toss a coin into this iconic fountain, which is free to visit.

5\. The Spanish Steps - Climbing this monumental staircase offers panoramic views of Rome and is also free to visit.

6\. The Colosseum - While admission to the Colosseum can be pricey, simply admiring this ancient wonder from the outside is free and just as impressive.

7\. Villa Borghese - This expansive park offers stunning views of Rome and is free to enter. It also features several museums and galleries at added cost.

8\. Appian Way - This ancient road offers a glimpse into Rome's rich history and can be explored for free.

9\. Trastevere - This charming neighborhood is full of narrow alleys and colorful buildings, and is free to explore on foot.

10\. Santa Maria in Trastevere - This beautiful church is one of Rome's oldest and is free to enter.
