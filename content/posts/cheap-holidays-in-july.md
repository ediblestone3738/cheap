## How to Find Affordable Accommodation for a Cheap Holiday in July

When planning a summer holiday in July, many people have a budget to stick to. Finding affordable accommodation doesn't have to be difficult. With research and planning, great holidays can still be enjoyed without breaking the bank.

Think about staying at **budget hotels or hostels** instead of expensive resorts. These places usually cater to bargain-hunting travelers, and provide bedding and bathrooms for low prices. It's not as luxurious, but you'll save money.

Airbnbs and **private guestrooms** are also good options. Look out for _discounts or promotions_ that could make the lodgings even cheaper. Many hosts offer discounts for multiple nights.

Finally, don't forget to check with _family members or friends_ who live in the city. They'll likely welcome you with open arms and offer their place with **no (or minimal) cost** in exchange for quality time together!

## Tips for Saving Money on a Cheap Holiday in July

Planning a **low-cost holiday in July** can be challenging. Prices tend to go up during the summer season since there's more demand. However, you can still get great offers with some planning and research.

Think about when you book. Many hotels and tour companies offer discounts at the weekends or during _less popular times_ such as mid-week or outside of school holidays. Also remember that the cost of accommodation changes depending on _how many people you're sharing with_. Consider splitting costs between friends and family or travelling as a big group to get cheaper rates in total. Tell travel agents about your budget before booking, so they can give you recommendations.

Look online for deals. Sites like **Secret Escapes** and **Wowcher** offer discounted breaks at a small price. But check all conditions before committing to them, as some have _hidden charges, fees or restrictions_.

Finally, if you have time, develop relationships with local businesses - tour operators and hoteliers. They may alert you to **last minute offers** only through their communication channels. You might also be able to _negotiate discounts_ if you book directly, instead of through third party online bookings websites.
