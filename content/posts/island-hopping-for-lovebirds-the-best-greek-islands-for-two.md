Island Hopping for Lovebirds: The Best Greek Islands for Two

Greece, with its striking limestone cliffs, white-sand beaches, and crystal-clear waters, is a dream destination for many couples. The nation consists of more than 6,000 islands, with over 200 of them inhabited, meaning there's a lot of island hopping that couples can do. Island hopping, as the name suggests, entails traveling from one island to another, and Greece is the ideal location to do so. If you're searching for the ideal Greek island hopping itinerary for two, keep reading.

The Best Greek Islands for Two - Island Hopping for Lovebirds

1\. Santorini
Santorini is the most well-known of the Greek islands and is a must-see destination for any couple. It's known for its blue-domed churches, whitewashed buildings, and stunning caldera views. The donkey rides up to the top of the village and sunset on Oia are must-see activities on this island.

2\. Mykonos
Mykonos is known for its party culture, exceptional beaches, and fabulous nightlife. The island is particularly lively in July and August, and couples may enjoy the fun and dance parties. Paradise and Super Paradise are the most popular beaches, and couples can enjoy the romantic sunsets from Little Venice.

3\. Paros
Paros, with its charming ports, long sandy beaches, and welcoming locals, is a must-see location. This island is ideal for couples who want to unwind as they wander the charming streets of its towns, such as Naoussa's famous nightlife.

4\. Naxos
Naxos, the most extensive island in the Cyclades, is renowned for its citrus trees, impressive mountains, and stunning beaches. Couples can rent scooters to explore the island's rich history, including the temple of the mighty Zeus and Tranquil Plaka beach.

5\. Crete
Crete is Greece's largest island and the ideal destination for couples who want to explore the nation's rich history and sense of civilization. Couples can explore the awe-inspiring Gorge of Samaria, which is Europe's longest gorge, and the Palace of Knossos, dating back to 2000 BC.

6\. Ios
Ios is a tiny island only 18 kilometers long with pristine beaches and some of the best nightclubs in Greece. It's located between Santorini and Mykonos and is ideal for couples looking for a lively nightlife.

7\. Milos
Milos is a lesser-known island with hidden coves, turquoise waters, and magnificent seashores formed by volcanic activity. Couples can have one of the best days in Sarakiniko beach, wander the cobbled streets of the charming capital, or take a romantic boat ride on the island's crystal-clear waters.

8\. Skiathos
Skiathos, Greece's smallest inhabited island, is more than its size suggests. It offers attractive beaches with clear turquoise seas and pine forests going approximately to the beach's shores. Couples can relax and sunbathe on the island's most famous beach, Koukounaries, listed among the most beautiful beaches in Europe.

9\. Rhodes
Rhodes island is located just off the Turkish coast and is a testament to Greek civilization's impressive history. Couples can explore the Old town, which is a walled city with impressive medieval architecture or relax on the island's golden beaches.

10\. Samos
Samos is a small island located in the Aegean sea and is famous for its vineyards, exceptional hiking trails, and quaint villages. Couples can visit Heraion, the astonishing Temple of Goddess Hera, explore Ancient Samos, or relax on the serene beaches of Psili Ammos.

FAQs

1\. Are the Greek islands costly?

The Greek islands are typically more expensive from June to September, the peak tourist season, with airfare and accommodation rates increasing considerably. When traveling in the offseason, such as October, November, and April, couples will find more affordable rates.

2\. What language do they speak in Greece?
The official language spoken in Greece is Greek, but many people also speak English as a secondary language, particularly in high tourist areas.

3\. How long do couples require to travel the Greek Islands?
It requires at least two weeks to traverse the Greek Islands to have ample time to explore and unwind.

4\. What is the cost of transportation on the Greek Islands?

The ferry is more cost-effective than any other form of transportation, with the average cost being around 10-30 euros between islands. The ferry journey durations differ, lasting from 15 minute to 12 hours.
