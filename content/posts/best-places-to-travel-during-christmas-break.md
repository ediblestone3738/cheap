# Best Places To Travel During Christmas Break

The holiday season is a magical time of year, and there's no better way to celebrate than by exploring new destinations. Whether you're looking for a winter wonderland, a tropical escape, or a cultural experience, there are countless places to visit during Christmas break. In this article, we'll explore some of the best destinations to make your holiday season unforgettable.

## 1. New York City, USA

New York City is synonymous with Christmas cheer. The city transforms into a winter wonderland with festive decorations, holiday markets, and iconic attractions.

- **Rockefeller Center Christmas Tree**: Witness the dazzling lights of the Rockefeller Center Christmas Tree, a symbol of the holiday season in NYC.
- **Ice Skating at Central Park**: Glide across the ice at the Wollman Rink, surrounded by the stunning backdrop of Central Park.
- **Holiday Window Displays**: Stroll down Fifth Avenue to admire the elaborate holiday window displays at famous department stores like Macy's and Saks Fifth Avenue.
- **Broadway Shows**: Catch a holiday-themed Broadway show to add a touch of theatrical magic to your trip.

## 2. Lapland, Finland

For a truly enchanting Christmas experience, head to Lapland, Finland, the official home of Santa Claus.

- **Santa Claus Village**: Visit Santa Claus Village in Rovaniemi, where you can meet Santa himself and cross the Arctic Circle.
- **Northern Lights**: Marvel at the breathtaking Northern Lights, a natural phenomenon that illuminates the night sky with vibrant colors.
- **Reindeer Sleigh Rides**: Experience a traditional reindeer sleigh ride through the snowy landscapes of Lapland.
- **Snow Hotels**: Stay in a snow hotel for a unique and cozy experience, complete with ice sculptures and warm sleeping bags.

## 3. Vienna, Austria

Vienna is a city that exudes elegance and charm, especially during the Christmas season.

- **Christmas Markets**: Explore the city's famous Christmas markets, such as the Vienna Christmas World at Rathausplatz, offering handmade crafts and delicious treats.
- **Classical Concerts**: Attend a classical concert at one of Vienna's historic venues, like the Vienna State Opera or Musikverein.
- **St. Stephen's Cathedral**: Visit the iconic St. Stephen's Cathedral, beautifully adorned with holiday decorations.
- **Viennese Coffeehouses**: Warm up with a cup of Viennese coffee and a slice of Sachertorte in one of the city's traditional coffeehouses.

## 4. Quebec City, Canada

Quebec City offers a European-style Christmas experience without leaving North America.

- **Old Quebec**: Wander through the cobblestone streets of Old Quebec, a UNESCO World Heritage site, beautifully decorated for the holidays.
- **German Christmas Market**: Enjoy the festive atmosphere of the German Christmas Market, featuring traditional crafts and culinary delights.
- **Tobogganing**: Experience the thrill of tobogganing on the Dufferin Terrace, offering stunning views of the St. Lawrence River.
- **Ice Hotel**: Spend a night at the Hôtel de Glace, a stunning ice hotel with intricately carved ice sculptures.

## 5. Tokyo, Japan

Tokyo offers a unique blend of traditional and modern Christmas celebrations.

- **Illuminations**: Discover the city's spectacular illuminations, such as the Tokyo Midtown and Shibuya Blue Cave light displays.
- **Christmas Markets**: Visit the European-style Christmas markets in Roppongi Hills and Yokohama Red Brick Warehouse.
- **Tokyo Disneyland**: Experience the magic of Christmas at Tokyo Disneyland, with festive parades and decorations.
- **Shopping**: Explore the bustling shopping districts of Shibuya and Ginza for holiday gifts and seasonal treats.

## 6. Prague, Czech Republic

Prague is a fairy-tale city that becomes even more enchanting during the Christmas season.

- **Old Town Square Christmas Market**: Visit the Christmas market in Old Town Square, featuring a towering Christmas tree and traditional Czech crafts.
- **Charles Bridge**: Stroll across the historic Charles Bridge, offering stunning views of the city and its festive lights.
- **Prague Castle**: Explore Prague Castle, adorned with holiday decorations and hosting special Christmas events.
- **Czech Cuisine**: Indulge in traditional Czech holiday dishes, such as carp and potato salad, at local restaurants.

## 7. Sydney, Australia

For those seeking a warm-weather Christmas, Sydney offers a unique holiday experience.

- **Bondi Beach**: Celebrate Christmas on the iconic Bondi Beach, where you can enjoy a festive barbecue and soak up the sun.
- **Sydney Harbour**: Take a cruise on Sydney Harbour to admire the city's skyline and festive decorations.
- **Carols in the Domain**: Join the annual Carols in the Domain event, featuring live performances and a festive atmosphere.
- **New Year's Eve Fireworks**: Extend your stay to witness Sydney's world-famous New Year's Eve fireworks over the Harbour Bridge.

## 8. Strasbourg, France

Strasbourg is known as the "Capital of Christmas" and offers a quintessential European holiday experience.

- **Christkindelsmärik**: Explore the Christkindelsmärik, one of the oldest Christmas markets in Europe, offering a wide array of crafts and delicacies.
- **Cathedral of Notre-Dame**: Visit the stunning Cathedral of Notre-Dame, beautifully illuminated for the holiday season.
- **Alsatian Cuisine**: Savor traditional Alsatian dishes, such as choucroute and tarte flambée, at local restaurants.
- **La Petite France**: Wander through the charming district of La Petite France, adorned with festive decorations and twinkling lights.

## 9. Reykjavik, Iceland

Reykjavik offers a unique and adventurous Christmas experience with its stunning natural landscapes.

- **Northern Lights**: Chase the Northern Lights for a chance to see this mesmerizing natural phenomenon.
- **Blue Lagoon**: Relax in the geothermal waters of the Blue Lagoon, surrounded by snow-covered landscapes.
- **Yule Lads**: Learn about Iceland's unique Christmas traditions, including the mischievous Yule Lads.
- **Christmas Markets**: Visit Reykjavik's Christmas markets to find unique Icelandic gifts and enjoy festive treats.

## 10. Nuremberg, Germany

Nuremberg is home to one of the most famous Christmas markets in the world.

- **Nuremberg Christkindlesmarkt**: Explore the Nuremberg Christkindlesmarkt, known for its traditional wooden stalls and festive atmosphere.
- **Gingerbread**: Sample Nuremberg's famous gingerbread, a holiday treat that dates back centuries.
- **Medieval Architecture**: Admire the city's well-preserved medieval architecture, beautifully illuminated for the holidays.
- **Toy Museum**: Visit the Nuremberg Toy Museum to learn about the city's rich toy-making history.

## Conclusion

Whether you're dreaming of a snowy escape or a sun-soaked holiday, there's a perfect Christmas destination waiting for you. From the festive streets of New York City to the enchanting markets of Europe, these destinations offer unforgettable experiences that will make your Christmas break truly special. Pack your bags, embrace the holiday spirit, and create memories that will last a lifetime.
