History:

Puerto Rico has a complex history that dates back to the early 16th century when Christopher Columbus claimed the island for Spain during his second voyage to the Americas. The island was used as a military stronghold for Spain before being ceded to the United States in 1898 as part of the Treaty of Paris following the Spanish-American War. Puerto Ricans were granted US citizenship in 1917, and the island was granted commonwealth status in 1952, allowing it to have a degree of autonomy while remaining a territory of the US.

The US government has had a significant impact on Puerto Rico over the years. Puerto Ricans have fought in every US conflict since World War I, and the island is home to several US military bases.
