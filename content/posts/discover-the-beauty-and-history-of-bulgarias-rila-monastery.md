# Discover the Beauty and History of Bulgaria’s Rila Monastery

Nestled in the Rila Mountains of Bulgaria is the stunning Rila Monastery. Founded in the 10th century, this is the largest and most important monastery in the country and a symbol of Bulgarian national identity. It is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular tourist destination. Visitors come from all over the world to admire the monastery’s beautiful architecture, stunning mountain views, and rich history.

## History of the Rila Monastery

The Rila Monastery was founded in 927 AD by St. John of Rila, a hermit who lived in the nearby mountains. He was canonized in the 11th century and is now the patron saint of Bulgaria. In the centuries that followed, the monastery grew in size and importance. It was destroyed by fire in the 19th century and then rebuilt in the same style.

The monastery is a complex of several buildings, including a main church, chapels, a bell tower, and a library. The main church is the largest building and its exterior is decorated with elaborate frescoes. Inside, visitors will find a stunning gold-plated iconostasis and a wooden icon of St. John of Rila.

## Things to Do at the Rila Monastery

The Rila Monastery is a popular tourist destination and there are many things to do and see here. Visitors can explore the complex, admiring the beautiful architecture and frescoes. The monastery also has a museum with artifacts from its long history.

The surrounding mountains offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities. Visitors can go for a hike or take a cable car ride up to the peak of Mount Rila. There are also several ski resorts in the area.
