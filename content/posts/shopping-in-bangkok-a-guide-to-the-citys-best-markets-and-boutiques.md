Shopping in Bangkok: A Guide to the City's Best Markets and Boutiques

Bangkok is a city of contrasts, and this is especially true when it comes to shopping. From ultra-modern malls to traditional outdoor markets, Bangkok has got it all. Shopping is an integral part of the Thai culture, and the shoppers in this city are some of the most zealous in the world. Bangkok has been called the shopaholic's paradise, and it is easy to see why. Here is a guide to the city's best markets and boutiques.

Markets in Bangkok

Chatuchak Weekend Market

Chatuchak Weekend Market is arguably the biggest market in Bangkok, and a popular spot for both locals and tourists. Known locally as the JJ Market, the open-air bazaar is spread over 35 acres and is home to more than 15,000 stalls. You can find virtually anything you could think of here from clothing, antiques, homeware, souvenirs, and much more.

Sampeng Market

Sampeng Market is a paradise for bargain hunters. The market is located in Chinatown and is a maze of alleys and streets that are full of tiny shops and stalls. You can find a wide range of goods here, from jewellery and clothes to toys and home goods.

Pratunam Market

Pratunam Market is one of the best markets in Bangkok for fashion lovers. It is a maze of stalls selling everything from clothes and shoes to bags and accessories. It is best to visit early in the morning as the market becomes crowded as the day goes on.

Asiatique

Asiatique is a relatively new market in Bangkok, set on the banks of the Chao Phraya River. The market is a mix of shopping, dining, and entertainment that is open daily from 5 pm until midnight. Asiatique is a great place to spend your evenings, and you can find a range of goods, from accessories to home decor.

Boutiques in Bangkok

Emporium

Emporium is a high-end shopping mall located in Sukhumvit which is the heart of Bangkok's business and shopping district. The mall has eight floors and features a variety of luxury brands.

Siam Paragon

Siam Paragon is another large mall located in the Siam area of Bangkok. The mall has everything from designer stores to luxury cinemas, food courts, and a multitude of dining options.

Central World

Central World is one of the largest shopping malls in Bangkok and is located in the Ratchaprasong area. The mall houses more than 500 stores, a wide variety of dining options, and an ice skating rink.

FAQs

1\. What is the best time to go shopping in Bangkok?
The best time to go shopping in Bangkok is early in the morning when the markets and malls are less crowded. However, most markets and malls in Bangkok are open until late in the evening.

2\. How do I bargain when shopping in Bangkok?
Most of the markets in Bangkok operate on a bargaining system, and it is expected that you will negotiate the price. When bargaining, be polite, and don't be afraid to start with a low offer. However, don't bargain too much if you feel that the seller has been fair.

3\. Are credit cards widely accepted in Bangkok markets?
Credit cards are commonly accepted in the larger malls in Bangkok, but it is best to carry cash when shopping in markets.

4\. What is the typical cost of goods in Bangkok markets?
The cost of goods in Bangkok markets varies, but generally, they are lower than the prices you would find at western stores.

5\. Can I try on clothes in market stalls?
It is not always possible to try on clothes in market stalls, but some clothing sellers will allow it. It is best to ask the seller directly.
