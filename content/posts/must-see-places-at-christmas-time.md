# Must See Places At Christmas Time

Christmas is a magical time of year when cities around the world transform into winter wonderlands, each offering its own unique charm and festive spirit. Whether you're seeking traditional Christmas markets, dazzling light displays, or snow-covered landscapes, there's a perfect destination waiting for you. In this article, we'll explore some must-see places at Christmas time that promise to fill your holiday season with joy and wonder.

## 1. New York City, USA

New York City is synonymous with Christmas magic. The city that never sleeps becomes even more vibrant during the holiday season, offering a plethora of activities and sights that capture the essence of Christmas.

- **Rockefeller Center Christmas Tree**: A visit to NYC during Christmas wouldn't be complete without seeing the iconic Rockefeller Center Christmas Tree. The towering tree, adorned with thousands of lights and a stunning Swarovski star, is a sight to behold.
- **Ice Skating at Central Park**: Glide across the ice at the Wollman Rink in Central Park, surrounded by the city's skyscrapers and the park's natural beauty.
- **Holiday Window Displays**: Stroll down Fifth Avenue to admire the elaborate holiday window displays at famous department stores like Macy's, Saks Fifth Avenue, and Bergdorf Goodman.
- **Radio City Christmas Spectacular**: Experience the magic of the Rockettes at the Radio City Music Hall, a beloved holiday tradition featuring high-kicking precision dance routines.

## 2. Vienna, Austria

Vienna is a city that embraces the Christmas spirit with elegance and charm. Its historic architecture and festive markets create a fairy-tale atmosphere that enchants visitors from around the world.

- **Vienna Christmas Markets**: Explore the city's numerous Christmas markets, including the Christkindlmarkt at Rathausplatz, where you can enjoy mulled wine, roasted chestnuts, and handcrafted gifts.
- **Belvedere Palace**: Visit the Christmas Village at Belvedere Palace, where the baroque architecture provides a stunning backdrop for festive stalls and twinkling lights.
- **St. Stephen's Cathedral**: Attend a Christmas concert at St. Stephen's Cathedral, where the soaring Gothic architecture and beautiful music create a memorable experience.
- **Schönbrunn Palace**: Wander through the Christmas market at Schönbrunn Palace, offering a delightful mix of traditional crafts, culinary delights, and festive entertainment.

## 3. Lapland, Finland

For a truly magical Christmas experience, head to Lapland, the official home of Santa Claus. This winter wonderland offers a unique blend of natural beauty and festive activities.

- **Santa Claus Village**: Meet Santa Claus himself at the Santa Claus Village in Rovaniemi, where you can cross the Arctic Circle and send postcards from Santa's official post office.
- **Northern Lights**: Witness the breathtaking Northern Lights, a natural phenomenon that illuminates the night sky with vibrant colors.
- **Reindeer Sleigh Rides**: Experience the thrill of a reindeer sleigh ride through the snow-covered forests, guided by local Sami herders.
- **Husky Safaris**: Embark on a husky safari, where you can mush your own team of dogs through the pristine Arctic wilderness.

## 4. Strasbourg, France

Known as the "Capital of Christmas," Strasbourg boasts one of the oldest and most famous Christmas markets in Europe. The city's half-timbered houses and cobblestone streets provide a picturesque setting for holiday festivities.

- **Christkindelsmärik**: Explore the Christkindelsmärik, located in the heart of the city, offering a wide array of artisanal products, regional delicacies, and festive decorations.
- **Cathedral of Notre Dame**: Admire the stunning Gothic architecture of the Cathedral of Notre Dame, beautifully illuminated during the Christmas season.
- **Petite France**: Wander through the charming district of Petite France, where the festive atmosphere is enhanced by the twinkling lights and decorated facades.
- **Alsatian Cuisine**: Indulge in traditional Alsatian dishes such as tarte flambée, choucroute, and bredele cookies, which are especially popular during the holiday season.

## 5. Tokyo, Japan

Tokyo offers a unique blend of traditional and modern Christmas celebrations, with dazzling illuminations and festive events throughout the city.

- **Tokyo Midtown Christmas**: Visit Tokyo Midtown for its spectacular light displays, including the Starlight Garden, where thousands of LED lights create a mesmerizing show.
- **Roppongi Hills**: Explore Roppongi Hills, where the Christmas Market and stunning illuminations provide a festive atmosphere.
- **Tokyo Disneyland**: Experience the magic of Christmas at Tokyo Disneyland, where the park is transformed with holiday decorations, parades, and special events.
- **Ginza**: Stroll through the upscale district of Ginza, where the streets are adorned with elegant Christmas lights and luxury stores offer festive window displays.

## 6. Quebec City, Canada

Quebec City is a charming destination that exudes old-world charm and festive cheer during the Christmas season. Its European-style architecture and snow-covered streets create a picturesque winter wonderland.

- **Old Quebec**: Wander through the historic streets of Old Quebec, where the cobblestone lanes and centuries-old buildings are beautifully decorated for the holidays.
- **German Christmas Market**: Visit the German Christmas Market in the heart of the city, offering traditional crafts, mulled wine, and delicious treats.
- **Château Frontenac**: Admire the iconic Château Frontenac, which is beautifully illuminated and offers stunning views of the city and the St. Lawrence River.
- **Tobogganing**: Enjoy a thrilling toboggan ride at the Terrasse Dufferin, a popular winter activity that offers fun for the whole family.

## 7. Prague, Czech Republic

Prague is a city that captivates visitors with its stunning architecture and rich history. During Christmas, the city becomes even more enchanting with its festive markets and holiday traditions.

- **Old Town Square Christmas Market**: Explore the Christmas market at Old Town Square, where you can find a variety of handcrafted gifts, traditional Czech foods, and festive entertainment.
- **Prague Castle**: Visit Prague Castle, where the Christmas market offers a unique setting with panoramic views of the city.
- **Charles Bridge**: Stroll across the Charles Bridge, where the views of the city and the Vltava River are especially beautiful during the holiday season.
- **Czech Christmas Traditions**: Experience traditional Czech Christmas customs, such as the lighting of the Advent wreath and the baking of vánočka, a sweet braided bread.

## 8. Reykjavik, Iceland

Reykjavik offers a unique Christmas experience with its stunning natural landscapes and vibrant holiday celebrations. The city's festive spirit is complemented by the breathtaking beauty of Iceland's winter scenery.

- **Yule Lads**: Discover the Icelandic tradition of the Yule Lads, mischievous characters who visit children during the 13 nights leading up to Christmas.
- **Northern Lights**: Witness the awe-inspiring Northern Lights, which can often be seen dancing across the Icelandic skies during the winter months.
- **Christmas Markets**: Explore Reykjavik's Christmas markets, where you can find Icelandic crafts, local delicacies, and festive entertainment.
- **Blue Lagoon**: Relax in the warm waters of the Blue Lagoon, surrounded by snow-covered landscapes, for a truly unique holiday experience.

## 9. Nuremberg, Germany

Nuremberg is home to one of the most famous Christmas markets in the world, the Christkindlesmarkt. This historic city offers a quintessential German Christmas experience with its festive atmosphere and traditional offerings.

- **Christkindlesmarkt**: Visit the Christkindlesmarkt, where you can browse stalls selling handmade ornaments, gingerbread, and mulled wine.
- **Nuremberg Castle**: Explore Nuremberg Castle, which provides a stunning backdrop for the city's Christmas celebrations.
- **Lebkuchen**: Sample Nuremberg's famous lebkuchen, a traditional gingerbread that is especially popular during the holiday season.
- **Children's Christmas Market**: Bring the family to the Children's Christmas Market, offering rides, games, and activities for kids of all ages.

## 10. Edinburgh, Scotland

Edinburgh is a city that knows how to celebrate Christmas in style. With its historic architecture and festive events, it offers a magical holiday experience for visitors of all ages.

- **Edinburgh Christmas Market**: Explore the Christmas market in East Princes Street Gardens, offering a variety of crafts, foods, and festive entertainment.
- **Ice Skating**: Glide across the ice at the St Andrew Square ice rink, surrounded by the city's beautiful architecture.
- **Edinburgh Castle**: Visit Edinburgh Castle, where the views of the city and the surrounding landscapes are especially stunning during the winter months.
- **Hogmanay**: Extend your holiday celebrations with Edinburgh's famous Hogmanay festival, a New Year's celebration featuring fireworks, concerts, and a torchlight procession.

## Conclusion

From the bustling streets of New York City to the serene landscapes of Lapland, these must-see places at Christmas time offer a diverse range of experiences that capture the magic of the holiday season. Whether you're seeking traditional markets, dazzling light displays, or unique cultural traditions, these destinations promise to fill your Christmas with joy and wonder. So pack your bags, embrace the festive spirit, and embark on a holiday adventure you'll never forget.
