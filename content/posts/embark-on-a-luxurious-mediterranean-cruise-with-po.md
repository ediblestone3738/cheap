So, what can you expect from a P&O Mediterranean cruise, and where should you go? Here's everything you need to know.

Where does a P&O Mediterranean cruise go?

P&O offers a range of itineraries that take in some of the Mediterranean's most popular destinations, as well as some hidden gems. Some of the highlights of a P&O Mediterranean cruise include:

\- The Greek Islands: Discover the ancient history and stunning scenery of the Greek Islands, including Santorini, Mykonos, Rhodes, and Crete.
\- The Amalfi Coast: Explore the picturesque towns and breathtaking coastline of the Amalfi Coast, including Sorrento, Positano, and Capri.
\- The French Riviera: Soak up the glamour and romance of the French Riviera, from the stylish streets of Cannes to the Monte Carlo casino.
\- Barcelona: Immerse yourself in the colorful culture and architecture of Barcelona, from the Gothic Quarter to the surreal designs of Gaudi.
\- Dubrovnik: Step back in time in the walled city of Dubrovnik, known as the "pearl of the Adriatic" for its photogenic red roofs and stunning views.

What can you expect from a P&O Mediterranean cruise?

P&O prides itself on offering a luxurious, all-inclusive experience for its passengers. From the moment you step on board, you'll be greeted with a range of amenities and activities to keep you entertained and pampered throughout your cruise. Here are just a few of the things you can expect:

\- Multiple restaurants and bars: Whether you're in the mood for fine dining or a casual snack, P&O offers a range of options to suit every taste and budget.
\- Spa and wellness facilities: With indulgent treatments and state-of-the-art gyms and pools, P&O's spa and wellness facilities offer the ultimate in relaxation and rejuvenation.
\- Entertainment: From live music and theater shows to comedy and cabaret, P&O's entertainment options are second-to-none.
\- Shore excursions: P&O offers a range of shore excursions that allow you to explore your chosen destinations in depth, with expert guides and exclusive access to sights and attractions.
\- Luxury accommodations: P&O's cabins and suites are designed with comfort, style, and convenience in mind, with everything from private balconies to personal butlers.

What are the top 10 tourist attractions on a P&O Mediterranean cruise?

If you're planning a Mediterranean cruise with P&O, you won't want to miss these top 10 tourist attractions:

1\. The Parthenon in Athens, Greece: One of the most iconic landmarks of ancient Greece, the Parthenon is a majestic temple dedicated to the goddess Athena.

2\. The Blue Grotto in Capri, Italy: This natural sea cave is famous for its electric blue waters, created by the reflection of sunlight on the underwater rocks.

3\. The Alhambra in Granada, Spain: This stunning Moorish palace complex is a masterpiece of Islamic architecture, with intricate details and beautiful gardens.

4\. The Colosseum in Rome, Italy: One of the world's most famous landmarks, the Colosseum is a testament to the grandeur and brutality of Ancient Rome's gladiatorial games.

5\. The Sagrada Familia in Barcelona, Spain: This surreal basilica is the masterpiece of Catalan architect Antoni Gaudi, whose designs are a visual feast for the senses.

6\. The Old Town of Dubrovnik, Croatia: With its impressive city walls and red-roofed buildings, Dubrovnik's Old Town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that dates back to the 7th century.

7\. The Cisterns of Istanbul, Turkey: This subterranean Byzantine cathedral is a mesmerizing sight, with its towering columns and eerie reflections in the water.

8\. Monte Carlo Casino in Monaco: A symbol of the French Riviera's glamour and opulence, the Monte Carlo Casino is a must-visit destination for anyone who loves luxury and golden-age Hollywood.

9\. Pompeii in Italy: This ancient Roman city was buried under ash and lava when Mount Vesuvius erupted in 79 AD, but its ruins have been remarkably well-preserved, offering a fascinating glimpse into daily life in ancient times.

10\. The Acropolis Museum in Athens, Greece: This state-of-the-art museum displays a range of artifacts from Ancient Greece, including sculptures, vases, and jewelry, all housed in a sleek modernist building.

FAQs about a P&O Mediterranean cruise

1\. How many days is a typical P&O Mediterranean cruise?

P&O offers a range of itineraries, from short three-day cruises to longer voyages of a week or more. The length of your cruise will depend on the destinations you want to visit and your budget.

2\. What is the best time of year to take a P&O Mediterranean cruise?

The Mediterranean is at its busiest in the summer months, with June to August being the peak season. However, if you prefer milder weather and fewer crowds, you may want to consider traveling in April, May, or September.

3\. Do I need a visa for a P&O Mediterranean cruise?

It depends on your nationality and the countries you'll be visiting. You'll need to check the visa requirements for each destination on your itinerary and ensure you have any necessary documents in advance.

4\. What should I pack for a P&O Mediterranean cruise?

You'll want to pack comfortable, lightweight clothing that's suitable for warm weather, as well as a few dressier outfits for formal nights on board. Don't forget to bring sunscreen, a hat, and comfortable shoes for shore excursions.

5\. What safety measures does P&O have in place for COVID-19?

P&O has implemented a range of safety protocols to ensure the health and safety of its passengers and crew during the pandemic, including enhanced cleaning and disinfection measures, mandatory masks in public areas, and pre-boarding health screenings.
