# Christmas Places To Travel

The holiday season is a magical time of year when cities and towns around the world transform into winter wonderlands. From twinkling lights and festive markets to snow-covered landscapes and cozy traditions, there's no shortage of enchanting destinations to explore. Whether you're seeking a classic Christmas experience or something a bit more unique, here are some of the best places to travel during the Christmas season.

## 1. Vienna, Austria

Vienna is renowned for its elegant architecture, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene. During Christmas, the city becomes even more enchanting with its traditional markets and festive atmosphere.

- **Christmas Markets**: Vienna's Christmas markets are among the most famous in Europe. The Christkindlmarkt at Rathausplatz is a must-visit, offering a wide array of handcrafted gifts, delicious treats, and warm beverages.
- **Ice Skating**: The ice rink in front of the Vienna City Hall is a popular spot for both locals and tourists. Glide across the ice surrounded by the stunning backdrop of the illuminated Rathaus.
- **Concerts and Performances**: Vienna's musical heritage shines during the holiday season with numerous concerts and performances. The Vienna Boys' Choir and the Vienna Philharmonic Orchestra offer unforgettable experiences.

## 2. Lapland, Finland

For a truly magical Christmas experience, head to Lapland, the official home of Santa Claus. This winter wonderland offers a unique blend of natural beauty and festive cheer.

- **Santa Claus Village**: Located in Rovaniemi, the Santa Claus Village is a must-visit. Meet Santa himself, send postcards from the Arctic Circle, and explore the charming shops.
- **Northern Lights**: Witness the breathtaking Northern Lights dancing across the Arctic sky. Lapland's remote location makes it an ideal spot for viewing this natural phenomenon.
- **Reindeer and Husky Safaris**: Experience the thrill of a reindeer or husky safari through the snow-covered landscapes. These tours offer a glimpse into traditional Sami culture and the beauty of the Arctic wilderness.

## 3. New York City, USA

New York City is a vibrant metropolis that knows how to celebrate Christmas in style. From iconic landmarks to festive events, the Big Apple offers a holiday experience like no other.

- **Rockefeller Center**: The towering Christmas tree at Rockefeller Center is a symbol of the holiday season in New York. Don't miss the chance to ice skate beneath its sparkling lights.
- **Holiday Windows**: Stroll along Fifth Avenue to admire the elaborate holiday window displays at department stores like Macy's and Saks Fifth Avenue. Each window tells a unique story, captivating visitors of all ages.
- **Broadway Shows**: Catch a holiday-themed Broadway show, such as "The Nutcracker" or "A Christmas Carol," for a dose of festive entertainment.

## 4. Strasbourg, France

Known as the "Capital of Christmas," Strasbourg is home to one of the oldest and most beautiful Christmas markets in Europe. The city's charming half-timbered houses and cobblestone streets add to its festive allure.

- **Christkindelsmärik**: This historic market dates back to 1570 and features over 300 stalls offering crafts, decorations, and regional delicacies. The market's centerpiece is the towering Christmas tree in Place Kléber.
- **Gastronomy**: Indulge in Alsatian specialties like tarte flambée, bretzels, and mulled wine. Strasbourg's culinary scene is a delightful blend of French and German influences.
- **Illuminations**: The city is adorned with thousands of twinkling lights and decorations, creating a magical ambiance. Take a leisurely stroll through the streets to soak in the festive spirit.

## 5. Tokyo, Japan

Tokyo offers a unique take on Christmas, blending traditional Japanese culture with Western holiday traditions. The city's dazzling illuminations and festive events make it a captivating destination during the holiday season.

- **Illuminations**: Tokyo is famous for its stunning light displays. Areas like Shibuya, Shinjuku, and Roppongi Hills are transformed into luminous wonderlands with millions of LED lights.
- **Christmas Markets**: Experience a taste of Europe at Tokyo's Christmas markets. The Roppongi Hills Christmas Market and the Tokyo Christmas Market in Hibiya Park are popular spots to find festive treats and gifts.
- **Seasonal Cuisine**: Enjoy a Japanese twist on Christmas cuisine with dishes like KFC fried chicken and Christmas cake. These have become popular holiday traditions in Japan.

## 6. Quebec City, Canada

Quebec City is a charming destination that exudes old-world charm and festive spirit. Its cobblestone streets and historic architecture provide a picturesque backdrop for a Christmas getaway.

- **Old Quebec**: The historic district of Old Quebec is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a winter wonderland during the holiday season. Explore the quaint streets adorned with lights and decorations.
- **German Christmas Market**: Held in the heart of Old Quebec, this market offers a taste of European Christmas traditions with its wooden stalls, crafts, and delicious treats.
- **Winter Carnival**: Although it takes place after Christmas, the Quebec Winter Carnival is worth mentioning. This lively event features ice sculptures, parades, and outdoor activities.

## 7. Prague, Czech Republic

Prague is a city that seems straight out of a fairy tale, and its Christmas celebrations only enhance its enchanting atmosphere. The city's stunning architecture and festive markets make it a top destination for the holidays.

- **Old Town Square Market**: The Christmas market in Old Town Square is one of the most beautiful in Europe. The square is adorned with a massive Christmas tree and stalls offering traditional Czech crafts and foods.
- **Charles Bridge**: Take a stroll across the iconic Charles Bridge, which is beautifully illuminated during the holiday season. The views of Prague Castle and the Vltava River are breathtaking.
- **Midnight Mass**: Attend a midnight mass at St. Vitus Cathedral for a spiritual and cultural experience. The cathedral's Gothic architecture and choral music create a memorable atmosphere.

## 8. Reykjavik, Iceland

Iceland's capital, Reykjavik, offers a unique and cozy Christmas experience. With its stunning natural landscapes and vibrant cultural scene, it's an ideal destination for those seeking a winter adventure.

- **Yule Lads**: Discover the Icelandic tradition of the Yule Lads, mischievous characters who visit children during the 13 nights leading up to Christmas. Each lad has a unique personality and leaves small gifts or treats.
- **Geothermal Pools**: Relax in one of Reykjavik's geothermal pools, such as the Blue Lagoon or Laugardalslaug. The warm waters provide a soothing contrast to the chilly winter air.
- **Northern Lights Tours**: Embark on a tour to see the Northern Lights. Reykjavik's location makes it a prime spot for witnessing this natural spectacle.

## 9. Edinburgh, Scotland

Edinburgh is a city steeped in history and tradition, and its Christmas celebrations are no exception. From festive markets to Hogmanay, Scotland's New Year celebration, there's plenty to enjoy during the holiday season.

- **Edinburgh Christmas Market**: Located in East Princes Street Gardens, this market offers a variety of gifts, crafts, and delicious food. The nearby ice rink and Ferris wheel add to the festive fun.
- **Hogmanay**: Celebrate New Year's Eve in style with Edinburgh's Hogmanay festival. The event includes a torchlight procession, street parties, and a spectacular fireworks display over Edinburgh Castle.
- **Royal Mile**: Explore the historic Royal Mile, which is beautifully decorated for the holidays. The street is lined with shops, cafes, and attractions, making it a perfect place for a winter stroll.

## 10. Nuremberg, Germany

Nuremberg is famous for its Christkindlesmarkt, one of the oldest and most traditional Christmas markets in Germany. The city's medieval architecture and festive spirit make it a must-visit destination during the holiday season.

- **Christkindlesmarkt**: Held in the Hauptmarkt square, this market features over 180 stalls offering handmade gifts, ornaments, and delicious treats like gingerbread and bratwurst.
- **Nuremberg Castle**: Visit Nuremberg Castle for panoramic views of the city and its festive decorations. The castle's history and architecture add to the charm of the holiday season.
- **Children's Christmas Market**: Located on Hans-Sachs-Platz, this market is designed for children and features a carousel, a miniature Ferris wheel, and a variety of activities and treats.

## Conclusion

The holiday season is a time for joy, celebration, and exploration. Whether you're drawn to the traditional markets of Europe, the winter wonderlands of the Arctic, or the vibrant cities of North America and Asia, there's a Christmas destination to suit every traveler's taste. Embrace the festive spirit, create lasting memories, and experience the magic of Christmas in these enchanting places.
