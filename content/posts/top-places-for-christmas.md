# Top Places For Christmas

The holiday season is a magical time of year when cities and towns around the world transform into winter wonderlands. From twinkling lights to festive markets, each destination offers its own unique charm and traditions. Whether you're dreaming of a white Christmas or a tropical escape, here are some of the top places to celebrate Christmas.

## 1. New York City, USA

New York City is synonymous with Christmas cheer. The Big Apple comes alive with festive decorations, ice skating rinks, and holiday markets.

- **Rockefeller Center Christmas Tree**: The iconic tree lighting ceremony is a must-see. The massive tree, adorned with thousands of lights, is a symbol of the holiday season.
- **Ice Skating at Central Park**: Glide across the ice at the Wollman Rink, surrounded by the stunning skyline of Manhattan.
- **Holiday Window Displays**: Stroll down Fifth Avenue to admire the elaborate holiday window displays at stores like Macy's and Saks Fifth Avenue.
- **Radio City Christmas Spectacular**: Enjoy the world-famous Rockettes in a dazzling performance that has been a holiday tradition since 1933.

## 2. Lapland, Finland

For a truly magical Christmas experience, head to Lapland, the official home of Santa Claus. This winter wonderland offers a unique blend of natural beauty and festive activities.

- **Santa Claus Village**: Meet Santa himself and explore his village, complete with reindeer and elves.
- **Northern Lights**: Witness the breathtaking aurora borealis, a natural light display that illuminates the Arctic skies.
- **Husky Sledding**: Experience the thrill of a husky sled ride through the snowy landscapes.
- **Snowmobile Safaris**: Explore the pristine wilderness on a snowmobile, an exhilarating way to see the sights.

## 3. Vienna, Austria

Vienna is renowned for its elegant Christmas markets and classical music concerts. The city's historic architecture provides a stunning backdrop for holiday festivities.

- **Christkindlmarkt at Rathausplatz**: One of the most famous Christmas markets in Europe, offering handcrafted gifts, mulled wine, and traditional Austrian treats.
- **Vienna Boys' Choir**: Attend a concert by the world-famous choir, performing holiday classics in stunning venues.
- **Belvedere Palace**: Visit the Christmas Village at Belvedere Palace, where you can shop for unique gifts and enjoy delicious food.
- **Ice Dream**: Skate on the large ice rink in front of the City Hall, surrounded by festive lights and decorations.

## 4. Quebec City, Canada

Quebec City is a charming destination that feels like stepping into a European fairy tale. The city's cobblestone streets and historic buildings are beautifully decorated for the holidays.

- **Old Quebec**: Explore the historic district, where you'll find quaint shops, cozy cafes, and festive decorations.
- **German Christmas Market**: Discover unique gifts and enjoy traditional German food and drinks at this popular market.
- **Toboggan Slide**: Experience the thrill of the toboggan slide on Dufferin Terrace, offering stunning views of the city.
- **Carnaval de Québec**: Although it takes place after Christmas, this winter carnival is a festive celebration of Quebec's culture and traditions.

## 5. Tokyo, Japan

Tokyo offers a unique blend of traditional and modern Christmas celebrations. The city's vibrant lights and festive events make it a memorable destination for the holidays.

- **Illuminations**: Enjoy the spectacular light displays throughout the city, including the famous Tokyo Midtown and Shibuya Blue Cave.
- **Christmas Markets**: Visit the European-style Christmas markets, where you can find handmade crafts and delicious treats.
- **Tokyo Disneyland**: Experience the magic of Disney with special holiday parades and decorations.
- **Christmas Eve Dinner**: Indulge in a traditional Japanese Christmas meal, often featuring fried chicken and Christmas cake.

## 6. Prague, Czech Republic

Prague is a city that exudes old-world charm, especially during the holiday season. Its Gothic architecture and festive markets create a magical atmosphere.

- **Old Town Square Christmas Market**: Explore the market stalls offering handmade gifts, traditional Czech food, and mulled wine.
- **Charles Bridge**: Take a stroll across the iconic bridge, adorned with festive lights and offering stunning views of the city.
- **St. Vitus Cathedral**: Attend a Christmas concert in this magnificent cathedral, a masterpiece of Gothic architecture.
- **Petrin Hill**: Enjoy panoramic views of the city from the top of Petrin Hill, accessible by a funicular railway.

## 7. Sydney, Australia

For those seeking a warm-weather Christmas, Sydney offers a unique holiday experience with its sunny beaches and festive events.

- **Bondi Beach**: Celebrate Christmas with a beach barbecue and enjoy the sun and surf.
- **Carols in the Domain**: Join thousands of locals for a festive evening of carol singing in the heart of the city.
- **Sydney Harbour**: Take a cruise on Sydney Harbour to see the city skyline illuminated with holiday lights.
- **Christmas Markets**: Explore the various markets around the city, offering unique gifts and delicious food.

## 8. Strasbourg, France

Strasbourg is known as the "Capital of Christmas" and is home to one of the oldest and most famous Christmas markets in Europe.

- **Christkindelsmärik**: Wander through the market stalls offering handcrafted gifts, ornaments, and Alsatian delicacies.
- **Strasbourg Cathedral**: Admire the stunning Gothic architecture of the cathedral, beautifully illuminated for the holidays.
- **Petite France**: Explore the picturesque district of Petite France, with its charming half-timbered houses and festive decorations.
- **Christmas Concerts**: Attend a concert in one of the city's historic churches, featuring traditional holiday music.

## 9. Reykjavik, Iceland

Reykjavik offers a unique Christmas experience with its stunning natural landscapes and festive traditions.

- **Yule Lads**: Learn about Iceland's unique Christmas folklore, featuring 13 mischievous Yule Lads who visit children in the days leading up to Christmas.
- **Northern Lights**: Take a tour to see the mesmerizing aurora borealis, a highlight of any winter trip to Iceland.
- **Christmas Markets**: Explore the markets in downtown Reykjavik, offering local crafts and traditional Icelandic treats.
- **Geothermal Pools**: Relax in one of the city's geothermal pools, a perfect way to warm up during the chilly winter months.

## 10. Edinburgh, Scotland

Edinburgh is a city that knows how to celebrate Christmas, with its historic charm and festive events.

- **Edinburgh's Christmas**: Enjoy the city's winter festival, featuring a Christmas market, ice skating rink, and carnival rides.
- **Hogmanay**: Although it takes place after Christmas, Edinburgh's New Year celebration is one of the largest in the world, with fireworks and street parties.
- **Edinburgh Castle**: Visit the iconic castle, beautifully illuminated for the holidays, and enjoy panoramic views of the city.
- **Royal Mile**: Stroll along the historic Royal Mile, lined with shops, cafes, and festive decorations.

## Conclusion

Whether you prefer a snowy escape or a sunny retreat, these destinations offer something for everyone during the holiday season. From traditional markets to modern light displays, each city provides a unique and memorable Christmas experience. So pack your bags, embrace the festive spirit, and create unforgettable memories in one of these top places for Christmas.
