# Fun Places To Travel At Christmas

The holiday season is a magical time of year, and there's no better way to celebrate than by traveling to some of the most festive destinations around the world. Whether you're looking for a winter wonderland, a tropical escape, or a cultural experience, there are countless places that offer unique and memorable Christmas celebrations. In this article, we'll explore some of the best places to travel at Christmas, each offering its own special charm and holiday spirit.

## 1. New York City, USA

New York City is a classic Christmas destination, known for its dazzling lights, festive decorations, and iconic holiday events. Here are some must-see attractions:

- **Rockefeller Center Christmas Tree**: Witness the grandeur of the iconic Christmas tree, adorned with thousands of lights and a stunning Swarovski star.
- **Ice Skating at Rockefeller Center**: Glide across the ice at this world-famous rink, surrounded by the city's twinkling lights.
- **Radio City Christmas Spectacular**: Enjoy a performance by the Rockettes, a beloved holiday tradition featuring high-energy dance numbers and festive music.
- **Holiday Window Displays**: Stroll down Fifth Avenue to admire the elaborate and creative window displays at department stores like Macy's and Saks Fifth Avenue.

## 2. Lapland, Finland

For a truly magical Christmas experience, head to Lapland, Finland, the official home of Santa Claus. This winter wonderland offers:

- **Santa Claus Village**: Visit Santa's official residence, meet the man himself, and explore the charming village filled with holiday cheer.
- **Northern Lights**: Witness the breathtaking aurora borealis, a natural light display that illuminates the Arctic sky.
- **Reindeer Sleigh Rides**: Experience a traditional reindeer sleigh ride through the snowy forests, a quintessential Lapland adventure.
- **Snow Hotels**: Spend a night in a hotel made entirely of snow and ice, complete with cozy sleeping bags and warm drinks.

## 3. Vienna, Austria

Vienna is a city that truly embraces the Christmas spirit, with its elegant architecture and festive markets. Highlights include:

- **Viennese Christmas Markets**: Explore the city's numerous Christmas markets, offering handcrafted gifts, delicious treats, and warm mulled wine.
- **Belvedere Palace**: Visit this stunning baroque palace, which hosts a beautiful Christmas market in its gardens.
- **Vienna Boys' Choir**: Attend a concert by the world-renowned Vienna Boys' Choir, performing classic Christmas carols and hymns.
- **St. Stephen's Cathedral**: Marvel at the Gothic architecture of this iconic cathedral, beautifully decorated for the holiday season.

## 4. Tokyo, Japan

Tokyo offers a unique blend of traditional and modern Christmas celebrations, making it an exciting destination for the holidays. Key attractions include:

- **Illuminations**: Experience the city's spectacular light displays, with millions of LED lights adorning streets, parks, and buildings.
- **Tokyo Disneyland**: Enjoy a magical Christmas celebration at Tokyo Disneyland, featuring festive parades, decorations, and special holiday-themed attractions.
- **Christmas Markets**: Visit European-style Christmas markets throughout the city, offering seasonal treats and unique gifts.
- **Christmas Eve Dinner**: Indulge in a traditional Japanese Christmas Eve dinner, often featuring fried chicken and strawberry shortcake.

## 5. Quebec City, Canada

Quebec City offers a charming and picturesque setting for a traditional Christmas celebration. Highlights include:

- **Old Quebec**: Wander through the cobblestone streets of Old Quebec, beautifully decorated with lights and festive displays.
- **German Christmas Market**: Explore the European-inspired market, offering handmade crafts, delicious food, and warm drinks.
- **Tobogganing**: Experience the thrill of tobogganing down the famous Dufferin Terrace slide, a popular winter activity.
- **Ice Hotel**: Stay at the Hôtel de Glace, a stunning ice hotel featuring intricately carved ice sculptures and themed suites.

## 6. Sydney, Australia

For those seeking a warm-weather Christmas, Sydney offers a vibrant and sunny holiday experience. Key attractions include:

- **Bondi Beach Christmas**: Celebrate Christmas Day with a beach picnic or barbecue at the iconic Bondi Beach.
- **Carols in the Domain**: Attend Australia's largest free Christmas concert, featuring performances by popular artists and a festive fireworks display.
- **Sydney Harbour**: Enjoy a Christmas cruise on Sydney Harbour, taking in the stunning views of the Opera House and Harbour Bridge.
- **Christmas Lights Spectacular**: Visit the Hunter Valley Gardens for a dazzling display of over two million Christmas lights.

## 7. Prague, Czech Republic

Prague is a fairy-tale city that comes alive during the Christmas season, offering a magical atmosphere and rich traditions. Highlights include:

- **Old Town Square Christmas Market**: Explore one of Europe's most beautiful Christmas markets, set against the backdrop of the historic Astronomical Clock and Gothic churches.
- **Charles Bridge**: Stroll across the iconic Charles Bridge, adorned with festive lights and offering stunning views of the city.
- **Prague Castle**: Visit the majestic Prague Castle, beautifully illuminated and hosting its own Christmas market.
- **Traditional Czech Cuisine**: Indulge in hearty Czech dishes like roasted ham, trdelník (sweet pastry), and mulled wine.

## 8. Reykjavik, Iceland

Reykjavik offers a unique and enchanting Christmas experience, with its stunning natural landscapes and cozy holiday traditions. Key attractions include:

- **Yule Lads**: Learn about Iceland's unique Christmas folklore, featuring the mischievous Yule Lads who visit children in the days leading up to Christmas.
- **Northern Lights**: Take a tour to see the mesmerizing northern lights, a must-see natural phenomenon during the winter months.
- **Christmas Markets**: Explore Reykjavik's charming Christmas markets, offering handmade crafts, local delicacies, and festive entertainment.
- **Geothermal Pools**: Relax in one of Iceland's famous geothermal pools, such as the Blue Lagoon, surrounded by snowy landscapes.

## 9. Nuremberg, Germany

Nuremberg is home to one of the oldest and most famous Christmas markets in the world, making it a must-visit destination for holiday enthusiasts. Highlights include:

- **Christkindlesmarkt**: Explore the historic market, featuring over 180 stalls selling traditional crafts, toys, and delicious treats like gingerbread and bratwurst.
- **Nuremberg Castle**: Visit the medieval Nuremberg Castle, offering panoramic views of the city and hosting festive events during the holiday season.
- **Toy Museum**: Discover the history of toys at the Nuremberg Toy Museum, a fun and nostalgic experience for all ages.
- **St. Lorenz Church**: Attend a Christmas concert at this beautiful Gothic church, known for its impressive architecture and stunning stained glass windows.

## 10. Edinburgh, Scotland

Edinburgh offers a magical Christmas experience, with its historic charm and festive events. Key attractions include:

- **Edinburgh Christmas Markets**: Explore the city's bustling Christmas markets, offering unique gifts, delicious food, and festive entertainment.
- **Hogmanay**: Celebrate New Year's Eve with Edinburgh's famous Hogmanay festival, featuring street parties, concerts, and a spectacular fireworks display.
- **Edinburgh Castle**: Visit the iconic Edinburgh Castle, beautifully illuminated and offering stunning views of the city.
- **The Royal Mile**: Stroll down the historic Royal Mile, adorned with festive lights and decorations.

## Conclusion

Traveling during the Christmas season offers a unique opportunity to experience the holiday spirit in different cultures and settings. Whether you're drawn to the snowy landscapes of Lapland, the vibrant lights of Tokyo, or the historic charm of Prague, there's a destination for every type of traveler. Embrace the magic of the season and create unforgettable memories by exploring these fun places to travel at Christmas.
