Kos is an island that lies in the Aegean Sea, situated off the coast of Turkey. With its long stretches of exquisite beaches, charming bars, and restaurants that offer a mix of delicate and authentic Greek cuisine, it’s no wonder that Kos has become a popular destination for tourists from all over the world.

In this article, we’ll take a closer look at the top beaches, restaurants, and nightlife spots that you should visit on your next holiday to Kos. We’ll also provide answers to some of the most frequently asked questions about the island, along with the top tourist attractions that you can’t afford to miss.

Top Beaches in Kos
Kos is known for its magnificent beaches, many of which boast calm and crystal-clear turquoise waters, stunning cliffs, and idyllic natural surroundings.

1\. Tigaki Beach - If you’re looking for a sun-soaked day out, Tigaki Beach is a must-see. The long stretch of sand is lined with beach bars and restaurants, yet is secluded enough to feel peaceful and intimate.

2\. Kefalos Beach - If you’re looking for a more secluded beach experience, Kefalos Beach is perfect. The remote bay is also well-suited for snorkeling and features several taverns and cafes located along the shoreline that offer excellent seafood dishes.

3\. Paradise Beach - As its name suggests, Paradise Beach is a paradise for sunbathers and swimmers alike. White sands and clear blue waters beckon visitors here, along with a range of beach chairs, umbrellas and water sport facilities.

Top Restaurants in Kos
One of the best ways to experience Kos culture is through its food. Here are some of the top restaurants that offer a blend of traditional and modern Greek cuisine.

1\. Mylos Restaurant - Located on the outskirts of Kos Town and sitting atop a hill, Mylos Restaurant offers stunning views of the surroundings. The menu comprises fresh seafood, organic meats, and vegetables, mostly from the restaurant’s gardens.

2\. Agkyra Restaurant - Situated along the harbor in Kos Town, Agkyra Restaurant offers a selection of traditional Greek cuisine along with an extensive wine list. The rooftop terrace offers amazing views of the harbor at night.

3\. To Nisi - This open-air and idyllic restaurant is a short drive away from Kefalos Beach. The menu features traditional Greek dishes with innovative twists, and guests can dine while enjoying a spectacular view of the sea.

Top Nightlife Spots in Kos
After a day spent at the beach, there’s no better way to spend your evenings on Kos than enjoying the island’s vibrant nightlife.

1\. Bar Street - Located in the heart of Kos Town, Bar Street’s trendy bars and clubs offer drinks and music to get you dancing the night away. The street is pedestrian-only, which means a relaxed atmosphere without worrying about the traffic.

2\. Salt & Pepper - With a resident DJ spinning chart-topping tunes every night, Salt & Pepper in Kefalos is the perfect spot for those looking for a lively night out. The cocktails are top-notch, and people watching here is top class.

3\. Astra Night Club - This open-air club in the center of Kos Town is famous among locals for its high energy and lively atmosphere. The club’s unique layout allows guests to party in the open air courtyard, complete with a retractable roof for a perfect night's sky view.

FAQs about Kos
1\. What is Kos famous for?
Kos is famous for its sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, vibrant nightlife, and historical sites, including ancient ruins.

2\. What is the best time to visit Kos?
The best time to visit Kos is between April to November, when the weather is warm and there are fewer crowds.

3\. What are the transportation options on Kos?
Kos has public transportation, including buses and taxis. Visitors can also rent a car or a bike to explore the island at their own pace.

4\. Can I use euros in Kos?
Yes, the official currency of Kos is the euro.

5\. Do I need a visa to visit Kos?
Citizens of many countries, including the United States, Canada, and the United Kingdom do not require a visa for visits to Kos, unless they plan on staying for more than 90 days.

Top Tourist Attractions in Kos
1\. Ancient Agora - These well-preserved ruins in Kos Town are an impressive example of ancient Greek architecture and worth a visit for history buffs.

2\. Asklepion - The Asklepion sanctuary was an important wellness center in ancient Greece and is said to have been a destination of Hippocrates himself, considered the father of modern medicine.

3\. Casa Romana - This mansion in Kos Town is an excellent example of the Roman way of life, with vivid depictions of how the rich lived in ancient times.

4\. Neratzia Castle - Built in the 14th century, Neratzia castle was used to defend the town and its people from marauding pirates, and today one can explore the historic ramparts and enjoy breathtaking views from the top.

5\. Asclepieion Spa - Built over the Asklepion ruins, this modern spa is an ideal place to relax after a day spent exploring the island.

6\. Hippocrates Tree - Thought to be the “tree of Hippocrates,” this ancient tree in Kos Town is where the physician is said to have taught his pupils.

7\. Thermal Springs of Embros Therma- These thermal springs have been famous for their medicinal properties since ancient times, and today visitors can enjoy this natural hot springs pool.

8\. Plaka Forest - This idyllic forest in the center of the island is a popular spot for picnics and hiking.

9\. Kos Marina - Situated a few steps from the town center, Kos Marina is an ideal spot to enjoy the view, take some pictures and admire the flow of boats entering and leaving the harbor.

10\. Salt Lake - This shallow lake in Tigaki is home to a variety of migratory birds, making it a great spot of bird watching enthusiasts.

Conclusion
Kos is a gem of an island in the Aegean Sea that offers a wide range of activities and experiences to visitors. From gorgeous beaches to unforgettable food to vibrant nighttime entertainment, there is something for everyone on this island. So whether you’re looking for a romantic escape or an adventure-filled vacation, Kos has got you covered.
