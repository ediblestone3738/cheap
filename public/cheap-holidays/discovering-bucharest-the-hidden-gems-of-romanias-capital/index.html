Bucharest, the capital city of Romania, is a thriving city that boasts of a rich history, culture, architecture, and cuisine. However, the city remains underrated, and many tourists overlook it in favor of more popular destinations in Europe. But, the city is full of surprises and has a lot to offer visitors who take the time to explore it. This article takes an in-depth look at Discovering Bucharest: The Hidden Gems of Romania’s Capital.

Architecture

Bucharest’s architecture is a blend of styles ranging from Romanian to Byzantine to Art Nouveau. The most notable feature of the city’s architecture is the mixture of old and new buildings that coexist harmoniously.

The Village Museum

The Village Museum, located in the Herastrau Park, is one of the best representations of Romania’s rural architecture. The museum showcases traditional Romanian homes, churches, and other structures from various regions of the country. Visitors can walk around the village and learn about traditional Romanian culture, customs, and daily life.

The Palace of the Parliament

The Palace of the Parliament is one of the most imposing structures in Bucharest. It was built by the communist dictator Nicolae Ceaușescu and is the second-largest administrative building in the world. Visitors can take guided tours of the building, which includes the National Museum of Contemporary Art.

The Athenaeum

The Athenaeum, one of Bucharest’s iconic landmarks, is a concert hall that dates back to the early 1900s. The building is an example of Neo-classical architecture and is renowned for its impressive acoustics. Visitors can attend concerts or take guided tours of the building.

Parks and Gardens

Bucharest has several parks and gardens that provide a tranquil escape from the bustling city. The parks are ideal for picnics, jogging, cycling, and other recreational activities.

Herastrau Park

Herastrau Park is one of the largest green spaces in Bucharest, covering an area of about 110 hectares. The park has a lake, several restaurants, and numerous bike and walking trails. Visitors can also rent boats and paddle around the lake.

Carol Park

Carol Park is a public park located in the southern part of Bucharest. The park was established in the early 1900s and is famous for the Mausoleum of the Unknown Soldier, which commemorates the Romanian soldiers who died during World War I. Visitors can also see a statue of King Carol I, the founder of modern Romania.

Cuisine

Romanian cuisine is a blend of Balkan, Hungarian, and Turkish influences. The cuisine features hearty dishes made from meat, cheese, and vegetables.

Caru’ cu Bere

Caru’ cu Bere is a traditional Romanian restaurant that offers authentic Romanian dishes and drinks. The restaurant is housed in a historic building and has live music and dance performances in the evenings.

Hanul lui Manuc

Hanul lui Manuc is a 19th-century inn that has been restored and converted into a restaurant. The inn serves traditional Romanian dishes and offers a glimpse of the country’s history and architecture.

FAQs

What is the best time to visit Bucharest?

The best time to visit Bucharest is between May and September when the weather is warm and dry. However, the city can be crowded during this period, and prices may be higher.

What is the currency used in Bucharest?

The currency used in Bucharest is the Romanian leu.

Is English spoken in Bucharest?

English is widely spoken in Bucharest, especially in restaurants, hotels, and tourist attractions.

What are the transportation options in Bucharest?

Bucharest has an extensive public transportation system consisting of buses, trams, trolleybuses, and a metro system. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available.
