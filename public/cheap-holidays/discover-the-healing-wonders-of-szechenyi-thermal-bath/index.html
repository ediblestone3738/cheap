When visiting Budapest, nothing compares to taking a dip in the natural warm waters of the Széchenyi Thermal Bath. This bath covers a huge area of 18 acres and contains 15 indoor baths and three outdoor pools, and is one of the most popular spas in Budapest. This is the perfect spot to relax, unwind and have a unique bathing experience, especially if you’re in search of some therapeutic relief.

The Healing Properties of Széchenyi Thermal Bath

The thermal waters of the Széchenyi bath are from two different sources: the hot springs in the City Park and the springs from Margaret Island. These waters are naturally enriched with minerals such as calcium, sulphate, magnesium, sodium and fluoride, and are believed to have various healing properties. The minerals have numerous benefits to the body, such as relieving pain, increasing blood circulation, reducing stress levels, and revitalizing the skin.

In Hungary, the thermal baths have a long history of use, with medicinal springs thought to have healing properties since ancient times. That’s why Széchenyi Thermal Bath is a perfect destination for anyone who wants to experience the benefits of thermal water.

The History of Széchenyi Thermal Bath

Széchenyi Thermal bath was founded in 1913 and since then has welcomed millions of guests, making it one of the largest and most famous baths in Europe. The original building was designed in Art Nouveau architectural style and has been restored over the years to ensure it is still an impressive site to see. In 1999, the spa underwent major renovations, which lasted three years and cost 9.5 billion Hungarian forints (about 34 million USD). The aim was to become more commercial, improve the facilities, and revitalize the spa.

Széchenyi Thermal Bath is open all year round, so it’s a perfect place to visit even when the weather is mild. Here are some of the benefits and amenities you can expect while at Széchenyi Thermal Bath:

Indoor Pools: The Spa is flanked by 15 indoor pools with three different water temperatures. The pools are perfect for swimming or just relaxing in the water.

Outdoor Pools: The Spa has three outdoor pools, and they are the biggest in the city. The outdoor pools are perfect for swimming or relaxing in a unique setting.

Massage: In addition to various recreational activities, the spa offers several massage treatments to help you relax, recharge, and rejuvenate.

Steam Rooms: To take the relaxation to another level, the spa offers steam rooms, which can help to relieve stress, detoxify the body, and open air passages.

Sauna: The Spa has 11 different kinds of saunas, each with its unique benefits.

Herbal Baths: The spa offers several herbal baths filled with natural, therapeutic water to help soothe your mind and body.

Big and Small Artesian Pools: The two large arthritic pools are perfect for people suffering from joint pain or who want to increase their body’s flexibility.

Food and Beverage: Széchenyi Thermal Bath also offers a restaurant, which serves traditional Hungarian and international cuisine. The restaurant’s menu is a blend of classic and modern recipes.

Changing Rooms: Széchenyi Thermal Bath offers spacious, clean changing rooms, lockers, and rental towels to ensure guests have a comfortable and enjoyable experience.

FAQs about Széchenyi Thermal Bath

Q: How much does it cost to visit Széchenyi Thermal Bath?

A: The pricing depends on what package you choose. The cost ranges from 18.3 to 52 EUR.

Q: What is the opening hours?

A: The thermal bath is open every day from 9 A.M. - 10 P.M.

Q: What should I bring with me to the Széchenyi Bath?

A: You should bring your towel, swimsuit, and flip flops. However, if you forget any of the essentials, the facility rents towels and swimsuits.

Q: Can I take pictures in the thermal bath?

A: It is strictly prohibited to take pictures in the baths, but you can take pictures outside the pool areas.

Q: Are there any restrictions for pregnant women in the thermal bath?

A: Pregnant women are not allowed to use the hot pools.
