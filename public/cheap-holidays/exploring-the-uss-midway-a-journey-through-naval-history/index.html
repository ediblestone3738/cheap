The USS Midway, an aircraft carrier that served in the U.S. Navy from 1945 to 1992, is now a museum dedicated to educating visitors about naval history. The museum is located in downtown San Diego and attracts over a million visitors each year. A visit to the USS Midway is not just a stroll through a museum, but an immersive experience that takes visitors through the ship’s history and provides insight into the lives of the sailors who served on it.

History

The USS Midway was commissioned in 1945, just after World War II. It was the largest ship in the world at the time, with a length of 1,001 feet and a crew of over 4,000 sailors. It saw action during the Vietnam War and the Persian Gulf War and was decommissioned in 1992. After several years in storage, it was opened to the public as a museum in 2004.

Exhibits

The USS Midway Museum features over 60 exhibits that showcase the ship’s history and life onboard. Visitors can explore the ship’s bridge, engine room, and sleeping quarters, as well as the flight deck and hangar bay. The museum also has numerous displays and interactive exhibits that highlight the ship’s various missions and technologies used during its service in the Navy.

One of the most popular exhibits on the USS Midway is the Top Gun Experience. Visitors can simulate a flight in an F-14 Tomcat, the same plane used in the movie Top Gun. The experience includes a briefing in a replica of the Top Gun locker room and a simulated flight where visitors can take control of the plane.

Visitors can also tour the ship’s medical facilities and learn about the Navy’s medical procedures and practices. The museum features a recreated sickbay, which shows the ship’s medical facilities as they were during the Vietnam War.

Other exhibits on the USS Midway include the Engine Room, showcasing the ship’s massive power plant and the crew’s mechanical skills; the Brig, where visitors can see the cramped living quarters for those who misbehaved on board; and the Ready Room, where pilots would receive briefings before each mission.

Audio tours are available to all visitors, providing a detailed explanation of each exhibit and its significance.

FAQs

Here are some of the most frequently asked questions about visiting the USS Midway:

Q: Can I bring a backpack or bag on the ship?

A: Yes, but it will be searched at the entrance for security reasons.

Q: Are pets allowed onboard?

A: Only service animals are allowed.

Q: Is the museum wheelchair accessible?

A: Yes, the museum is fully accessible to wheelchairs and mobility scooters.

Q: Can I take photographs on the ship?

A: Yes, photography is allowed throughout the museum, except in certain areas where it is prohibited for security reasons.

Q: Is food available on the ship?

A: Yes, there are several places to eat on the ship, including a café and a snack bar.
