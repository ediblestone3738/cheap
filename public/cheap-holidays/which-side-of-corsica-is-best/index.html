Which side of Corsica is best?

Mar 20, 2023

[ad_1] Introduction

Corsica is a stunning French island that is located in the Mediterranean Sea. Known for its rugged coastline and jagged peaks, Corsica has become a popular destination for those who are looking for a unique and unforgettable travel experience. But with so much to see and explore on this beautiful island, it can be difficult to decide which side of Corsica is the best to visit. In this in-depth travel article, we will explore the different sides of Corsica and help you decide which one is best suited for your travel needs.

The West Coast of Corsica

The west coast of Corsica is known for its stunning coastline, turquoise waters, and quaint seaside towns. This side of the island is perfect for those who are looking for a relaxing beach vacation, as there are plenty of sandy beaches and crystal-clear waters to enjoy. The west coast is also home to some of Corsica’s most picturesque towns, including Ajaccio, Sagone, and Calvi.

Ajaccio

Ajaccio is the capital city of Corsica and is located on the west coast of the island. This charming town is known for its stunning architecture, including the Maison Bonaparte, which was the birthplace of Napoleon Bonaparte. Visitors can explore the narrow streets of the old town, relax on the sandy beach of Plage du Trottel, or stroll along the waterfront and take in the stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea.

Sagone

Sagone is a picturesque town located in the Gulf of Sagone on the west coast of Corsica. This charming town is known for its stunning coastline, which is home to some of the island’s most beautiful beaches. Visitors can enjoy water sports, such as kayaking and paddleboarding, or simply relax on the beach and soak up the sun. Sagone is also a great base for exploring the nearby mountains, which offer stunning views of the sea and coastline.

Calvi

Calvi is a beautiful town located on the northwest coast of Corsica. This historic town is known for its stunning citadel, which overlooks the bay and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can explore the narrow streets of the old town, visit the cathedral of Saint-Jean-Baptiste, or relax on the sandy beach of Plage de Calvi. Calvi is also a great base for exploring the nearby mountains, which offer some of the best hiking and trekking opportunities on the island.

The East Coast of Corsica

The east coast of Corsica is known for its rugged coastline, steep cliffs, and stunning mountain scenery. This side of the island is perfect for those who are looking for a more adventurous and active vacation, as there are plenty of opportunities for hiking, trekking, and exploring the wild and untamed landscape.

Porto-Vecchio

Porto-Vecchio is a beautiful town located on the east coast of Corsica. This charming town is known for its stunning coastline, which is home to some of the island’s most beautiful beaches, including Palombaggia and Santa Giulia. Visitors can explore the narrow streets of the old town, take a boat trip to the nearby islands, or hike through the nearby mountains to discover stunning views of the sea and coastline.

Bastia

Bastia is a historic town located on the northeast coast of Corsica. This charming town is known for its stunning architecture, including the citadel, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can explore the narrow streets of the old town, visit the cathedral of Sainte-Marie, or relax on the sandy beach of Arinella. Bastia is also a great base for exploring the nearby mountains, which offer some of the best hiking and trekking opportunities on the island.

FAQ

Q: When is the best time to visit Corsica?

A: The best time to visit Corsica is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, these months are also the busiest and most expensive, so it’s important to book accommodation and activities in advance. Spring and autumn are also good times to visit, as the weather is still pleasant and the crowds are smaller.

Q: What is the cuisine like in Corsica?

A: Corsican cuisine is a unique blend of French and Italian influences, and is known for its hearty and rustic dishes. Some of the most popular dishes include wild boar stew, veal with olives, and pumpkin soup. Local produce, such as chestnuts, figs, and cheeses, are also widely used in Corsican cuisine.

Q: What are some of the best hiking trails in Corsica?

A: Corsica is known for its stunning mountain scenery, and there are plenty of hiking trails to explore on the island. Some of the best trails include the GR20, which is a challenging trek through the rugged mountains of central Corsica, and the Mare e Monti, which offers stunning views of the sea and coastline.

Q: Is it easy to get around Corsica?

A: Getting around Corsica can be a bit challenging, as public transport is limited and the roads can be narrow and winding. The best way to explore the island is by car, as this gives you the freedom to explore at your own pace and visit the more remote areas of the island. Alternatively, there are also ferry services that connect the main towns and cities on the island.

Conclusion

Corsica is a beautiful and diverse island that offers something for everyone. Whether you are looking for a relaxing beach vacation or an adventurous trek through the mountains, Corsica has it all. We hope that this in-depth travel article has helped you decide which side of Corsica is the best to visit, and that you have a wonderful and unforgettable vacation on this stunning French island.
