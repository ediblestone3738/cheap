[ad_1] Discovering Belgrade: Hidden Gems of Serbia’s Capital

Belgrade is the capital of Serbia and it has a rich history and culture that is often overlooked by tourists. But the city is full of hidden gems that are waiting to be discovered. From traditional cuisine to ancient ruins, Belgrade has a lot to offer for adventurous travelers.

In this article, we will explore some of the hidden gems of Belgrade that you can add to your itinerary.

FAQs:

1. What is the best time to visit Belgrade?

The best time to visit Belgrade is from April to October when the weather is favorable for outdoor activities.

2. What is the currency in Belgrade?

The currency in Belgrade is Serbian dinar.

3. Do people speak English in Belgrade?

Yes, many people in Belgrade speak English.

4. Is Belgrade safe for tourists?

Belgrade is generally a safe city for tourists, but like any major city, it’s important to take safety precautions.

Food and Drink:

1. Skadarlija District

Skadarlija District is a bohemian area that was once the center of Belgrade’s art scene in the 19th and early 20th centuries. Today, Skadarlija is a tourist destination filled with traditional Serbian restaurants, where you can enjoy local specialties such as grilled meat, kabobs, and traditional Balkan cuisine. The district comes alive at night with live music and street performers.

2. Zlatiborac Smokehouse

Zlatiborac Smokehouse is famous for their smoked meats and sausages. You can visit their shop to buy sausage or ham and enjoy a taste of Serbia.

3. Rakia Bar

Belgrade is famous for its rakia, a fruit brandy that is popular in Serbia. The Rakia Bar is the perfect place to try various kinds of rakia, such as plum, apricot, and grape.

Historical Sites and Monuments:

1. Kalemegdan Fortress

Kalemegdan Fortress is a historic landmark that dates back to the Roman Empire. The fortress offers stunning views of the city and the Sava and Danube rivers. You can explore ancient ruins, watch the changing of the guard, visit the military museum, or enjoy a picnic in the park.

2. Nikola Tesla Museum

Nikola Tesla is one of the greatest inventors of all time and his museum is a tribute to his life and work. The museum features interactive exhibits and demonstrations of his inventions.

3. Belgrade Underground

Belgrade Underground is a network of tunnels, catacombs, and bunkers that were used throughout history as shelters and hideouts. You can take a tour of the underground tunnels to learn more about the city’s history.

Shopping:

1. Bajloni Market

Bajloni Market is a traditional farmer’s market that sells fresh fruits, vegetables, and handmade products. You can find artisanal cheeses, honey, and jams, as well as souvenirs and gifts.

2. Knez Mihailova Street

Knez Mihailova Street is a pedestrian shopping street in the heart of Belgrade. The street is lined with shops, cafes, and restaurants, and it’s the perfect place for a leisurely stroll.

Entertainment and Nightlife:

1. Ada Ciganlija

Ada Ciganlija is a peninsula on the Sava River that is known as Belgrade’s “Summer City”. The area features a lake with beaches, water sports, biking and walking paths, and restaurants and cafes.

2. Savamala District

Savamala District is an up-and-coming area that is home to galleries, cafes, bars, and nightclubs. The district is known for its street art and trendy atmosphere.

Nature and Parks:

1. Avala Mountain

Avala Mountain is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking or mountain biking. At the top of the mountain, you can visit the Avala Tower, which offers panoramic views of the city.

2. Topcider Park

Topcider Park is a serene oasis in the heart of the city. The park features walking paths, gardens, and a historic palace that was once the residence of Serbian royals.

Belgrade Safety Tips:

Belgrade is generally safe for tourists, but like any major city, it’s important to take safety precautions:

1. Avoid dark and isolated areas at night. 2. Be aware of your surroundings and keep your valuables secure. 3. Use licensed taxis and avoid unmarked cabs. 4. Avoid political rallies and demonstrations.

Belgrade Public Transportation:

Belgrade has a well-developed public transportation system that includes buses, trams, and trolleybuses. You can purchase tickets at kiosks or vending machines, and the same ticket can be used for all types of public transportation.

Belgrade Hidden Gems:

1. House of Flowers

The House of Flowers is the final resting place of Josip Broz Tito, a former Yugoslav leader. The museum features his personal belongings and exhibits about his life and work.

2. Zemun

Zemun is a quaint suburb of Belgrade that is located on the banks of the Danube River. The area features narrow streets, charming cafes, and a hilltop fortress that offers panoramic views of the city.

3. Zvezdara Forest

Zvezdara Forest is a nature reserve that is located in the heart of Belgrade. The forest features walking and biking paths, a lake, and a variety of plants and animals.

Conclusion:

Belgrade has a lot to offer for adventurous travelers who are looking for something beyond the typical tourist attractions. From traditional cuisine to ancient ruins, Belgrade has hidden gems that are waiting to be discovered. Make sure to add these hidden gems to your itinerary and experience the best of Serbia’s capital. [ad_2]
