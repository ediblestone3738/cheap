Top 10 Cheapest European Holiday Destinations

Dec 20, 2022

Looking for a European holiday on a budget? Look no further than this list of the top 10 cheapest European destinations. From Portugal to Bulgaria, there’s something for everyone – and all without breaking the bank. So what are you waiting for? Start planning your next adventure today!

Top 10 cheapest European holiday destinations

1. Bulgaria

A popular destination with both sun-seekers and snow bunnies, Bulgaria boasts a stunning coastline along the Black Sea, slap-bang in the middle of Eastern Europe. The country is incredibly cheap, with food and drink often coming in at less than €2.50 per head. Accommodation is also affordable, with many apartments and guesthouses available for rent at around €10 per person, per night.

2. Hungary

The capital city Budapest is one of the most beautiful – and affordable – cities in Europe. A meal for two with wine will cost you around €20, while a pint of beer will set you back just €1.50. Accommodation varies from cheap hostels to renovated apartments, many of which come complete with fantastic views of the cityscape.

3. Czech Republic

If you’re looking for an inexpensive city break this year, Prague should be high on your list of destinations. Although it’s become increasingly popular in recent years – meaning prices have gone up slightly – it’s still possible to find a meal for two including wine for under €30. Cheap accommodation is also plentiful, making Czech Republic an ideal choice for budget travelers.

4 Serbia

This underrated Balkan country has plenty to offer tourists, from its capital city Belgrade – where a three-course meal for two will cost you around €24 – to its stunning mountains and riverside towns. You can find cheap accommodation throughout Serbia, with many B&Bs and guesthouses offering rooms from as little as €8 per night.

5 Romania

Although it doesn’t have the same reputation as some of its European neighbors, Romania is well worth a visit if you’re on a tight budget this year. Transylvania is especially popular with tourists thanks to its association with Vlad the Impaler (aka Dracula), but the whole country has plenty to offer visitors on a shoestring budget, including cheap food and accommodation options.

How to save money on your European holiday

Europe is a hugely popular destination for tourists from all over the world, but it can also be one of the most expensive places to travel. If you’re looking for ways to save money on your European holiday, here are 10 top tips:

1. Travel in shoulder season – This is the time between the end of summer and the start of winter, when temperatures are cooler and there are fewer tourists. This can also be a great time to find cheaper accommodation and flight prices.

2. Fly into smaller airports – Major airports are usually located close to major cities, so they can be more expensive to fly into. If you’re flexible with your travel plans, consider flying into a smaller airport that’s located further away from your final destination. You may be able to find cheaper flights this way.

3. Stay in hostels or Airbnbs – Hostels are a great way to meet other travelers and save money on accommodation costs. Airbnbs can also be a cheaper alternative to hotels, especially if you’re traveling in a group or family.

4. Avoid tourist traps – Tourist traps are usually crowded places that charge higher prices for food, drinks, and souvenirs. If you want to save money, avoid these areas and look for hidden gems instead.

5. Eat like a local – Eating out in restaurants can be expensive, so try to eat like a local by buying food from supermarkets or markets. This will help you save money and also allow you to try some traditional dishes.

6 Take advantage of free activities – There are often plenty of free activities available in European cities, such as walking tours, museum visits, and concerts in parks or squares. Do some research before you leave so you can plan your itinerary around these free activities.

7 Use public transportation – Public transportation is usually cheaper than taking taxis or renting cars . The most budget-friendly European ski resorts

If you’re looking for a cheap European ski resort, Bulgaria is the place to go – Bansko, in particular. A six-day lift pass costs just £140, while a meal in a local restaurant will set you back an average of £15.

In second place is Slovenia’s Kranjska Gora, where a six-day lift pass is £154 and a meal in a local restaurant costs around £16. Another cheap option is Slovakia’s Jasna, where the six-day lift pass costs £168 and meals out will average at £16.

Romania’s Poiana Brasov comes in at fourth place – a six-day lift pass here will cost you £174 on average, and you can get a meal for under £17. Fifth place goes to Bulgaria’s Pamporovo, where the six-day lift pass costs £182 and meals out cost an average of £17.

If you’re looking for cheap accommodation as well as cheap ski lift passes and food, then Serbia’s Kopaonik is the place for you – here, you can find a bed in a hostel from just £4 per night.
