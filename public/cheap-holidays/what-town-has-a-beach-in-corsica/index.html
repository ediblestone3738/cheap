What town has a beach in Corsica?

Mar 20, 2023

[ad_1] Introduction

Corsica is an island in the Mediterranean Sea that offers visitors breathtaking beaches and picturesque towns. One such town is Calvi, situated in the northwest region of the island. It is an ideal destination for tourists looking for a peaceful and relaxing beach vacation.

In this travel article, I will highlight Calvi’s beach and what makes it a unique destination in Corsica. Additionally, I will provide a list of FAQs to address common queries about traveling to Calvi.

Calvi Beach

Calvi beach is a stunning crescent-shaped beach spanning over five kilometers with turquoise waters and fine sand. The beach is located in the heart of Calvi and is an ideal place to relax and sunbathe. The bay is sheltered from the wind and the waves, a perfect spot for swimming and water sports.

The beach is divided into three main sections: La Pinède, Colombaja, and Calvi-Marine. La Pinède is located in the heart of the town and is a popular spot for families with children. It provides a safe and calm environment due to the shallow water and is surrounded by trees, providing shade for visitors.

Colombaja is a quieter section of the beach, located at the foot of La Revellata. It provides a peaceful and romantic setting, ideal for couples looking for a relaxing getaway. The beach boasts stunning views of the surrounding cliffs, making it one of the most picturesque beaches in Corsica.

Calvi-Marine is the liveliest section of the beach, located next to the marina. It is one of the busiest areas, especially during the high season, with various activities such as water sports, boat excursions, and restaurants.

The beach is well-equipped with showers, toilets, and changing rooms. It is also accessible to people with disabilities, with ramps and walkways leading to the beach. Visitors can rent sunbeds and umbrellas or bring their equipment and enjoy the beach for free.

Calvi Town

Calvi is a charming town with a rich history and culture. The town is built around a citadel that overlooks the sea, providing stunning views of the bay. The citadel dates back to the 13th century and has been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The town has a vibrant atmosphere, especially during the summer months, with various events and festivities such as music festivals, markets, and exhibitions. Visitors can explore the narrow, cobbled streets lined with restaurants, cafes, and shops, offering local products and souvenirs.

Calvi also has a port, a marina, and a ferry terminal, making it an accessible destination for tourists arriving by sea. There are various boat excursions available, allowing visitors to explore the nearby coastline, including the famous Scandola Nature Reserve.

FAQs

1. How do I get to Calvi?

There are various ways to get to Calvi, including by plane, ferry, and car. The town has an airport, Calvi-Sainte-Catherine Airport, and ferry connections from mainland France and Italy. The town is also accessible by car, with a well-maintained road network connecting it to other parts of the island.

2. What is the best time to visit Calvi?

The best time to visit Calvi is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, ideal for beach activities. However, it is also the busiest time, so visitors should book their accommodation and activities in advance.

3. What are some other attractions near Calvi?

There are various attractions near Calvi, including the Scandola Nature Reserve, the Fangu River Gorges, and the Calanches de Piana. These natural wonders offer stunning landscapes, ideal for hiking and sightseeing.

4. Is Calvi family-friendly?

Yes, Calvi is family-friendly, with a safe and shallow beach, various activities for children, and accessible facilities. The town also has various parks and playgrounds, ideal for family outings.

Conclusion

Calvi is a unique destination in Corsica, offering visitors a beautiful beach, charming town, and natural wonders. The beach is a stunning crescent-shaped bay with clear waters and fine sand, divided into three sections, providing visitors with various options for beach activities. The town has a rich history and culture, with a citadel, narrow streets, and various events and festivals. Visitors can also explore nearby attractions, including the Scandola Nature Reserve and the Fangu River Gorges. Calvi is an ideal destination for a peaceful and relaxing beach vacation, suitable for families, couples, and solo travelers. [ad_2]
