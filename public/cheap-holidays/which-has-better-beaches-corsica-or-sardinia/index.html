Which has better beaches Corsica or Sardinia?

Mar 21, 2023

[ad_1] Corsica and Sardinia are two of the most popular holiday destinations in Europe, with miles of pristine coastline, crystal clear waters, and charming seaside towns. Both of them are located in the Mediterranean and are known for their natural beauty, history, and culture. However, when it comes to beaches, which one is better – Corsica or Sardinia? In this article, we will delve deep into the beaches of these two islands and try to answer this question.

Corsica – the Island of Beauty

Corsica is a French island in the Mediterranean, located southeast of France and west of Italy. It is known as the Island of Beauty, and rightly so, as it has some of the most stunning landscapes in Europe. Corsica’s coastline is over 1000 km long, and there are plenty of beaches to choose from. The beaches in Corsica are known for their pristine waters, white sand, and rugged rocky landscapes.

Best Beaches in Corsica

1. Santa Giulia Beach

Santa Giulia Beach is one of the most popular beaches in Corsica, located south of Porto Vecchio. What makes this beach stand out is its crystal clear turquoise waters that are ideal for swimming and snorkeling. The white sand and the rocky formations provide a picturesque landscape that is perfect for relaxation and sunbathing.

2. Palombaggia Beach

Palombaggia Beach is another popular beach in Corsica, located near Porto Vecchio. This beach is known for its long stretch of white sand and crystal clear waters that are perfect for swimming and sunbathing. There are plenty of water sports activities to do here, like jet-skiing, kayaking, and windsurfing. The beach is surrounded by lush greenery and has a serene atmosphere that is perfect for a relaxing holiday.

3. Saleccia Beach

Saleccia Beach is located in the north-west of Corsica, and it is one of the most secluded beaches on the island. It can only be accessed by boat, but the journey is worth it as the beach is stunning. The white sand and turquoise waters provide a tranquil setting that is perfect for a peaceful getaway. There are no facilities or restaurants here, so make sure to bring your own food and drinks.

Sardinia – the Emerald Island

Sardinia is an Italian island in the Mediterranean, located south-west of Corsica. It is known as the Emerald Island, and it is renowned for its stunning beaches, turquoise waters, and sandy coves. Sardinia’s coastline is over 2000 km long, and there are more than 400 beaches to choose from. The beaches in Sardinia are known for their crystal clear waters, white sand, and lush greenery.

Best Beaches in Sardinia

1. La Pelosa Beach

La Pelosa Beach is one of the most beautiful beaches in Sardinia, located in the north-west of the island. It is known for its turquoise waters, white sand, and stunning views of the nearby Asinara Island. The beach is popular among tourists, so it can get crowded during peak season. There are plenty of facilities and restaurants nearby, making it a convenient place to spend a day.

2. Cala Luna Beach

Cala Luna Beach is located in the east of Sardinia, near the town of Cala Gonone. It is known for its crystal clear waters and the stunning limestone cliffs that surround the beach. The beach can only be accessed by boat or by hiking, which makes it more secluded than other beaches on the island. The beach has a natural setting, and there are no facilities or restaurants nearby, so make sure to bring your own essentials.

3. Spiaggia del Principe

Spiaggia del Principe is located in the north-east of Sardinia, near the town of Porto Cervo. It is known for its crystal clear waters, white sand, and stunning rock formations. The beach is surrounded by lush greenery, and there are plenty of secluded coves to explore nearby. The beach is less crowded than other popular beaches on the island, and there are a few facilities and restaurants nearby.

Which has better Beaches – Corsica or Sardinia?

Both Corsica and Sardinia have stunning beaches that are worth visiting. However, choosing one over the other depends on your preferences and what you’re looking for in a beach holiday. Corsica is known for its rugged landscapes, crystal clear waters, and secluded beaches. The beaches in Corsica are more natural and have a laid-back atmosphere that is perfect for a relaxing holiday. On the other hand, Sardinia’s beaches are more luxurious and sophisticated, with plenty of facilities, restaurants, and water sports activities. The beaches in Sardinia are more accessible, making it a convenient place to stay.

FAQs

Q: When is the best time to visit Corsica and Sardinia?

A: The best time to visit Corsica and Sardinia is during the summer months, from June to September. The weather is warm and sunny, and the water is perfect for swimming and water sports activities.

Q: Can I rent a car in Corsica and Sardinia?

A: Yes, you can rent a car in Corsica and Sardinia. It is the most convenient way to explore the islands, as public transport is limited, especially in Corsica.

Q: Are there any nudist beaches in Corsica and Sardinia?

A: Yes, there are nudist beaches in Corsica and Sardinia, but they are not very common. Make sure to check the rules and regulations before visiting a nudist beach.

Q: Are there any pet-friendly beaches in Corsica and Sardinia?

A: Yes, there are some pet-friendly beaches in Corsica and Sardinia, but they are not very common. Make sure to check the rules and regulations before visiting a pet-friendly beach.

Q: Is it safe to swim in the sea in Corsica and Sardinia?

A: Yes, it is safe to swim in the sea in Corsica and Sardinia, but make sure to check the weather conditions and any warnings issued by the authorities. Some beaches have strong currents or waves that may make swimming dangerous.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Corsica and Sardinia are both stunning destinations with beautiful beaches that are worth visiting. Both islands have their unique charm and appeal, and choosing one over the other depends on your preferences and what you’re looking for in a beach holiday. Corsica’s beaches are more secluded and natural, while Sardinia’s beaches are more luxurious and convenient. Whatever your choice may be, you’re guaranteed to have an unforgettable holiday in either of these beautiful islands. [ad_2]
