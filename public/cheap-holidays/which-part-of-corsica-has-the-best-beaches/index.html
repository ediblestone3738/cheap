Which part of Corsica has the best beaches?

Mar 21, 2023

[ad_1] Introduction: Corsica, also known as the ‘Island of Beauty,’ is an extraordinary destination for beachgoers. The island is blessed with a diverse topography, from the picturesque coastline to the rugged mountain ranges, making it an excellent place for outdoor activities. Corsica is a perfect beach destination, and it is possible to spend weeks exploring the island’s coastline. With different types of beaches, including long stretches of white sand and hidden coves, choosing which part of Corsica has the best beaches can be challenging. In this article, we will explore the different beaches in Corsica and help you decide the best place to go for a beach vacation.

HTML Heading - The Best Beaches in the north of Corsica: The north of Corsica is known for its stunning coastal views, crystal-clear water, and rugged cliffs. The beaches in the north are ideal for snorkelling and swimming in shallow water.

1. Plage de Saleccia Plage de Saleccia is one of Corsica’s most stunning beaches, located northwest of the island. The beach stretches over three kilometres of amazing turquoise water and fine white sand. Access to the beach is only by boat, which adds to its secluded and peaceful nature. The scenery is breathtaking, and the crystal-clear water is excellent for swimming and snorkeling.

2. Ostriconi Beach Ostriconi Beach is also located in the northwest of the island and is one of Corsica’s hidden gems. The beach is secluded and has a natural beauty that is hard to describe. The beach is surrounded by hills that provide a sheltered cove, perfect for swimming and sunbathing. The water’s depths are shallow, making it an ideal place for children to learn to swim.

3. Calvi Beach Calvi Beach is a beautiful beach located in the northwest of the island. The beach is long and wide, with fine, warm sand and clear turquoise water. The beach is perfect for all kinds of activities such as swimming, windsurfing, and kayaking. The beachfront has a wide range of cafes and restaurants, so there is plenty to enjoy after a long day in the sun.

HTML Heading - The Best Beaches in the south of Corsica: The southern region of Corsica is blessed with numerous beautiful beaches, characterized by crystal-clear water, white sand, and a warm sea breeze. The coastal drive from Ajaccio to Bonifacio is spectacular, with stunning views of the coastline’s sandy beaches.

1. Palombaggia Beach Palombaggia Beach is a beautiful beach located in Porto-Vecchio, in the southeastern part of the island. The beach is known for its red cliffs, transparent turquoise water, and white sand. The beach is often crowded due to its popularity, but it still retains its beauty. Water activities such as snorkelling, jet skiing, and windsurfing are available on this beach.

2. Santa Giulia Beach Santa Giulia Beach is another fantastic beach located southeast of the island. The beach’s water is incredibly clear and warm, and the fine sand is perfect for sunbathing. The beach is also popular with water sports enthusiasts and offers activities such as snorkelling, kayaking, and jet skiing. There are several cafes and restaurants along the beachfront where you can enjoy a meal or a drink.

3. Rondinara Beach Rondinara Beach is situated in the southeast of the island and is a favourite destination for locals and visitors alike. The beach is surrounded by a natural amphitheatre of hills, making it an idyllic location. The water is crystal clear, and the sand is soft and white. There are several eating places near the beach, making it an excellent spot to spend a day with family and friends.

FAQs: Q. What is the best time to visit Corsica? A. The best time to visit Corsica is from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny. The water temperature is also the warmest at this time.

Q. Are the beaches in Corsica crowded? A. The beaches in Corsica can get crowded during the peak season, especially those located in the south of the island. However, there are several hidden beaches on the island that are less crowded.

Q. What is the best way to get around Corsica? A. The best way to get around Corsica is by car. Public transport is not very efficient, especially if you want to explore the island’s beaches.

Q. Can I swim in the sea in Corsica? A. Yes, sea swimming in Corsica is safe, and the water is clear and warm during the peak season. However, be aware of the undercurrent and follow the guidelines set by the local authorities.

Q. Are there beaches in Corsica suitable for families? A. Yes, Corsica has several family-friendly beaches, especially those located in the north of the island. The waters are shallow, making them ideal for children to paddle in, and there are several water activities available.

Conclusion: Corsica is a unique island with magnificent beaches that offer breathtaking scenery, crystal-clear waters, and peace and tranquillity. Whether you are looking for a secluded cove or a bustling beach, the island has something for everyone. The beaches in the north are more secluded and perfect for those who love privacy, while the beaches in the south are livelier, with more facilities and restaurants. We hope that this article has helped you decide which part of Corsica has the best beaches and that your next beach vacation will be to Corsica. [ad_2]
