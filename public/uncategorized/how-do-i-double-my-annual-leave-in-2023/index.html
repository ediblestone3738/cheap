The Basics of Annual Leave

Annual leave is the amount of paid time off that you are entitled to each year. The amount of annual leave you are entitled to depends on your job and the laws in your country. Generally, full-time employees are entitled to at least 20 days of paid leave each year.

For example, in the United States, there are 10 public holidays that fall on a weekday in 2023, including New Year’s Day (2 Jan 2023), Memorial Day (30 May 2023), Independence Day (4 July 2023), Labor Day (4 Sept 2023), and Thanksgiving (23 Nov 2023).

Once you’ve identified the public holidays that fall on a weekday in 2023, you can start planning your annual leave. The key is to take off the day before and the day after the public holiday. For example, if you take off the 31 Dec 2022 and the 3 Jan 2023, you’ll have effectively taken off four days of annual leave (two days before and two days after the public holiday).

Tips for Maximizing Your Annual Leave

If you want to maximize your annual leave in 2023, there are a few tips you can follow:

  • Plan ahead: Start planning your annual leave as early as possible so you can make sure you get the days off you want.
