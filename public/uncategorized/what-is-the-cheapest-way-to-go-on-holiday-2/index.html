What is the Cheapest Way to Go on Holiday?

Going on holiday can be expensive, but there are ways to save money and still have a great time. Here are 23 tips to help you cut the cost of your holiday and get the most out of your budget.

1. Buy Cheap Flights with Price Comparison Search Engines

The cost of flights can make up a large portion of your holiday budget, so it pays to shop around. Price comparison search engines like Skyscanner and Kayak can help you find the best deals on flights. You can also set up price alerts so you can be notified when the price of a flight you’re interested in drops.

2. Keep Within the Luggage Allowances

If you’re flying, make sure you’re aware of the airline’s luggage allowances. If you exceed the weight limit, you’ll be charged extra for your luggage. If you’re travelling light, you can often get away with taking just hand luggage, which can save you money.

3. Avoid Extra In-Flight Costs

In-flight meals and drinks can be expensive, so it’s worth bringing your own snacks and drinks with you. If you’re flying long-haul, you may be able to get a meal deal from the airport, which can save you money.

4. Travel by Coach or Train Instead of Flying

If you’re travelling within Europe, it can often be cheaper to travel by coach or train than to fly. Coach companies like Eurolines and Flixbus offer cheap fares, and you can often get discounted train tickets if you book in advance.

5. Get Affordable Travel Insurance

Travel insurance is essential if you’re travelling abroad, but it can be expensive. Shop around to find the best deal, and make sure you read the small print to make sure you’re covered for the activities you’ll be doing.

6. Find Deals on Private Travel Sales

Private travel sales sites like Secret Escapes and Wowcher offer discounted holidays and travel packages. You can often find great deals on flights, hotels and activities.

7. Book Low-Cost Types of Accommodation

Accommodation can be one of the biggest expenses when you’re travelling, so it pays to shop around. Airbnb and hostels are often cheaper than hotels, and you can often find great deals on last-minute bookings.

8. Stay in Hostels

Hostels are a great way to save money on accommodation. They’re usually much cheaper than hotels, and you can often find great deals on last-minute bookings. Hostels also offer a great way to meet other travellers and make new friends.

9. Use Discount Codes

Discount codes can be a great way to save money on flights, hotels and activities. Sign up to email newsletters and follow companies on social media to get the latest discounts and offers.

10. Use Public Transport

Public transport is often much cheaper than taxis, and it’s a great way to get around and explore a new city. Most cities have a good public transport system, so make sure you take advantage of it.

11. Use Cashback Sites

Cashback sites like Quidco and TopCashback offer cashback on flights, hotels and activities. Sign up for an account and you can get money back on your bookings.

12. Book Last-Minute Deals

If you’re flexible with your travel dates, you can often find great last-minute deals on flights and accommodation. Airlines and hotels often offer discounted rates for last-minute bookings, so it pays to keep an eye out for these deals.

13. Eat Out at Local Restaurants

Eating out can be expensive, but you can save money by eating at local restaurants. Local restaurants often offer cheaper prices than tourist hotspots, and you can get a better taste of the local cuisine.

14. Use Free Attractions

Many cities have free attractions, so make sure you take advantage of them. Museums, galleries and parks are often free to visit, and they’re a great way to explore a new city.

15. Take a Free Walking Tour

Free walking tours are a great way to explore a new city. Most cities have free walking tours, and they’re a great way to get an introduction to the city and learn about its history.

16. Take Advantage of Free Wi-Fi

Most cities have free Wi-Fi hotspots, so make sure you take advantage of them. This can save you money on data roaming charges, and it’s a great way to stay connected while you’re travelling.

17. Book Group Tours

Group tours can be a great way to save money on activities. You can often get discounted rates if you book a group tour, and it’s a great way to meet other travellers and make new friends.

18. Look for Local Deals

Local newspapers and websites often have deals and discounts on attractions and activities. Keep an eye out for these deals and you can save money on your holiday.

19. Use Coupon Sites

Coupon sites like Groupon and LivingSocial offer discounts on attractions and activities. Sign up for an account and you can get money off your bookings.

20. Take a Staycation

If you’re looking for a cheap holiday, why not take a staycation? You can explore your own city and save money on accommodation and travel costs.

21. Book Early

If you’re planning a holiday, it pays to book early. Airlines and hotels often offer discounted rates for early bookings, so make sure you book as early as possible.

22. Travel During the Off-Season

If you’re flexible with your travel dates, it pays to travel during the off-season. Prices are often lower during this time, and you can often find great deals on flights and accommodation.

23. Use Reward Points

If you have reward points from a credit card or loyalty program, you can use them to book flights, hotels and activities. This can save you money on your holiday.

Conclusion

Going on holiday doesn’t have to be expensive. By following these tips, you can save money and still have a great time. So what are you waiting for? Start planning your next holiday today!
