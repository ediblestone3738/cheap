Where is the Cheapest Place to Fly to in Europe This Summer?

Are you looking for the best place to fly to in Europe this summer? If so, you’re in luck. The answer is Reykjavik, Iceland. This is one of the most affordable destinations in Europe, and it’s also a great place to explore.

Why Reykjavik, Iceland?

Reykjavik, Iceland is the cheapest place to fly to in Europe this summer. Depending on the time of year and where you’re flying from, you can expect to pay anywhere between $260-$550 roundtrip on Icelandair.

Reykjavik is the capital of Iceland and is known for its stunning natural beauty. The city is surrounded by mountains, glaciers, and volcanoes, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. It’s also home to some of the best museums, galleries, and restaurants in the country.

What to Do in Reykjavik

Reykjavik is a great place to explore. There are plenty of things to do in the city, from visiting the National Museum of Iceland to exploring the vibrant street art scene. You can also take a dip in the Blue Lagoon, a geothermal spa located just outside of the city.

If you’re looking for some adventure, you can take a day trip to the Golden Circle, a popular route that takes you to some of Iceland’s most stunning natural attractions. You can also explore the nearby lava fields and take a boat tour to the nearby islands.

When to Visit Reykjavik

The best time to visit Reykjavik is during the summer months, from June to August. This is when the weather is warmest and the days are longest. The city is also a great place to visit during the winter months, when you can experience the Northern Lights.

How to Get to Reykjavik

The best way to get to Reykjavik is by flying. The city is served by Keflavik International Airport, which is located just outside of the city. You can find flights from most major US cities, including New York, Los Angeles, and Chicago.

Where to Stay in Reykjavik

Reykjavik has a wide range of accommodation options, from budget hostels to luxury hotels. You can also find plenty of Airbnb rentals in the city.

Conclusion

Reykjavik, Iceland is the cheapest place to fly to in Europe this summer. Depending on the time of year and where you’re flying from, you can expect to pay anywhere between $260-$550 roundtrip on Icelandair. The city is surrounded by stunning natural beauty and is home to some of the best museums, galleries, and restaurants in the country. If you’re looking for a cheap and exciting destination this summer, Reykjavik is the perfect place to visit.
