FAQs

Q: What is the best time to visit Kefalonia?

A: The best time to visit Kefalonia is from May to October. The weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is perfect for swimming and water activities. However, July and August can be crowded, so if you want to avoid the crowds, it’s better to visit in May, June or September.

Q: How do I get to Kefalonia?

A: Kefalonia has an international airport that serves flights from Athens, Thessaloniki, and other European cities. You can also reach Kefalonia by ferry from the mainland or other islands.

Q: What are the best beaches in Kefalonia?

A: Kefalonia has many beautiful beaches, but some of the most popular are Myrtos, Antisamos, Lourdas, and Skala. Each beach offers something different, from crystal-clear waters and white sand to pebbled shores and rugged cliffs.

Q: What are the top activities to do in Kefalonia?

A: Kefalonia offers a variety of activities for all ages and interests. Some of the top activities include swimming, snorkeling, hiking, cycling, boat tours, and visiting historical and cultural sites.

Q: Is Kefalonia family-friendly?

A: Yes, Kefalonia is a family-friendly destination that offers activities, accommodation, and amenities for families and children. The island has many beaches with shallow waters, playgrounds, and family-friendly restaurants.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Kefalonia

1. Myrtos Beach: Considered one of the most beautiful beaches in Greece, Myrtos boasts turquoise waters and white pebbled shores that offer a stunning contrast to the surrounding cliffs and greenery.

2. Melissani Cave: A unique geological formation that houses a subterranean lake with crystal-clear waters and natural light that creates a mesmerizing blue-green effect.

3. Assos Village: A picturesque fishing village built around a beautiful bay with colorful houses, a Venetian castle, and breathtaking views of the sea and the forested hills.

4. Fiskardo Village: A charming harbor town that is famous for its colorful traditional houses, cosmopolitan atmosphere, and luxury yachts.

5. Mount Ainos National Park: The highest mountain in the Ionian islands that offers adventurous hiking trails, beautiful nature and panoramic views of the island.

6. Lixouri Town: A lively town that is known for its lively market, local cuisine, historical sites and the nearby Petani Beach.

7. Argostoli Town: The capital town that boasts a harbor, a pedestrian shopping street, historical monuments, and a buzzing nightlife.

8. Skala Beach: A long sandy beach that is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, water sports, and family activities.

9. St. George Castle: Located in Peratata village, the castle was built by the Venetians in the 16th century and offers panoramic views of the island and the sea.

10. Robola Winery: An award-winning winery that produces the famous Robola wine, a dry white wine that is unique to Kefalonia and has a distinct flavor and aroma.
