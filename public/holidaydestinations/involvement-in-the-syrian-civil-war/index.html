Background of the Syrian Civil War

The Syrian Civil War began in 2011, when protests against the government of President Bashar al-Assad erupted in several cities. The protests were met with a violent response from the government, which led to an armed insurgency against the government. This eventually led to a full-scale civil war, with multiple factions fighting against each other. The conflict has been further complicated by the involvement of regional and international powers, including Russia, Iran, Turkey, and the United States.

Syria’s Role in the War
