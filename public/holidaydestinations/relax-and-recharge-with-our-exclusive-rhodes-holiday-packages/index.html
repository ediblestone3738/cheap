Why travel to Rhodes Island? Rhodes Island is a popular destination for travelers from Europe and the UK because it offers an ideal combination of activities, relaxation and a rich history & culture. From pristine beaches to ancient ruins like the medieval palace of the Grand Masters or the ruined temple of Apollo at Lindos, the island offers a chance to explore Greece’s ancient past. The Old Town of Rhodes City is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a must-visit spot for those interested in historical architecture, winding streets, and picturesque buildings.

Most Asked Questions: Q: What is the best time of year to travel to Rhodes Island? A: The best time to visit is between May and October when the weather is warm and sunny, and the chance of rain is low.

Q: What type of accommodation is available for travelers? A: There is a wide range of accommodation options available, from budget-friendly hotels and guesthouses to luxurious resorts.

Q: Do I need a visa to visit Rhodes Island? A: If you are a citizen of the European Union, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, or Liechtenstein, you do not need a visa to visit Rhodes Island. Citizens of other countries must check the visa requirements and apply accordingly.

Q: What type of cuisine should I expect in Rhodes Island? A: Greek cuisine is diverse and delicious. Expect to indulge in dishes like moussaka, souvlaki, tzatziki, and spanakopita. Fresh seafood is also a staple and is a must-try while visiting the island.
