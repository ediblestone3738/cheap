[ad_1] Located in the heart of the Ionian Sea, Kefalonia could only be described in one word - mesmerizing. With its rugged cliffs, lush countryside, and turquoise waters, Kefalonia offers a picture-perfect landscape filled with hidden gems to explore. And what better way to do that than taking a scenic drive along the island’s coastal villages, past stunning beaches, and through picturesque towns, all while enjoying the lush Mediterranean greenery?

Kefalonia’s scenic drives, aside from offering some of the most breathtaking views, provide an opportunity to discover the charming treasures tucked away in the island’s unspoiled landscapes. This island boasts of hidden gems that make it a dream destination for anyone looking to go off the beaten path.

Whether you’re a first-time visitor or an experienced traveler, here’s a detailed guide to Kefalonia’s scenic drives - exploring the island’s hidden gems.

Getting There

Kefalonia is accessible via air, sea, and land. Flights depart from various international airports, and the island has two main harbors in Sami and Poros. Ferries and speedboats also offer daily services to the island from various ports in Greece.

Getting around the island is easy, with an extensive road network. To make the most of your visit to the island, renting a car is highly recommended. Most car rental companies are available in the main towns in Kefalonia, including the airport and harbors.

Best Time to Visit

Kefalonia has a Mediterranean climate, making it an ideal destination all year-round, but the peak tourist season falls between June - August. For those who prefer to enjoy the island’s scenic drives without the tourists, the best time to visit Kefalonia is in the months between April - June and September - October.

Kefalonia’s Scenic Drives

1. The Lighthouse route

The Lighthouse route is one of the most popular scenic drives in Kefalonia. This drive takes you on a journey through stunning countryside, picturesque villages, and long winding roads. The route starts from the main town of Argostoli and leads to the Lighthouse of Saint Theodoroi, located at the end of the peninsula. Along the route, you can stop at various beaches, including the stunning Makris Gialos and Platis Gialos beaches.

2. The Mount Aenos Route

The Mount Aenos route takes you on a winding journey to the highest peak in Kefalonia, Mount Aenos. The drive offers panoramic views of the Ionian Sea and the rolling hills of Kefalonia. The route passes through picturesque villages, including the charming town of Fiskardo. Along the way, you can stop at various viewpoints, including the Byron’s Rock viewpoint and the Diana’s viewpoint.

3. The Cave Route

The Cave route takes you on a journey to Melissani Lake Cave, one of the most popular tourist attractions in Kefalonia. The drive takes you to the stunning ancient lake hidden beneath the earth. Along the way, you can visit the Drogorati cave and local villages, including the charming town of Sami.

4. The Coastal Route

The Coastal route takes you on a journey alongside the stunning coastline of Kefalonia. The route starts from the town of Lourdas and travels past the charming town of Skala, leading down to the enchanting village of Poros. Along the way, you can stop at various stunning beaches, including Skala Beach and Poros Beach.

5. The Wine Route

The wine route takes you through Kefalonia’s vineyards, leading you to some of the island’s best wineries. The route passes through villages, including Minies, where you can indulge in wine tasting and other local delicacies. The route leads to the village of Karavados, where you can visit the Robola wine cooperative and learn more about the island’s wine culture.

FAQs

What is the currency used in Kefalonia?

The currency used in Kefalonia is the Euro.

What language do they speak in Kefalonia?

The official language spoken in Kefalonia is Greek, but most locals speak English.

What time zone is Kefalonia in?

Kefalonia is in the Eastern European Time zone.
