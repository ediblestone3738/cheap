Keukenhof Gardens Located in Lisse, Keukenhof Gardens is one of the most popular tourist attractions in the country. The park is famous for its wide collection of tulips, hyacinths, and other flowers. The park covers 32 hectares, and more than 7 million bulbs are planted every year. The gardens are open from mid-March to mid-May, and it’s recommended to visit during mid-April to see the tulips in full bloom.

Hoge Veluwe National Park The Hoge Veluwe National Park is a unique destination for nature lovers. The park is situated in the Gelderland province, and it covers an area of 55 square kilometers. It’s the largest national park in the Netherlands and features a diverse range of landscapes, including forests, heathlands, and sand dunes. The park also has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails, which makes it a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Wadden Sea National Park The Wadden Sea National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is the largest tidal wetlands system in the world. The park is home to a vast number of birds and seals. It covers an area of 10,000 square kilometers and stretches from the Netherlands to Germany and Denmark. The park also offers guided tours, which provide insights into the ecosystem and the wildlife that lives there.

Delta Works The Delta Works is a network of dams, dikes, and storm surge barriers that protect the Netherlands from flooding. The system was built after the floods of 1953, which claimed the lives of more than 1,800 people. The Delta Works is one of the largest engineering projects in the world and is a testament to the Dutch’s dedication to water management.

Kinderdijk Windmills The Kinderdijk Windmills are a group of 19 windmills located in the province of South Holland. The windmills were built in the 18th century to pump water out of the low-lying polder, which is below sea level. The windmills are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and are an iconic symbol of the Dutch countryside.

De Biesbosch Nature Reserve De Biesbosch is a wetland nature reserve located in the province of North Brabant. It covers an area of 90 square kilometers and is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna. The reserve is a popular destination for birdwatchers, who can spot some rare species here. The reserve also offers boat tours, which are a great way to explore the area.

Zuid-Kennemerland National Park Zuid-Kennemerland National Park is located in the province of North Holland and is known for its sand dunes, forests, and coastline. The park covers an area of 38 square kilometers and is home to some rare species of flora and fauna. The park also has a network of hiking trails that provides stunning views of the sea and the surrounding countryside.

Oostvaardersplassen Nature Reserve The Oostvaardersplassen Nature Reserve is located in the province of Flevoland and is a unique destination for birdwatchers. The reserve covers an area of 56 square kilometers and is home to more than 300 bird species. The reserve also has a population of free-roaming wild horses, deer, and cattle, which add to the overall experience of visiting the reserve.

Dunes of Texel National Park The Dunes of Texel National Park is located on the Texel island and is a unique destination for beach lovers. The park covers an area of 43 square kilometers and has a vast network of cycling and walking trails. The park also has several beaches, which are perfect for swimming and sunbathing.

Amsterdam Waterland Amsterdam Waterland is located just outside the city of Amsterdam and is a unique destination for nature lovers. The area is known for its wide network of canals, which are home to a variety of bird species. Visitors can explore the area on foot, by bike, or by boat.
