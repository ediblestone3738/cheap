Getting There

Rome and Pompeii are two separate destinations that are not too far away from each other. The best way to get to both destinations is by flying into Rome Fiumicino airport, which is the main airport serving Rome. From here, you can take a train to Naples and then transfer to Pompeii.

Alternatively, you can take a direct train from Rome to Pompeii which takes around two hours. If you prefer to drive, it will take around three hours to drive from Rome to Pompeii.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions

1. Colosseum, Rome

The Colosseum is the largest amphitheater ever built and is one of the most iconic landmarks of Rome. It was built in AD 70-80 and was used for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles.

2. Vatican City, Rome

Vatican City is home to some of the world’s most famous landmarks, including St. Peter’s Basilica and the Sistine Chapel. It is also the smallest country in the world and is located within the city of Rome.

3. The Pantheon, Rome

The Pantheon is a stunning temple that was built in AD 125 by Emperor Hadrian. It is one of the best-preserved ancient Roman buildings and is still in use today.

4. Pompeii Archaeological Site

A visit to Pompeii offers a glimpse into the ancient Roman civilization. It is a well-preserved ancient city that was destroyed by the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in AD 79.

5. Mount Vesuvius, Naples

Mount Vesuvius is an active volcano that is located near Naples. It is one of the most famous and dangerous volcanoes in the world and last erupted in 1944.

6. Herculaneum Archaeological Site

Herculaneum is another ancient Roman city that was destroyed by the eruption of Mount Vesuvius. It is smaller than Pompeii but better-preserved with some of the buildings still intact.

7. Trevi Fountain, Rome

The Trevi Fountain is one of the most famous fountains in the world and is located in the heart of Rome. The fountain was completed in 1762 and is known for its stunning Baroque architecture.

8. Spanish Steps, Rome

The Spanish Steps are located in the Piazza di Spagna in Rome and are a popular meeting place for visitors. The steps were built in the 18th century and are known for their stunning architecture.

9. Pompeii Forum

The Pompeii Forum is located in the heart of the city and was the center of all public life in Pompeii. It is a well-preserved site that offers an insight into the ancient Roman civilization.

10. Piazza del Popolo, Rome

Piazza del Popolo is one of the most iconic squares in Rome and is located at the base of the Pincio hill. It is known for its stunning Baroque architecture and is a popular spot for tourists.

FAQs

Q: What is the best time to visit Rome and Pompeii?

A: The best time to visit Rome and Pompeii is during the shoulder season, which is from March to May and September to November. During these months, the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller than during the peak summer months.

Q: What is the best way to get around Rome and Pompeii?

A: The best way to get around Rome is by foot, as there are many iconic landmarks located close to each other. In Pompeii, it is best to take a guided tour to get the most out of your visit.

Q: How long should I spend in Rome and Pompeii?

A: Ideally, it is best to spend at least four days in Rome and two days in Pompeii. This will give you enough time to explore the key attractions and also experience the local culture.

Q: What should I pack for a holiday to Rome and Pompeii?

A: It is best to pack comfortable walking shoes, sunscreen, a hat, and a reusable water bottle. Also, remember to dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious landmarks.

Q: What is the currency used in Rome and Pompeii?

A: The currency used in Italy is the Euro.
