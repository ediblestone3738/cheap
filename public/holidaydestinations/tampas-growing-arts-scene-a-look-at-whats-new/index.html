Tampa may be known as the cigar city, but it has also been building a reputation as a center of arts and culture. The city’s arts scene has been expanding in recent years with new museums, galleries, and events being added regularly. From the Tampa Museum of Art to the public art installations, there’s plenty to see and experience in the city’s art community. In this article, we’ll dive into what’s new in Tampa’s growing arts scene, along with the top ten tourist attractions in the city.

What’s New in Tampa’s Growing Arts Scene?

Tampa’s arts scene is dynamic and constantly evolving. Here are some of the latest additions and exhibitions to the city’s arts and cultural landscape.

1. Contemporary Art Museum (CAM)- CAM is a new museum dedicated entirely to contemporary art. The museum has been designed by renowned architect Stanley Saitowitz and features a blend of interior and exterior spaces that create a unique and immersive experience for visitors.

2. Florida Museum of Photographic Arts - The Florida Museum of Photographic Arts is another new museum that focuses on photography from around the world. The museum showcases a diverse range of photography styles and formats, including modern and experimental work.

3. The Rialto Theatre- The Rialto Theatre has been a Tampa landmark for over a century. The theatre has recently undergone a renovation, bringing it back to its original glory. The Rialto is now a multi-use event space, hosting a range of concerts, plays, and performances.

4. Tampa’s Downtown Murals- Public art is one of the most vibrant aspects of Tampa’s arts scene. Recently, a new series of murals have been painted downtown, featuring work from local artists, as well as national and international artists.

5. Tampa Bay History Center- The Tampa Bay History Center is a must-visit destination for history buffs. The museum features interactive exhibits on Tampa’s rich history, from the Native Americans who lived in the area to modern-day Tampa.

FAQs

Q: When is the best time to visit Tampa’s arts scene? A: Tampa’s arts scene is vibrant year-round, but the best time to visit depends on what you’re interested in. The summer months are packed with outdoor events, while the winter months are ideal for indoor exhibitions and performances.

Q: Are there any free events or exhibitions in Tampa’s arts scene? A: Yes, many of Tampa’s cultural institutions offer free admission on certain days or evenings. Additionally, there are several outdoor events, such as weekly farmers markets, that are free to attend.

Q: What types of art can I expect to see in Tampa? A: Tampa’s arts scene is diverse and eclectic, featuring a wide range of art forms, including traditional paintings and sculptures, contemporary installations, photography, murals, and more.

Q: Where can I find information on current exhibitions and events in Tampa’s arts scene? A: The best place to find up-to-date information on exhibitions and events in Tampa’s arts scene is the Visit Tampa Bay website. There are also several local arts organizations, such as the Tampa Museum of Art and the Tampa Bay Cultural Alliance, that offer event calendars and exhibition listings.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions Located in Tampa’s Growing Arts Scene:

1. Tampa Museum of Art- One of the most renowned museums in Tampa, the Tampa Museum of Art is home to a collection of modern and contemporary art. The museum has an extensive collection of glass art and also displays a range of rotating exhibitions.

2. The Ringling- The Ringling is a must-visit destination for art and history lovers. The museum features an incredible collection of art from around the world, as well as the historic Ca’ d’Zan mansion and beautiful gardens.

3. Ruth Eckerd Hall- A premier performing arts venue, Ruth Eckerd Hall hosts a range of concerts, theater shows, and dance performances throughout the year.

4. Gasparilla Music Festival- This annual music festival takes place in downtown Tampa and features a diverse lineup of national and local musicians.

5. Tampa Theatre- The Tampa Theatre is a historic movie palace that has been restored to its original 1920s grandeur. The theatre hosts a range of film screenings, concerts, and other events throughout the year.

6. Henry B. Plant Museum- Located on the campus of the University of Tampa, the Henry B. Plant Museum is housed in the historic Tampa Bay Hotel. The museum features exhibits on the hotel’s history, as well as a collection of artifacts from the Gilded Age.

7. Tampa Bay Symphony- The Tampa Bay Symphony is a professional orchestra that performs throughout the year at various venues in Tampa and the surrounding area.

8. Straz Center for the Performing Arts- The Straz Center is a premier performing arts venue that hosts Broadway shows, musicals, operas, and other performances throughout the year.

9. The Tampa Mural Tour- The Tampa Mural Tour is a walking tour of some of the city’s most vibrant and interesting murals. The tour takes visitors through different neighborhoods of the city, highlighting the unique character of each area.

10. Frank Lloyd Wright’s Spring House- Frank Lloyd Wright’s Spring House is a historic home located in nearby Tallahassee. The house is one of only a few Frank Lloyd Wright-designed buildings in Florida and is open for tours throughout the year. [ad_2]
