Hidden Gems

1. Monolithos Castle

The Monolithos Castle is one of Rhodes’ most underrated treasures. Located on the west coast of the island, this castle offers panoramic views of the Aegean Sea. The castle was built in the 15th century by the Knights of Saint John and was used to protect the island from invaders. Today, the castle is in ruins, but its impressive walls and towers are still worth a visit.

2. Profitis Ilias Mountain

Profitis Ilias is the highest mountain on Rhodes and offers unparalleled views of the island. You can take a scenic drive up to the top or hike up the mountain for a more challenging adventure. At the top, you can visit a small chapel dedicated to the prophet Elijah.
