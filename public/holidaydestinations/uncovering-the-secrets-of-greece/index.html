Greece is a country that is full of history and culture. It is a country that is always ready to welcome travelers with open arms. For years, Greece has been one of the most popular tourist destinations in the world, and for a good reason. It is a country that has something for everyone, from sightseeing to eating delicious food. In this travel article, we will uncover the secrets of Greece, including the must-visit tourist attractions and things to do.

The best time to visit Greece

Greece is a country that enjoys a Mediterranean climate, which means that it is hot and dry during the summer months, and mild during winter. The best time to visit Greece is from April to June or September to November. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are not as intense as they are during the summer.

How to get to Greece

Greece is a country that is easy to get to, with numerous international airports, including Athens International Airport, which is the busiest airport in the country. There are also many ferry services that connect Greece to other Mediterranean countries.

Things to do in Greece

1. Explore the Acropolis in Athens

The Acropolis is one of the most visited tourist attractions in the world. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that is home to some of the most significant remains of ancient Greek civilization, including the Parthenon and Erechtheion.

2. Visit the Temple of Olympian Zeus

The Temple of Olympian Zeus is another must-visit attraction in Athens. It is a massive temple that was built in 515 BC and took over 700 years to complete.

3. Head to Santorini

Santorini is a breathtaking island in Greece that is famous for its white-washed buildings, blue domes, and stunning sunsets. It is one of the most visited islands in Greece and is the perfect place to relax and unwind.

4. Visit the Palace of Knossos in Crete

The Palace of Knossos is a Bronze Age palace complex that is located on the island of Crete. It is the largest Bronze Age archaeological site in Greece and is believed to be the center of the Minoan civilization.

5. Walk around the Meteora Monasteries

The Meteora Monasteries are a group of six Eastern Orthodox monasteries that are built on top of enormous rock formations in central Greece. They are considered one of the most significant complexes of the Eastern Orthodox Church globally and are a popular attraction for tourists.

6. Delphi

Delphi is a must-visit attraction for history buffs. It was once considered the center of the world and was home to the Temple of Apollo. The site is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is one of the most important archeological sites in Greece.

7. Visit the old town of Nafplion

Nafplion is a picturesque town that is located on the Peloponnese peninsula in Greece. It is the perfect place to wander around the cobbled streets, explore the Venetian castle, and dine in one of the many traditional Greek tavernas.

8. Explore the island of Corfu

Corfu is one of the most beautiful islands in Greece. It is a lush green island that is surrounded by crystal-clear waters. It is the perfect place to visit if you want to explore ancient fortifications, picturesque villages, and stunning beaches.

9. Visit Mykonos

Mykonos is a popular tourist destination that is located in the Cyclades group of islands. It is known for its cosmopolitan nightlife, romantic atmosphere, and stunning beaches.

10. Go hiking in Mount Olympus

Mount Olympus is the highest mountain in Greece and is the perfect place for adventurers to explore. It is a mountain renowned for its mythological significance and boasts a range of challenging hiking trails.

FAQs

Q: Is Greece safe for tourists?

Yes, Greece is a safe country for tourists. However, as with any country in the world, you should take precautions and be aware of your surroundings.

Q: What currency is used in Greece?

The currency used in Greece is the Euro.

Q: Is it customary to tip in Greece?

Yes, it is customary to tip in Greece, especially in restaurants and cafes. A tip of around 10% is usually sufficient.

Q: What is the official language of Greece?

The official language of Greece is Greek.

Q: Do Greeks speak English?

Yes, most Greeks speak English, especially those in the tourism industry.

Whether you are interested in visiting ancient archeological sites, exploring picturesque villages, or relaxing on some of the most stunning beaches in the world, Greece has something for everyone.
