Hidden Gems in Athens: Uncovering the City's Best-Kept Secrets

Apr 13, 2023

[ad_1] Athens, the capital of Greece, is undoubtedly famous for its iconic landmarks like the Acropolis and the Parthenon. Besides these top tourist attractions, Athens is home to numerous hidden gems that are yet to be discovered by travellers. These hidden gems in Athens are well worth a visit and can provide a unique and authentic experience of the city. In this article, we will uncover some of the best-kept secrets in Athens, including some FAQs and the top 10 tourist attractions located in these hidden gems.

Exploring Hidden Gems in Athens

Athens is a city rich in history, culture and architecture. The hidden gems in Athens offer a glimpse of the city that is not well-known to the masses. Here are some of the lesser-known attractions and neighborhoods to explore during your visit to Athens.

1. Psyrri- This is a vibrant neighbourhood located in the heart of Athens, known for its nightlife and street art. The narrow alleys are filled with bars, cafes and restaurants serving traditional Greek food, and it’s the perfect place to experience the local way of life. Psyrri is also home to some of the best street art in the city, and you can see some of the works by taking a stroll through the neighbourhood.

2. Pynx Hill- Pynx Hill is located opposite the Acropolis and is an ancient site that was used as a gathering place for the Athenians. This site is important in the history of democracy as it was here that the public assembly used to meet. The hill offers incredible views of the Acropolis and is a great spot to watch the sunset.

3. Anafiotika- Located at the base of the Acropolis, Anafiotika is a charming neighbourhood with narrow cobbled streets and traditional whitewashed houses. The architecture here is influenced by the Cycladic islands, and it’s one of the few places in Athens that have maintained its original look and feel.

4. Mount Lycabettus- Mount Lycabettus is the highest point in Athens, and it offers breathtaking views of the city. You can hike up to the top of the mountain, take a cable car or a taxi to reach the top. The surrounding park is also perfect for a picnic or a relaxed stroll.

5. Benizelou Mansion- This is a stunning neoclassical building in the city center that dates back to the early 20th century. The building has been restored and turned into a cultural center showcasing Greek art and music. It’s a great place to learn more about Greek culture and history.

6. Gazi- Gazi is a former gasworks factory that has been transformed into an entertainment hub with clubs, restaurants, and bars. It’s a popular spot for young Greeks and tourists alike, and it’s a great place to experience the nightlife in Athens.

7. Plaka- Plaka is one of the oldest neighbourhoods in Athens, and it’s located at the base of the Acropolis. The area is known for its narrow, winding streets, traditional tavernas and handicraft shops. It’s the perfect place to walk around and soak up the atmosphere.

8. The National Archaeological Museum- The National Archaeological Museum is one of the largest and most important museums in Greece, and it houses an impressive collection of ancient artefacts from all over the country. The museum is often overlooked by visitors, but it’s definitely worth a visit.

9. Exarcheia- Exarcheia is a neighborhood that is home to artists, intellectuals, and anarchists. It’s a bohemian area with street art, bookshops, and cafes. It’s also known for its political murals and graffiti, and it’s a great place to explore the alternative side of Athens.

10. Monastiraki- Monastiraki is a bustling market district located in the heart of Athens. The area is known for its flea markets, antique shops, and street vendors selling all kinds of wares. It’s a great place to pick up souvenirs and taste traditional Greek street food.

FAQs

1. What is the best time to visit Athens?

The best time to visit Athens is from March to May and from September to November when the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds.

2. Do I need a visa to visit Athens?

If you are a citizen of the EU, Canada, or the USA, you do not need a visa to visit Athens for up to 90 days. Citizens of other countries should check with their local Greek embassy or consulate.

3. How do I get around Athens?

Athens has a well-developed public transport system, including buses, trams, metro, and suburban railway. Taxis and car rentals are also available, but traffic can be heavy in the city center.

4. Is Athens safe for tourists?

Athens is generally a safe city, but visitors should take care with their belongings and avoid dark or isolated areas at night. It’s also important to be aware of pickpockets in crowded tourist areas.

5. What currency is used in Athens?

The currency used in Athens is the euro.
