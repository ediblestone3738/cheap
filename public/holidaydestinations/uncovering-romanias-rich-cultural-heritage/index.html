Discovering Romania’s Rich Cultural Heritage

Romania has a rich cultural heritage that dates back centuries. In the past, Romania was part of the Roman Empire and was under the rule of the Ottoman Empire. Due to this, Romania’s culture is a blend of both European and Turkish influence.

Aside from the beautiful countryside and welcoming people, you can also explore some of the historical sites and landmarks that contribute to Romania’s rich culture. Below are the top 10 tourist attractions located in Uncovering Romania’s Rich Cultural Heritage.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Uncovering Romania’s Rich Cultural Heritage

1. Bran Castle

Bran Castle, also known as Dracula’s Castle, is located in the Transylvania region of Romania. The castle dates back to the 14th century and has a long and fascinating history. The castle is surrounded by beautiful greenery and offers stunning views of the Romanian countryside.

2. Peles Castle

Located in Sinaia, Peles Castle was built in the late 19th century for King Carol I of Romania. The castle is a masterpiece of German Neo-Renaissance architecture and contains an extensive collection of art and sculpture.

3. Merry Cemetery

The Merry Cemetery is a unique and intriguing location that is located in Maramures. The cemetery dates back to the 15th century and features vibrant and colorful tombstones. It is said that the local community created these tombstones to commemorate their loved ones in a positive and vibrant way.

4. Sibiu

Sibiu is a beautiful medieval town located in the heart of Romania. The town has a rich cultural history and features many historical buildings and landmarks.

5. Voronet Monastery

The Voronet Monastery is a magnificent 15th-century Orthodox monastery located in the region of Bucovina. The monastery is known for the stunning blue frescoes that adorn the church’s exterior walls.

6. Palace of the Parliament

The Palace of the Parliament is a colossal building located in the heart of Bucharest. The building was constructed in the late 20th century and is one of the largest administrative buildings in the world.

7. Danube Delta

The Danube Delta is a beautiful and unique ecosystem in Romania that is home to a wide range of flora and fauna. The area is a nature lover’s paradise and offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, fishing, and birdwatching.

8. Corvin Castle

Corvin Castle, also known as Hunedoara Castle, is a medieval fortress located in the Transylvania region of Romania. The castle has a long and interesting history and features many architectural details that are unique to this region.

9. Black Sea Coast

The Black Sea Coast is a stunning stretch of coastline that offers visitors the opportunity to explore ancient ruins, beautiful beaches, and quaint coastal villages.

10. Painted Monasteries

The Painted Monasteries of Bucovina are a group of unique and stunning architectural landmarks that are located in the northern part of Romania. The monasteries are known for their vibrant frescoes and colorful exterior walls, which depict biblical stories and historical events.

Q: What is the currency used in Romania? A: The currency used in Romania is the Romanian leu.

Q: What is the language spoken in Romania? A: The official language of Romania is Romanian.

Q: Do I need a visa to visit Romania? A: Visitors from most countries do not need a visa to visit Romania. However, it is always best to check with your embassy to confirm.
