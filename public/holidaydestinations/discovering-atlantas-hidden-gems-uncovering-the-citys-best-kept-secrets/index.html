Discovering Atlanta's Hidden Gems: Uncovering the City's Best-Kept Secrets

Mar 16, 2023

[ad_1] Atlanta, the capital city of Georgia, is famous for its world-renowned attractions such as the Georgia Aquarium, World of Coca-Cola, and the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site. However, there are some hidden gems in Atlanta that are yet to be discovered. In this article, we will explore the city’s best-kept secrets and uncover the hidden gems that lie within Atlanta’s streets and neighborhoods.

1. A Walk through the Oakland Cemetery

The Oakland Cemetery might not be one of the top things that come to mind when you think of Atlanta, but it is undoubtedly one of the city’s hidden gems. The cemetery is a historical landmark, with over 70,000 graves of notable people such as Margaret Mitchell (the author of Gone with the Wind) and Bobby Jones, the golfing legend. A stroll through the cemetery’s lush greens is like walking through a park, making it ideal for picnics or a quiet escape.

2. Explore the Atlanta Street Art

Atlanta is known for its vibrant street art that adorns many of its walls and buildings. This art ranges from large murals to spray-painted messages that make for excellent photo opportunities. Going on a street art tour will expose you to Atlanta’s thriving creative culture and the beauty of its unconventional art.

3. The Candler Park Hidden Forest

The Candler Park hidden forest is a tucked-away natural preserve on the east side of Atlanta, an excellent escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. The forest spans over 55 acres of green space, with beautiful hiking paths to explore. It’s an excellent spot for nature enthusiasts, and you might even spot some wildlife while on your walk.

4. Have a Meal at the Busy Bee Cafe

If you are looking for a taste of southern cuisine, the Busy Bee Cafe is the place to be. The restaurant has been serving up mouth-watering dishes since 1947 and is a regular spot for locals. The restaurant has a homely feel, and its famous dishes include fried chicken, mac and cheese, and sweet potato pie.

5. Visit the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historical Park

The Martin Luther King Jr. National Historical Park is a must-visit attraction in Atlanta, but it’s still considered a hidden gem. The park is a tribute to the civil rights leader and includes his birthplace, church, and burial site. There are several tours and exhibits that give visitors an insight into the Civil Rights movement and the role Martin Luther King Jr. played.

6. Walk the Atlanta Beltline

The Atlanta Beltline is a former railway line that has been converted into a multi-use trail that runs through the city. The trail is perfect for walking, jogging, or cycling and has excellent views of the city skyline. There are several public art installations along the way that add to the trail’s charm, making it an excellent spot for a leisurely stroll.

7. Visit the Sweet Auburn Curb Market

The Sweet Auburn Curb Market is an indoor market that has been in existence for over 100 years. The market has a collection of vendors selling fresh produce, meats, cheeses, baked goods, and other local goodies. It’s an excellent spot to sample Atlanta’s local flavors and to pick up some fresh ingredients to cook with.

8. The Krog Street Tunnel

The Krog Street Tunnel is a graffiti-filled underpass that connects two of Atlanta’s neighborhoods. The tunnel is a popular spot for photographers and street artists, making it an ever-changing backdrop for visitors. The tunnel is also close to several bars and restaurants, making it an excellent spot for a night out.

9. The Center for Puppetry Arts

The Center for Puppetry Arts is the largest organization in North America dedicated to the art of puppetry. The center has a museum that showcases puppets from around the world and has several interactive exhibits. There are also regular puppet shows, making it an entertaining spot for both kids and adults.

10. The Atlanta History Center

The Atlanta History Center is a museum that focuses on the history of Atlanta and the South. The museum is located in 33 acres of land and has several exhibits on topics such as the Civil War, Civil Rights Movement, and Southern Folk art. The museum also has several gardens and historic buildings on the grounds, making it an all-encompassing attraction.

4. What is Atlanta known for? Atlanta is known for its rich history, vibrant arts and culture scene, and, of course, its southern cuisine.

From street art to hidden forests, these gems are waiting to be discovered.
