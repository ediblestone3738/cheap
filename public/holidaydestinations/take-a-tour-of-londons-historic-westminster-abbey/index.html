Take a Tour of London’s Historic Westminster Abbey

London’s Westminster Abbey is one of the most iconic and historically significant sites in the city. It is the traditional coronation church of the British monarchy, and has been the site of many royal weddings, including the marriage of Prince William and Kate Middleton in 2011. The abbey is a stunning example of Gothic architecture, and is home to a wealth of art, artefacts and monuments. A tour of Westminster Abbey is an absolute must for any visitor to London.

History of Westminster Abbey

The site of Westminster Abbey has a long and varied history. It is believed that a church was first established here in the 7th century, and the abbey was built in its current form in the 13th century. The abbey has been the site of numerous coronations and royal weddings, including the coronation of Queen Elizabeth II in 1953 and the wedding of Prince William and Kate Middleton in 2011. The abbey is home to a wealth of art, artefacts and monuments, and is a popular tourist attraction.

What to See on a Tour of Westminster Abbey

A tour of Westminster Abbey is an absolute must for any visitor to London. The abbey is home to a wealth of art, artefacts and monuments, and a tour will give you the chance to explore the history of the abbey and its many treasures. Highlights of a tour of Westminster Abbey include the Coronation Chair, the tombs of some of England’s most famous monarchs, and the stunning Gothic architecture of the abbey itself.

How to Visit Westminster Abbey

Westminster Abbey is open to visitors every day except Sundays and religious holidays. You can purchase tickets for a guided tour, which lasts approximately 90 minutes, or you can explore the abbey at your own pace with an audio guide. Tickets for a guided tour can be purchased online or at the abbey itself.

Top 5 Tourist Attractions in Westminster Abbey

1. Coronation Chair – This is the chair in which all British monarchs have been crowned since 1308.

2. Tomb of Queen Elizabeth I – This is the tomb of the beloved Queen Elizabeth I, who reigned from 1558 to 1603.

3. Tomb of Mary, Queen of Scots – This is the tomb of Mary, Queen of Scots, who was executed in 1587.

4. Poets’ Corner – This is a section of the abbey dedicated to some of the greatest poets in British history, including Geoffrey Chaucer, William Shakespeare and John Milton.

5. The Chapter House – This is the oldest part of the abbey, and is home to a number of stunning medieval sculptures.
