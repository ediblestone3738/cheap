Spain is a country that is well-known for its sunny beaches, crystal clear waters, and refreshing cocktails like Sangria. Every year, millions of tourists flock to its resorts to experience the best of sun, sea, and Sangria. If you are planning a vacation to Spain and don’t know where to stay, this guide will help you choose the perfect resort for your taste and preference.

Marbella

Marbella is one of Spain’s most popular resort towns, located on the Mediterranean coast. It is known for its luxurious villas, upscale restaurants, designer boutiques, and vibrant nightlife. The town has 27 km of sandy beaches, including the famous Puerto Banús Beach, where you can sunbathe, swim, or take part in water activities. Marbella’s Old Town is also worth visiting, with its charming narrow streets, white-washed buildings, and historic sites.

Ibiza

Ibiza is one of the Balearic Islands, located in the Mediterranean Sea. It is known worldwide for its electronic music, wild parties, and stunning beaches. The island has something for everyone, whether you are a party-goer or a nature lover. Ibiza Town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, with its ancient fortifications and narrow streets. The island also has many unspoiled coves, such as Cala Comte and Cala Bassa, with crystal clear waters and breathtaking views.

Costa Brava

Costa Brava is a stunning stretch of coastline in northeastern Spain, known for its rugged cliffs, hidden coves, and charming fishing villages. The region has many beautiful beaches, such as Platja d’Aro, Tossa de Mar, and Lloret de Mar. Costa Brava is also home to some of Spain’s most famous landmarks, such as the Salvador Dalí Museum in Figueres and the medieval walled town of Girona.

Costa del Sol

Costa del Sol, also known as the “Sun Coast,” is a paradise for sun-seeking tourists. It is located in southern Spain, on the Mediterranean Sea, and has more than 300 days of sunshine a year. The region has many popular resorts, such as Torremolinos, Benalmádena, and Fuengirola, each with its own unique charm. The beaches here are sandy and wide, such as Playa del Palo, and there are plenty of water activities to enjoy.

FAQs

Q: What’s the best time to visit Spain’s resorts?

A: The peak tourist season is from June to September, but the best time to visit is in the shoulder seasons of May and October when the weather is still pleasant, and the crowds are thin.

Q: Is it safe to visit Spain’s resorts?

A: Spain is generally safe, but travelers should always be aware of their surroundings, especially in crowded places, and be cautious of pickpockets.

Q: How much does it cost to stay in Spain’s resorts?

A: Accommodation prices vary depending on the resort and season. Expect to pay more in the high season and for luxury resorts. Budget accommodation starts at about €40 a night.

Q: Do I need a visa to visit Spain?

A: Travelers from most countries do not need a visa for a stay of fewer than 90 days. Check with your embassy to confirm visa requirements.
