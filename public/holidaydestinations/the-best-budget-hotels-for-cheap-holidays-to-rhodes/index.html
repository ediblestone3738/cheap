Top 10 Tourist Attractions

1. Old Town of Rhodes

The Old Town of Rhodes is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most popular tourist attractions in Rhodes. This well-preserved medieval town is filled with narrow streets, stunning architecture, and ancient ruins.

2. Lindos Acropolis

The Lindos Acropolis is an ancient temple that dates back to the 3rd century BC. This impressive site is located on a hill overlooking the town of Lindos and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

3. Antony Quinn Bay

Antony Quinn Bay is a beautiful cove located on the east coast of Rhodes. This crystal-clear bay is perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.

4. Valley of the Butterflies

The Valley of the Butterflies is a stunning nature reserve located in the western part of Rhodes. This lush valley is home to thousands of butterflies that migrate to the area each year.

5. Palace of the Grand Master of the Knights of Rhodes

The Palace of the Grand Master of the Knights of Rhodes is an impressive fortress located in the heart of the Old Town of Rhodes. This impressive building was built in the 14th century and served as the headquarters for the Knights of Rhodes.

6. Rhodes Archaeological Museum

The Rhodes Archaeological Museum is located in the town of Rhodes and houses a variety of ancient artifacts, including pottery, statues, and jewelry.

7. Monolithos Castle

The Monolithos Castle is an ancient castle located on a hill overlooking the sea. This impressive site dates back to the 15th century and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

8. Rhodes Aquarium

The Rhodes Aquarium is located in the town of Rhodes and is home to a variety of marine creatures, including sharks, sea turtles, and octopuses.

9. Ancient Kamiros

Ancient Kamiros is an ancient city located on the northwest coast of Rhodes. This impressive site dates back to the 5th century BC and offers a glimpse into the life of ancient Greeks.

10. Rhodes Beaches

Rhodes is home to some of the most beautiful beaches in Greece. Some of the most popular beaches include Tsambika Beach, Faliraki Beach, and Lindos Beach.

FAQs

Q: What is the best time to visit Rhodes?

A: The best time to visit Rhodes is during the summer months of June, July, and August, when the weather is warm and sunny.

Q: What is the currency in Rhodes?

A: The currency in Rhodes is the Euro.

Q: What language do they speak in Rhodes?

A: The official language in Rhodes is Greek, but English is also widely spoken.

Q: Do I need a visa to travel to Rhodes?

A: Citizens of the EU and many other countries do not need a visa to travel to Rhodes. Please check with your local embassy for specific requirements.

Q: Are there any direct flights to Rhodes?

A: Yes, there are several airlines that offer direct flights to Rhodes from various European cities.
