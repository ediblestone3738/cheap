Shopping in New Delhi: A Guide to the Best Markets

[ad_1] Shopping in New Delhi: A Guide to the Best Markets

New Delhi is a shopper’s paradise. From traditional markets to modern shopping centers, the city offers everything that shoppers could desire. The city is known for its diversity in markets, be it designer showrooms, street markets or malls, each has its own charm. If you are planning to visit New Delhi and are looking to go on a shopping spree, then this guide will help you find the best markets to shop in the city.

  1. Dilli Haat

Dilli Haat is a popular market that showcases the culture of India through its handicrafts, textiles, jewelry and other traditional items. Each region of India is represented in a separate section here, which sells goods such as leather items, pottery, brassware, silk and cotton textiles, shawls and stoles, showpieces, and home decor. There is also a food court that offers a variety of traditional Indian cuisines.

  1. Chandni Chowk

Chandni Chowk is one of the oldest markets in New Delhi, known for its traditional Indian goods such as gold and silver jewellery, textiles, and spices. The market also houses famous food stores like Parathewali Gali and Karim’s. This market can be overwhelming for first-time visitors, so plan to spend several hours exploring it.

  1. Sarojini Nagar

Sarojini Nagar Market is popular for its vast collection of trendy and affordable clothes, accessories, and footwear. It is the go-to destination for budget shopping in Delhi. You can find designer clothes for a fraction of the cost of their original price, if you are willing to bargain.

  1. Khan Market

Khan Market is a high-end market popular for its designer stores, bookshops, and restaurants. From clothing to accessories, home decor to book stores, everything is available here. Khan Market is also popular for its fine dining restaurants offering cuisine from all over the world.

  1. Connaught Place

Connaught Place is a circular market located in the heart of Delhi. It houses several stores and showrooms of international and Indian brands, jewellery shops, bookstores, and restaurants. It is one of the most popular shopping destinations for both tourists and locals.

  1. Janpath and Tibetan Market

Janpath is known for its collection of handcrafted goods including clothes, jewellery, home decor, and accessories. The Tibetan Market, located next to the Janpath, offers a range of handicrafts and souvenirs, including t-shirts, jackets, and other fashionable items.

  1. Lajpat Nagar

Lajpat Nagar is a popular market known for its ethnic wear, jewellery, and footwear. It is an ideal destination for those looking for wedding shopping, as Lajpat Nagar offers a range of bridal wear and ethnic dresses.

  1. Paharganj

Paharganj is a budget market that is popular with backpackers and budget travellers. The market offers a range of items such as clothes, footwear, bags, accessories, and souvenirs at reasonable prices.

  1. South Extension

South Extension is a high-end market that offers a range of designer stores and showrooms for international and Indian brands. The market is popular for its collection of ethnic and fusion wear, jewellery, and accessories.

  1. Select Citywalk

Select Citywalk is a modern shopping mall located in Saket. It offers a range of international and Indian brands, designer stores, and restaurants. It is the perfect place for family outings and also offers a range of entertainment options like a cinema hall, gaming zone and a kids’ play area.

FAQs

Q. What is the best time to go shopping in Delhi?

A. The best time to go shopping in Delhi is during the winter months, from November to February, when the weather is cool and pleasant.

Q. Can I bargain in Delhi markets?

A. Yes, bargaining is possible in most of the markets in Delhi, though it’s not recommended in high-end stores.

Q. Is it safe to shop in Delhi markets?

A. Yes, it is safe to shop in Delhi markets, but be cautious of pickpockets and touts.

Q. What are the payment options available in Delhi markets?

A. Payment options available in Delhi markets include cash, credit/debit cards, and mobile payment apps.

Q. Which market is best for budget shopping in Delhi?

A. Sarojini Nagar Market and Paharganj are the best markets for budget shopping in Delhi.
