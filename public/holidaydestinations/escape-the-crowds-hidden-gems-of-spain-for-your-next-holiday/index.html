Spain is a country of diversity, with stunning landscapes, beautiful architecture, rich histories, and unique cultures, making it one of the most popular tourist destinations in Europe. However, as Spain’s popularity continues to grow, so do the crowds, making it increasingly difficult to escape the noise and find a little peace and tranquillity. Thankfully, there are still plenty of hidden gems in Spain that offer just that. These lesser-known, off-the-beaten-path destinations provide an opportunity for visitors to experience the country’s culture, history, and natural beauty away from the crowds.

In this travel article, we will be exploring the best-hidden gems in Spain for your next holiday, how to get there, and what each destination has to offer.

FAQs:

Q. What is the best time of year to visit these hidden gems in Spain? A. The best time to visit these destinations varies depending on the location. However, the most popular time to travel to Spain is between June and August. Therefore, if you would like to avoid the crowds, we recommend visiting during the shoulder season, which occurs between April and May or September and October.

Q. Can you recommend any travel packages for these hidden gems in Spain? A. Various travel packages offer excursions to some of Spain’s most beautiful hidden gems. We recommend checking out companies such as Trafalgar, Insight Vacations, and Intrepid Travel, as they offer a range of packages to suit different budgets and preferences.

Q. Will English be spoken in these locations? A. While Spanish is the official language, and it’s worth having some knowledge of the language, many people involved in the tourist industry speak at least some English.

Q. Are these destinations accessible by public transport? A. Most of these destinations can be reached by public transport. Still, we recommend renting a car, as it will allow you to explore more of the surrounding areas and give you greater flexibility.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Escape the Crowds: Hidden Gems of Spain

1. Ronda Located in the Andalusia region of Southern Spain, Ronda is a beautiful hidden gem that sits perched on the edge of a cliff. Ronda is famous for its unique architecture, stunning views of the surrounding landscape, and its beautiful gardens, such as the Alameda del Tajo. If you enjoy wine, a visit to the Bodegas La Sangre de Ronda winery is a must, offering tours, tastings, and beautiful views of the surrounding vines.

2. Frigiliana Frigiliana is a picturesque village located in the Malaga province in Southern Spain. The village boasts cobbled streets, traditional whitewashed houses, and a range of unique shops and restaurants selling locally sourced products. Frigiliana is also known for its delicious honey, which can be purchased from one of the local producers.

3. Cadaques Located in the Catalonia region of Spain, Cadaques is a beautiful coastal town that has managed to retain its traditional charm. The town is famous for its winding streets, white-washed houses, and beautiful beaches. Cadaques is also the former home of artist Salvador Dali, and visitors can visit his house, which is now a museum.

4. Montserrat Montserrat is a beautiful mountain range located in the Catalonia region of Northern Spain. The mountains offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape and are home to a Benedictine monastery that dates back to the 11th century. Visitors can climb to the top of the mountains or take a scenic cable car ride up to the monastery.

5. Ribeira Sacra Located in the Galicia region of Spain, Ribeira Sacra is a beautiful wine region known for its vineyards and stunning scenery. Visitors can take a tour of the vineyards, sample the local wines, and enjoy a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, kayaking, and cycling.

6. Valldemossa Valldemossa is a beautiful village located in the Mallorca province of Spain. The village is home to a stunning monastery, where composer Frederic Chopin and writer George Sand lived for a short period. Visitors can visit the monastery, explore the traditional cobblestone streets, and sample the local food and wine.

7. Grazalema Located in the Andalusia region of Spain, Grazalema is a beautiful mountain village that is steeped in history. The village boasts stunning views of the surrounding mountains and is famous for its hiking trails, traditional architecture, and local cheeses.

8. Albarracin Albarracin is a beautiful fortified town located in the Aragon region of Spain. The town boasts stunning views of the surrounding countryside and is known for its unique architecture, narrow streets, and beautiful Gothic cathedral.

9. Cuenca Cuenca is a stunning medieval city located in the Castilla-La Mancha region of Spain. The city is famous for its hanging houses, which sit perched on the edge of a cliff overlooking the Jucar river. Visitors can explore the narrow streets, visit the Gothic cathedral, and sample the local cuisine.

10. Tossa de Mar Located in the Catalonia region of Spain, Tossa de Mar is a beautiful coastal town that is famous for its sandy beaches, crystal clear waters, and stunning scenery. The town is also home to a beautiful medieval castle, which stands guard over the town and offers magnificent views of the surrounding landscape.

In conclusion, Spain is a beautiful country with many hidden gems that are waiting to be discovered. Whether you are looking for stunning mountain views, charming medieval towns, or coastal paradises, Spain has it all. So why not consider escaping the crowds on your next holiday and discovering some of Spain’s beautiful hidden gems? You won’t be disappointed! [ad_2]
