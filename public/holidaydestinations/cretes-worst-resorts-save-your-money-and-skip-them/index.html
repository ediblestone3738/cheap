Crete's Worst Resorts: Save Your Money and Skip Them

Apr 13, 2023

[ad_1] Crete is a beautiful and diverse island in Greece, boasting a rich history, stunning landscapes, and delicious cuisine. However, not all resorts on the island are created equal. In this article, we will be exploring Crete’s worst resorts, highlighting why they should be avoided, and offering alternative options for a perfect holiday experience.

While there are many great resort destinations on Crete, there are also some that have earned a reputation for being subpar. These resorts are often plagued with a range of issues, from poor service and outdated facilities to dirty beaches and overcrowding. If you’re looking for a relaxing and enjoyable holiday, we highly recommend avoiding these resorts at all costs.

Here are the top 5 worst resorts on Crete:

1. Malia Malia is known for its party scene, with an abundance of bars and clubs catering to young tourists. While some may enjoy the lively atmosphere, many travelers find the resort to be overcrowded, loud, and lacking in charm.

2. Hersonissos Hersonissos is another resort that attracts a young crowd, with many party-goers flocking to its clubs and bars. However, the resort has become infamous for its overcrowded beaches, hordes of tourists, and a lack of cleanliness.

3. Agios Nikolaos While Agios Nikolaos has some beautiful scenery, the resort is generally viewed as being outdated and lacking in modern amenities. Tourists may also be disappointed with its small beaches and limited dining options.

4. Stalida Stalida is another resort that caters to party-goers, with many bars and clubs offering cheap drinks and wild nights. However, the resort can be noisy and crowded, with little to offer in terms of attractions or activities.

5. Gouves Gouves is often described as being a “typical tourist trap,” with little to offer in terms of culture or authenticity. Visitors may be disappointed with its lack of character, crowded beaches, and high prices.

While these resorts may not be ideal for a perfect holiday, there are many other destinations on Crete that offer a more authentic and enjoyable experience. From quiet fishing villages to charming hilltop towns, there is something for every type of traveler on the island.

FAQs about Crete’s Worst Resorts:

1. Is it safe to visit these resorts? While these resorts are generally safe, travelers may want to take extra precautions during peak season when crowds are at their highest.

2. Are there any alternative resort destinations on Crete? Yes, there are many charming villages and towns on the island that offer a more authentic and enjoyable experience.

3. What are some of the best attractions on Crete? Some of the top attractions on the island include the Palace of Knossos, Elafonisi Beach, Samaria Gorge, and the Venetian Harbor in Chania.
