A Foodie's Guide to Kefalonia: Where to Find the Island's Best Eats

Apr 15, 2023

[ad_1] Kefalonia is a Greek island located in the Ionian Sea. Known for its breathtaking beaches, crystal-clear waters, and picturesque architecture, Kefalonia is also a haven for food lovers. The cuisine on the island is a blend of traditional Greek dishes with Italian and Mediterranean influences. A foodie’s guide to Kefalonia is essential to explore the best of the island’s culinary delights. In this article, we will explore where to find the island’s best eats, its most asked questions, and the top 10 tourist attractions located in A Foodie’s Guide to Kefalonia.

Where to Find the Island’s Best Eats:

1. En Kefallinia

Located in Argostoli, En Kefallinia is a must-visit for seafood lovers. The restaurant serves fresh fish and seafood dishes cooked to perfection.

2. Taverna Dimitris

Taverna Dimitris offers an authentic dining experience with traditional Greek dishes. The restaurant is located in the village of Vlachata, and its menu features homemade food cooked in clay pots.

3. La Mer

La Mer is situated on the seafront of Lixouri, offering panoramic views of the Ionian Sea. The restaurant serves a wide range of dishes, including seafood, meat, and vegetarian options.

4. To Perasma

To Perasma is a popular restaurant in Fiskardo, known for its delicious Greek cuisine. The restaurant’s menu features a range of appetizers, salads, and main courses sure to satisfy any foodie.

5. Butlers House

Butlers House is a family-run restaurant in Skala, serving homemade dishes made with local ingredients. The menu features traditional Greek dishes with a modern twist, and the restaurant also offers a selection of vegan and vegetarian options.

6. Acqua Alaties

Acqua Alaties is an upscale restaurant located in the village of Karya. The menu features a fusion of Italian and Greek cuisine, and the restaurant is renowned for its homemade pasta dishes.

7. Platanos

Platanos is a cozy, family-run restaurant in Sami, serving authentic Greek dishes with a touch of Italian influence. The menu features a range of fresh seafood and meat dishes, along with vegetarian and vegan options.

8. Oinops Wine Bar

Oinops Wine Bar is located in the picturesque village of Assos, serving a range of Greek and Mediterranean dishes. The restaurant’s menu is created based on local ingredients and seasonal products, accompanied by a wine list of over 200 labels.

9. Nefeli

Nefeli is situated in the village of Lourdata and is famous for its seafood dishes. The restaurant’s menu features Greek cuisine with a modern twist, using local ingredients.

10. Sunset Taverna

Sunset Taverna is a seafood restaurant located on a rocky cliff overlooking the sea. The restaurant’s menu features fresh fish dishes cooked to perfection, accompanied by excellent views of the Ionian Sea.

Most Asked Questions:

1. What is the best time to visit Kefalonia?

The best time to visit Kefalonia is from April to October. The weather is pleasant during this period, and most restaurants and tourist attractions are open.

2. Is Kefalonia expensive?

Kefalonia is relatively affordable compared to other European destinations. However, prices may vary depending on the location and the season.

3. What are the must-visit tourist attractions in Kefalonia?

Some of the top tourist attractions in Kefalonia include the Melissani Cave, Myrtos Beach, Fiskardo, Assos, and the Monastery of Agios Andreas Milapidias.

4. What cuisine is Kefalonia known for?

Kefalonia’s cuisine is a blend of traditional Greek dishes with Italian and Mediterranean influences. The island is famous for its fresh seafood and meat dishes, accompanied by local wines.

5. Is it safe to drink tap water in Kefalonia?

Yes, tap water is drinkable in Kefalonia. It is also safe to drink bottled water, which is widely available on the island.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions:

1. Melissani Cave

The Melissani Cave is a breathtaking natural beauty located in the village of Karavomylos. The cave is a geological wonder, with crystal clear blue waters and rock formations.

2. Myrtos Beach

Myrtos Beach is one of the most beautiful beaches in the world, located in the northwest of Kefalonia. The beach is renowned for its crystal-clear waters and dramatic cliffs.

3. Fiskardo

Fiskardo is a charming village located on the northern coast of Kefalonia. The village features Venetian-style buildings, an ancient Roman cemetery, and a harbor filled with luxury yachts.

4. Assos

Assos is a picturesque fishing village located on the northwest coast of Kefalonia. The village is known for its scenic views and historical landmarks, including the Venetian Castle.

5. Monastery of Agios Andreas Milapidias

The Monastery of Agios Andreas Milapidias is a well-preserved Byzantine monument located in the village of Peratata. The monastery features beautiful frescoes and ancient artifacts.

6. Mount Ainos

Mount Ainos is the tallest mountain in Kefalonia, standing at 1,628 meters. The mountain is a popular hiking destination, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

7. Robola Wine Cooperative

The Robola Wine Cooperative is located in the village of Fragata, famous for its production of Robola wine. The cooperative offers tours and tastings, where visitors can sample the island’s famous wines.

8. St. Gerasimos Monastery

The St. Gerasimos Monastery is a significant religious monument located in the village of Omala. The monastery honors the patron saint of Kefalonia and features stunning Byzantine architecture.

9. Castle of Agios Georgios

The Castle of Agios Georgios is a Venetian fortress located on a hilltop overlooking the Ionian Sea. The castle is a popular tourist attraction, offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

10. Drogarati Cave

The Drogarati Cave is a stunning natural wonder located near Sami. The cave features a vast network of limestone formations, including stalactites and stalagmites.

Kefalonia is a food lover’s paradise, offering a wide range of traditional Greek dishes with Italian and Mediterranean influences. A Foodie’s Guide to Kefalonia is essential to explore the island’s best eats. The island is also home to several tourist attractions, including natural wonders, historical landmarks, and picturesque villages.
