Spoiling Your Vacation: The Worst Crete Resorts to Avoid

Apr 10, 2023

[ad_1] Crete is a popular tourist destination, known for its beautiful beaches, historic sites, and lively culture. However, not all resorts in Crete are created equal, and some are downright disappointing. In this article, we’re going to explore the worst Crete resorts to avoid, so you can avoid the hassle and heartache of a ruined vacation.

Worst Crete Resorts to Avoid

1. Sun Beach Hotel Located in Hersonissos, the Sun Beach Hotel is notorious for its dirty rooms, rude staff, and subpar amenities. The hotel is old and run-down, and guests have reported issues like broken showers, uncomfortable beds, and moldy bathrooms. The pool area is also unsanitary and poorly maintained.

2. Sergios Hotel This hotel is located in Rethymnon and has a reputation for being outdated and unclean. The rooms are small and cramped, with old furniture and stained carpets. Guests have also reported issues with the plumbing and lack of hot water. The staff is unhelpful and unaccommodating.

3. Cactus Beach Hotel Situated in Stalis, the Cactus Beach Hotel has received numerous complaints from guests about its unhygienic conditions. Moldy bathrooms, dirty bedding, and insect infestations are just some of the issues guests have faced. The food is also subpar and lacks variety.

4. Silva Beach Hotel This hotel, located in Hersonissos, is known for its uncomfortable beds, noisy air conditioning, and lack of cleanliness. Guests have reported moldy bathrooms, dirty floors, and unpleasant odors in the rooms. The staff is unresponsive and unhelpful.

5. Aquila Rithymna Beach Hotel This hotel in Rethymnon has received criticism from guests for its poor service and outdated amenities. The rooms are unclean and have outdated furnishings. The food is bland and unappetizing, and the staff is unresponsive to guests’ complaints.

FAQs

1. How can I find out if a hotel in Crete is good or bad before booking? Researching reviews from previous guests is a good way to get an idea of the quality of a hotel. Websites like TripAdvisor and Booking.com allow guests to leave reviews and ratings for hotels they have stayed in. You can also check the hotel’s website and social media pages for more information.

2. Are there any all-inclusive resorts in Crete that are worth staying at? Yes, there are many all-inclusive resorts in Crete that are highly rated by guests. The Iberostar Creta Panorama & Mare in Panormos and the Grecotel Club Marine Palace & Suites in Panormo are two examples.

3. What should I do if I have a problem with my hotel room or the service? If you have an issue with your room or the service at your hotel, you should speak to the front desk staff immediately. They may be able to rectify the issue or offer you a different room. If the issue is not resolved, you can contact the hotel’s management or the booking website you used to make your reservation.
