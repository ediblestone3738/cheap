The Best Things to Do & See in Kefalonia: Your Ultimate Guide

Apr 24, 2023

[ad_1] Kefalonia, located in the Ionian Sea, is said to be a paradise on earth. The Greek island is known for its breathtaking beaches, beautiful landscapes, and historic sites. In this ultimate guide, we will show you all the best things to do and see in Kefalonia.

1. Visit Myrtos Beach Myrtos Beach is, without a doubt, the most famous and well-known beach on the island. Its picturesque white cliffs and turquoise waters will leave you in awe. Take a dip in the crystal-clear waters or relax on the soft sand while soaking up the sun.

2. Explore Melissani Cave Melissani Cave is a natural wonder located in the town of Sami. Its underground lake appears blue under the sunlight shining through the cave’s opening. Visitors can take a boat ride on the lake and marvel at the cave’s incredible stalactites and stalagmites.

3. Discover Assos Village Assos Village is a charming and picturesque village located on a peninsula. Its colorful houses and tranquil atmosphere are perfect for a peaceful afternoon stroll. Visit the 16th-century fortress located on a hill overlooking the village for stunning views of the Ionian Sea.

4. Take a Hike at Mount Ainos National Park Mount Ainos National Park is located in the south of the island and is home to the highest peak in Kefalonia. Nature lovers will enjoy the park’s lush forests, diverse wildlife, and fantastic hiking trails.

5. Learn About Kefalonia’s History at St George Castle St George Castle is located in the town of Peratata and dates back to the late 12th century. It has played a significant role in the island’s history, from being a base for pirates to serving as a prison for political prisoners. Visitors can tour the castle and learn about its fascinating history.

6. Taste the Local Cuisine Kefalonia is known for its delicious cuisine, including dishes such as Kreatopita (meat pie) and Skordostoumbi (garlic dip). Visit local restaurants and taverns to try some of the island’s traditional meals.

7. Relax in the Village of Fiscardo Fiscardo Village is a charming fishing village located on the northern part of the island. Its colorful houses, beautiful harbor, and excellent cafes and restaurants make it a great place to relax and unwind.

8. Visit the Monastery of Sission The Monastery of Sission is a historic monastery located on Mount Ainos. It was founded in the 13th century and is home to an impressive collection of Byzantine icons and religious artifacts.

9. Go Diving at Foki Beach Foki Beach is located near the village of Fiscardo and is known for its clear waters and spectacular underwater scenery. Go diving or snorkeling to explore the colorful marine life and caves.

10. Experience the Nightlife in Argostoli Argostoli is the capital of Kefalonia and the heart of the island’s nightlife. Visit the bars and clubs in the town’s main square to dance the night away.

FAQs:

Q: When is the best time to visit Kefalonia? A: The best time to visit Kefalonia is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny.

Q: How do I get to Kefalonia? A: Kefalonia has an international airport located in the town of Argostoli. Alternatively, you can take a ferry from mainland Greece or other Greek islands.

Q: What is the currency in Kefalonia? A: The currency in Kefalonia is the Euro.

Q: Can I rent a car in Kefalonia? A: Yes, visitors can rent a car at the airport or in major towns.

Q: Is Kefalonia safe to visit? A: Yes, Kefalonia is a safe place to visit, and crime rates are low.
