Nestled between Poland, Ukraine, Lithuania, Latvia, and Russia, Belarus often remains overshadowed by its neighboring countries in terms of tourism. However, this hidden gem is a captivating destination that offers a wealth of history, culture, and natural beauty waiting to be discovered. And here’s a secret: If you hold a Russian visa, you’re in for a treat! Yes, dear adventurers, you can easily venture into the enchanting world of Belarus. So, let’s embark on a journey together and unravel the mysteries of this fascinating destination!

As you cross the border into Belarus, you’ll be greeted by a land steeped in history. From grand castles and fortresses to quaint villages frozen in time, there’s something for every history enthusiast. Start your adventure in Minsk, the capital city, and prepare to be amazed by its stunning architecture, wide boulevards, and vibrant atmosphere. Take a stroll through the Independence Square, where you’ll witness the captivating blend of Soviet-era monuments and contemporary structures.

Don’t miss the chance to visit the impressive Mir Castle, a UNESCO World Heritage site that showcases the country’s rich medieval heritage. Marvel at the intricate architecture and explore the castle’s magnificent grounds. Nearby, you’ll find another hidden gem – Nesvizh Palace. This stunning estate boasts beautifully landscaped gardens and breathtaking interiors, offering a glimpse into the opulence of the past.

For nature lovers, Belarus is a paradise waiting to be explored. Head to the famous Belovezhskaya Pushcha National Park, one of the last and largest remnants of Europe’s primeval forests. Home to the European bison, this protected area allows visitors to immerse themselves in the untouched beauty of nature. Walk along the peaceful trails and spot other wildlife, such as wolves, elk, and lynx.

If you’re an architecture enthusiast, the small town of Brest will mesmerize you with its unique blend of styles. The Brest Fortress, a symbol of the Soviet resistance during World War II, stands as a testament to the city’s resilience. Marvel at its towering walls and visit the myriad of museums inside, dedicated to the heroic defenders of the fortress.

Now, let’s address the most important question. Can Russian visa holders travel to Belarus? The answer is a resounding YES! As of 2017, visitors from 74 countries, including Russia, are eligible for a 30-day visa-free stay in Belarus. This means that if you hold a valid Russian visa, you can explore this captivating country hassle-free. However, there are certain requirements to keep in mind. Firstly, your stay in Belarus must not exceed 30 days, and you should enter and exit the country through Minsk National Airport. Moreover, you must possess valid medical insurance covering a minimum of €10,000 for the duration of your stay.

Here are a few frequently asked questions to help you plan your trip:

Q: Is English widely spoken in Belarus?

A: While Russian and Belarusian are the official languages, English is not commonly spoken. It’s advisable to have a translation app or basic knowledge of Russian or Belarusian to ease communication.

Q: How easy is it to travel within Belarus?

A: Belarus has an extensive transportation system, including buses and trains that connect major cities and tourist destinations. Intercity buses are comfortable and affordable, making it easy to explore various regions of the country.

Q: Are credit cards widely accepted in Belarus?

A: Cash is still king in Belarus, especially in smaller towns and rural areas. It’s recommended to carry enough Belarusian rubles for your daily expenses. However, credit cards are accepted in most hotels, restaurants, and larger establishments in cities.

Q: What is the best time to visit Belarus?

A: Belarus experiences distinct seasons, with mild summers and harsh winters. The ideal time to visit is during spring (May-June) or autumn (September-October) when the weather is pleasant, and nature is at its finest.

Q: Are there any hidden gems in Belarus?

A: Absolutely! In addition to the popular attractions, Belarus is full of hidden gems waiting to be discovered. Explore the charming town of Polotsk, visit the stunning Braslav Lakes National Park, or immerse yourself in the beauty of the Pripyatsky National Park.

This captivating destination will leave you awe-struck at every turn, from its rich history and culture to its stunning landscapes.
