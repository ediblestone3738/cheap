Greece is known for its beautiful landscapes, cultural diversity, and delicious food. However, there is one destination that is often overlooked by many travelers – Kavos. Located in the southern part of the island of Corfu, Kavos is a gem that offers a wide range of activities and attractions for all types of travelers. Whether you are looking for a relaxing getaway, a party destination, or an adventure-filled holiday, Kavos has it all. Let’s discover the best kept secret of Greece!

Getting There

Kavos is located in the southern part of Corfu Island, which is easily accessible by plane or ferry. Corfu International Airport is the closest airport to Kavos, and there are direct flights from many European cities such as London, Manchester, Berlin, and Amsterdam. Ferry links also connect the island to various cities in Greece, Albania, and Italy.

Accommodation

Kavos offers a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious villas. For those who want to be in the heart of the action, there are plenty of hotels and apartments located on the main strip. If you prefer a more peaceful setting, there are many villas and apartments located away from the center of town.

Nightlife

Kavos is known as the party capital of Corfu Island. The main strip is lined with bars, clubs, and pubs that offer entertainment until the early hours of the morning. Some of the most popular clubs are Atlantis, Future Club, and Limelight. If you prefer a more laid-back atmosphere, there are plenty of beach bars where you can relax with a cocktail while enjoying the sunset.

Beaches

Kavos boasts some of the most beautiful beaches on Corfu Island. The main beach in Kavos is a long strip of golden sand that stretches for 3km. The beach is lined with beach bars and restaurants, making it the perfect place to spend an entire day. If you prefer a quieter beach, there are plenty of secluded coves and bays to explore. Some of the best beaches in Kavos are Agios Petros, Arkoudilas, and Issos.

Water Sports

Kavos is a paradise for water sports lovers. The clear waters and the windy conditions make it the perfect place to go windsurfing, kite surfing, and parasailing. There are plenty of water sports centers that offer equipment rental and lessons for all levels.

Culture and History

Although Kavos is known for its party atmosphere, it also has a rich history and culture. The village of Lefkimmi is located nearby and is worth a visit to see the traditional architecture and taste the local cuisine. The Achilleion Palace, built in 1890 by Empress Elisabeth of Austria, is another must-see attraction on the island.

FAQs

Q: When is the best time to visit Kavos? A: The best time to visit Kavos is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny. The high season is from July to August when the town is busiest and the prices are highest.

Q: Is Kavos suitable for families with children? A: Kavos is primarily a party destination and may not be suitable for families with young children. However, there are plenty of activities and beaches that cater to families.

Q: Can I rent a car in Kavos? A: Yes, there are plenty of car rental companies located in Kavos. However, it is important to note that the roads can be narrow and winding, so it is important to drive carefully.
