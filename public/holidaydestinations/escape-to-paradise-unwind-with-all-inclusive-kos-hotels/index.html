Top Tourist Attractions in Kos

Kos is a small island, but it is packed with attractions for every taste. Here are some of the must-visit places on the island:

1. Asklepion: Asklepion is an ancient medical center located on a hilltop, a few kilometers from Kos town. The center was dedicated to Asklepios, the god of healing, and was a hub for medicine and philosophy in ancient times. The ruins of the center are well-preserved and offer a glimpse into the lives of ancient Greeks.

2. Kos Town: Kos town is the capital of the island and is filled with history and culture. The town has a medieval castle, the Neratzia Castle, which was built by the Knights of Saint John in the 14th century. The town also has several museums, such as the Archaeological Museum of Kos, which houses artifacts from ancient times.

3. Beaches: Kos is famous for its stunning beaches, and there is a beach for every taste. Agios Stefanos is a quiet beach, ideal for relaxation, while Tigaki is a bustling beach with several water sports activities. Paradise Beach, as the name suggests, is a stunning beach with crystal clear waters and a breathtaking view of the neighboring island of Pserimos.

4. Embros Therma: Embros Therma is a natural hot spring located on the eastern side of the island. The hot spring is rich in minerals and is said to have healing properties. The area around the hot spring is also a popular hiking spot, with several trails leading to stunning vistas.

5. Plaka Forest: Plaka Forest is a protected area on the island, with a variety of flora and fauna. The forest is ideal for picnics, hiking, and bird watching. The forest also has several traditional taverns where you can taste local delicacies.

6. Zia Village: Zia is a picturesque village located on the slopes of Mount Dikeos, the highest mountain on the island. The village offers stunning views of the Aegean Sea and the neighboring islands. The village has several traditional taverns and shops selling local handicrafts.

7. Antimachia Castle: Antimachia Castle is a medieval castle located in the village of Antimachia. The castle was built by the Knights of Saint John in the 14th century and was used as a defensive structure against the Ottomans. The castle offers a stunning view of the surrounding area and is ideal for photography.

8. Hippocrates Garden Cultural Center: The Hippocrates Garden Cultural Center is a unique attraction on the island, offering a glimpse into the life and teachings of Hippocrates, the father of modern medicine. The center has a variety of interactive exhibits, including a replica of an ancient medical center and a herb garden.

9. Lido Water Park: Lido Water Park is a popular attraction for families with children. The park has several water slides, pools, and a lazy river. The park also has several food and beverage outlets.

10. Casa Romana: Casa Romana is a Roman villa located in Kos town. The villa was discovered in the early 20th century and has been restored to its former glory. The villa offers a glimpse into the life of a wealthy Roman family.
