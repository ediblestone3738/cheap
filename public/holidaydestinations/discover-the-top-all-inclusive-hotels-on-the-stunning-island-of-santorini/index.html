Discover the top all-inclusive hotels on the stunning island of Santorini

Apr 12, 2023

[ad_1] Located in the Aegean Sea, Santorini is a beautiful island known for its stunning views, beautiful sunsets, and vibrant nightlife. Boasting natural landscapes that are a mix of black, white, and red volcanic sand beaches, Santorini is the perfect getaway for those looking for a truly magical and romantic vacation.

Whether you’re a honeymooner or a solo traveler, one of the best ways to make your Santorini trip unforgettable is to stay at an all-inclusive hotel. With some of the most beautiful and luxurious all-inclusive accommodations in the world, Santorini has everything you need to relax and unwind. In this article, we’ll be exploring the top all-inclusive hotels on the island, and providing you with a brief list of the top 10 tourist attractions that you can visit while you’re there.

Top All-Inclusive Hotels on Santorini

1. Santo Maris Oia, a Luxury Collection Resort

Located in the peaceful village of Oia, Santo Maris Oia is the epitome of luxury. This property boasts five stunning swimming pools, including a panoramic rooftop pool that provides guests with breathtaking views of the surrounding Aegean Sea. With 44 suites and villas spread across a large parcel of land, guests can enjoy the feeling of having their own private villa while still being part of a larger, vibrant resort.

2. The Majestic Hotel

The Majestic Hotel sits on a hilltop in the heart of Fira - the capital city of Santorini. This 5-star property features 53 spacious rooms and suites, all tastefully decorated with Greek-inspired interiors. Guests can enjoy stunning views of the sea from the hotel’s outdoor pool, terrace, or rooftop bar. With a range of in-house facilities, including a spa, gym, and gift shop, The Majestic Hotel has everything you need to have the perfect vacation.

3. Astarte Suites

Another all-inclusive hotel in Santorini that provides guests with a stunning view of the Aegean Sea is Astarte Suites. Located in the peaceful village of Akrotiri, Astarte Suites boasts a unique blend of Cycladic architecture and contemporary design. This adults-only property features just 9 suites and provides guests with a high level of privacy and seclusion. With its stunning infinity pool and private balconies that offer uninterrupted views of the sea, Astarte Suites is a true gem on the island.

4. Katikies Santorini

Katikies is one of the most famous all-inclusive hotels on the island, and for good reason. With its stunning white-washed architecture and outstanding location in the heart of Oia, this property is the perfect place to experience the true beauty of Santorini. Guests can choose from a range of deluxe rooms, suites, and villas, all of which are furnished with luxurious amenities and provide panoramic views of the sea. With a range of in-house dining options, including an award-winning gourmet restaurant, Katikies Santorini is sure to delight your taste buds as well.

5. Astra Suites

Astra Suites is a boutique all-inclusive hotel that is perfect for those looking for a more intimate and romantic Santorini experience. Located in the charming village of Imerovigli, Astra Suites offers stunning views of the island’s iconic caldera. Guests can choose from a range of suites and villas, each of which is uniquely designed with traditional Aegean architecture and modern amenities. With its stunning infinity pool, sun terrace, and on-site spa, Astra Suites is the ultimate in luxury.

FAQs

1. What’s the minimum age for guests in all-inclusive resorts in Santorini?

This varies from one hotel to another. Some hotels, such as Astarte Suites, only accept guests who are 18 years old and above, while others, such as Santo Maris Oia or The Majestic Hotel, accept children of all ages. It’s best to check with your hotel of choice before making your reservation, so you can avoid any misunderstandings or surprises when you arrive.

2. Are all-inclusive resorts in Santorini more expensive than regular hotels?

As a general rule, all-inclusive resorts in Santorini are more expensive compared to regular hotels. However, this is usually offset by the fact that you won’t have to pay for food, drinks, or extra services, such as spa treatments or water sports. Additionally, the quality of the service, the variety of the amenities, and the exclusivity of the experience are also factors that need to be taken into account.

3. Can I bring my pet to all-inclusive resorts in Santorini?

Most all-inclusive resorts in Santorini don’t allow pets. However, some hotels, such as The Majestic Hotel or Santo Maris Oia, might have specific pet-friendly rooms or villas that you can book. It’s best to inquire with your hotel of choice before making any reservations to avoid any issues upon arrival.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Santorini

1. Oia Village

Oia is the most iconic village in Santorini, and it’s known for its stunning sunsets, traditional architecture, and many art galleries, cafes, and restaurants.

2. Fira Town

Fira is the capital city of Santorini, and it’s the perfect place to enjoy a mix of ancient and modern Greek cultures. Visit the Archaeological Museum, walk down its cobbled streets, and enjoy a meal or a drink in one of its many cafes or tavernas.

3. Akrotiri Archaeological Site

Akrotiri is an ancient Minoan city that was buried under volcanic ash during the eruption of Santorini’s volcano. It’s one of the most famous archaeological sites in Greece.

4. Red Beach

Located near Akrotiri, Red beach is famous for its dramatic red and black cliffs, and its crystal clear turquoise waters.

5. Perissa Beach

Perissa Beach is one of the most popular beaches in Santorini, and it’s known for its black sand and the many water sports activities available.

6. Ancient Thera

Ancient Thera is an ancient Greek city that dates back to the 9th century BC. It’s located on a hilltop and provides visitors with stunning views of the island’s caldera and the Aegean Sea.

7. Pyrgos Village

Pyrgos is a traditional village that’s located on a hilltop next to Kamari. It’s famous for its winding cobbled streets, stunning views, and the many tavernas and cafés.

8. Amoudi Bay

Amoudi Bay is a small fishing village that’s located at the base of Oia’s cliffs. It’s famous for its fresh seafood, the stunning views of the sea, and the many boats that take visitors to nearby beaches.

9. Santo Winery

Santo Winery is one of the most famous wineries in Santorini, and it’s located on a hilltop that provides visitors with panoramic views of the island’s caldera. Visitors can take part in wine tastings, guided tours, and enjoy a meal or a snack in its café or restaurant.

10. Caldera Walk

Finally, no trip to Santorini would be complete without a walk along the island’s stunning caldera. The caldera walk is a 10-kilometer trek that starts in Oia and ends in Fira, and provides visitors with some of the most stunning views of the Aegean Sea and the island’s volcanic landscapes.

In conclusion, Santorini is a vibrant and stunning island in Greece that is known for its luxurious all-inclusive hotels, amazing attractions, and beautiful landscapes. Whether you want to relax on a beach, explore ancient ruins, or indulge in fine dining and flowing champagne, Santorini has something for everyone.
