Discover the hidden gems of Mykonos: An ultimate holiday guide

Mar 28, 2023

[ad_1] Mykonos is a stunning island situated in the Aegean Sea and is ranked as one of the hottest tourist destinations in Greece. This small island has it all, from scenic beaches, colorful architecture, delicious cuisine, and a vibrant nightlife, there is something for everyone. However, most of the time, tourists flock to the crowded beaches and only explore the popular attractions of the island. When planning a trip to Mykonos, it is highly recommended to explore the hidden gems of this beautiful island for an unforgettable experience. In this ultimate holiday guide, we will take you through all the hidden gems of Mykonos, including a FAQs section of the most asked questions.

1. Little Venice

Little Venice is situated in the heart of Mykonos town and is a hidden gem that should not be missed. This beautiful spot was once the homes of wealthy merchants, and now it has been transformed into bars, cafes, and restaurants. The buildings in Little Venice are painted bright colors, and their balconies hang over the deep blue sea, making it the perfect spot for a romantic sunset.

2. Ano Mera

Ano Mera is a traditional village located in the center of the island, away from the hustle and bustle of the touristy areas. Here you can witness the traditional lifestyle of the locals and experience the Greek culture firsthand. The village offers various cafes, restaurants, and shops selling traditional Greek products.

3. Ftelia Beach

Ftelia Beach is a beautiful and secluded beach located on the northern coast of the island. It is known for its crystal clear waters, golden sand, and excellent windsurfing conditions. The beach is less crowded and less commercialized, making it an ideal spot for people who want to relax and get away from the crowds.

4. Armenistis Lighthouse

Armenistis Lighthouse is considered to be one of the most beautiful lighthouses of Greece. It is situated on the northwestern coast of the island and offers sweeping views of the Aegean Sea. The lighthouse was built in the 19th century, and it is surrounded by beautiful gardens.

5. Agios Sostis Beach

For those looking for a unique and tranquil experience, Agios Sostis Beach is the perfect destination. This peaceful beach is situated on the northern coast of the island, and it is known for its beautiful turquoise waters and golden sands. The beach is accessible on foot via a hiking trail or by car.

6. The Folklore Museum

The Folklore Museum is located in Mykonos Town and is considered to be one of the most important cultural attractions on the island. The museum showcases the traditional way of life of the locals, and it is housed in an old two-story house.

7. Panagia Paraportiani

Panagia Paraportiani is a famous church situated in Mykonos Town. This unique church is made up of five smaller churches and is considered to be a masterpiece of Cycladic architecture. The church is open to the public and is a must-visit for those interested in culture and history.

8. The Windmills

The Windmills are one of the most iconic landmarks of Mykonos, located on a hill overlooking Mykonos Town. These windmills were once used to mill wheat and are now preserved as a symbol of traditional architecture.

9. Delos Island

Delos Island is another hidden gem located close to Mykonos. It is an uninhabited island, and it is said to be the birthplace of the Greek God Apollo. The island is home to ancient ruins and is a UNESCO World Heritage site. Visitors can take a boat trip from Mykonos to Delos and explore the ancient ruins.

10. Psarou Beach

Psarou Beach is a popular beach located on the southern coast of the island. Known for its crystal clear waters, golden sand, and beach clubs, this beach is a hub for celebrities and VIPs. If you want to experience the high-end lifestyle of Mykonos, this beach is a must-visit.

FAQs Section:

Q. What is the best time to visit Mykonos? A. The best time to visit Mykonos is from mid-May to September, as the weather is perfect for beach activities and outdoor events.

Q. Is Mykonos expensive? A. Mykonos is known to be an expensive destination, however, there are ways to save money, such as staying in a hotel or Airbnb away from the popular areas and dining at local tavernas.

Q. How do I get around in Mykonos? A. The best way to get around Mykonos is by car or ATV, which can be rented from various locations on the island. Taxis, buses, and cabs are also available.

Q. What should I pack for Mykonos? A. Pack plenty of sunscreen, a hat, swimwear, comfortable walking shoes, and light clothing, as the weather can get quite hot.

Q. Are the beaches in Mykonos crowded? A. Yes, the popular beaches can get crowded, especially during peak season. However, there are plenty of secluded and hidden beaches that are less crowded.
