[ad_1] Why Lefkada Should Be Your Next Island Destination

Lefkada, also known as Lefkas, is a Greek island located in the Ionian Sea, just off the west coast of mainland Greece. It is a hidden gem that offers everything a traveler could want from an island: majestic beaches, crystal-clear waters, rich history, and mouth-watering cuisine. Despite its beauty, Lefkada is not as well-known as other Greek islands, which makes it the perfect destination for those looking for an authentic and less crowded Greek experience. In this article, we’ll explore why Lefkada should be your next island destination and what it has to offer.

Lefkada is easy to reach

One of the best things about Lefkada is that it is easily accessible. You can either fly directly to Aktion Airport, located on the mainland, or take a ferry from nearby islands such as Corfu, Kefalonia, and Zakynthos. Once you arrive, you can rent a car or use public transportation to explore the island.

Lefkada has some of the most beautiful beaches in Greece

Lefkada is home to some of the most stunning beaches in Greece. The island boasts over 40 beaches, each with its own unique charm. Porto Katsiki, considered one of the top beaches in Greece, is a must-visit. It is located on the southwest coast of the island and features turquoise waters, white sand, and steep cliffs. Egremni Beach, another popular beach in Lefkada, offers a long stretch of white sand and is famous for its crystal-clear waters.

Lefkada has a rich history and culture

Lefkada has a rich history that dates back to ancient times. The island was originally inhabited by the Corinthians before becoming a part of the Roman Empire. In the Middle Ages, it was ruled by the Byzantines and later by the Venetians. Today, you can still see remnants of these civilizations scattered throughout the island. The town of Lefkada, the island’s capital, features a Venetian castle and several churches dating back to the 17th and 18th centuries.

Lefkada offers delicious cuisine

Greek cuisine is known for being fresh, healthy, and flavorful, and Lefkada is no exception. The island offers a variety of delicious dishes, from seafood to meat, as well as vegetarian options. Some of the must-try dishes include moussaka, grilled octopus, and tzatziki. Also, the island is known for its wines, particularly the Robola wine, which is produced locally.

Lefkada offers outdoor activities

If you’re an adventure seeker, Lefkada has plenty of outdoor activities to offer. You can try windsurfing, kitesurfing, and sailing at Vassiliki Beach or go hiking in the mountainous areas of the island. You can also rent a bike or a car and explore the scenic routes of the island.

FAQs about Lefkada

Q: What is the best time to visit Lefkada?

A: The best time to visit Lefkada is from May to September when the weather is warm and most of the tourist attractions are open. July and August are the busiest months, so if you’re looking for a quieter experience, consider visiting in May or September.

Q: Do I need a car to explore Lefkada?

A: While it is possible to explore Lefkada using public transportation, it is recommended to rent a car to get around the island more easily. This will give you the freedom to explore the off-the-beaten-path locations and remote beaches that are not accessible by public transportation.

Q: Is Lefkada expensive?

A: Lefkada is less expensive than other popular Greek islands such as Santorini and Mykonos. However, prices may vary depending on the season and the location of your accommodation and dining choices.

Q: Are there any festivals in Lefkada?

A: Yes, Lefkada hosts several festivals throughout the year. The most famous festival is the International Folklore Festival, which takes place in August and features traditional music and dancing from different countries.
