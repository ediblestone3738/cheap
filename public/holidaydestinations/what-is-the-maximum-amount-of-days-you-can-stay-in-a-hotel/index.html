What is the Maximum Amount of Days You Can Stay in a Hotel?

Hotels and motels are a great way to stay when you’re travelling. Whether you’re on vacation or business, you can find a hotel or motel that fits your needs. But how long can you stay in a hotel or motel? The answer depends on the hotel’s policy.

Hotel Policies on Maximum Stay

Under hotel policy, guests at a residential hotel may stay at the hotel for months or years at a time so long as they leave their unit for 24 hours every 28 days. This is to avoid the guest becoming a permanent resident. California hotels and motels believe that by kicking out a guest for 24 hours before the guest stays for 30 consecutive days, they avoid the guest becoming a permanent resident.
