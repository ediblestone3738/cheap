Top 10 Tourist Attractions located in Kefalonia

1. Myrtos Beach – A must-visit beach located in the north of Kefalonia, known for its intense blue watercolor and white pebble coastline. A great place to relax and admire the view.

2. Fiscardo Village – This little fishing village is one of the most photographed spots in the Ionian Islands. Its picturesque harbor is home to a variety of colorful shops, cafes, and restaurants.

3. Melissani Cave – One of Kefalonia’s most significant natural attractions, Melissani Cave is an underground lake with sparkling blue-green waters that shine with the sunlight entering through the cave’s roof.

4. Assos Village – A small village in northwestern Kefalonia with colorful little houses by the sea. A tranquil spot to enjoy traditional Greek cuisine.

5. Saint Gerasimos Church – A religious icon in Kefalonia, this church is dedicated to Saint Gerasimos, the island’s patron saint. The church houses his remains in a silver casket and is venerated by many believers.

6. Mount Ainos – The highest mountain in Kefalonia that offers stunning views from its summit, including panoramic views of the Ionian Sea and the neighboring islands.

7. Agia Efimia Village – A bustling harbor village located in the east of Kefalonia that offers a variety of local shops, cafes, and restaurants.

8. Skala Village – A picturesque village on the south-east coast of Kefalonia that offers stunning views of the sea and the surrounding hills, as well as a pristine long beach.

9. Lighthouse of Fiscardo – The Fiscardo Lighthouse offers amazing views from its top and is a place to witness the island’s sunset.

10. Xi Beach – A beach in the south of Kefalonia with reddish sand and crystal-clear water. It’s a perfect spot for swimming and sunbathing.

FAQs

1. How do I get to Kefalonia?

Kefalonia International Airport is the main entry point for the island with direct flights from several major European cities. Alternatively, you can take a ferry from the nearby island of Lefkada or the mainland Greek port of Patras.

2. Are there services to rent a car in Kefalonia?

Yes, a wide range of car rentals is available on the island, from economy models to luxury cars. You can book your rental online or through a travel agent.

3. What is the best time to visit Kefalonia?

The best time to visit Kefalonia is from May to October. The weather is warm and sunny during this period, with pleasant sea temperatures for swimming and water sports.

4. Is there a hospital or clinic on the island?

Kefalonia has a modern health center in Argostoli, as well as a number of private clinics in the cities and towns on the island.

5. Are credit cards accepted in Kefalonia?

Yes, credit cards are widely accepted in restaurants, shops, and tourist attractions, although it’s always best to carry some cash as well.

Whether you choose to soak up some sun at Myrtos Beach, explore the Melissani Cave, or enjoy traditional Greek cuisine in the picturesque Fiscardo village, Kefalonia is a destination that will leave you feeling relaxed, refreshed, and rejuvenated.
