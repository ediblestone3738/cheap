Introduction to Castel Nuovo

Castel Nuovo, also known as the Maschio Angioino, is a majestic medieval castle located in the heart of Naples, Italy. Built-in 1279 by Charles I of Anjou and rebuilt many times over the centuries, Castel Nuovo has been an essential landmark of Naples for nearly 800 years.

Designed with five cylindrical towers, each over 30 meters tall, the castle dominates the city skyline with its imposing presence. It was constructed with a mixture of Gothic and Renaissance architectural styles. Elaborate decorations and intricate carvings make it a masterpiece of medieval design. Castel Nuovo features a magnificent triumphal arch built-in 1470, commissioned by King Ferdinand I of Naples.

Today, the castle houses the Civic Museum, where visitors can explore the rich history of Naples through its collection of art, sculptures, and artifacts. Castel Nuovo is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in medieval history and architecture.

History and Architecture of Castel Nuovo

Castel Nuovo, also known as Maschio Angioino, is a medieval castle in Naples, Italy. It has played a crucial role in shaping the city’s history and architecture. Built-in 1279 by Charles I of Anjou, the castle has undergone several renovations and expansions over the centuries.

The castle’s architecture is a blend of Gothic, Renaissance, and Baroque styles, with impressive features such as an ornamental marble arch, a triumphal arch, and a grand staircase leading to a courtyard decorated with frescoes and statues. Castel Nuovo has also served as the residence of various rulers, including Charles III of Spain and Vittorio Emanuele III of Italy. Today, it is a designated museum that houses precious artifacts and works of art from the Angevin, Aragonese, and Bourbon periods. Visiting Castel Nuovo is an opportunity to immerse oneself in the rich history and art of Naples. Castel Nuovo also has a rich history of traditions. One of the most notable is the " egg miracle," which takes place on the first Sunday of May. According to the tradition, a fresco of the Virgin Mary in the castle’s chapel is said to have shed tears and caused an egg to hatch miraculously. Popular Attractions at Castel Nuovo
