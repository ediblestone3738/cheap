Why is it Called Motel?

When you hear the word “motel”, you likely think of a roadside hotel that provides travelers with a place to rest for the night. But did you know that the term “motel” originated with the Motel Inn of San Luis Obispo, originally called the Milestone Mo-Tel? This motel was constructed in 1925 by Arthur Heineman, and its name was the result of a clever abbreviation.

The History of the Motel

The Motel Inn of San Luis Obispo was the first motel ever built. It was constructed in 1925 by Arthur Heineman, who wanted to provide travelers with a place to rest for the night. Heineman originally wanted to call his hotel the “Milestone Motor Hotel”, but he quickly realized that the words wouldn’t fit on the rooftop. So, he abbreviated “motor hotel” to “mo-tel”, and the term “motel” was born.

Additionally, many motels offer amenities such as swimming pools, laundry facilities, and free breakfast.
