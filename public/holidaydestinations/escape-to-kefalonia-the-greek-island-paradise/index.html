Kefalonia, Greece’s largest Ionian Island, offers the perfect combination of natural beauty, cultural significance, and modern amenities. It’s an island that showcases the true essence of Greece- crystal clear waters, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality. Kefalonia may be a hidden gem, but its breathtaking views and cultural richness undoubtedly deserve to be explored.

Getting There

The easiest way to get to Kefalonia is by air. Kefalonia International Airport is located just outside the island’s capital, Argostoli, and offers flights from major European cities. Alternatively, visitors can take a ferry from the nearby islands, such as Zakynthos, Corfu, or Italy, to the island’s port.

The Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Kefalonia is from May to June or from September to October. During this time, the temperatures are mild, and the island is less crowded, allowing visitors to enjoy the island’s beauty in peace.

Where to Stay

Kefalonia offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury resorts to more budget-friendly hotels and apartments. The Island’s most popular resorts are located in the southern part of the island, such as Lassi, Skala, or Katelios. However, visitors can also find hidden gems in the island’s northern part, such as Assos or Fiskardo.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions

1. Myrtos Beach Known as one of the most photographed beaches in Greece, Myrtos Beach features turquoise waters, white pebbles, and dramatic cliffs, a picturesque combination that makes it a must-see on the island.

2. Melissani Cave The Melissani Cave is a natural wonder that displays the natural beauty of the island of Kefalonia. Inside the cave, one can admire the crystal-clear waters, whilst taking a boat ride through the cave’s tunnels.

3. Assos The charming village of Assos is located on a peninsula, at the end of a winding road offering a breathtaking view of the picturesque village and the Ionian Sea.

4. Argostoli The lively capital of Kefalonia, Argostoli, offers a range of restaurants, bars, and shopping options. Visitors can also watch caretta-caretta turtles swimming by the Argostoli harbor.

5. Fiskardo The colorful village of Fiskardo is a famous attraction amongst tourists for its lively atmosphere and traditional architecture. It is considered to be the most well-preserved Venetian port in Greece.

6. Lixouri The island’s second city, Lixouri, is located on the western side of Kefalonia and is a place that holds historical and cultural significance due to Napoleon Bonaparte’s brief stay on the island.

7. Petani Beach Nestled between the steep cliffs, Petani Beach offers breathtaking sea views and magical sunsets.

8. Mount Enos Mount Enos is the highest mountain in Kefalonia, and it’s located on the southern part of the island. It’s a paradise for nature lovers and hikers who can enjoy a walk in the oak forest’s peaceful surroundings.

9. Drogarati Cave The Drogarati cave is another wonder of nature located on the island of Kefalonia. It has an impressive height of 95 meters and is thousands of years old.

10. Argostoli Lagoon The Argostoli Lagoon is a saltwater lake located on the outskirts of Argostoli. It’s a natural habitat for various bird species, including flamingos.

FAQs

Q: What currency is used in Kefalonia? A: Kefalonia uses the Euro.

Q: Is it safe to swim in Kefalonia’s waters? A: Yes, Kefalonia’s waters are generally safe to swim in. However, visitors should be cautious of strong winds and currents.

Q: What is the dress code in Kefalonia? A: Kefalonia is a laid-back destination, and visitors can dress casually during the day. However, modest clothing is required for entering churches or religious sites.

Q: What is the island’s cuisine? A: Kefalonia’s cuisine is heavily influenced by Greek and Mediterranean flavors, including seafood, grilled meats, and locally sourced fruits and vegetables.

Q: Do I need a visa to visit Kefalonia? A: Visitors who are citizens of EU countries or the Schengen area do not need a visa to visit Kefalonia. Travelers from other countries should check the visa requirements before traveling.

In conclusion, Kefalonia is a Greek island paradise that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and culture. Whether visitors are looking for a relaxing beach vacation or a hike through the island’s wilderness, there is something for everyone. With its stunning landscapes, rich culture, and excellent cuisine, Kefalonia is a destination that should not be missed. [ad_2]
