[ad_1] You Won’t Believe These Greece Resorts Exist

When one thinks of Greece, images of ancient ruins, blue-topped churches, and the sparkling Mediterranean sea come to mind. However, Greece offers so much more than just history and stunning vistas. Hidden amidst the islands and coastline, there are resorts that will take your breath away. From the luxurious to the unique, here are some of the Greece resorts you never knew existed.

1. Santorini Suites

Santorini Suites is not your typical resort. The suites are located in a cave in the heart of Santorini. The design details include exposed rock and authentic furnishings. The resort offers a stunning view of the Caldera, with its turquoise waters and charming villages. The resort boasts a private pool with a jacuzzi, making it perfect for honeymooners or a romantic getaway.

2. Evia Silence

Evia Silence is located in a remote area of Greece and is just what you need to unplug from the demands of the modern world. The resort is surrounded by pine forests and the stylish rooms are decorated with natural materials. The resort boasts a 20-meter outdoor pool and guests have access to yoga and meditation classes. Take a stroll through the gardens and rediscover your connection with nature.

3. Cretan Malia Park

Cretan Malia Park is a family-friendly resort located on the Island of Crete. The resort is surrounded by verdant gardens and is a stone’s throw from the beach. The hotel’s design is reminiscent of a traditional Cretan village, echoing the island’s long and rich history. The resort offers numerous amenities, including an on-site spa and kid’s club.

4. Domes Noruz Chania

Located on the island of Crete, Domes Noruz Chania is an adult-only resort that caters to modern tastes. The resort prides itself on its 1950s-inspired design and offers spectacular views of the Aegean Sea. The resort boasts four swimming pools, a beach club, and a rooftop bar. The hotel has a private chapel, making it perfect for intimate weddings or vow renewals.

5. Porto Zante Villas and Spa

Porto Zante Villas and Spa is a five-star resort that is located in Zakynthos. The resort boasts luxury villas, designed with intense attention to detail. The villas have private infinity pools and stunning sea views. The resort also has an on-site spa where guests can indulge in massages and other treatments.

6. Blue Palace, a Luxury Collection Resort & Spa, Crete

Blue Palace is a tranquil retreat that is located on Crete’s east coast. The resort has 142 guest rooms, each with a private balcony or terrace with sea or garden views. The resort has five outdoor pools, including a child-friendly pool, a spa, and a range of sports facilities such as tennis, basketball, and mini-golf. The resort boasts a private beach and is perfect for those looking for a peaceful escape.

7. Mykonos Blu Grecotel Exclusive Resort

Mykonos Blu is located on one of Greece’s most popular islands, Mykonos. The resort is picturesque, with white-washed walls and beautiful blue accents. Each suite or villa comes with a private pool or hot tub and beautiful sea or garden views. The resort also boasts a world-class spa, a fitness center, and a variety of water sports.

8. Aqua Blu Boutique Hotel + Spa

Aqua Blu is located on the island of Rhodes and is a unique combination of a boutique hotel and luxurious spa. The resort’s design takes its cue from the historical island’s architecture and boasts a beautiful garden oasis. Each suite has a private pool or hot tub and the interiors are chic and modern. The resort also has an excellent restaurant, where guests can sample local Greek cuisine.

9. Amanzoe

Amanzoe is located in the eastern Peloponnese Peninsula and is a luxurious retreat set among olive groves and overlooking the Aegean Sea. Each villa has a private pool, garden and sun terrace, and the resort boasts five restaurants, a beach club and an excellent spa. With breathtaking views and world-class amenities, Amanzoe is an idyllic resort.

10. Ikos Oceania

Ikos Oceania is located in the picturesque Halkidiki peninsula and is a family-friendly resort with a range of activities and entertainment for all ages. The resort boasts several outdoor pools, a private beach, a children’s club and a range of sports facilities. The rooms are elegantly decorated, and many feature stunning sea views. The resort also has a spa and several restaurants.

FAQs

1. What is the best time to visit Greece?

The best time to visit Greece is between April and October. The weather is warm, and flights and accommodations are readily available. However, beware of peak season, which is July and August when the country sees the most tourists.

2. Do resorts in Greece have private beaches?

Many Greece resorts have private beaches. However, this should be confirmed at the time of booking to avoid disappointments.

3. What are Greece’s top tourist attractions?

Greece’s top tourist attractions include the Acropolis, the ancient city of Delphi, the island of Santorini, the historic city of Thessaloniki, and the Meteora monasteries.

4. Do resorts in Greece offer all-inclusive packages?

Yes, some resorts in Greece offer all-inclusive packages that include meals, drinks, and other amenities.

5. Are children allowed in adult-only resorts in Greece?

No, children are not allowed in adult-only resorts in Greece, and it is advisable to confirm the hotel policies before booking.
