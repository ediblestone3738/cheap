Current Situation in Syria

The Syrian opposition is a loosely organized and diverse group of political and military factions dedicated to eliminating the regime of President Bashar al-Assad. Since the start of the Syrian civil war in 2011, the opposition has been fighting against the Syrian government and its allies, including Russia and Iran.

In March 2021, the Biden administration announced that it was increasing support for the Syrian opposition by providing aid and assistance worth $10 million. This came after years of limited engagement with the opposition under the previous administration.

The US has also been working with Turkey, which hosts a large number of Syrian refugees, to create a safe zone within Syria’s borders. This zone would be a sanctuary for the opposition to operate and would also offer a haven for Syrian civilians. The ultimate goal of these efforts is to create a stable and democratic Syria that respects human rights and offers a better future for its citizens.

What Does It Mean for Travelers?

The strengthening of ties between the US and the Syrian opposition offers an opportunity for travelers to explore one of the oldest continuously inhabited countries in the world. Syria boasts a rich cultural heritage that spans thousands of years, from the ancient city of Damascus to the Roman ruins of Palmyra.

There are several challenges to traveling to Syria, including the ongoing civil war and the COVID-19 pandemic. However, with careful planning and preparation, visitors can have a wonderful and meaningful experience in this unique country.

FAQs

Q: Is it safe to travel to Syria?

A: The US Department of State currently advises against all travel to Syria due to the ongoing civil war and the risk of terrorism. Travelers who decide to visit Syria should take the necessary precautions, including obtaining a reliable local guide, staying up to date on current events, and avoiding areas where violence has occurred.

Q: What are some of the top tourist attractions in Syria?

A: There are many sites of historical and cultural significance in Syria, including the citadel in Aleppo, the Umayyad Mosque in Damascus, and the ancient ruins at Palmyra. Visitors can also explore the vibrant markets, sample local cuisine, and learn about the country’s rich history and culture.

Q: What is the best time to visit Syria?

A: The best time to visit Syria is in the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Summers can be oppressively hot, while winters can be cold and wet.
