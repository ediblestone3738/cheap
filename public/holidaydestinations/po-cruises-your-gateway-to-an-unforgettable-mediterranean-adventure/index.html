What is P&O Cruises?

P&O Cruises is a British-American cruise line that has been operating since the early 19th century. The company specializes in Mediterranean cruises but also offers trips to destinations throughout the world. With over 200 years of experience, P&O Cruises knows how to provide travelers with the ultimate cruising experience. From the ship’s décor to the food on board, every detail has been meticulously planned to provide a luxurious and unforgettable journey.

2. Wide Range of Activities: From swimming pools and fitness centers to theaters and casinos, P&O Cruises offers a wide range of activities for travelers to enjoy throughout their journey.

What Can You Expect on a P&O Cruises Mediterranean Journey?

Once aboard a P&O Cruises ship, travelers will enjoy world-class amenities providing luxury, comfort and relaxation. What Are the Top 10 Tourist Attractions Located in P&O Cruises’ Mediterranean Adventures?

1. The Acropolis in Athens, Greece: Travelers can take a tour to explore the iconic ruins of the ancient Greek city of Athens. Visitors can learn about the culture and history of Greece as they’re transported back in time to experience the Acropolis’ grand temples and monuments.

2. The Colosseum in Rome, Italy: One of the most significant historical landmarks in the world, The Colosseum is a must-see attraction for anyone exploring the Mediterranean’s treasures. Visitors can explore the amphitheater’s underground tunnels and imagine the ancient gladiatorial battles that took place there.

3. The Blue Grotto in Capri, Italy: Guests can board a boat to view the famous Blue Grotto, a natural sea cave that glimmers in the sunlight to create a stunning blue glow.

4. The Rock of Gibraltar: The strategic rock played a significant role in the history of British and Spanish empires in the Mediterranean Sea since ancient times. Visitors can take a guided tour through the tunnel system that once housed a military garrison.

5. The Palace of Knossos in Crete, Greece: Travelers can discover the Palace of Knossos, one of the most significant archaeological sites from the Minoan Civilization. Ancient frescoes and the palace’s incredible multiskeletal structure make for an awe-inspiring experience.

6. The Leaning Tower of Pisa in Pisa, Italy: A visit to Pisa is not complete without seeing the Leaning Tower. The landmark leans at a gravity-defying angle and is one of the world’s most iconic landmarks.

7. The Amalfi Coastline: A picturesque stretch of coastline in southern Italy, this region features crystal clear waters, small towns, and lively markets. Visitors can experience traditional Italian culture and fascinating modern day life in a rich and quaint seafaring environment.

8. Dubrovnik, Croatia: Affectionately called “the Pearl of the Adriatic,” Dubrovnik is a magnificent walled city famous for its beauty and cultural significance. Cruise guests can explore the city’s Renaissance architecture and historic landmarks like the Dubrovnik Cathedral and the Old City Walls.

9. Cannes, France: A bustling seaside town in the south of France, Cannes is famous for its luxurious hotels and yacht lifestyle. Visitors can take a stroll down the famous Boulevard de la Croisette or visit the city’s modern art museum.

10. Barcelona, Spain: A city that combines classic Gothic architecture with a modern, vibrant culture, Barcelona is a must-visit destination for travelers. Guests can explore Gaudi’s fantastic creations like the Park Guell, La Sagrada Familia, and Casa Batllo, or relax in the city’s chic bars and restaurants.

FAQs

1. How long do P&O Cruises typically last?

P&O Cruises offer an extensive range of itinerary lengths, usually ranging from seven to fourteen nights. Some special cruises may last longer.

2. What should I pack for my P&O Cruise?

Remember to pack comfortable clothing, footwear, and swimwear, depending on your cruise itinerary. You should also bring along sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat.

3. Can I take part in shore excursions even if I don’t speak the local language?

Yes! All shore excursions are led in English, and local guides will be able to communicate with participants who do not speak the local language.

4. What is the minimum cruising age allowed by P&O Cruises?

The minimum age for P&O Cruises is 6 months. However, for the sake of general welfare, some specific itineraries may require children to be older.

5. Are meals included in P&O Cruises?

Yes! P&O Cruises provide for all meals, snacks, and non-alcoholic drinks. Some restaurants are charged extra.
