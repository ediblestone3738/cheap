Denver's Growing Arts Scene

Mar 31, 2023

[ad_1] Denver has long been known for its stunning mountain views and exceptional outdoor activities. However, in recent years, the city has become a hot spot for those seeking out a vibrant arts scene. From street art to contemporary museums, Denver’s arts scene is on the rise and captivating audiences from around the world.

The Mile High City’s growing arts scene has given rise to an explosion of creative energy that can be seen in every corner of the city. With new galleries and exhibits opening frequently, Denver has proven itself to be a city that is dedicated to the arts.

In this travel article, we will explore Denver’s growing arts scene, including the top 10 tourist attractions within it. We will also answer the most frequently asked questions about the city’s arts scene.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Denver’s Growing Arts Scene

1. Denver Art Museum - The Denver Art Museum (DAM) boasts an impressive collection of over 70,000 works of art from around the world. The museum has nine permanent collections that include art from Asia, Africa, Europe, and Native America.

2. Museum of Contemporary Art Denver - This museum has quickly become the center of Denver’s burgeoning contemporary art scene. Visitors can expect to see bold and varied exhibitions showcasing local, national, and international artists.

3. Street Art in RiNo - The River North Art District (RiNo) is home to some of the most vibrant street art in the country. Located in the heart of Denver, RiNo is teeming with colorful murals and installations.

4. Clyfford Still Museum - This museum is dedicated entirely to the works of abstract expressionist painter Clyfford Still. The museum’s collection comprises over 95% of Still’s lifetime output of paintings, sculptures, and works on paper.

5. Red Rocks Park and Amphitheatre - Red Rocks is a natural amphitheater located just outside of Denver. The venue has hosted some of the world’s most famous musicians and provides a breathtaking view of the surrounding mountains.

6. Denver Botanic Gardens - These stunning gardens are nestled in the heart of Denver and boast a dazzling array of plants from around the world. Visitors can take guided tours, participate in plant sales, and attend special events throughout the year.

7. Kirkland Museum of Fine & Decorative Art - This museum showcases Colorado artists and designers from the 19th century to the present day. The museum is housed in a stunning building designed by architect Jim Olson.

8. Denver Center for the Performing Arts - The Denver Center for the Performing Arts is the largest performing arts center in the region. Visitors can enjoy plays, musicals, concerts, and other live performances in one of the center’s numerous intimate theaters.

9. First Friday Art Walks - On the first Friday of every month, galleries and studios throughout Denver open their doors to the public for a night of art and culture. The event is free to attend and a great way to experience Denver’s vibrant arts scene.

10. Denver Museum of Nature & Science - This museum is a great place to learn about the natural history of Colorado and the surrounding region. Visitors can explore exhibits on everything from dinosaurs to space travel.

Denver’s Growing Arts Scene - FAQs

Q: What is the best time of year to visit Denver’s arts scene? A: Denver’s arts scene is lively year-round, but the summer months (June – August) tend to be the most active. During this time, there are countless festivals and events taking place throughout the city.

Q: What is the River North (RiNo) Art District? A: The River North Art District is a neighborhood just north of downtown Denver that has become a hub for artists and creatives. The area is known for its street art and galleries, as well as its breweries, restaurants, and shops.

Q: Is the Denver Art Museum free to visit? A: No, admission to the Denver Art Museum is ticketed. However, the museum does offer free admission on the first Saturday of every month.

Q: What is the Clyfford Still Museum? A: The Clyfford Still Museum is a museum dedicated entirely to the works of the abstract expressionist painter Clyfford Still. The museum boasts over 95% of Still’s lifetime output of paintings, sculptures, and works on paper.

Q: What is the Denver Center for the Performing Arts? A: The Denver Center for the Performing Arts is the largest performing arts center in the region. It houses ten performance spaces, including theaters, concert halls, and studio spaces.

In conclusion, Denver’s growing arts scene has become a premier destination for art lovers from around the world. From world-class museums to vibrant street art, Denver offers something for everyone.
