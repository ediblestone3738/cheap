A Guide to the Best of Denmark's Attractions

Mar 14, 2023

[ad_1] Denmark is a small country that packs a punch in terms of attractions and tourist hotspots. From the picturesque countryside to the colorful harbor towns, Denmark has something for everyone. In this article, we will guide you through what we believe to be the best of Denmark’s attractions.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Denmark

1. Tivoli Gardens - Located in the heart of Copenhagen, Tivoli Gardens is Denmark’s most famous amusement park. Opened in 1843, the park has since grown to include over 25 rides and attractions, beautiful gardens, and world-renowned restaurants.

2. Legoland - A children’s paradise, Legoland opened in Billund in 1968, and now boasts over 60 rides and attractions.

3. The Little Mermaid - A statue based on the Hans Christian Andersen fairytale, the Little Mermaid is one of Copenhagen’s most iconic landmarks.

4. Kronborg Castle - Located in the town of Helsingor, Kronborg Castle is famous for being the setting for William Shakespeare’s “Hamlet”.

5. Nyhavn - A harbor town in Copenhagen, Nyhavn is known for its brightly colored buildings, lively bars, and restaurants.

6. Viking Ship Museum - Located in Roskilde, the Viking Ship Museum is home to five original Viking ships and offers insight into Denmark’s Viking past.

7. National Museum of Denmark - Located in Copenhagen, the National Museum of Denmark boasts over 2 million artifacts detailing Denmark’s history and culture.

8. The Round Tower - Located at the heart of Copenhagen, the Round Tower is a tower built in the 17th century, and provides stunning panoramic views of the city.

9. Egeskov Castle - A classic Renaissance castle located on the island of Funen, Egeskov Castle features beautiful gardens and an extensive art collection.

10. Ribe - Denmark’s oldest town, Ribe is full of winding cobbled streets, charming houses, and rich historical architecture.

A Guide to the Best of Denmark’s Attractions

In addition to the top 10 tourist attractions mentioned above, here are some more of Denmark’s must-see places:

Copenhagen - Denmark’s capital city and one of the most famous Scandinavian cities, Copenhagen is an absolute must-see. Beyond the Little Mermaid and Tivoli Gardens, Copenhagen is home to a plethora of cultural experiences such as Christiania (an alternate living community in the heart of the city), the Design Museum (showcasing Danish design at its best) and the Royal Palace.

Aarhus - Known for its many museums, Aarhus is the second-largest city in Denmark. The highlight of Aarhus is the ARoS Museum of Art, which is a modern, vibrant museum with many exhibitions. Additionally, the city has a thriving nightlife and is home to many great restaurants.

Odense - The birthplace of Hans Christian Andersen, this charming city is a must-visit for literature lovers. The museum dedicated to Andersen is impressive and provides insight into the life and works of the much-beloved creator of The Little Mermaid, The Ugly Duckling, and The Emperor’s New Clothes.

Skagen - Located at the northernmost point of Denmark, Skagen is a picturesque fishing village with a long history of attracting artists and writers. Skagen is renowned for its unique light, which has inspired many painters in the past.

Bornholm - Located in the Baltic Sea, Bornholm is a beautiful island famous for its beaches, picturesque villages, and distinct churches. The island is also a popular center for glass blowing and pottery, and it has several galleries and museums showcasing local crafts.

FAQs

Q: What is the currency in Denmark? A: The currency in Denmark is the Danish Krone.

Q: How can I get around Denmark? A: Denmark has a well-developed public transportation system, which includes trains, buses, and ferries. Additionally, cycling is a popular way to get around, as Denmark is very bike-friendly.

Q: When is the best time to visit Denmark? A: The best time to visit Denmark is during its mild summer months, between June and August.

Q: What is Danish cuisine like? A: Danish cuisine is a mix of traditional dishes such as smorrebrod, open-faced sandwiches, and modern cuisine. Danish cuisine is known for its focus on simple, quality ingredients, and fresh seafood.

Q: Is Denmark expensive to travel to? A: Denmark can be expensive to travel to, particularly in major cities such as Copenhagen. However, there are plenty of options for budget-conscious travelers, including hostels and affordable restaurants.

Conclusion

Denmark has a wide range of attractions and experiences that make it an ideal holiday destination. Whether you prefer to experience the bustling cities or the peaceful countryside, Denmark has it all. The attractions listed in this article provide excellent insight into what Denmark has to offer, and we encourage travelers of all ages and interests to explore this beautiful country. [ad_2]
