Mumbai, the capital city of Maharashtra, is considered India’s economic hub, but there’s much more to explore than just its financial district. The city is a melting pot of cultures and traditions, creating a unique culinary scene that combines Indian, Persian and international flavors. Join us on a journey to discover the best of Mumbai’s cuisine, from street food to high-end restaurants.

Street Food Delights

One of the best ways to experience Mumbai’s cuisine is through its vibrant street food scene. Known for its lip-smacking and affordable local street food, you can spend an entire day wandering the city and trying out different street snacks.

One such famous street food is Vada Pav, a Mumbai specialty, a popular Indian burger made with a deep-fried potato patty, sandwiched between a bun with chutney and spices.

Another must-try is Pav Bhaji, a delicious mix of mashed vegetables, spices, and butter, served with warm bread rolls. The dish is usually made on a big iron griddle known as tawa, which gives it a unique smoky flavor.

Seafood Specialties

Mumbai’s proximity to the sea makes it a hot spot for seafood lovers. The city is known for its delicious seafood specialties like Bombay duck (fish), Prawns Koliwada, and Fish Thali.

Make your way to one of the many seafood shacks or local restaurants, where you’ll find freshly caught seafood dishes cooked to perfection. Try out the spicy Pomfret Fry, a shallow fried fish coated with chili and turmeric powder or the tangy Fish Curry made from freshly caught fish and spices.

Authentic Indian Cuisine

Mumbai boasts of some of the finest Indian restaurants, each offering a unique culinary experience. From North Indian cuisine to South Indian cuisine, the city has a wide range of options to explore.

For those who love kebabs, try out Bademiya, an open-air restaurant that started out as a street food shack. Their Chicken Malai Tikka and Mutton Seekh Kebab are to die for!

Another must-visit is Britannia and Co, an iconic Parsi restaurant known for its signature dishes like Berry Pulao, Sali Boti, and Dhansak. The restaurant has been serving the locals since 1923 and is considered a food landmark in Mumbai.

International Flavors

Mumbai’s cosmopolitan culture has made it a hot spot for global cuisine. From Italian and Japanese to Lebanese and Chinese, the city has it all.

Try out the famous Lebanese restaurant, Maroush, for their Hummus and Shawarma or head over to Hakkasan for their dim sum and Cantonese dishes.

High-End Restaurants

If you’re looking for a fine dining experience, Mumbai has no shortage of high-end restaurants. The city is home to some of the finest restaurants in the country, each offering a unique culinary journey.

One such restaurant is Wasabi by Morimoto at the Taj Mahal Palace Hotel, which offers a unique blend of Japanese and Indian cuisine. Try out their Spicy Tuna Roll or the Black Cod Miso, which is considered a signature dish.

FAQs:

Q. What is Mumbai famous for when it comes to cuisine? A. Mumbai is famous for its street food, seafood, and authentic Indian cuisine.

Q. Where can I find the best street food in Mumbai? A. Head over to Mohammed Ali Road, Juhu Beach, or Crawford Market for some of the best street food in Mumbai.

Q. What are the must-try seafood dishes in Mumbai? A. Some must-try seafood dishes in Mumbai include Bombay duck (fish), Prawns Koliwada, and Fish Thali.
