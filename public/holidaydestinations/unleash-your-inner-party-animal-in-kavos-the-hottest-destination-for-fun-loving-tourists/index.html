Kavos, the party capital of Corfu, has been a popular summer vacation spot for young and adventurous tourists for several years now. The beach is beautiful and the nightclubs are always open, offering a wide range of music and entertainment options. Therefore, it’s no surprise that Kavos attracts thousands of partygoers every year, making it one of the most exciting destinations in Greece.

Kavos is a small village situated on the southern coast of Corfu, in the Ionian Sea. It’s only 40 kilometers away from the Corfu International Airport, taking you around 40 minutes. The beach in Kavos is scenic and stretches for several kilometers. The water is turquoise blue and transparent, making it a perfect place for swimming and sunbathing. If you’re planning to visit Kavos during summer, you can be sure that it’ll be hot and sunny all day long. The temperature usually ranges between 25-35°C, so make sure to bring enough sunscreen to protect your skin.

FAQs

Q: What’s the legal age to drink in Kavos?

A: The legal drinking age in Greece is 18 years old. Therefore, you must be 18 or above to buy and consume alcohol in Kavos.

Q: What’s the dress code for the nightclubs in Kavos?

A: The dress code in Kavos nightclubs is quite casual. Wear something comfortable and light, as it can get hot and humid on the dance floor.

Q: Is Kavos safe?

A: Kavos is generally a safe place. However, like any other busy tourist destination, it’s important to be careful and watch out for pickpockets and scammers.

Q: How can I get to Kavos?

A: You can get to Kavos by flying to Corfu International Airport and then taking a taxi or bus to Kavos. There are also ferry services operating from the Greek mainland and Italy.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Kavos

1. Kavos Beach

One of the main attractions of Kavos is its beautiful beach. It’s over 3 kilometers long, with golden sands and crystal-clear waters. Whether you want to take a dip, sunbathe or simply relax, Kavos Beach has plenty to offer.

2. Water Sports

If you’re an adventure seeker, Kavos has many water sports activities available. You can try jet-skiing, parasailing, banana boat riding or rent a paddleboard and explore the coastline on your own.

3. Kavos Nightlife

Kavos has a reputation of being a party town, offering an exciting nightlife experience. Many bars and nightclubs are located here, each with its own vibe, playing different genres of music.

4. Kavos Strip

The Kavos strip is an area of the town where most of the bars and nightclubs are located. It’s a lively and busy area, open until the early hours of the morning.

5. Island Hopping

Corfu is surrounded by many small islands, which can be visited on a day trip. Paxos, Antipaxos or the Diapontia Islands, each offers its own unique charm and beauty.

6. Corfu Town

Corfu Town is a world heritage site and offers a rich cultural experience. It has a mixture of Venetian, French and British architectural styles, with its old town well preserved.

7. Aqualand Water Park

Aqualand Water Park is a popular attraction for families and offers a range of water activities for all ages. The park is set in an area of natural beauty, surrounded by green hills and trees.

8. Corfu Donkey Sanctuary

The Corfu Donkey Sanctuary is a charitable organization that provides care for abandoned, injured and mistreated donkeys. You can visit and interact with them, and learn more about this amazing animal.

9. Agios Gordios Beach

Agios Gordios is a stunning beach located on the west coast of Corfu. It’s surrounded by lush greenery and cliffs and offers a relaxing and peaceful environment.

10. Paleokastritsa

Paleokastritsa is a village located on the north-west coast of Corfu. It’s a scenic and historic town, with stunning views of the coastline and the cliffs. You can visit the Paleokastritsa Monastery, which is dedicated to the Virgin Mary.
