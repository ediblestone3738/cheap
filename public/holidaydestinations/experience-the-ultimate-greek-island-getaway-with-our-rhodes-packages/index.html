FAQs (Frequently Asked Questions)

1. What is the best time to visit Rhodes? Rhodes is an all-year-round destination, but the best time to visit is between May and October. During these months, the weather is warm, and the water is perfect for swimming.

2. Can I use my credit card in Rhodes? Yes, most shops, restaurants, and hotels in Rhodes accept credit cards. However, it’s always wise to carry some cash.

3. What are the most popular beaches in Rhodes? Rhodes offers numerous white sand beaches, the most popular among which include Faliraki Beach, Tsambika Beach, and Lindos Beach.

4. What should I wear in Rhodes? Rhodes is warm during the summer months, so it’s advisable to pack light clothing such as shorts, dresses, and t-shirts. It’s also advisable to bring a hat, sunglasses, and sunscreen.

5. What is the local currency in Rhodes? The local currency in Rhodes is the Euro.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Rhodes

1. The Palace of the Grand Master of the Knights of Rhodes: This 14th-century palace built by the Knights of St. John houses a museum with exhibits that showcase the island’s rich history.

2. Lindos Acropolis: The Acropolis of Lindos is an incredibly well-preserved ancient Greek architectural marvel that boasts a magnificent fortress and a stunning view of the Aegean Sea.

3. Archaeological Museum of Rhodes: This museum houses a vast collection of ancient artifacts dating back to ancient Greece and Byzantine times.

4. The Valley of Butterflies: Located in the Western part of the island, the Valley of Butterflies is a lush green site where hundreds of thousands of butterflies gather each year.

5. Ancient Kamiros: An important archaeological site from the 5th century BC, Kamiros features impressively preserved ruins from the Temple of Athena Kameiros.

6. Rhodes Old Town: The medieval town of Rhodes is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that features notable historic structures, narrow alleyways known as “Kalderimia,” and local markets where you can purchase souvenirs.

7. Anthony Quinn Bay: One of the most striking bays in Rhodes is the Anthony Quinn Bay, located in the north-eastern corner of the island.

8. Rhodian House: The Rhodian House, located in the old town, gives a glimpse of life on Rhodes from the 16th to the 19th centuries.

9. Seven Springs: In the center of Rhodes, the Seven Springs is a scenic natural water spring that provides a cool retreat from the heat of the sun.

10. Tsambika Monastery: A 15th-century Byzantine hilltop monastery located at the foot of Tsambika Mountain, it offers panoramic views of the island.
