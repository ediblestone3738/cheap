Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Rome and Pompeii

1. The Colosseum: The iconic amphitheater is a must-visit in Rome. This ancient landmark is a symbol of Rome’s power and prowess back in the day.

2. The Roman Forum: The ruins of the Roman Forum evoke the glory days of the Roman Empire, giving visitors a glimpse of Rome’s impressive history.

3. Vatican City: The smallest independent country in the world, Vatican City is home to some of the world’s most impressive artwork, including St. Peter’s Basilica and the Sistine Chapel.

4. The Pantheon: This imposing temple has been around for over 2,000 years and is known for its impressive dome.

5. Pompeii Archaeological Site: The ruins of Pompeii are incredibly well preserved despite being destroyed by a volcanic eruption in 79 AD.

6. Herculaneum Archaeological Site: Located near Pompeii, Herculaneum is another ancient city completely destroyed by the eruption of Mount Vesuvius.

7. Capri Island: A must-visit for nature lovers, Capri is an island off the coast of Naples that’s known for its stunning landscapes and turquoise waters.

8. Amalfi Coast: A UNESCO world heritage site, the Amalfi Coast features stunning towns that wind along the cliffside and overlook the sea.

9. Trevi Fountain: Rome’s famous landmark is a popular destination, known for its stunning Baroque design and the legend of throwing a coin over your shoulder for good luck.

10. The Spanish Steps: Another iconic landmark in Rome, the Spanish Steps are famous for their elegant design, grandeur, and sweeping views of the city.
