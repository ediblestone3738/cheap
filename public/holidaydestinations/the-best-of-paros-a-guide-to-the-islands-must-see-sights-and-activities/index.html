The Best of Paros: A Guide to the Island's Must-See Sights and Activities

Apr 21, 2023

[ad_1] Nestled in the heart of the Cyclades, the beautiful island of Paros is a true gem of the Aegean Sea. With its crystal-clear waters, sandy beaches, and charming white-washed villages, Paros has something to offer for every kind of traveller. Whether you’re seeking a romantic getaway, a family vacation or an adventure-packed trip, Paros has it all. Read on for our guide to the best of Paros, including must-see sights and activities.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Paros

1. Naoussa Village – This charming village is located on the northern coast of the island and is perfect for a romantic stroll. Wander through the picturesque narrow streets, browse local shops, and enjoy a glass of wine in one of the many charming tavernas.

2. Parikia Town – The main town of Paros, Parikia is a bustling hub of activity. Discover ancient ruins, traditional architecture, and delicious local cuisine.

3. Golden Beach – This stunning stretch of golden sand is a must-visit for beach lovers. With crystal-clear waters and a relaxed vibe, it’s the perfect spot for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

4. Lefkes Village – Sitting on the slopes of a mountain, Lefkes is one of the most picturesque villages on the island. Enjoy scenic walks, traditional Greek architecture, and stunning views.

5. Kolimbithres Beach – This unique beach is known for its peculiar-shaped rocks that emerge from the sea. With crystal-clear water, it’s a great spot for swimming and snorkelling.

6. Antiparos – Just a short ferry ride away, this small and charming island is worth a visit. Wander through the quaint alleys, visit the famous Cave of Antiparos, or relax on the sandy beaches.

7. Paros Park – This natural park boasts stunning views, walking trails, and even a small ancient theatre. It’s the perfect spot for nature lovers and those interested in history and archaeology.

8. Panagia Ekatontapiliani – This impressive Byzantine church is located in Parikia and is believed to date back to the 4th century. With stunning mosaics and intricate architecture, it’s a must-visit for history buffs.

9. Punda Beach Club – For those seeking a bit of nightlife, Punda Beach Club is the place to be. With a great vibe, live music, and party atmosphere, it’s the perfect spot for dancing the night away.

10. Windsurfing and kitesurfing – Thanks to the strong winds that blow in Paros, the island is renowned for its windsurfing and kitesurfing scene. Head to Chrisi Akti beach or Pounda beach for some adrenaline-fuelled water sports.

Must-See Sights and Activities in Paros

1. Discover the island’s beaches – Paros is home to some of the most stunning beaches in Greece, with crystal-clear water and soft golden sand. Spend your days lazing in the sun, swimming, and enjoying water sports.

2. Visit traditional villages – Paros is home to many charming villages, each with their own unique character. Wander through the narrow streets, admire the traditional architecture, and sample local cuisine.

3. Enjoy water sports – With its strong winds, Paros is the perfect destination for windsurfing, kitesurfing and other water sports. Try your hand at surfing, paddleboarding, or jet skiing for an adrenaline-packed adventure.

4. Explore ancient ruins – Paros is home to many ancient ruins, including the Parikia Archaeological Museum and the Ancient Quarries. Discover the island’s fascinating history and architecture.

5. Take a day trip to nearby islands – Paros is perfectly located for day trips to nearby islands such as Antiparos, Mykonos, and Santorini. Discover new landscapes, culture, and traditions.

FAQs about Paros

1. How do I get to Paros?

Paros is easily accessible by ferry from the mainland port of Piraeus, as well as other nearby islands such as Mykonos and Santorini. You can also fly to Paros airport from Athens and other major cities.

2. What’s the best time to visit Paros?

The best time to visit Paros is from late May until early October, when the weather is warm and sunny. July and August can be very busy, so plan accordingly.

3. What’s the currency in Paros?

The currency in Paros is the euro.

4. Is English spoken in Paros?

English is widely spoken in Paros, especially in tourist areas.

5. What’s the food like in Paros?

Paros is known for its delicious local cuisine, which includes fresh seafood, traditional meze (small dishes), and locally produced wine and beer.

6. Are there any special rules or customs to be aware of in Paros?

As with most places in Greece, it’s important to dress modestly when visiting churches or other religious sites. It’s also customary to say “kalimera” (good morning), “kalispera” (good evening) and “efharisto” (thank you) when interacting with locals.

Conclusion

Paros offers an incredible variety of sights and activities, making it a destination that offers something for everyone. From stunning beaches to ancient ruins, traditional villages to thrilling water sports, Paros is a true Greek paradise that should be on every traveller’s bucket list.
