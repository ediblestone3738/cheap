Overview of Micronesia

Micronesia is located in the western Pacific Ocean, northeast of Papua New Guinea. It consists of 4 main island groups which are the Federated States of Micronesia, Kiribati, Marshall Islands, and Palau. Micronesia has a rich diversity of flora and fauna, which includes swaying palm trees, tropical fruit trees, and a variety of marine life. The people of Micronesia have direct connections with the ocean, and their traditional living and culture are strongly linked to the sea.

What to Expect When Traveling to Micronesia

Micronesia is a tropical paradise with a tropical climate, so bring lots of sunscreen, and be prepared for lots of time on the water! Traveling around the islands is mostly via a small plane or boat. Domestic airlines operate inter-island flights, and most islands have minimal paved roads, so you need to be prepared to walk. Micronesia caters well to tourists and is relatively easy to navigate, with plenty of English speakers available who will be able to help you out with directions and local customs.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions Located in Micronesia

1. Chuuk Lagoon - This is a diving spot and home to shipwrecks dating back to WW2.

2. The Blue Hole - A popular diving spot off the coast of Palau.

3. Pohnpei Island - The greenest island in Micronesia, with hiking trails that lead to stunning waterfalls.

4. Nan Madol - A set of ruins that sprawls over 92 artificial islands linked by canals on the island of Pohnpei.

5. Yap Island - Where the stone money is located. The stone currency is a type of currency made from giant solid-limestone coins that weigh up to 4 tons.

6. Gaferut Island - A coral island teeming with sea life, and a perfect place for snorkeling.

7. Palau - World-renowned diving spot and famous for its unique jellyfish lake and the rock islands.

8. Northern Mariana Islands Saipan and Tinian - Historically significant and offers a chance to reflect on World War II history.

9. Kosrae Island - Micronesia’s best-kept secret, with Mangroves and deep caves.

10. Blue Lagoon - This is a lagoon located in Chuuk and is home to a variety of marine life.

FAQs

1. What is the best time to visit Micronesia? The best time to visit is during the dry season, from November to May.

2. What is the currency in Micronesia? The US dollar is the official currency of Micronesia.

3. Is there a visa required for traveling to Micronesia? No. Tourist visas are not required for stays under 30 days.

4. What is the official language of Micronesia? The official language of Micronesia is English.

5. Are there any particular customs to be aware of when traveling to Micronesia? It is customary to remove your shoes before entering a home or business. Also, women should dress modestly.
