Explore the Best Kept Secrets of the Greek Isles

Mar 30, 2023

[ad_1] The Greek Isles have always been a popular travel destination, with stunning beaches, delicious food, and rich history. However, there are hidden gems amongst the islands that are not as well-known to tourists. In this article, we will explore the best kept secrets of the Greek Isles and provide a list of the top 10 tourist attractions.

1. Hydra Located in the Saronic Gulf, Hydra is a small and peaceful island with no cars allowed on the island. Visitors must rely on donkeys or their own two feet to get around, adding to the charm of the island. This island is perfect for those seeking a quiet getaway and it offers breathtaking scenery and crystal clear waters.

2. Naxos Naxos is the largest island in the Cyclades and is often overshadowed by more popular islands such as Santorini and Mykonos. However, Naxos offers its own unique experience, with charming villages and pristine beaches. Plus, it is one of the few islands that is still flourishing with agriculture, meaning that visitors can indulge in locally grown produce.

3. Symi Symi is a picturesque island located near Rhodes and is famous for its colorful neoclassical houses. It offers a relaxed atmosphere and stunning beaches, making it the perfect place to unwind and soak up the sun.

4. Astypalea Astypalea is a small and peaceful island located between the Cyclades and Dodecanese archipelagos. It is known for its whitewashed buildings and cobblestone streets, giving visitors a taste of traditional Greek architecture. The island is also home to beautiful beaches and crystal-clear waters.

5. Amorgos Amorgos is a rugged and unspoiled island located in the Cyclades. The island is dotted with dramatic cliffs and unspoiled beaches, making it a haven for nature lovers. The island is also home to the iconic Monastery of Hozoviotissa, which is perched on a cliff overlooking the Aegean Sea.

6. Ikaria Ikaria is a remote island located in the Eastern Aegean Sea that is known for the longevity of its residents. The island’s relaxed pace of life and stress-free lifestyle contribute to the health of its residents. Visitors can experience this healthy lifestyle by indulging in fresh food, thermal hot springs, and scenic hiking trails.

7. Ithaca Ithaca is a small island and the mythical home of Odysseus in Homer’s Odyssey. The island is full of history and natural beauty, with charming villages and peaceful beaches. It is the perfect place to escape the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

8. Folegandros Folegandros is a tranquil island in the Cyclades that has retained its traditional character. Visitors can explore quaint streets and traditional tavernas, followed by a swim in the crystal-clear waters that surround the island.

9. Serifos Serifos is an island that is often overlooked but should not be missed. It is known for its beautiful sandy beaches and dramatic cliffs. The island is also home to the picturesque village of Chora, which is perched on a hill with stunning views of the Aegean Sea.

10. Anafi Anafi is a small and unspoiled island located near Santorini. The island is perfect for those seeking a quiet escape, with beautiful beaches and crystal clear waters. Visitors can also explore the island’s history by visiting the archaeological site of Ancient Anafi.

FAQs:

1. What is the best time to visit the Greek Isles? The best time to visit the Greek Isles is from May to September, with the peak season being from June to August. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, making it perfect for swimming and sunbathing.

2. Is it safe to travel to the Greek Isles? Yes, the Greek Isles are generally safe for tourists. However, like any travel destination, it is always important to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions to ensure your safety.

3. What is the currency in Greece? The currency in Greece is the Euro.

4. Do I need a visa to travel to Greece? Citizens of the EU and the USA do not need a visa to travel to Greece for stays up to 90 days. However, visitors from other countries should check with their embassy or consulate to see if they need a visa.

5. What is the cuisine like in the Greek Isles? The cuisine in the Greek Isles is known for its fresh ingredients and Mediterranean flavors. Popular dishes include moussaka, souvlaki, and Greek salad.
