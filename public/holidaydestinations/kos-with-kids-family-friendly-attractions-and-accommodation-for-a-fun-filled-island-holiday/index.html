Kos with Kids: Family-friendly Attractions and Accommodation for a Fun-filled Island Holiday

Apr 11, 2023

[ad_1] Kos, one of Greece’s Dodecanese islands, is a perfect destination for a family holiday. With its beautiful beaches, family-friendly attractions, and kid-friendly accommodation options, your whole family is likely to have a great time. In this article, we will explore the top 10 tourist attractions located in Kos with kids and provide answers to some of the most frequently asked questions about this island.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Kos with Kids

1. Lido Water Park: This water park is an absolute must-visit for families looking to cool off and have some fun in the sun. With over 20 slides and water attractions, it’s the perfect place to spend an entire day with the whole family.

2. Asklepion: The Asklepion is an ancient medical center located on the island. This site is an excellent place to visit with older kids as it has historical significance and includes a temple dedicated to Hippocrates, the father of modern medicine.

3. Hippocrates Tree: Speaking of Hippocrates, the oldest tree on the island bears his name. Located in the center of Kos Town, the Hippocrates Tree is a must-visit for any family looking to learn about the island’s rich history.

4. Aquatica Water Park: This smaller water park is perfect for families with younger children. With a wide range of pools and water slides, it’s a great place to spend a relaxing day in the sun.

5. Plaka Forest: Want a break from the beach? The Plaka Forest is a beautiful green oasis on the island. It’s perfect for a family hike or picnic and is an excellent way to take a break from the hustle and bustle of the town.

6. Nautical Museum of Kos: Another great way to explore Kos’s rich history is at the Nautical Museum of Kos. This museum has exhibits on shipping, sailing, and fishing on the island and is a fun and educational experience for the whole family.

7. Casa Romana: The Casa Romana dates back to the 3rd century BC and is a fantastic example of Roman architecture. The site is well-preserved and includes beautiful mosaics and murals that the whole family will enjoy.

8. Plaka Beach: Plaka Beach is perfect for families looking for a quieter, more secluded beach experience. Located on the eastern coast of the island, it’s an excellent place to spend a day lounging in the sun or playing in the water.

9. Kos Town Castle: The castle in Kos Town is situated in the heart of the city and was built by the Knights of St. John. The castle has a fascinating history and is a great place to visit for families interested in medieval history.

10. Paradise Beach: This aptly named beach is one of the most popular on the island. It’s perfect for families looking for a lively, social beach experience and has plenty of amenities, including beach bars and restaurants.

FAQs about Kos with Kids

1. What’s the best way to get around Kos with kids?

There are a variety of transportation options available on the island, including rental cars, scooters, and bicycles. However, if you’re traveling with young children, it might be best to opt for a private transfer, taxi, or bus to ensure their safety.

2. Is it safe to swim in the sea in Kos?

Yes, the waters around Kos are generally safe for swimming, but it’s always wise to exercise caution, especially if your kids are not strong swimmers. Stick to designated swimming areas and avoid swimming in choppy or rough conditions.

3. Is it easy to find kid-friendly accommodation on the island?

Yes, there are several hotels and resorts on the island that cater specifically to families with children. Look for hotels with amenities like kids’ clubs, playgrounds, and family-sized rooms.

4. What’s the best time of year to visit Kos with kids?

The most popular time to visit Kos is during the summer months when the weather is consistently warm and sunny. However, the crowds can be overwhelming, so consider visiting in the shoulder season (May or September) for a more relaxed experience.

5. Are there any food options for picky eaters on the island?

Yes, there are plenty of restaurants and cafes on Kos that cater to picky eaters. Look for restaurants that serve classic Greek dishes like souvlaki, gyros, and moussaka, as well as international options like pizza and pasta.

In Conclusion

Kos is a great destination for families with kids, with plenty of family-friendly attractions and accommodation options. From water parks and ancient ruins to secluded beaches and green forests, there’s something for everyone on this beautiful Greek island. Whether you’re looking for a relaxed beach holiday or an action-packed adventure, Kos is sure to deliver a fun-filled experience that your whole family will remember for years to come. [ad_2]
