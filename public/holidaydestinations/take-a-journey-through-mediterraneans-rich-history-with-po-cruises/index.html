Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the best time to take a Mediterranean Cruise?

A: The best time to take a Mediterranean cruise is between April and November. This is because the weather is beautiful, sunny, and warm. You can escape for a fun-filled vacation when you need it, especially during the winter months when there are fewer daylight hours.

Q: Are there any restrictions when visiting religious sites in the Mediterranean?

A: Yes, several religious sites have dress codes that visitors must follow. For instance, in Italy, the Vatican Museum, St. Peter’s Basilica, and the Sistine Chapel, visitors must cover their legs and shoulders. Thus, it is best to dress appropriately to respect their culture and traditions.

Q: What documents do I need to sail on a Mediterranean Cruise?

A: To travel on a Mediterranean cruise, you must possess a valid passport or another approved travel document. Passengers who require a visa must have a passport valid for at least six months after the intended return date.

Q: What language do I need to speak on a Mediterranean cruise vacation?

A: English is the primary language spoken on board. A tour guide, who speaks the local language and English, is usually provided during shore excursions. Therefore, you do not have to worry about communication issues.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in the Mediterranean

1. Athens, Greece

Athens, the capital city of Greece, is a popular destination for Mediterranean cruises. It has impressive ancient monuments such as the Acropolis, Parthenon, and Temple of Olympian Zeus. You can also explore the city’s modern architecture, markets, and enjoy local cuisine.

2. Venice, Italy

Venice is one of the most visited cities in the Mediterranean. It is famous for its romantic atmosphere, narrow streets, canals, and beautiful buildings. The city offers a range of attractions, including St. Mark’s Basilica, the Venice Biennale, and the Grand Canal.

3. Dubrovnik, Croatia

Dubrovnik is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Adriatic Sea. Its old town boasts several historic structures, including the magnificent City Walls, Church of St. Blaise, and the Rector’s Palace. Dubrovnik’s stunning sunset views are also a must-see.

4. Barcelona, Spain

Barcelona is a vibrant city that is home to several UNESCO sites, including the Park Guell and the Sagrada Familia. The city is also known for its delicious food, lovely beaches, and vibrant nightlife.

5. Monte Carlo, Monaco

Monte Carlo lies on the Mediterranean coastline and is known for its beautiful architecture, stunning harbor, and the Monte-Carlo Casino. Monte Carlo is a beautiful city and has plenty of beautiful things to explore, including the Prince’s Palace and the Marina.

6. Valletta, Malta

Valletta is Malta’s capital city and has beautiful architecture and a rich history. It is home to several UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including the Upper Barrakka Gardens and St. John’s Co-Cathedral.

7. Mykonos, Greece

Mykonos is an idyllic island located in the Aegean Sea. It has picturesque windmills, beautiful beaches, and a lively nightlife. You can explore the island’s ancient tourism sites or relax on the beach.

8. Cairo, Egypt

Cairo is home to several historic attractions, including the magnificent Pyramids of Giza, Sphinx, and the Egyptian Museum. Cairo is a must-see destination for travelers interested in ancient civilizations.

9. Marseille, France

Marseille, a charming port city, is known for its beautiful sandy beaches, museums, and delicious seafood. Visitors can see the iconic Notre-Dame de la Garde, Palais Longchamp, and the Vieux-Port (Old Port).

10. Santorini, Greece

Santorini is an island in the southern Aegean Sea with natural beauty and traditional architecture. The island is home to ancient archeological sites, beaches, and postcard-worthy sunsets.
