Greece’s Top Holiday Spots: From Picturesque Villages to Bustling Cities.

Apr 30, 2023

[ad_1] Greece is a country that lies in the southeastern part of Europe. It’s famed for its ancient history, sizzling cuisine, mesmerizing islands, and majestic beaches. Greece boasts of over 6,000 islands set in the Aegean and Ionian Seas, however, only 227 of the islands are inhabited. The destination is home to some of the world’s most iconic attractions, such as the Parthenon in Athens, Santorini, and Meteora. Greece is always among the top tourist destinations around the world. Here’s a guide through the top holiday spots in Greece, from picturesque villages to bustling cities.

Santorini

Santorini is known for its stunning views of the Mediterranean as well as the islands dotted around it. The island has a history that dates back over 3,500 years, but today, it is more famous for its white-washed buildings with blue-domed roofs, luxury resort hotels, and boutique shops. The island is located in the southern Aegean Sea, about 200 km from the Greek mainland, and it has some of the most stunning sunsets in the world.

Mykonos

Mykonos is a party island, famous for its cosmopolitan atmosphere and vibrant nightlife. The island has stunning beaches, crystal clear waters, and whitewashed buildings. The town of Mykonos is the island’s heart, with its iconic windmills and picture-perfect cobblestone streets. Mykonos also has world-class dining and shopping, perfect for those who enjoy the best things in life.

Athens

Athens is the capital and largest city of Greece. It is one of the world’s oldest cities, with a history that dates back over 3,400 years. The city’s historic landmarks, such as the Acropolis, the Parthenon, and the Temple of Olympian Zeus, attract millions of visitors every year. Athens has a sophisticated nightlife, art scene, and a wide range of culinary experiences, making it an ideal destination for tourists seeking a perfect blend of history, culture, and modern-day pleasures.

Crete

Crete is the largest Greek island, located in the southern Aegean Sea. It is a top holiday spot, popular for its tranquil atmosphere, white sandy beaches, and breathtaking landscapes. It is said that Crete houses some of the most beautiful beaches in the Mediterranean. Visitors can explore the magnificence of the Minoan civilization, a true treasure of archeological sites. Crete boasts a rich cultural heritage, with unique customs, lively and hearty cuisine, and colorful folkloric traditions.

Rhodes

Rhodes is one of the largest Greek islands, located in the eastern Aegean Sea. Rhodes is famous for its long history, the walled city of Rhodes is a UNESCO World Heritage site, and its unique blend of European and Turkish cultures. The island’s architecture, with its medieval palaces, castles, and buildings, is unique and interesting. The beaches of Rhodes await you with crystal clear waters making it an ideal spot for a relaxing beach holiday.

Skiathos

Skiathos is a small yet beautiful island located in the southwest Aegean Sea. It is a perfect holiday destination featuring sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and lush green hills covered with pine and oak trees. The island is ideal for sunbathing, enjoying the sea, and relaxing under the sun. This is coupled by the vibrant nightlife that Skiathos has to offer.

FAQs

Q1) When is the best time to visit Greece?

The best time to visit Greece is during the spring from April to June, and the fall period from September to November. During these periods, the weather is warm but not too hot, the crowds are small, and prices are more affordable.

Q2) Are the islands in Greece expensive?

Greece is a relatively affordable tourist destination. Island prices for food and accommodation are generally low compared to other destinations. However, prices vary. High-end tourist spots like Santorini and Mykonos tend to have more expensive prices compared to other islands.

Q3) Do I need a visa to visit Greece?

For stays of less than 90 days within a six-month period, citizens of the European Union and the European Economic Area do not require a visa to visit Greece. Travelers from other countries should check visa requirements before booking their trip.
