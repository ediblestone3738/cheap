From Beaches to Ancient Ruins: The Top 10 Must-Visit Spots in Greece

Apr 2, 2023

[ad_1] Greece, a country that’s rich in history and culture, has been a favorite tourist destination for centuries. Visitors from all around the world flock to this beautiful Mediterranean country to soak in its warm sunshine, visit its world-renowned ancient ruins, and explore its countless beaches. In this article, we’ll take a look at the top 10 must-visit spots in Greece that you simply can’t afford to miss.

1. Athens

Athens, the capital of Greece, is a city that’s steeped in history and culture. It’s home to some of the world’s most famous ancient landmarks, including the Acropolis and the Parthenon. It’s also a bustling metropolis, with plenty of modern amenities and a vibrant nightlife.

2. Crete

Crete is the largest island in Greece and is known for its crystal-clear waters, sandy beaches, and rugged terrain. It’s a scenic paradise that’s perfect for beachgoers, hikers, and history buffs alike. Some of the island’s most famous landmarks include the Venetian Harbor in Chania, the Palace of Knossos, and the Samaria Gorge.

3. Santorini

Santorini, with its stunning sunsets and white-washed buildings perched on the cliffs above the Aegean Sea, is one of Greece’s most iconic destinations. This volcanic island boasts excellent wine, delicious cuisine and luxurious accommodation, it’s the perfect destination for a romantic getaway.

4. Mykonos

Mykonos, known for its chic nightlife, stunning beaches, and iconic windmills, is a must-visit destination for party-goers and beach lovers. In this island, you’ll find plenty of beach clubs, water sports, trendy bars, and restaurants that cater to all tastes.

5. Delphi

Delphi was the site of the ancient Greek Oracle, and it’s easy to see why this place was considered to hold such mystical powers. The ruins of the Temple of Apollo and the Amphitheatre mark this site, and it is beautifully located next to a mountain.

6. Meteora

Meteora, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is home to several ancient Orthodox monasteries that are perched precariously atop towering rock formations. These fascinating monasteries offer an incredible glimpse into the history and spirituality of the region, and the stunning views are sure to take your breath away.

7. Rhodes

Rhodes is an island in the eastern Aegean Sea, and it’s known for its medieval Old Town and stunning beaches. Visit the Palace of the Grand Master of the Knights of Rhodes, stroll along the quaint Rhodes Old Town alleyways, or swim and bask in the sun at one of the island’s pristine beaches.

8. Olympia

The ancient site of Olympia offers a fantastic insight into the origins of the Olympic games. Located in the western Peloponnese peninsula, the site is in stunning countryside and offers a fantastic insight into the ancient games that founded the modern-day Olympics.

9. Naxos

Naxos is an idyllic island that offers plenty of walking trails, pristine beaches, and fascinating ancient ruins. Its whitewashed towns, charming seaside villages, and traditional tavernas are enchanting, and you’ll find plenty of places to relax and unwind on this stunning island.

10. Hydra

Hydra is a picturesque island that’s car-free and offers an unforgettable getaway from the hustle and bustle of modern city life. The island’s narrow streets and stone houses seem frozen in time, and the picturesque harbor offers the perfect backdrop for painting, writing or relaxation.

FAQs

Q: What is the best time to visit Greece? A: The best time to visit Greece is during the peak travel season, which runs from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you prefer fewer crowds and milder temperatures, you can visit in spring from mid-March to mid-June or autumn from mid-September to mid-November.

Q: Do I need a visa to visit Greece? A: If you are an EU citizen, you do not need a visa to visit Greece. If you are from the USA or Canada, you do not need a visa to visit Greece for less than 90 days. However, if you are from another country, you may need to apply for a visa before traveling to Greece. You can check the requirements on the website of the Greek embassy in your country.

Q: What are the best beaches in Greece? A: Greece is home to some of the most beautiful beaches in the world. Some of the best ones include Myrtos in Kefalonia, Elafonissi in Crete, Navagio in Zakynthos, and Sarakiniko in Milos.

Q: Is the tap water safe to drink in Greece? A: The tap water in Greece is generally safe to drink, but it’s recommended to drink bottled water instead, especially when visiting the islands.
