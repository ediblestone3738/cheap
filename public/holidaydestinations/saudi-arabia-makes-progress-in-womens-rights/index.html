Saudi Arabia Makes Progress in Women's Rights

Apr 3, 2023

[ad_1] Saudi Arabia has long been known for its strict adherence to traditional forms of Islam, but recent years have seen significant progress in women’s rights. From the lifting of the ban on women driving to increased participation in politics and business, Saudi Arabia is making strides towards gender equality. This article will explore the progress being made for women’s rights in Saudi Arabia, as well as the top 10 tourist attractions in the country.

The Progress of Women’s Rights in Saudi Arabia

For decades, women in Saudi Arabia had limited rights and freedoms, from the wearing of the abaya (a long, black cloak) to strict gender segregation. However, in recent years, the country has taken significant steps towards granting greater freedoms and rights to women.

Driving

One of the most significant changes came in 2018 when the driving ban on women was lifted. Prior to this, women in Saudi Arabia were not allowed to drive and had to rely on male relatives or paid drivers for transportation. At the time, the lifting of the ban was seen as a major shift in the country’s attitude towards women’s rights.

Participation in Politics and Business

Another area where women’s rights have been expanding in Saudi Arabia is in politics and business. In 2015, women were allowed to run and vote in municipal elections, and in 2019, women were allowed to travel abroad without the permission of a male guardian. Additionally, the country is actively trying to increase the number of women in the workforce, with a goal of reaching 30% by 2030.

Education

Education is another key area where women’s rights have been expanding in Saudi Arabia. In recent years, more women have been enrolling in universities, and in 2019, women were allowed to study law and work in the field of family law. Additionally, the country has been investing in programs to support literacy and education for women, with a focus on improving access to education in rural areas.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Saudi Arabia

1. The Masmak Fortress

Located in Riyadh, the Masmak Fortress is a historic castle that played a key role in the unification of Saudi Arabia. Today, it is a popular tourist destination and features exhibits on the history of the country.

2. Al-Ula

Al-Ula is a desert oasis located in the northwestern part of Saudi Arabia. It is home to the ancient city of Hegra, which was once an important stop on the trade route between the Mediterranean and the Arabian Peninsula.

3. Madain Saleh

Located in Al-Ula, Madain Saleh is an ancient city that was once home to the Nabataean civilization. It is best known for its well-preserved tombs and rock-carved monuments.

4. Mount Uhud

Located in Medina, Mount Uhud is a mountain that played a significant role in the early history of Islam. It is also home to a number of historic sites, including the Prophet’s Mosque and the Quba Mosque.

5. Al-Balad

Located in Jeddah, Al-Balad is a historic district that features traditional architecture and narrow streets. It is also home to a number of ancient mosques and other historic sites.

6. Turaif District

Located in Riyadh, the Turaif District is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that features traditional mud-brick buildings and historic mosques.

7. The Red Sea Coast

The Red Sea Coast is a popular destination for tourists looking to relax on pristine beaches and enjoy water sports such as snorkeling and scuba diving.

8. The Edge of the World

Located just outside of Riyadh, the Edge of the World is a stunning rock formation that offers panoramic views of the surrounding desert.

9. Wadi Hanifa

Wadi Hanifa is a beautiful park located in Riyadh that features a network of hiking trails, lush greenery, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

10. King Fahd Cultural Centre

The King Fahd Cultural Centre in Riyadh is a modern complex that features a range of cultural activities, including concerts, theater performances, and art exhibits.

A: While women can travel alone in Saudi Arabia, it is important to be aware of local customs and laws. It may be safer and more comfortable for women to travel with a group or a local guide.
