Top 10 Tourist Attractions located in Discover Spain’s Hidden Gems: The Best Kept Secrets of Spanish Resorts:

1. Pals, Costa Brava

Pals is a picturesque town located on the Costa Brava, which boasts a beautiful medieval center with narrow streets, old cobblestones, and gorgeous arches. Visitors can explore the narrow streets and alleys, admire the stone houses with their characteristic balconies, and visit the town’s castle and church.

2. La Alpujarra, Granada

La Alpujarra is a stunning mountain range located in the Granada province. It is an area of outstanding natural beauty, where you can explore the many small villages and hamlets that are nestled in the valleys. Visitors can go hiking, enjoy the flora and fauna of the area, and taste the traditional regional cuisine.

3. Teruel, Aragon

Teruel is a historic city located in the Aragon region of Spain. It boasts a unique Mudéjar architecture, which is a blend of Islamic and Gothic styles. Visitors can explore the city’s cathedral, castle, and many museums, and get a real taste of Spanish history and culture.

4. Altea, Costa Blanca

Altea is a charming little town located on the Costa Blanca. It boasts a delightful old town full of cobbled streets, white-washed houses, and historic sites such as the Church of Our Lady of Consolation. Visitors can also explore the beaches, cliffs, and coves that surround the town.

5. Peniscola, Costa del Azahar

Peniscola is a beautiful seaside town located on the Costa del Azahar. It is home to a magnificent castle which was built by the Knights Templar in the 13th century. Visitors can also explore the town’s narrow streets, see the old town walls, and visit the many local markets and festivals.

6. Mijas, Costa del Sol

Mijas is a picturesque town located on the Costa del Sol. It boasts a charming old quarter, where visitors can explore the narrow winding streets, admire the white-washed houses with their brightly colored balconies, and enjoy the stunning views from the hilltops.

7. Cuenca, Castilla-La Mancha

Cuenca is a beautiful medieval city located in the Castilla-La Mancha region of Spain. It boasts a stunning old town, with many historic landmarks such as the Gothic cathedral and the Hanging Houses. Visitors can also explore the many museums and galleries, and enjoy the local cuisine and wine.

8. Ronda, Andalusia

Ronda is a picturesque town located in the Andalusia region of Spain. It is home to a famous bullring, which is considered to be one of the oldest and most beautiful in Spain. Visitors can also explore the town’s many museums, visit the stunning Tajo Gorge, or just enjoy the local flora and fauna.

9. Oviedo, Asturias

Oviedo is a historic city located in the Asturias region of Spain. It boasts a unique Romanesque architecture, charming old town, and a wealth of historical landmarks such as the Cathedral of San Salvador and the Palace of Camposagrado. Visitors can also enjoy the local festivals and try traditional Asturian cuisine.

10. Cadaques, Costa Brava

Cadaques is a picturesque town located on the Costa Brava. It is home to many small coves and beaches, which are perfect for swimming and water sports. Visitors can also explore the town’s unique white-washed houses framed by the deep blue sea and try local seafood delicacies.
