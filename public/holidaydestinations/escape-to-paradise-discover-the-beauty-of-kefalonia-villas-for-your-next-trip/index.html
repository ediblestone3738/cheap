FAQs

Q. What is the best time to visit Kefalonia?

A. The best time to visit Kefalonia is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny and the sea is perfect for swimming. However, if you prefer a quieter holiday, you may want to consider visiting in the spring or autumn when the crowds have gone and the island is calmer.

Q. How do I get to Kefalonia?

A. The easiest way to get to Kefalonia is by flying into the island’s international airport, which is located in the capital, Argostoli. You can also reach the island by ferry from other parts of Greece, such as Patras and Igoumenitsa.

Q. What is the currency in Kefalonia?

A. The currency in Kefalonia is the Euro.

Q. What language do they speak in Kefalonia?

A. The official language in Kefalonia is Greek, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Escape to Paradise: Discover the Beauty of Kefalonia Villas for Your Next Trip!

1. Myrtos Beach - Considered one of the most beautiful beaches in Greece, Myrtos Beach is a must-visit spot. The white sand, blue sea, and steep cliffs make for a stunning backdrop.

2. Melissani Cave - This impressive cave is filled with crystal-clear water and features a natural skylight which creates a beautiful effect cast upon the water’s surface.

3. Assos Village - A quaint fishing village tucked away in a scenic bay, this spot offers stunning views, stones streets and unique resting places in tavernas with local fresh seafood and ouzo, a traditional Greek drink.

4. Fiskardo Village - Another picturesque spot, Fiskardo Village, is well-known for its colorful houses and yachts crowding the harbour It is where you can experience Greek life, stroll over paves streets around the charming alleyways and little square while enjoying a glass of local wine.

5. Drogarati Cave - The magnificence of the stalactites and stalagmites in this cave will leave you in awe. The mineral-rich underground atmospheric rock formations inside Drogarati cave is a unique experience to adopt.

6. Lassi Beach - One of the most popular beaches in Kefalonia, Lassi is a beautiful spot to visit. With smooth and crystal-clear shallow waters, beautiful sand and scenic views, Lassi Beach is perfect for sunbathing and relaxing.

7. Argostoli Town - As the capital, it’s worth giving at least an afternoon in the splendid town of Argostoli, with the series of bars and cafes that provide excellent places for people-watching.

8. Katelios Beach - Located in a small fishing village, Katelios Beach is ideal for those looking for a peaceful spot. It’s known for its pristine waters and tasteful restaurants with fresh seafood.

9. Skala Village - Skala is Kefalonia’s largest tourist resort and a destination with a wide variety of restaurants, tavernas, and bars offering top-notch cuisines from Greek dishes, locally caught fish to international cuisine.

10. St. George’s Castle - Although in ruins, the Castle is set amidst exceptional natural beauty, surrounded by lush greenery, and offers a spectacular bird’s-eye view of the island.
