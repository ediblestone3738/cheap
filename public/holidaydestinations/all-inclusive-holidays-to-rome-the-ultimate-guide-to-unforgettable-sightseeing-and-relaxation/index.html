Top 10 Tourist Attractions in All-Inclusive Holidays to Rome: The Ultimate Guide to Unforgettable Sightseeing and Relaxation

1. The Colosseum: One of Rome’s most iconic tourist attractions, the Colosseum is a must-visit for anyone travelling to the city. This impressive amphitheatre was built in 70-80 AD for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles. A guided tour will give you an insight into its fascinating history.

2. The Vatican Museums: Home to some of the world’s most famous art pieces, including the Sistine Chapel and Michelangelo’s famous fresco, the Vatican Museums should be at the top of your list. This is one of the busiest attractions in Rome, so booking an all-inclusive tour is highly recommended to avoid long queues.

3. The Pantheon: This well-preserved ancient Roman temple, now a church, is a true architectural masterpiece. Dating back to 27 BC, the dome of the Pantheon is still one of the largest in the world.

4. The Roman Forum: The Roman Forum is a sprawling complex of ruins that was once the centre of political and social activity in ancient Rome. A visit to the Forum, located right next to the Colosseum, is a fascinating look into the city’s past.

5. The Trevi Fountain: The magical Trevi Fountain is one of Rome’s most iconic landmarks. The fountain’s baroque style and intricate sculptures make it a true work of art. It’s also tradition to throw a coin in the fountain, ensuring your return to the Eternal City.

6. The Spanish Steps: Another one of Rome’s most popular landmarks, the Spanish Steps offer a stunning view of the city. Be sure to visit the nearby Piazza del Popolo for even more breathtaking views.

7. The Catacombs of Rome: Located on the outskirts of the city, the Catacombs are ancient underground burial grounds. A guided tour will take you through the dark, narrow corridors and give you an insight into how the early Christians buried their dead.

8. The Castel Sant’Angelo: This impressive fortress was initially built as a mausoleum for Emperor Hadrian before being turned into a papal stronghold. Today, it’s a museum and a great place to get panoramic views of the city.

9. Trastevere: This neighbourhood, located on the west bank of the Tiber River, is a charming and picturesque area to explore. Known for its cobblestone streets, colourful buildings and lively nightlife, Trastevere is a great place to dine or have a drink with friends.

10. The Appian Way: This ancient road was one of Rome’s most important trade routes and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. A visit to the Appian Way, lined with historic ruins and churches, is a peaceful break from the busy city centre.
