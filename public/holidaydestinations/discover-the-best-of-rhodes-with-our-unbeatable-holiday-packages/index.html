Frequently Asked Questions about Rhodes:

1. What’s the best time to visit Rhodes? The peak season in Rhodes is between June and September, when the weather is hottest, and the crowds are biggest. If you prefer a quieter atmosphere, consider visiting during the off-season between October and May.

2. Can I bring my pets to Rhodes? Most hotels on the island don’t allow pets. However, if you’re traveling with your furry friends, you can find some hotels and apartments that are pet-friendly.

3. Is Rhodes safe? Rhodes is a safe destination for travelers. Like anywhere, tourists should be vigilant when exploring unfamiliar places, but the island is generally very safe.

4. What’s the currency used in Rhodes? The currency used in Rhodes (and throughout Greece) is the Euro. Most hotels, restaurants, and shops will accept major credit cards.

5. Do I need a visa to travel to Rhodes? If you’re traveling from an EU country, the US, Canada, Australia or New Zealand, you don’t need a visa to visit Rhodes for stays up to 90 days.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Rhodes

1. The Palace of the Grand Master of the Knights of Rhodes: This 14th-century castle is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Rhodes. The architecture is stunning, and there are a lot of exhibits about the history of Rhodes.

2. The Medieval Town of Rhodes: The old town of Rhodes is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is a must-visit destination. You can wander the narrow cobbled streets, see the castle walls and gates, and enjoy the countless souvenir shops.

3. Lindos: Lindos is a stunning coastal village located on the southeast coast of Rhodes. The preserved ancient city’s main attractions include the Acropolis of Lindos, and the St. Paul’s Beach and Bay.

4. Anthony Quinn Bay: Anthony Quinn Bay in Faliraki is one of the most famous beaches on the island. The clear blue waters are surrounded by stunning cliffs and coves.

5. Symi Island: Located about one hour away from Rhodes by ferry, Symi is a small and picturesque island with stunning beaches, turquoise waters, and colorful houses.

6. The Seven Springs: The Seven Springs, located about 30 minutes away from Rhodes, is a natural oasis of waterfalls and forests that provide shade from the summer heat.

7. The Valley of the Butterflies: A natural reserve, this valley is home to thousands of butterflies. The best time to visit is from June to September, when the butterflies hatch from their cocoons.

8. Ancient Kamiros: This archaeological site is home to the ancient city of Kamiros, which was once a powerful city-state. Today, it’s a picturesque vestige of the past.

9. Italian Architecture: Rhodes has tons of examples of Italian architecture from when the island was ruled by Italy in the early 1900s. The picturesque, colorful and art nouveau buildings can be found all over the city.

10. Prassonissi Beach: This is a famous beach located in the southern tip of Rhodes, famous for being windsurfers’ paradise, surfing and kite surfing are famous sports in this place.
