Zante, also known as Zakynthos, is the third largest of the Ionian Islands, located off the west coast of Greece. This island paradise is known for its beautiful sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and scenic coastline. With its relaxed atmosphere and natural beauty, Zante is the perfect destination for anyone looking for an unforgettable holiday experience.

If you’re planning a trip to Zante in 2023 or beyond, here’s everything you need to know.

Getting There

The easiest way to reach Zante is by plane, with direct flights from a number of European cities including London, Amsterdam, Berlin, and Brussels. The flight time from London is approximately 3 hours and 20 minutes. Alternatively, you can take a ferry from the mainland port of Kyllini, which takes around one hour and 15 minutes.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Zante is from late May to early October, when the weather is warm and sunny. July and August are the busiest months, with temperatures averaging around 30Â°C. If you prefer a quieter atmosphere, consider visiting in May or September.

Where to Stay

Zante has a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. If you’re looking for a more peaceful retreat, head to the quieter areas of Vassilikos or Keri. For lively nightlife, stay in Laganas or Tsilivi.

FAQs

Q: Is Zante safe for tourists? A: Yes, Zante is generally a safe destination for tourists. As with any location, it’s important to take precautions such as following basic safety guidelines and keeping an eye on your belongings.

Q: What currency is used in Zante? A: The currency used in Zante is the Euro.

Q: What language is spoken in Zante? A: The official language of Zante is Greek, but many locals also speak English.

Q: What should I pack for a trip to Zante? A: Pack lightweight clothing, comfortable shoes, sunscreen, and swimwear. Don’t forget to bring a hat, sunglasses, and a reusable water bottle.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Zante

1. Navagio Beach - Also known as Shipwreck Beach, this beautiful sandy beach is home to a famous shipwreck that washed up on the shore in 1980.

2. Blue Caves - Take a boat tour to explore the stunning blue caves along the island’s coastline.

3. Marathonisi Island - Known as Turtle Island for its population of loggerhead turtles, Marathonisi is a protected nature reserve with beautiful beaches and crystal-clear waters.

4. Zante Town - The island’s capital is a charming town with a mix of Venetian and traditional Greek architecture, museums, and restaurants.

5. Keri Lighthouse - This picturesque lighthouse offers panoramic views over the island and the Ionian Sea.

6. Dionysios Solomos Square - Located in Zante Town, this lively square is home to a statue of the Greek poet Dionysios Solomos, who wrote the national anthem of Greece.

7. Alykes Beach - This family-friendly beach has shallow waters, water sports, and plenty of beachfront restaurants and cafes.

8. Mt. Skopos - Hike to the summit of this mountain for spectacular views over the island and the sea.

9. Porto Limnionas - This stunning cove is a popular spot for swimming and snorkeling, with crystal-clear waters and picturesque cliffs.

10. Gerakas Beach - This scenic beach is a popular nesting spot for loggerhead turtles, so be sure to follow the rules and regulations to avoid disturbing them.

In conclusion, Zante is a beautiful and relaxing destination with something for everyone, from stunning beaches and natural wonders to charming towns and lively nightlife.
