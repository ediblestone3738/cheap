When it comes to luxury travel, a resort or villa is a must. Top Spanish Resorts and Villas for Your Next Vacation

1. Marbella Club Hotel - Marbella, Costa del Sol

Located in the heart of the Costa del Sol, Marbella Club Hotel is a luxurious retreat that has been favored by international celebrities and royalty for generations. With a stunning beachfront location, private gardens, and a world-class spa, this hotel offers the ultimate in relaxation and opulence.

2. Finca Cortesin Hotel, Golf & Spa - Casares, Costa del Sol

Finca Cortesin is an exclusive resort located in the hills of Casares, offering views of the Mediterranean Sea and the mountains of Africa. It features a top-rated golf course, Michelin-starred dining, a luxurious spa, and spacious villas with private pools.

3. La Manga Club - Cartagena, Murcia

La Manga Club is a 1,400-acre resort located on the southeastern coast of Spain. It includes three championship golf courses, a tennis center, a luxurious spa, and a variety of restaurants and bars. Accommodations range from hotel rooms to private apartments and villas.

4. Hacienda Na Xamena - Ibiza, Balearic Islands

Hacienda Na Xamena is a secluded resort located on the cliffs of Ibiza’s northern coast. It offers spectacular views of the Mediterranean Sea, and features an outdoor pool, a rooftop bar, and a wellness center. Accommodations include rooms, suites, and villas with private pools.

5. The Ritz-Carlton, Abama - Guia de Isora, Tenerife

The Ritz-Carlton, Abama is located on the island of Tenerife in the Canary Islands. It features a stunning beachfront location, a championship golf course, seven outdoor pools, a spa, and a variety of dining options. Accommodations include guest rooms, suites, and private villas with ocean views.

FAQs About Indulging in Luxury in Spain

1. What is the best time to visit Spain?

The best time to visit Spain is during the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) when the weather is mild and crowds are fewer.

2. What is the currency in Spain?

The currency in Spain is the Euro (€).

3. What is the time zone in Spain?

Spain is in the Central European Time Zone (CET), which is one hour ahead of Coordinated Universal Time (UTC+1).

4. Do Spanish resorts and villas offer all-inclusive packages?

Yes, many Spanish resorts and villas offer all-inclusive packages that include meals, drinks, and activities.

5. What are the top tourist attractions in Spain?

Some of the top tourist attractions in Spain include the Alhambra in Granada, Park Güell in Barcelona, the Prado Museum in Madrid, and the Sagrada Familia in Barcelona.
