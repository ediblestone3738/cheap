Spain is one of the most beautiful countries in the world, and every year, it receives millions of tourists from all parts of the globe. The country boasts a unique blend of its ancient history and its modernity, its beautiful countryside landscapes, and its beautiful beaches. However, choosing your perfect Spanish getaway can be a daunting task, as there are many beautiful holiday resorts to visit. From the golden beaches of the south to the lush green valleys of the north, Spain boasts a varied landscape that appeals to all kinds of tourists. In this article, we take a detailed look at the top holiday resorts to visit in Spain, with a focus on the things to do and see in each of the resorts.

1. Costa del Sol

Costa del Sol is one of the most popular holiday destinations in Spain, attracting millions of tourists every year. Located in the south of Spain, Costa del Sol boasts beautiful beaches, luxury resorts, and a rich cultural heritage. The resort town is home to some of the most exclusive hotels and restaurants in the country, making it perfect for those looking for exquisite cuisines and a luxurious lifestyle. Some of the top tourist attractions in Costa del Sol include the beaches of Marbella, Puerto Banus, and Estepona.

2. Costa Brava

Costa Brava is another popular holiday destination in Spain, and it is located in the northeast of the country, near the French border. The area boasts a beautiful coastline, with plenty of opportunities for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports. Costa Brava is also home to many scenic medieval villages, which offer a glimpse into the country’s ancient past. Some of the top tourist attractions in Costa Brava include the beaches of Tossa de Mar, Lloret de Mar, and Blanes, as well as the picturesque village of Cadaques.

3. Costa Blanca

Costa Blanca is located in the southeast of Spain and is known for its beautiful beaches and stunning mountain ranges. The area boasts a beautiful coastline and is home to several luxury resorts, making it a popular tourist destination for those looking for a relaxing holiday. Some of the top tourist attractions in Costa Blanca include the beautiful beaches of Benidorm, Alicante, and Denia, as well as the picturesque villages of Altea and Javea.

4. Ibiza

Ibiza is known for its vibrant nightlife and is a popular tourist destination in Spain, attracting party-goers from all over the world. The island boasts beautiful beaches, crystal clear waters, and a rich cultural heritage. Some of the top tourist attractions in Ibiza include the beaches of Cala Conta, Es Vedra, and Playa d’en Bossa, as well as the historic Old Town in Ibiza town.

5. Majorca

Majorca is the largest island in the Balearic Islands archipelago and is a popular tourist destination in Spain. The island boasts beautiful beaches, crystal clear waters, and a rich cultural heritage. Some of the top tourist attractions in Majorca include the beautiful beaches of Cala d’Or, Alcudia, and Palma Nova, as well as the historic Old Town in Palma de Mallorca.

6. Tenerife

Tenerife is the largest of the Canary Islands and is a popular tourist destination in Spain, attracting millions of visitors every year. The island boasts beautiful beaches, stunning mountain ranges, and a rich cultural heritage. Some of the top tourist attractions in Tenerife include the beaches of Playa de las Americas, Los Cristianos, and Puerto de la Cruz, as well as the Teide National Park.

7. Gran Canaria

Gran Canaria is another popular holiday destination in Spain, attracting thousands of tourists every year. The island boasts beautiful beaches, stunning landscapes, and a rich cultural heritage. Some of the top tourist attractions in Gran Canaria include the beaches of Maspalomas, Puerto Rico, and Las Palmas de Gran Canaria, as well as the Maspalomas Natural Park.

8. Menorca

Menorca is the second-largest island in the Balearic Islands archipelago and is known for its beautiful beaches and stunning landscapes. The island is also home to several historic landmarks and cultural attractions. Some of the top tourist attractions in Menorca include the beaches of Cala Macarella, Cala Galdana, and Binibeca Vell, as well as the historic town of Mahón.

9. Lanzarote

Lanzarote is another popular tourist destination in Spain, attracting thousands of tourists every year. The island boasts an otherworldly landscape, with stunning volcanic peaks and craters. Lanzarote is also home to several beautiful beaches and historic landmarks. Some of the top tourist attractions in Lanzarote include the beaches of Playa Blanca, Arrecife, and Famara, as well as the Timanfaya National Park.

10. Algarve

Algarve is located in the south of Portugal but is still considered part of the Spanish holiday destination due to its proximity to Spain. The area boasts beautiful beaches, stunning cliffs, and a rich cultural heritage. Algarve is also home to several luxury resorts and golf courses, making it a popular destination for those looking for a relaxing holiday. Some of the top tourist attractions in Algarve include the beaches of Lagos, Albufeira, and Praia da Rocha, as well as the historic town of Silves.

FAQs:

1. What is the best time to visit Spain?

The best time to visit Spain is during the months of May, June, September, and October, when the weather is warm but not too hot, and the tourist crowds are not too overwhelming.

2. Is it safe to travel to Spain?

Spain is a relatively safe country to travel to, but visitors should take necessary precautions, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping their valuables safe.

3. What is the currency used in Spain?

The official currency used in Spain is the euro.

4. What is the official language spoken in Spain?

The official languages spoken in Spain are Spanish, Catalan, Galician, and Basque.

5. What is the local time zone in Spain?

The local time zone in Spain is Central European Time (CET), which is one hour ahead of Greenwich Mean Time (GMT+1).
