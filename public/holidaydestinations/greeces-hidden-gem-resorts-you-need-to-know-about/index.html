[ad_1] Greece’s Hidden Gem Resorts You Need to Know About

Greece has always been known for its beautiful shoreline, crystal clear waters, and sandy beaches. However, there is so much more to Greece than just its popular tourist destinations. Greece has several hidden gem resorts scattered throughout the country that are worth exploring.

These resorts offer unique experiences that are not commonly found in the most popular tourist areas. Whether you’re looking for a quiet and secluded getaway, a cultural experience, or an adventure-filled vacation, Greece’s hidden gem resorts have something for everyone.

Here are some of the best-hidden gem resorts in Greece that you need to know about:

1. Koufonisia

Koufonisia is an island located in the Cyclades Archipelago. The island is relatively small and has a population of around 400 people. However, what it lacks in size, it makes up for in beauty.

Koufonisia is a peaceful and serene island that offers stunning natural landscapes. The island’s main attraction is its beaches. The beaches in Koufonisia are known to be some of the best beaches in Greece, with crystal clear waters and pristine sand.

2. Nisyros

Nisyros is a small island located in the Aegean Sea. The island is home to a sleeping volcano that last erupted in 1888. The volcano is a popular tourist attraction and can be accessed by hiking or taking a bus to the top.

Nisyros is also home to the beautiful seaside town of Mandraki. The town is known for its traditional architecture and stunning views of the harbor.

3. Kastellorizo

Kastellorizo is a remote island located in the southeastern part of Greece. The island is known for its colorful houses, crystal clear waters, and stunning scenery. The island’s main attraction is the Blue Cave, which is located beneath the island’s cliffs.

The Blue Cave is a must-visit for anyone traveling to Kastellorizo. The cave is accessible only by boat and is filled with crystal clear blue waters that are perfect for swimming.

4. Zagori

Zagori is a region located in the northwestern part of Greece. The region is known for its traditional stone-built villages, stunning natural landscapes, and endless hiking trails. The region is home to the Vikos Gorge, which is the second-deepest gorge in the world.

Zagori is the perfect destination for anyone looking to disconnect from the world and reconnect with nature.

5. Lefkada

Lefkada is an island located in the Ionian Sea. The island is known for its stunning beaches, crystal clear waters, and traditional villages. The island is more popular with local tourists, but it is still relatively unknown to international travelers.

Lefkada is the perfect destination for anyone looking to escape the crowds of other popular tourist destinations in Greece.

6. Kythira

Kythira is an island located in the southeastern part of Greece. The island is known for its stunning natural landscapes, crystal clear waters, and traditional villages. The island has a very laid-back atmosphere, making it the perfect destination for anyone looking to unwind and relax.

Kythira is home to the picturesque village of Chora, which is known for its narrow alleys and traditional architecture.

7. Kavala

Kavala is a city located in the northern part of Greece. The city is known for its stunning beaches, picturesque harbor, and rich history. The city has a more urban feel than some of the other destinations on this list, but it still offers plenty of opportunities for relaxation and adventure.

Kavala is home to the ancient city of Philippi, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

8. Messinia

Messinia is a region located in the southwestern part of Greece. The region is known for its stunning natural landscapes, traditional villages, and ancient ruins. The region is home to the ruins of the ancient city of Messini, which is a must-visit for anyone interested in Greek history.

Messinia is also home to the picturesque town of Pylos, which is known for its beautiful harbor and stunning views.

9. Serifos

Serifos is an island located in the western part of the Cyclades Archipelago. The island is known for its stunning beaches, crystal clear waters, and traditional villages. The island has a more laid-back feel than some of the more popular tourist destinations in Greece, making it the perfect destination for anyone looking to escape the crowds.

Serifos is also home to the picturesque Chora, which is known for its narrow streets and stunning views.

10. Astypalaia

Astypalaia is a small island located in the Dodecanese Archipelago. The island is known for its traditional white-washed houses, stunning natural landscapes, and crystal clear waters. The island is still relatively unspoiled by tourism, making it the perfect destination for anyone looking for a peaceful and secluded getaway.

The island is also home to the Venetian Castle, which offers stunning views of the island and the Aegean Sea.
