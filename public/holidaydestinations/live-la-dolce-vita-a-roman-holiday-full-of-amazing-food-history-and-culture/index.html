FAQs

Q: When is the best time to visit Rome?

A: The best time to visit Rome is during the shoulder seasons, from April to May or from September to October. During these times, the weather is mild, and the city is not as crowded as during the peak season.

Q: Is Rome safe for travellers?

A: As with any major city, Rome has its share of petty crime. However, it’s generally safe for travellers. Visitors are advised to be mindful of their belongings in crowded areas and to take necessary precautions.

Q: What is the best way to get around Rome?

A: The best way to get around Rome is on foot. The city is compact, and most of the major tourist attractions are within walking distance of each other. Alternatively, visitors can take the metro or the bus.

Q: What is the dress code in Rome?

A: There is no strict dress code in Rome. However, visitors are advised to dress modestly when visiting religious sites and to avoid wearing shorts or revealing clothing in these areas.

Q: What is the cuisine like in Rome?

A: Roman cuisine is renowned for its rich and savoury flavours. Dishes such as carbonara, pasta al pomodoro, and saltimbocca alla romana are must-try dishes. Additionally, Rome is famous for its gelato and pizza.
