Top Things to Do in Corfu – A Vacationer's Bucket List

Apr 12, 2023

[ad_1] Corfu, an enchanting island located in Greece, is known for its rich history, beautiful beaches, and incredible landscape. With stunning resorts and beautiful coastlines, this island is the perfect holiday destination for anyone looking to soak up the sun and relax.

There are a multitude of things to do in Corfu that cater to all interests, from exploring ancient ruins, hiking beautiful trails, to indulging in delicious cuisine. Here we present a travel guide on the top things to do in Corfu – A Vacationer’s Bucket List.

1. Visit the Historic Old Town of Corfu

The Old Town of Corfu is an UNESCO World Heritage Site and is one of the most picturesque neighborhoods in the city. This old town is home to Venetian-style buildings, cobbled streets, and beautiful gardens. Walking around the old town is a great way to explore the city, as you take in the beautiful architecture and intricate details of the buildings.

2. Sunbathe in the Beautiful Beaches

Corfu is known for its stunning beaches, which include picturesque coves, crystal clear waters, and sandy shorelines. The most popular beaches include Paleokastritsa, Glyfada, and Ipsos. Whether you’re looking for a secluded cove or a lively beach bar, Corfu has you covered.

3. Explore the Achilleion Palace

Achilleion Palace is a beautiful neoclassical mansion located in the village of Gastouri. This palace was once owned by the Empress Elizabeth of Austria, who was also known as Sisi. The palace is filled with beautiful gardens and sculptures, and visitors can tour the palace to marvel at its grandeur and learn about the history of the palace.

4. Hike the Corfu Trail

The Corfu Trail is a scenic hiking trail that takes you through beautiful landscapes and mountain views. This hiking trail is perfect for those who enjoy outdoor activities and love exploring beautiful landscapes.

5. Take a Boat Tour around the Island

There are many boat tours available that take you around the island of Corfu. These tours offer a unique perspective of the island and allow you to see its beautiful coastline from a different angle. You can also soak up the sun and relax on the deck, as you cruise around the island.

6. Visit the Mon Repos Estate

The Mon Repos Estate is a beautiful mansion located on a hill surrounded by beautiful gardens. This estate is known for being the birthplace of Prince Philip, the husband of Queen Elizabeth II. Visitors can tour the mansion and learn about its history, as well as explore the beautiful gardens.

7. Indulge in Delicious Cuisine

Corfu is known for its delicious cuisine, which includes fresh seafood, Greek salads, and traditional souvlaki. There are also many local specialties to try, including stifado, which is a delicious beef stew.

8. Visit the Vlacherna Monastery

The Vlacherna Monastery is a beautiful and tranquil monastery located on a small island just off the coast of Corfu. Visitors can take a boat over to the island and explore the monastery, which has a beautiful courtyard and stunning views of the surrounding area.

9. Explore the Corfu Museum of Asian Art

The Corfu Museum of Asian Art is a beautiful museum located in the Palace of St Michael and St George. This museum has a beautiful collection of Asian art, including Japanese pottery, Chinese ceramics, and Indian sculptures.

10. Visit the Paleokastritsa Monastery

The Paleokastritsa Monastery is a beautiful monastery located on a cliff overlooking the sea. This monastery has stunning views of the surrounding landscape, as well as beautiful gardens and a museum.

FAQ’s

1. What is the best time to visit Corfu?

The best time to visit Corfu is during the months of May to October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are open.

2. Is Corfu safe for tourists?

Corfu is generally a very safe place for tourists. However, like any tourist destination, you should take precautions to protect your valuables and yourself.

3. What is the currency in Corfu?

The currency in Corfu is the Euro.

4. What language do they speak in Corfu?

The main language spoken in Corfu is Greek. However, many locals also speak English, so it is easy to communicate with them.

5. Do I need a visa to visit Corfu?

If you are a citizen of the EU, you do not need a visa to visit Corfu. However, if you are from a country outside the EU, it is best to check with the Greek embassy in your country to see if you need a visa.
