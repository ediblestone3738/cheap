[ad_1] Escape to Paradise: The Top Greek Islands for Couples

Greece is a country of ancient history and stunning natural beauty, making it a top travel destination to visit. Among the islands of Greece, there’s something unique and captivating about each one. If you’re travelling with a special someone and want to soak up some sun, swim in crystal clear waters, and indulge in the island life, you’ll find that Greece’s islands will fulfill all your wishes. Here are some of the best Greek islands couples can visit.

Santorini

Santorini is renowned for its cliffs and white-washed villas that offer unparalleled views of the Aegean Sea. The sunset in Santorini is a sight like no other, with the sky turning shades of pink and orange that perfectly contrast with the white walls of the villages. Santorini’s beaches come in different shades of black, due to their volcanic origin, but they are still a must-visit. You can also hike to the top of the caldera, or take a sailboat tour to enjoy the island from a different angle.

Mykonos

Mykonos is known for its nightlife, with the famous Paradise Beach being a popular destination for party-goers. Despite that, Mykonos has more to offer than parties, it also boasts a unique amorous atmosphere with its charming alleys winding through classic Cycladic whitewashed buildings. Navigational challenges are countered with a small camera to take advantage of the photo opportunities, as Mykonos is undeniably photogenic.

Crete

Crete is the largest island in Greece and has everything a couple needs for a romantic getaway: picturesque towns, enchanting beaches, and superb food. A visit to the Samaria Gorge is perfect for couples who enjoy hiking trails, rewarding them with the refreshment of Venetian harbor towns overlooking blue-green waters. Couples who love to relax will appreciate Elafonissi, a pink sand beach that also provides a romantic ambiance as the sun sets with the surrounding coral reefs and gentle waves.

Rhodes

The island of Rhodes is home to the largest medieval city and a place where history comes alive. Couples who enjoy art and history can visit the Palace of the Grand Master, the historic sites of Lindos, or the Archaeological Museum. While the Kallithea Springs is considered one of the most romantic attractions in Rhodes—its crystal-clear waters and the Greek architecture of its facilities great for mesmerizing couples.

Corfu

The island of Corfu, also known as Kerkyra, is a crowded island during the summer, but it stills provides sufficient privacy when required. With its lush forests, hidden coves and medieval towns its perfect for couples who seek to wander and discover amazing landscapes. Head to the charming old town of Corfu, which is inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, or visit the secluded beach of Agios Gordios.

Kos

Kos is known for its relaxing beaches and crystal-clear waters. You can find unspoiled beaches such as the peaceful Tigaki beach, where couples can spend the day snorkeling and swimming in the shallow azure waters or go to the famous Paradise Beach, where music accompanies the relaxation time.

FAQs

Q: What is the best time to visit Greece? A: The best time to visit Greece is from late April to early November when the weather is warm and sunny, and the crowds are at their lowest.

Q: How do I get to the Greek islands? A: The Greek islands are accessible by ferry, plane, or speedboat.

Q: Can I drink tap water in Greece? A: It is generally safe to drink tap water in Greece, but bottled water is recommended for those with a sensitive stomach.

Q: Do Greek islands have a dress code? A: No, there is no specific dress code for the Greek islands. However, lightweight and comfortable clothing that covers your shoulders is recommended when entering a church or religious site.

Q: What is the currency in Greece? A: The currency in Greece is the Euro.

The country is rich in history, culture and stunning natural sites, making it the perfect destination for couples who are looking for a romantic getaway.
