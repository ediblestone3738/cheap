The Best Gelato and Pizza in Rome: A Foodie's Guide to the City

Mar 31, 2023

[ad_1] Rome is undoubtedly one of the most beautiful cities in the world and also one of the best travel destinations for foodies. Its charming streets, ancient ruins and delicious cuisine make it the perfect place for a vacation. Italian food is famous worldwide, and for good reason. It’s not only delicious but also diverse in flavors and ingredients. If you’re planning a trip to Rome, you should definitely know where to find the best gelato and pizza in the city.

In this article, we’ll provide you with a foodie’s guide to Rome, including the top 10 tourist attractions located in the best gelato and pizza spots in the city. We’ve also answered some of the most frequently asked questions about Rome’s food scene.

The Best Gelato in Rome:

Gelato is one of the most popular desserts in Rome, and there are numerous gelato parlors throughout the city. However, not all of them are created equal. Here are a few places where you can find the best gelato in Rome:

Giolitti:

Since 1890, Giolitti has been a Rome staple and is one of the most famous gelaterias in the city. Located near the Pantheon, they have a wide range of flavors, including classic options such as chocolate and hazelnut, as well as more exotic flavors, including green tea and whisky. Prices are reasonable, and the quality is top-notch.

Gelateria del Teatro:

This artisanal gelateria is located near Piazza Navona and is a must-visit for those looking for unique flavors. Their gelato is made using fresh ingredients and their flavors change seasonally. Some of the most popular flavors include ricotta and fig or cherry blossom.

Fatamorgana:

This gelateria has several locations in Rome, and they are known for their creative and innovative flavors. They make their gelato using only natural ingredients and offer unique options such as pumpkin and cinnamon, sweet potato, and pear and gorgonzola.

The Best Pizza in Rome:

Pizza is to Rome as the Mona Lisa is to the Louvre – it’s a must! Rome has been known for its pizza-making for centuries, and there are so many great pizzerias to choose from. Here are a few of our favorite spots:

Da Remo:

This pizzeria is located in the Testaccio neighborhood and is famous for its thin crust pizza. They use high-quality ingredients, including buffalo mozzarella and San Marzano tomatoes. Be prepared to wait, as the restaurant is always crowded.

Pizzeria La Montecarlo:

Located near Piazza del Popolo, this family-run pizzeria has been around since 1906. They have a wide range of pizza options, including classic margherita and more creative options such as pizza with truffle and burrata cheese. Be sure to try their delicious fried pizza, which is a specialty of the house.

Dar Poeta:

This pizzeria is located in Trastevere and is famous for its thick-crust pizza. Their toppings are generous, and they offer some creative combinations such as zucchini blossoms and anchovies or gorgonzola cheese and walnuts.

FAQs about Rome’s Food Scene:

1. What is the best time to visit Rome to experience its food scene?

The best time to visit Rome to experience its food scene is from September through November. During this time, the weather is perfect, and there are plenty of food festivals taking place throughout the city.

2. Is street food safe to eat in Rome?

Yes, street food is safe to eat in Rome. However, it’s important to use your discretion and choose reputable vendors. Look for vendors who have a lot of customers, as this is a good indicator of their food quality.

3. What are some must-try dishes in Rome?

Some must-try dishes in Rome include carbonara pasta, cacio e pepe, fried artichokes, and suppli (fried rice balls).

4. Is it customary to tip at restaurants in Rome?

Tipping is not mandatory in Rome, but it’s usually appreciated. A tip of 10% is sufficient, and you can round up the bill.

5. What is the best way to learn about Rome’s food scene?

The best way to learn about Rome’s food scene is to take a food tour. These tours typically take you to several food establishments throughout the city, and you can learn about the history of the dishes and the ingredients used.

4. Campo de’ Fiori:

This bustling market square is located near Forno Campo de’ Fiori bakery and is a great place to buy fresh produce and street food.

This Rome neighborhood is known for its food scene, and Da Remo pizzeria is a must-visit in the area.

6. Trastevere:

This picturesque neighborhood is full of narrow streets and charming restaurants, including Dar Poeta pizzeria.

7. Galleria Borghese:

This beautiful museum houses a collection of sculptures and paintings, and is located near Fatamorgana gelateria.

8. Colosseum:

This ancient amphitheater is one of Rome’s most popular tourist attractions and is located near Pizzeria La Montecarlo.

9. Trevi Fountain:

This iconic staircase is located near Giolitti gelateria and is a great spot to people-watch while enjoying a cone of gelato.
