The Best Parks and Gardens in Madrid

Apr 1, 2023

[ad_1] Madrid, the capital of Spain, is renowned for its impressive architectural masterpieces, historical monuments, unparalleled culture, and traditions. However, if you’re looking to take a break from the hustle and bustle of the city and immerse yourself in nature’s tranquility, the city has some of the best parks and gardens to explore. The city features fascinating public parks, botanical gardens, palatial gardens, and green spaces that provide a serene atmosphere to relax, admire the beautiful landscapes, and soak up the sun. In this article, we’ll explore the best parks and gardens in Madrid and highlight ten top tourist attractions visitors must see when visiting these locations.

1. El Retiro Park

El Retiro Park is undoubtedly the most famous park in Madrid and is a must-visit destination for tourists. The park is located in the heart of the city, covering an area of 125 hectares. The park features an array of attractions, including hiking trails, gardens, a large lake where visitors can go boating, and the Crystal Palace. The park’s beauty makes it an ideal spot for picnics, jogging, and biking. Visitors can also enjoy the street performers and artists who frequent the park.

2. Sabatini Gardens

Sabatini Gardens are a Royal garden located in the Royal Palace of Madrid, which featured extensive renovation work in the 1930s. The garden’s design is a mix of neoclassical and formal gardens, which gives it a spectacular and romantic essence. The garden features numerous sculptures and water fountains that add to its beauty, and visitors can take leisurely strolls while admiring the breathtaking views of the garden’s landscape.

3. El Capricho Park

El Capricho Park, also known as The Whim, is another green space that must be on your list of things to visit when in Madrid. The park measures 14 hectares and is located in the Barajas district in the northeast of the city. The park’s design dates back to the 18th century, and its beautiful gardens, water features, and architecturally designed buildings make it an excellent place to relax and unwind.

4. Casa de Campo Park

Casa de Campo is one of Europe’s biggest public parks, measuring over 4,000 hectares. The park is home to the Madrid Zoo and a beautiful lake where visitors can go boating. The park also features various hiking trails and cycling paths for visitors to enjoy. Visitors can also take a cable car ride to see stunning views of the park and the city.

5. Jardín del Príncipe de Anglona

Jardín del Príncipe de Anglona, also known as the English Garden, is located in the La Latina neighborhood. The garden dates back to the 16th century and features different styles of gardens, including French, Italian, and English. Visitors can also marvel at the garden’s numerous fountains and sculptures and take walks down the green pathways.

6. Buen Retiro Gardens

Buen Retiro Gardens are located in the Arganzuela district, close to the old Viaducto de Segovia bridge. The garden’s main attraction is its historical gardens and arboretum. The garden also features numerous walking and cycling paths, and visitors can relax and admire swans and other wildlife in the garden’s ponds.

7. Campo del Moro

Campo del Moro is located behind the Royal Palace and is a fantastic place to enjoy a picnic or take a leisure walk. Visitors can enjoy the garden’s lush and vibrant flower beds, water fountains, and the Generalife’s outstanding views.

8. Royal Botanic Garden

The Royal Botanic Garden is located in the Retiro district, and its design dates back to the 18th century. The garden is an oasis in the city, featuring over 30,000 species of plants, including rare and endangered species. The garden comprises of different gardens, including medicinal plants, aquatic plants, aromatic plants, and a Japanese garden.

9. Parque de los Molinos

Parque de los Molinos is located near the Casa de Campo Park and is a leisure park that features a pond, children’s playground, and outdoor gym equipment. The park is ideal for family outings and picnics, and visitors can also hike and bike in the park.

10. Parque de Berlin

Located in the Chamartin district, Parque de Berlin is an excellent place to take a leisurely walk and enjoy the park’s contemporary sculptures and Berlin’s trademarks such as a segment of the Berlin Wall.
