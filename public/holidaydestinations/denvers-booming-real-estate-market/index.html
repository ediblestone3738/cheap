Denver’s Booming Real Estate Market: A Traveler’s Guide

Denver has long been a popular destination for travelers, but in recent years, its real estate market has been booming. Home prices have skyrocketed, making it one of the hottest real estate markets in the country. This guide will provide an overview of the city’s real estate market, as well as the top tourist attractions located in the area.

Overview of Denver’s Real Estate Market

Denver’s real estate market has been on an upward trajectory for years, with home prices rising more than 11% in 2020 alone. The city’s population growth has been a major driver of this growth, with the population increasing by more than 25% since 2010. This influx of new residents has led to increased demand for housing, driving up prices.

The city’s real estate market is also bolstered by its strong job market. Denver is home to a number of Fortune 500 companies, as well as a thriving tech industry. This has led to a steady stream of new residents and businesses, which has helped fuel the city’s real estate boom.

Top Tourist Attractions in Denver’s Real Estate Market

Denver is home to a number of popular tourist attractions. Here are the top 10:

1. The Denver Zoo: The Denver Zoo is one of the city’s most popular attractions, with more than 3 million visitors each year. The zoo is home to more than 4,000 animals, including polar bears, tigers, and gorillas.

2. Red Rocks Park and Amphitheatre: Red Rocks Park and Amphitheatre is a popular venue for concerts and other events. The park is home to stunning views of the Rocky Mountains, as well as a variety of trails for hiking and biking.

3. Denver Botanic Gardens: The Denver Botanic Gardens is a popular destination for visitors to the city. The gardens feature more than 45 acres of gardens and greenhouses, as well as a variety of educational programs.

4. Coors Field: Coors Field is home to the Colorado Rockies, the city’s Major League Baseball team. The stadium is a popular destination for baseball fans, with a capacity of more than 50,000.

5. Denver Art Museum: The Denver Art Museum is one of the city’s premier cultural institutions. The museum features more than 70,000 works of art, including pieces from the American West and Native American cultures.

6. Denver Museum of Nature and Science: The Denver Museum of Nature and Science is a popular destination for visitors of all ages. The museum features a variety of interactive exhibits, as well as a planetarium and IMAX theater.

7. Denver Union Station: Denver Union Station is a historic train station that has been renovated into a popular shopping and dining destination. The station features a variety of restaurants, as well as a variety of shops.

8. Denver Aquarium: The Denver Aquarium is home to more than 500 species of marine life, including sharks, rays, and sea turtles. The aquarium also features interactive exhibits, as well as a variety of educational programs.

9. 16th Street Mall: The 16th Street Mall is a popular destination for visitors to the city. The mall features a variety of shops and restaurants, as well as a free shuttle service that runs the length of the mall.

10. Capitol Hill: Capitol Hill is one of the city’s most popular neighborhoods. The area is home to a variety of restaurants, bars, and shops, as well as a vibrant nightlife scene.
