[ad_1] Pamper Yourself: The Most Luxurious Resorts in Greece

Greece is undoubtedly one of the world’s most popular tourist destinations, offering visitors a plethora of cultural, historical, and natural attractions. But what do you do when you’re tired of exploring ancient ruins, charming villages, and pristine beaches, and just need a little R&R? That’s where luxury resorts come in. Greece boasts some of the most luxurious accommodations in the world, offering guests unmatched comfort, pampering, and indulgence. Here are some of the most luxurious resorts in Greece that will provide the perfect getaway for those looking to pamper themselves.

1. Amanzoe, Peloponnese

Located in the Peloponnese peninsula, the Amanzoe resort offers stunning views of the Peloponnese countryside, the Aegean Sea, and the island of Spetses. Amanzoe is designed to imitate the ancient acropolis of nearby Epidavros, with a series of low-lying pavilions and luxurious private villas. The resort also boasts a state-of-the-art spa, a private beach club, and a restaurant that celebrates local Greek cuisine.

2. Cavo Tagoo, Mykonos

The Cavo Tagoo resort is located on the island of Mykonos, one of the most popular tourist destinations in Greece. The resort’s signature white-washed architecture perfectly complements the stark hills and turquoise waters of Mykonos. Cavo Tagoo offers guests unparalleled luxury, with spacious suites, a private beach, a world-class spa, and a rooftop restaurant with panoramic views of the Aegean Sea.

3. Elounda Beach Hotel & Villas, Crete

Elounda Beach Hotel is situated on the island of Crete, in the charming seaside village of Elounda. The resort is known for its private beach, one of the best in Greece, and its lush gardens that include over 150,000 plants and are tended to by a full-time botanist. Elounda Beach Hotel also features spacious suites, private villas with their own pools, a state-of-the-art spa, and a variety of restaurants.

4. Costa Navarino, Peloponnese & Messinia

Costa Navarino is a sustainable luxury resort located in the Peloponnese peninsula, spread over a stunning beachfront landscape of 321 hectares. The resort offers two five-star properties, The Westin Resort Costa Navarino and The Romanos, a Luxury Collection Resort, as well as world-class golf courses, a serene spa, multiple restaurants, and more than 20 km of walking, hiking and biking trails.

5. Katikies, Santorini

Katikies is located on the picturesque island of Santorini, famous for its beaches, white-washed buildings, and incredible sunsets. The resort offers guests private terraces with views of the caldera, infinity pools, a state-of-the-art spa, and multiple restaurants serving fresh, locally sourced Mediterranean cuisine.

6. Myconian Utopia Resort & Villas, Mykonos

The Myconian Utopia Resort & Villas is a luxurious resort located on the hillside of Elia Beach in Mykonos. The resort offers spacious rooms and private villas, most of which come with their own infinity pool or Jacuzzi, a spa, a fitness center, and multiple dining venues. The highlight of this resort is the mesmerizing view of the Aegean Sea.

7. Porto Zante Villas & Spa, Zakynthos

Located on the island of Zakynthos, Porto Zante Villas & Spa offers guests luxurious private villas with their own infinity pools and stunning views of the Ionian Sea. The resort features a private beach, a world-class spa, and a restaurant that serves Greek dishes, made with locally sourced ingredients.

8. Domes of Elounda, Autograph Collection, Crete

Domes of Elounda is a luxurious resort located on the island of Crete, in the charming village of Elounda. The resort offers spacious suites and private villas, most of which come with their own infinity pool or Jacuzzi, a state-of-the-art spa, and multiple dining venues. The resort also offers a variety of activities, including kayaking, windsurfing, and diving.

9. Eagles Palace, Halkidiki

Eagles Palace is a luxurious resort located in the Halkidiki peninsula, in northern Greece. The resort offers spacious rooms and suites, some with their own private pools, a state-of-the-art spa, and multiple dining venues, including a Michelin-starred restaurant. Eagles Palace is also known for its private beach and water sports facilities.

10. Grace Mykonos, Mykonos

Grace Mykonos is a boutique hotel located on the island of Mykonos, in the charming village of Agios Stefanos. The hotel offers luxurious suites and rooms, most of which come with their own private terrace or plunge pool, a world-class spa, and a restaurant that serves Mediterranean dishes made with locally sourced ingredients.

FAQs

1. What is the best time to visit Greece for a luxury vacation?

The best time to visit Greece for a luxury vacation is between May and October, when the weather is warm and sunny.

2. What’s the best way to travel between the different islands when staying at luxury resorts in Greece?

The best way to travel between different islands when staying at luxury resorts in Greece is by yacht or helicopter. Most resorts offer yacht or helicopter transfers to and from the islands.

3. Are these luxury resorts child-friendly?

Most luxury resorts in Greece are child-friendly, with family-friendly amenities and activities.

4. What is the average cost of a luxury resort in Greece?

The average cost of a luxury resort in Greece is around €500 to €1000 per night, depending on the resort and the season.
