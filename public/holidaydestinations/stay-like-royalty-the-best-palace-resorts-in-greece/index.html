[ad_1] Stay Like Royalty: The Best Palace Resorts in Greece

Greece is known for its beautiful islands, stunning coastlines, delicious cuisine and ancient history, but did you know that it also has some of the most luxurious palace resorts in the world? From historic castles to seaside mansions, staying in a palace resort in Greece is an experience like no other. Here are the top places to stay to live like a king or queen in Greece.

1. Amanzoe Resort – Peloponnese

Located in the heart of the Peloponnese, Amanzoe Resort is a true slice of paradise. Hidden amongst the rolling hills of the Greek countryside, this luxurious palace resort features 38 pavilions and private villas with stunning sea views. The villas are set amidst olive groves and natural scrubland, providing a peaceful retreat for the discerning traveler. The resort also boasts its own private beach club, an expansive spa and wellness center, and access to some of the best cultural sites in Greece.

2. Grand Resort Lagonissi – Athens Riviera

Situated on a peninsula just a stone’s throw from Athens, the Grand Resort Lagonissi is a true oasis of luxury. This palace resort is designed in a traditional style, with whitewashed villas overlooking the sparkling Aegean Sea. Each villa is fitted with its own private infinity pool and state-of-the-art amenities, ensuring that guests have everything they need to relax in style. The resort also features five private beaches, a range of watersports activities, and an award-winning spa.

3. Poseidonion Grand Hotel – Spetses Island

Located on the picturesque island of Spetses, the Poseidonion Grand Hotel is a beautiful example of neoclassical architecture. This luxury palace resort was built in 1914 and has been impeccably restored to its former glory. The hotel features a beautiful spa, a rooftop bar with panoramic sea views, and a private beach club on the nearby Agioi Anargyroi beach. Guests can relax in one of the hotel’s elegant suites, which are beautifully furnished and feature all the latest mod cons.

4. Navarino Dunes – Costa Navarino

The Navarino Dunes Resort in Costa Navarino is a true haven for the discerning traveler. This palace resort is set amongst lush gardens and features a range of luxurious villas and suites, all of which offer breathtaking views of the Ionian Sea. The resort’s spa is the perfect place to unwind after a day on the golf course, and there are plenty of other activities to keep guests entertained, including hiking, cycling, and tennis. For those who prefer their luxury with a side of culture, nearby attractions include the Palace of Nestor and the ancient city of Methoni.

5. Santorini Secret – Santorini Island

Santorini Secret is a beautiful palace resort located on the stunning island of Santorini. This boutique retreat boasts two swimming pools, a gourmet restaurant, and a range of luxurious suites with private terraces overlooking the Aegean Sea. The resort is located in the charming village of Oia, which is famous for its breathtaking sunsets and picturesque white-washed buildings. Guests can explore nearby attractions such as the ancient city of Akrotiri or take a boat tour of the island’s caldera.

FAQs

Q: Can I enjoy Greek cuisine in these palace resorts?

A: Absolutely. Most of these resorts have multiple restaurants featuring a range of Greek and international cuisine.

Q: What amenities do these palace resorts have?

A: The amenities vary depending on the resort, but most feature spas, private beaches, swimming pools, and a range of other activities such as golf or tennis.

Q: Are children allowed in these resorts?

A: Yes, all of these resorts are family-friendly and offer a range of amenities and activities for children.

Q: What is the best time of year to visit Greece and stay in these palace resorts?

A: The best time of year to visit is during the shoulder seasons of spring and autumn when the weather is still warm but the crowds are thinner.
