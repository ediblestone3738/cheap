Bengaluru: An Eclectic Mix of the Old and the New

Mar 21, 2023

[ad_1] Bengaluru, also known as the Silicon Valley of India, is a vibrant and bustling city located in the southern part of India. The city has witnessed an incredible transformation over the years, from being a quiet town to a cosmopolitan metropolis. Bengaluru is a city that is filled with a unique blend of old and new, with colonial architecture, ancient temples, and modern high-rises coexisting side by side.

History of Bengaluru

Bengaluru has a rich history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the Stone Age. The city was founded by Kempegowda, a feudal lord from the Yelahanka dynasty, in the 16th century. Under his rule, Bengaluru became a prominent trading center, where silk, spices, and precious stones were exchanged. The city also played a crucial role in the Indian independence movement and was witness to important events such as the Non-cooperation movement and the Quit India movement.

Old Bengaluru

One of the striking aspects of Bengaluru is how it has managed to preserve its old-world charm. Walking through the streets of old Bengaluru is like taking a step back in time. The architecture of the buildings, the narrow lanes, and the quaint old shops are a reminder of the city’s past.

One of the most prominent examples of colonial architecture is the Bangalore Palace. The palace was built in 1878 by the Maharaja of Mysore and was inspired by the Windsor Castle in England. The palace is open to the public and is a popular tourist spot.

Another must-visit attraction in old Bengaluru is the Bull Temple. The temple is dedicated to Nandi, the sacred bull of Lord Shiva. The temple’s architecture, with its Dravidian influences, is awe-inspiring, and the huge Nandi statue is a sight to behold.

New Bengaluru

Bengaluru has also undergone a massive transformation over the years, with the city now being a hub of technology, innovation, and creativity. The city’s skyline is dominated by modern high-rise buildings, and the streets are lined with upscale restaurants, shopping centers, and entertainment complexes.

One of the most iconic buildings in Bengaluru is the UB City. The building is home to luxury stores, fine dining restaurants, and art galleries. The UB City is a popular destination for shopping and entertainment.

Another must-visit spot in new Bengaluru is the Innovative Film City. The theme park is spread over 58 acres and offers a range of attractions, including a dinosaur park, a water park, and a wax museum.

FAQs

1. What is the best time to visit Bengaluru?

The best time to visit Bengaluru is between October and February when the weather is pleasant and the city is at its most beautiful.

2. What is the local language spoken in Bengaluru?

The local language spoken in Bengaluru is Kannada. However, most people in the city speak English, and it is easy to get by with just English.

3. What is the currency used in Bengaluru?

The currency used in Bengaluru is the Indian Rupee.

4. What are some of the must-visit attractions in Bengaluru?

Some of the must-visit attractions in Bengaluru include the Bangalore Palace, the Bull Temple, the Cubbon Park, the Lalbagh Botanical Garden, and the Innovative Film City.

5. How do I get around in Bengaluru?

Bengaluru has an extensive network of public transport, including buses, auto-rickshaws, and taxis. You can also use ride-sharing apps like Ola and Uber to get around.
