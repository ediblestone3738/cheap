Zante, also known as Zakynthos, is a Greek island located in the Ionian Sea. This beautiful island is the third largest of the Ionian Islands and is a popular tourist destination for many reasons. From stunning beaches, turquoise water, history, and delicious food, Zante offers a taste of Greek culture that travelers will never forget. In this article, we will take a closer look at the cuisine, history, and adventure that awaits visitors to Zante.

Cuisine:

Greek cuisine is famous for its simplicity and use of fresh, local ingredients. In Zante, visitors will find a wide range of delicious dishes that capture the essence of Greek cuisine. Some of the dishes you should not miss include:

1. Moussaka: This popular Greek dish is a layered casserole made with eggplant, ground beef, potatoes, and a creamy b√©chamel sauce.

2. Souvlaki: This Greek classic is grilled meat on skewers, often served with pita bread and a variety of toppings.

3. Saganaki: This dish consists of fried cheese, often served as an appetizer.

4. Greek Salad: A simple but delicious salad made with fresh tomatoes, cucumbers, onions, feta cheese, and olive oil.

5. Gyros: A popular street food in Greece, gyros are typically made with grilled meat (often pork or chicken), wrapped in pita bread, and served with tzatziki sauce.

History:

Zante has a rich history dating back to Ancient Greece. One of the island’s most famous landmarks is the Shipwreck Beach. This beach features the wreck of a smuggler’s ship that ran aground in 1980. The beach is accessible only by boat, but it is a must-see when visiting Zante.

Another site to visit is the Keri Lighthouse. Built in 1925, this lighthouse offers stunning views of the Ionian Sea and is a popular spot for watching the sunset.

Adventure:

Zante offers a wide range of outdoor activities for visitors. One of the most popular is sea kayaking. Visitors can explore the island’s coastline and hidden coves on guided tours.

Another popular activity is hiking. The island has several trails that snake through scenic forested areas and rocky coastlines. The most famous hike is the trek to the top of Mount Skopos, which offers breathtaking views of the island.

FAQs:

Q: What is the best time to visit Zante? A: The best time to visit Zante is between May and October when the weather is warm and sunny.

Q: What airport should I fly into to reach Zante? A: The nearest airport is Zakynthos International Airport, also known as Dionysios Solomos Airport.

Q: Is it safe to drink the tap water in Zante? A: No, it is recommended that visitors stick to bottled water.

Q: What currency is used in Zante? A: The official currency is the Euro.

Q: Do I need a visa to visit Zante? A: Visitors from the EU, USA, Canada, and Australia can stay up to 90 days in Greece without a visa.
