Corfu, the lush green island located off the western coast of Greece, is the perfect destination for those who want to soak up the sun, enjoy the beautiful beaches, explore ancient history and architecture or embark on an island-hopping adventure. With a rich history and a plethora of natural beauty, Corfu has something for everyone. Whether you’re travelling solo, with your family, or on a romantic getaway, here’s everything you need to know about From Lounging on the Beach to Island Hopping – Activities Your Guide to Corfu.

Getting There Corfu is easily accessible by air from all major cities of Europe. You can book your flight directly to Corfu International Airport (CFU), located just 3km south of Corfu Town. If you’re travelling from Athens, you can also opt for the ferry that takes you to the island in about 6 to 8 hours of travel time.

Weather and Best Time to Visit Corfu experiences a Mediterranean climate with mild winters and hot summers. The best time to visit Corfu is between April and October when the weather is sunny and warm, and the sea is perfect for swimming. July and August witness the peak season with high tourist crowds and expensive accommodation, so it’s best to avoid these months if you’re based on a budget.

Accommodation Corfu has a range of accommodation options, including hotels, resorts, villas and guesthouses. Depending on your budget and style, you can pick one that suits your needs. If you’re travelling with a family or group, villas can offer great comfort and flexibility, while hotels and resorts are ideal for those who want access to amenities, restaurants and sweep views of the sea.

Activities

Here are the top activities that you can engage in during your stay in Corfu:

1. Lounging on the beaches Corfu boasts of some of the most beautiful beaches in Greece, ranging from sandy coves to rocky and secluded bays. Paleokastritsa and Ipsos are two of the popular beach destinations where you can soak up the sun, take a dip in the crystal-clear waters or indulge in water sports such as windsurfing, jet skiing or snorkelling.

2. Sightseeing on Corfu Old Town Corfu Old Town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its neo-classical architecture, narrow alleys, pastel-hued houses, and historical sites. As you wander through the streets, you’ll marvel at the well-preserved Venetian fortresses, the Old Palace, the magnificent St. Spyridon Church, and the Bell Tower. You can also explore the museums and art galleries that showcase the island’s history and local arts.

3. Island Hopping Corfu is surrounded by other beautiful islands, such as Paxos, Antipaxos, and Albanian Riviera, which can be easily accessed by ferries or private boats. Going island hopping lets you explore the beauty, cuisine and culture of these regions and brings you closer to the locals. You can discover hidden beaches, sample the traditional seafood delicacies, and soak in the natural beauty of the landscapes.

4. Trekking Corfu’s hill country is a hiker’s paradise, with several trails winding through olive groves, woodlands, and quaint villages. One of the popular trekking routes is the Corfu Trail, a 220km journey that covers the entire island, from north to south. You can also hike to Mount Pantokrator, the highest peak in the island, and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding sea and landscapes.

5. Diving Corfu’s underwater world is a treasure trove of marine life, exotic plants, and colourful coral reefs. You can get a peek at the rich diversity by taking a diving or snorkelling tour with certified guides. Some of the popular sites for diving are Nissaki Beach, Kerasia Bay and the wreck of HMS Perseus.

6. Taking a Cooking Class Corfu’s cuisine is a blend of Mediterranean and Italian influences, with lots of fresh seafood, vegetables soaked in herbs, and aromatic spices. Taking a cooking class is a great way to discover the local cuisine, learn new recipes, and indulge in some fine dining. Many local houses and hotels offer hands-on cooking workshops where you make dishes such as pastitsada and sofrito from scratch and savour them with a glass of Corfiot wine.

7. Visiting Achillion Palace Achillion Palace is a stunning estate located on the outskirts of Corfu Town. Built in 1890 for Empress Elisabeth of Austria, the palace boasts of neoclassical architecture, vast gardens, and lavish interiors. You can take a guided tour of the palace to learn about its history, admire the ornate frescoes, and soak in the stunning views of Corfu Town and the sea.

8. Paragliding For those seeking an adrenaline rush, paragliding is the perfect activity in Corfu. You can soar through the skies and witness the stunning coastal views, the glistening blue waters and the greenery of the island. You can take a tandem flight with trained professionals, who will guide you through the experience and ensure your safety.

9. Horseback Riding Corfu’s idyllic rural landscapes are perfect for horseback riding. You can take a guided tour through the lush countryside, villages and olive groves, and soak up the natural surroundings. You can also ride horses on the beaches at sunset, creating a picture-perfect experience.

10. Taking a Boat Tour on the Canal D’Amour The Canal D’Amour is a stunning natural landmark located on the northwestern coast of Corfu. The canal is formed by rocky cliffs, with crystal-clear waters running between them. Taking a boat tour with a guide lets you explore this wonder and its mysteries while enjoying the stunning coastline.

FAQs

1. What is the currency used in Corfu? The currency used in Corfu is Euro.

2. What is the language spoken in Corfu? The official language of Corfu is Greek, and English is widely spoken in tourist areas.

3. Is Corfu safe to visit? Corfu is a safe destination, and the locals are warm and welcoming. However, it’s essential to take standard safety precautions and be vigilant while travelling.

4. How do I get around in Corfu? Corfu has an efficient public bus system, taxis, and rentals cars and bikes. You can also hire a private driver or guide for a more personal experience.

5. What is the typical cuisine of Corfu? Corfu cuisine is a blend of traditional Mediterranean and Italian influences, with an emphasis on fresh seafood, vegetables, and herbs. Some popular dishes include pastitsada, sofrito, bourdeto, and stuffed vine leaves.
