Does Travelodge Ask for ID?

Travelodge is one of the most popular hotel chains in the UK and many people choose to stay there when they are travelling. But one question that many people have is whether or not Travelodge asks for ID. The answer is yes, Travelodge does ask for ID when you check in to one of their hotels.

Why Does Travelodge Ask for ID?

Travelodge requires guests to show photo identification (such as a driver’s licence, passport or national ID card) or the credit or debit card used to make the booking when they check in. This is done to ensure that the person checking in is the same person who made the booking. This helps to protect the hotel from fraud and other criminal activities.

What Types of ID Does Travelodge Accept?

Travelodge accepts a variety of forms of identification, including driver’s licences, passports, national ID cards, and the credit or debit card used to make the booking. If you do not have any of these forms of identification, you may be asked to provide other forms of identification such as a utility bill or bank statement.

What Happens if I Don’t Have ID?

If you are unable to produce the required identification to the satisfaction of the hotel, your booking may be terminated without refund. This is why it is important to make sure that you have the necessary forms of identification with you when you check in to a Travelodge hotel.

Tips for Making Sure You Have the Right ID

If you are planning to stay at a Travelodge hotel, it is important to make sure that you have the right forms of identification with you. Here are some tips to help you make sure that you have the right ID:

  • Check the hotel’s website to make sure that you know what forms of identification they accept.
  • Make sure that your identification is up to date and valid.
  • If you are travelling with someone else, make sure that they also have the necessary forms of identification.
  • If you are using a credit or debit card to make the booking, make sure that you have the card with you when you check in.
