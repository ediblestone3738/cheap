Rhodes is one of the most popular destinations for tourists in Greece. It’s the largest of the Dodecanese islands and offers visitors a mix of ancient history, stunning beaches and charming small towns. Rhodes Town is the capital and a must-visit destination for tourists. It’s located on the northern tip of the island and has a rich history dating back to ancient times. Whether you’re interested in history, culture or just relaxing on a beautiful beach, Rhodes Town is the perfect destination. In this article, we’ll explore the top things to do in Rhodes Town and provide answers to some frequently asked questions.

FAQs about Rhodes Town

Q: When is the best time to visit Rhodes Town? A: The best time to visit Rhodes Town is from May to September when the weather is warm and the island is alive with tourists.

Q: What currency is used in Rhodes Town? A: The currency used in Rhodes Town is the Euro.

Q: Is Rhodes Town accessible for disabled visitors? A: Some parts of Rhodes Town may be challenging for disabled visitors due to uneven streets and stairs. However, most tourist sites are equipped to accommodate disabled visitors.

Q: What language is spoken in Rhodes Town? A: The official language in Greece is Greek. English is widely spoken in Rhodes Town due to the high number of tourists.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Rhodes Town

1. The Palace of the Grand Master of the Knights of Rhodes The Palace of the Grand Master is a medieval castle located in the old town of Rhodes. It was built in the 14th century and used by the Knights of Rhodes as their headquarters. Today, it’s a museum that houses a collection of ancient artifacts and art.

2. The Archaeological Museum of Rhodes The Archaeological Museum of Rhodes is located in the Old Town and houses an extensive collection of artifacts from ancient Rhodes. The museum showcases the history of the island from the Neolithic period to the Byzantine era.

3. The Acropolis of Rhodes The Acropolis of Rhodes is a hilltop fortress that offers stunning views of the city and the Aegean Sea. The site includes the remains of an ancient temple, an odeon and a stadium.

4. The Street of the Knights The Street of the Knights is a cobblestone street that runs through the Old Town of Rhodes. It’s lined with medieval buildings that were used by the Knights of Rhodes. Today, the buildings house restaurants and shops that attract tourists.

5. The Clock Tower The Clock Tower is a landmark of Rhodes Town. It stands at the entrance to the old town and offers a panoramic view of the city.

6. The Rhodes Jewish Museum The Rhodes Jewish Museum is located in the Jewish quarter of the Old Town. It showcases the history of the Jewish community in Rhodes and the contributions they made to the island.

7. The Rhodes Aquarium The Rhodes Aquarium is a small, but impressive aquarium that features exhibits of marine life found in the Aegean Sea. The museum also has a small botanical garden.

8. Elli Beach Elli Beach is one of the most popular beaches in Rhodes Town. It’s a pebbled beach located in the center of the city and offers visitors crystal-clear waters.

9. Mandraki Harbor Mandraki Harbor is the main harbor of Rhodes Town where visitors can take boat tours of the island. The harbor is also home to the famous Colossus of Rhodes, one of the ancient world’s Seven Wonders.

10. St. Paul’s Gate St. Paul’s Gate is one of the seven gates of the Old Town. It’s located on the west side of the city and was named after St. Paul, who is said to have entered the city through the gate.

Final Thoughts

Rhodes Town is a gem of the Aegean Sea. It’s full of history, culture and natural beauty. The stunning beaches, charming old town and ancient monuments make it a must-visit destination for tourists. Whether you’re interested in learning about ancient history, soaking up the sun on a beautiful beach or just wandering through the narrow streets of the Old Town, Rhodes Town has something for everyone.
