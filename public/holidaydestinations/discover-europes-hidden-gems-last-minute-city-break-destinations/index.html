Discover Europe’s Hidden Gems: Last Minute City Break Destinations

Looking for a quick getaway but want to avoid the crowds of popular tourist destinations? Europe is full of hidden gems that offer everything from historic charm to modern cityscapes. Whether you’re looking for a romantic weekend for two or a solo adventure, these last minute city break destinations will leave you feeling refreshed and rejuvenated.

1. Porto, Portugal

Situated on the banks of the Douro River, Porto is a charming city that combines historic architecture with a vibrant and lively atmosphere. Known for its stunning wine cellars, ancient churches, and bold street art, Porto is the perfect city break destination for those who want to explore a hidden gem off the beaten path.

Top things to do in Porto:

- Take a tour of the famous Port wine cellars and learn about the history of the region’s wine making process. - Visit the iconic Ribeira Square and enjoy the stunning views of the Douro River. - Wander through the winding streets of the historic Ribeira neighbourhood and see the colourful buildings and tiled facades. - Check out the street art scene and visit the free-spirited Plaza de las Cardosas. - Take a stroll through the Jardins do Palácio de Cristal and admire the beautiful gardens and spectacular views of the river.

2. Riga, Latvia

Riga is a city full of surprises, from its Art Nouveau architecture to its vast range of museums and galleries. Situated on the Baltic Sea, Riga offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. This Baltic gem is the perfect city break destination for those who want to explore a city rich in history and culture.

Top things to do in Riga:

- Visit the stunning Art Nouveau district and see the beautiful buildings and facades that are unique to Riga. - Explore the Old Town and see the city’s historic centre, including the Riga Castle and the famous St. Peter’s Church. - Check out the National Museum of Art and see the collection of Latvian art and masterpieces from other European artists. - Visit the famous Riga Central Market and see the bustling marketplace that offers a variety of local delicacies and traditional crafts. - Take a stroll through the city’s many parks, including Bastejkalns and Vērmanes Garden, and enjoy the natural beauty of the city.

3. Ghent, Belgium

With its stunning architecture, picturesque canals, and delicious cuisine, Ghent is a hidden gem that is often overlooked by tourists visiting Belgium. This charming city offers a unique blend of medieval and modern features that make it the perfect destination for a last minute city break.

Top things to do in Ghent:

- Take a boat tour of the city’s canals and see the beautiful architecture and landmarks from a different perspective. - Visit the city’s many museums and galleries, including the Museum of Fine Arts and the Design Museum Gent. - Check out the famous Ghent Altarpiece and see the incredible artwork that has been the subject of much debate and mystery. - Wander through the historic city centre and see the impressive Gravensteen castle and the famous Belfry tower. - Enjoy the local cuisine, including the famous Ghent waterzooi and the delicious Belgian waffles.

FAQs

Q: How do I find last minute deals for a city break?

A: There are several websites that offer last minute deals on city breaks, including Expedia, Booking.com, and Lastminute.com. You can also check with individual airlines and hotels for special offers and deals.

Q: How much does a city break in Europe cost?

A: The cost of a city break in Europe can vary depending on the destination, the time of year, and the type of accommodation you choose. On average, a city break in Europe can cost anywhere from £200 to £500 or more per person, depending on the factors listed above.

Q: What’s the best time of year to visit these destinations?

A: The best time of year to visit these destinations can vary depending on the climate and the activities you plan on doing. Porto and Riga are best visited in the spring or autumn when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. Ghent is best visited in the summer when the weather is warm and sunny.

Q: Do I need a visa to visit these destinations?

A: This will depend on your country of origin and the destination you plan on visiting. It’s important to check with the relevant embassy or consulate to confirm whether a visa is required. For example, UK citizens will not need a visa to visit any of these destinations.
