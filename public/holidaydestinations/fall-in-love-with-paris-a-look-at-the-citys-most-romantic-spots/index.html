Paris—the city of love, lights, and romance. Whether you’re visiting for the first time, returning for another visit or even a resident, this city provides infinite romantic activities, destinations and charm that will make your heart flutter.

With its breathtaking architecture, world-renowned cuisine, romantic language and lovely gardens, Paris sets the perfect scene for a luxurious and romantic vacation. Here are some of the most romantic spots in Paris that will have you falling in love all over again.

Pont des Arts

What can be more romantic than the idea of attaching a love lock to a bridge over the river Seine? The Pont des Arts, also known as the Love Lock Bridge, has long been one of the most romantic spots in Paris. It’s the perfect symbol of putting your love on display for the world to see.

Montmartre

Montmartre is a charming and bohemian neighborhood located in the north of Paris. Known for its steep streets, impressive views of the city and world-famous Sacre-Coeur Basilica, it’s a romantic spot for couples. You and your significant other can wander Montmartre’s narrow streets, enjoy the street art and explore the Montmartre Museum.

Seine River Cruise

Take a romantic boat ride along the Seine River to see Paris from a different angle. The Seine is the perfect way to explore the city and its famous landmarks, including the Eiffel Tower, Notre Dame Cathedral and Pont Neuf. You can also watch the sunset and sip champagne while seeing the city lights up at night.

Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is central to many couples visiting Paris as it’s one of the world’s most iconic and famous landmarks. The view overlooking the city from the top at night is spectacular and romantic for a proposal, anniversary or just to enjoy with your loved one. Another option is to have a picnic underneath the Eiffel Tower, just a few steps from the tower’s base.

Jardin des Tuileries

Located between the Louvre Museum and Place de la Concorde, Jardin des Tuileries is Paris’s most significant royal garden. Take a leisurely walk and admire the tranquil beauty of the surrounding landscape, fountains, sculptures, and ponds.

Le Marais

Le Marais is an historic and trendy neighborhood located in the heart of Paris. It’s a perfect blend of old-world charm and modern-day luxury. The quaint cafes, restaurants, and boutiques offer a unique romantic atmosphere you can’t find anywhere else.

Place des Vosges

If you’re looking for a quiet and peaceful spot in Paris, Place des Vosges is the perfect spot. With its stunning arcades, lovely gardens and world-class restaurants, It’s a great location for a romantic picnic or a quiet walk, hand in hand with your partner.

Musée Rodin

Musée Rodin has the most extensive collection of works by the famous sculptor Auguste Rodin. It’s a perfect place to spend an afternoon admiring his masterpieces, and enjoying the surrounding gardens. Be sure to visit “Le Baiser” or The Kiss, which is one of Rodin’s most famous sculptures.

Palais Royal Gardens

Palais Royal Gardens was first built for Cardinal Richelieu’s personal use, and now it’s a perfect spot for a romantic stroll. It’s a peaceful and quiet place with an elegant garden, fountains, sculptures and the famous black and white striped columns at the entrance.

Canal Saint-Martin

The Canal Saint-Martin is an exciting place to spend a romantic evening. You can take a canal boat ride, explore the surrounding trendy bars and restaurants, or go for a leisurely walk along the waterway, hand in hand with your partner.

FAQs - Most asked questions and answers about Paris’s most romantic spots

Q. What are the most romantic spots in Paris? A. The most romantic spots in Paris include the Pont des Arts, Montmartre, Seine River Cruise, Eiffel Tower, Jardin des Tuileries, Le Marais, Place des Vosges, Musée Rodin, Palais Royal Gardens, and Canal Saint-Martin.

Q. What are some other romantic things to do in Paris? A. Some other romantic things to do in Paris include a visit to the Palace of Versailles, visit a traditional French bakery, stroll along the Champs-Elysées, visit the Sainte-Chapelle, and take a cooking class together.

Q. When is the best time to visit Paris for a romantic vacation? A. Paris is suitable for romantic vacations year-round. However, the best time to visit is during the spring and autumn seasons when the city’s gardens are in full bloom, the weather is temperate, and the tourist crowds have thinned out.

Q. What are some romantic restaurants to visit in Paris? A. Some restaurants that are known for their romantic atmosphere include Le Jules Verne at the Eiffel Tower, Le Comptoir du Relais in Le Marais, Le Procope in Saint-Germain-des-Prés, and Lapérouse in the 1st arrondissement.
