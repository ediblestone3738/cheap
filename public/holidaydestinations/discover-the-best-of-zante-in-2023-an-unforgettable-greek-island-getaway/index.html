[ad_1] Discover the Best of Zante in 2023: An Unforgettable Greek Island Getaway

Located in the Ionian Sea, Zakynthos or Zante is a stunning Greek island that is popular for its natural beauty that boasts stunning sandy coves, crystal-clear waters, dramatic cliffs, and lush greenery. Blessed with a Mediterranean climate, you can enjoy Zante’s beauty year-round, but it’s best to visit from mid-June to mid-September to experience the full glory of the island’s summer. With so much to see and do, here’s what you can expect from a visit to Discover the Best of Zante in 2023.

Getting to Zante

Zante is easily accessible via the international airport located in the southern part of the island. You can fly direct from most cities in Europe, making Zante an easy and affordable destination to reach. Once you arrive, there are several options to get around the island, including car rentals, buses, scooters, or bicycles. The island is not too big, so it’s also possible to explore on foot.

Accommodations

There are plenty of accommodation options in Zante. You can find everything from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts. Most tourists choose to stay in the Zakynthos town or the popular resort areas like Laganas, Tsilivi, or Alykanas. If you’re looking for a quieter and more secluded experience, check out the more tranquil areas like Vassilikos, Porto Roxa, Porto Limnionas, or Keri.

Top attractions

Here are the top 10 tourist attractions located in Discover the Best of Zante in 2023:

1. Navagio Beach - This stunning beach, also known as Shipwreck Beach or Smuggler’s Cove, is one of Zante’s most iconic sights. Famous for the wrecked ship that lies on its shores, Navagio Beach boasts crystal clear turquoise waters and stunning white sand. You can access this beach via boat or by hiking down the cliffside.

2. Blue Caves - The Blue Caves are a series of natural sea caves located in the northern part of the island, and they are known for their stunning blue waters. You can take a boat tour to explore the caves and even take a swim in the crystal-clear waters.

3. Turtles’ Island - Zante is home to the endangered loggerhead turtle, and you can take a boat trip to Turtle Island and spot these majestic creatures in their natural habitat. Be sure to respect their natural environment and abide by responsible tourism practices.

4. Zakynthos Town - The island’s capital city is full of Venetian architecture, charming squares, and narrow streets. You can explore the town’s cultural heritage and enjoy authentic Greek cuisine in one of its many restaurants.

5. Keri Caves - Located in the southern part of the island, the Keri Caves are a series of stunning sea caves that can only be reached by boat. The caves are known for their crystal-clear waters and dramatic cliffs, making them a perfect spot for snorkeling or diving.

6. Bochali - This traditional hillside village located just outside of Zakynthos Town offers some of the best panoramic views of the island. You can enjoy the stunning views of the Ionian Sea while dining on a rooftop terrace of one of the village’s many restaurants.

7. Solomos Square - This charming square located in Zakynthos Town is the heart of the city’s cultural life. You can enjoy an evening stroll, admire the square’s stunning architecture, or even attend a concert or cultural event.

8. Monastery of Saint Dionysios - One of the island’s most significant religious landmarks, the Monastery of Saint Dionysios is dedicated to the island’s patron saint. You can admire the stunning frescoes and mosaics and even visit the saint’s relics.

9. Laganas Bay - Known for its bustling nightlife scene, Laganas Bay offers stunning sandy beaches and calm azure waters, perfect for swimming or sunbathing. But do remember to respect the natural environment and abide by responsible tourism practices.

10. Alykes - This quaint fishing village located on the northeast coast of the island offers miles of sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and plenty of water sports activities. The village is also known for its traditional tavernas and charming local markets.

FAQs about Zante

Q: What language do the locals speak in Zante? A: Greek is the official language, but most people speak English fluently, especially in tourist areas.

Q: What is the currency in Zante, and do they accept cards? A: The main currency is Euros, and most places do accept credit cards. But it’s always good to carry cash, especially in smaller villages where card payment may not be available.

Q: Do I need a visa to visit Zante? A: Citizens from most countries can enter Greece without a visa for up to 90 days. Be sure to check with your embassy or consulate for the latest information.

Q: What is the best time to visit Zante? A: The best time to visit Zante is between mid-June to mid-September when the weather is warm and sunny.

Q: Is Zante a safe destination? A: Zante is generally a safe destination, but it’s always good to take common safety precautions when travelling, such as not carrying too much cash, not leaving valuables unattended, and following local advice.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for an unforgettable Greek island getaway, Zante offers stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and plenty of exciting activities for all types of travelers. With so much to see and do, a visit to Discover the Best of Zante in 2023 is sure to be a memorable experience. Whether you’re a beach lover, a history buff, or a nature enthusiast, you’ll find something to love in Zante. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable adventure! [ad_2]
