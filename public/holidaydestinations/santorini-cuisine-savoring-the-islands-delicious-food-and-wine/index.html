[ad_1] Santorini is a small Greek island that has been attracting visitors from all around the world for years. The island’s culinary scene is diverse and definitely worth exploring, especially if you’re a foodie or a wine lover. The island’s cuisine is a combination of traditional Greek flavors and influences from neighboring countries such as Turkey and Italy. Here’s everything you need to know about Santorini cuisine.

Savoring the Island’s Delicious Food and Wine

Santorini is known for its seafood, which you can find in almost every taverna on the island. The island’s warm waters are home to a variety of fish, such as sea bream, red mullet, and octopus. Grilled fish is a popular dish on the island, and it’s usually served with a side of Santorini’s famous cherry tomatoes and capers.

Tomatokeftedes is another popular dish that you must try while in Santorini. They are deep-fried tomato balls that are filled with mint, onion, and feta cheese. They’re crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, and they’re usually served as an appetizer.

Santorini is also known for its fava dip, which is made from yellow split peas that are boiled and then mashed with olive oil, lemon juice, and onions. It’s usually served as a dip with pita bread or crackers.

Wine lovers will also enjoy the island’s famous wines. Santorini is home to some of the oldest vineyards in Europe, and the island’s volcanic soil gives its wines a unique taste. The island’s signature grape is the Assyrtiko, which produces a dry white wine that pairs perfectly with seafood.

Santorini’s wineries are scattered throughout the island, and many of them offer guided tours and tastings. Some of the most popular wineries on the island include Santo Wines, Venetsanos Winery, and Gaia Wines.

2. Akrotiri Archaeological Site: This ancient Minoan settlement was buried beneath volcanic ash during a massive eruption around 1627 BC. Today, visitors can explore the remains of this fascinating site.

3. Red Beach: This unique beach is known for its bright red sand and crystal-clear waters.

4. Ancient Thera: This ancient city was built atop a hill around the 9th century BC and was inhabited until the 8th century AD. Visitors can explore the ruins of this fascinating site.

5. Nea Kameni: This volcanic island is part of the Santorini caldera and is home to a dormant volcano. Visitors can hike to the summit and take in the magnificent views.
